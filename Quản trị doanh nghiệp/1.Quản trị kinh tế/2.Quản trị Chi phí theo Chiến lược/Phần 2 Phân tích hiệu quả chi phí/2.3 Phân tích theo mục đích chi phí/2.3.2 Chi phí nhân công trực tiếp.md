### Chi Phí Nhân Công Trực Tiếp (Direct Labour Cost)

Direct labour là **lao động trực tiếp tạo ra sản phẩm/dịch vụ** — có thể truy vết đến từng sản phẩm. Phân biệt với indirect labour (supervision, maintenance, quality control) — là overhead.

> **Bài học:** Direct labour cost management không phải là "trả lương thấp" — là tối ưu productivity: đúng người, đúng việc, đúng thời điểm, với đủ tool và training để làm việc hiệu quả nhất.

---

**Direct Labour Rate — cấu trúc đầy đủ:**

```
Gross wage:                    100%
+ BHXH employer (17.5%):        17.5%
+ BHYT employer (3%):            3%
+ BHTN employer (1%):            1%
+ Phụ cấp (nếu applicable):     varies
─────────────────────────────────────
Employer cost:                 ~122–125% of gross

Ví dụ: Worker lương gross 8 triệu/tháng
  True cost: 8 × 1.225 = 9.8 triệu/tháng
  Working hours: 22 ngày × 8h = 176h/tháng
  Direct labour rate: 9,800,000 / 176 = 55,682 VNĐ/giờ
```

---

**Labour Efficiency — Standard vs Actual:**

```
Standard Time (thời gian tiêu chuẩn): Thời gian "nên là" để làm 1 đơn vị
  → Xác định qua: time study, work measurement, historical data

Labour Rate Variance (LRV):
  = (Standard Rate − Actual Rate) × Actual Hours

Labour Efficiency Variance (LEV):
  = (Standard Hours − Actual Hours) × Standard Rate

Ví dụ:
  Standard: 0.5h/đv tại 55,000 VNĐ/h
  Thực tế sản xuất 1,000 đv, dùng 580h, trả 57,000 VNĐ/h

  LRV = (55,000 − 57,000) × 580 = −1,160,000 (U) → Trả cao hơn standard
  LEV = (500h − 580h) × 55,000 = −4,400,000 (U) → Chậm hơn standard
```

---

**Idle Time và Labour Efficiency:**

```
Total available hours (paid) = 580h
  − Idle time (machine breakdown, waiting for materials): 30h
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Productive hours: 550h (actual)
  Standard allowed: 500h

Labour Efficiency Variance separates:
  Idle time variance = −30h × 55,000 = −1,650,000 (machines, materials issue)
  Pure efficiency variance = (500h − 550h) × 55,000 = −2,750,000 (worker speed)
```

Phân tách giúp attribute variance đúng: idle time là operations issue (supply/maintenance); pure efficiency là HR/training issue.

---

**Productivity metrics trong direct labour:**

| Metric | Công thức | Đọc thế nào |
|---|---|---|
| **Labour productivity** | Output / Labour hours | Higher = more efficient |
| **Revenue per employee** | Revenue / Total headcount | Blended; higher = better |
| **Direct labour ratio** | Direct labour cost / COGS | Control target range |
| **OT ratio** | OT hours / Total hours | High % → capacity problem or planning issue |
| **Attendance rate** | Actual days / Available days | Absenteeism indicator |

**Automation vs Labour — decision framework:**

```
Should we automate task X?

Annual labour cost:   = FTE × annual cost × hours on task
Annual automation cost: = Equipment cost/useful life + maintenance + energy

Payback period = Automation investment / Annual savings

Rule of thumb:
  Payback <2 years → Strong case
  Payback 2–5 years → Evaluate strategically
  Payback >5 years → Usually marginal unless strategic rationale
```

**VN labour context:** Tối thiểu lương tăng ~5–7%/năm → automation economics improve over time. Machine cost: relatively stable or declining. Human cost: rising. Investment in automation that looks marginal today may be attractive in 3 years.

---

**Lean Labour Practices:**

```
Standardized Work:
  Document exact method for each task → reduces variability → more predictable cost

Cross-training:
  Workers trained for multiple tasks → flexible deployment → reduce idle time

Visual Management:
  Show output targets, current performance, and problems visibly
  → Workers self-monitor and correct → less supervision cost

Andon cord (or equivalent):
  Worker can flag problem immediately → stop line, fix before it compounds
  → Prevents defect escalation → reduces rework cost
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** VN's manufacturing competitiveness has relied heavily on low labour cost vs China/other markets. But rising wages (2x increase in minimum wage 2010–2023) and increasing competition from Bangladesh, Indonesia, Myanmar mean pure labour cost advantage is eroding. Future competitiveness requires: higher productivity per labour hour, automation, and moving up the value chain. Companies that invest in training and process standardization now build the human capital that enables this transition.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Measure labour cost without measuring labour output. "Our labour cost is 150 triệu/tháng" is meaningless without: how many units produced? At what quality level? Efficiency = Output per unit of labour input. Cost per unit produced is the relevant metric.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Not distinguish between direct and indirect labour in COGS. Supervisors, quality inspectors, material handlers are indirect labour → overhead. Mixing them with direct labour distorts product cost and makes it impossible to identify where productivity improvement opportunities lie.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Reduce direct labour cost by cutting training budget. Short-term: saves 10 triệu/month training cost. Long-term: skill degradation → higher defect rate → more rework → more material waste → potentially loses customer. Training là investment với return measured in quality, productivity, and retention — not just a cost.
