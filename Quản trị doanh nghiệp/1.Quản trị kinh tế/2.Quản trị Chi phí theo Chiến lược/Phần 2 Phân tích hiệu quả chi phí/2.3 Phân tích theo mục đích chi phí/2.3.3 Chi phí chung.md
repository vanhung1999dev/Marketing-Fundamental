### Chi Phí Chung (Overhead Costs)

Chi phí chung (overhead) là những chi phí **không thể truy vết trực tiếp đến một sản phẩm/dịch vụ cụ thể** — phát sinh để support hoạt động tổng thể nhưng không "đi vào" một đơn vị sản phẩm cụ thể.

Overhead thường là nguồn gốc của mọi debate và tranh luận về cost allocation — vì không có cách "đúng" duy nhất để phân bổ.

> **Bài học:** Overhead không thể được "quản lý" bằng cách ignore nó hay allocate nó đi cho product rồi thôi. Overhead phải được questioned (là value-adding không?), measured (đang tăng tốc nhanh hơn revenue không?), và controlled (có authority và accountability rõ ràng không?).

---

**Phân loại overhead:**

**Manufacturing Overhead (Production Overhead):**
- Lương supervisor sản xuất, QC, maintenance
- Điện, nước, gas cho nhà máy
- Khấu hao máy móc, thiết bị sản xuất
- Factory rent và utilities
- MRO (maintenance, repair, operations) supplies

**Non-Manufacturing Overhead:**
- Selling overhead: văn phòng sales, marketing support
- Administrative overhead: kế toán, HR, IT, CEO office
- Financial overhead: bank charges, insurance

---

**Overhead Absorption — phân bổ vào sản phẩm:**

**Traditional: Direct Labour Hour Rate (DLHR):**
```
Budgeted overhead:        500 triệu
Budgeted DL hours:        10,000h
Overhead absorption rate: 500,000,000 / 10,000 = 50,000 VNĐ/DL hour

Product A needs 0.5h DL → absorbs 25,000 overhead
Product B needs 2.0h DL → absorbs 100,000 overhead
```

**Problem with DLHR in modern manufacturing:**
- Manufacturing ngày càng automated → labour hours giảm, machine hours tăng
- Overhead không correlate với DL hours nữa → distorted product cost

**Better alternative: Machine Hour Rate**
```
For automated production:
Overhead rate = Budgeted overhead / Budgeted machine hours
```

**Best alternative: Activity-Based Costing (ABC):**
```
Instead of one rate → multiple rates based on cost drivers:

Cost Pool: Setup costs          → Cost driver: # of production runs
Rate: 2,000,000 VNĐ per run

Cost Pool: Quality inspection   → Cost driver: # of inspections
Rate: 500,000 VNĐ per inspection

Cost Pool: Procurement          → Cost driver: # of POs
Rate: 300,000 VNĐ per PO

Product X: 2 runs, 5 inspections, 10 POs
  Setup: 2 × 2M = 4M
  QC: 5 × 0.5M = 2.5M
  Procurement: 10 × 0.3M = 3M
  Total overhead allocated = 9.5M for batch of X units
```

---

**Over/Under Absorption:**

```
If actual overhead > absorbed overhead → Under-absorbed → charged to P&L as expense
If actual overhead < absorbed overhead → Over-absorbed → credit to P&L (unusual "profit")

Ví dụ:
  Absorbed (rate × actual hours): 450 triệu
  Actual overhead incurred:        510 triệu
  Under-absorbed:                   60 triệu → additional P&L charge

  Common cause of under-absorption: lower volume than budgeted → fewer hours → less absorbed
```

**Overhead cost management best practices:**

**1. Question every overhead bucket:**
```
For each overhead cost:
  □ Who uses this service?
  □ What would happen if we removed/reduced it?
  □ Is there a cheaper alternative?
  □ Is this growing faster than revenue? Why?
```

**2. Zero-based overhead review (annual):**
- Every overhead category must be justified from zero
- Particularly: subscriptions, memberships, allocated staff
- "We've always had it" is not a justification

**3. Overhead ratio tracking:**
```
Overhead / Revenue should trend downward as business scales
(Fixed overhead spread over more revenue = economies of scale)

Red flag: Overhead grows faster than revenue = operating deleverage
Target: Overhead ratio stable or declining
```

**4. Shared Service Cost Allocation:**
```
IT cost per user = Total IT cost / Total users → allocate to departments by headcount
Finance overhead = allocate to BUs by % of revenue or transaction volume
HR overhead = allocate by headcount
```

**Lean overhead thinking:**

Apply TIMWOOD waste to overhead functions:
- **Waiting:** Reports taking 2 weeks when they could take 2 days
- **Over-processing:** 4 approvals when 1 suffices
- **Motion:** Finance team manually entering data that could be automated
- **Defects:** Errors in payroll requiring reprocessing

Goal: zero non-value-adding overhead. Every overhead function must answer: "What would customers pay for this if they knew we were doing it?"

> **Phân tích sâu:** Emerson Electric under Chuck Knight (CEO 1973–2000) had legendary cost discipline: every year, business unit managers had to justify every overhead dollar, and "no such thing as a permanently good overhead structure." Result: Emerson achieved 43 consecutive years of earnings growth. Their approach: overhead is not inevitable — it's management's choice, and it must continuously earn its place.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Treat overhead as "fixed" and don't challenge it. Overhead is not fixed by nature — it's fixed by management inaction. Many overhead costs can be reduced or eliminated: subscriptions not used, headcount in non-value-add roles, space being underutilized. They don't reduce themselves; requires active management decision.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Apply blanket % cut to all overhead. "Cut all overhead by 10%." Some overhead is critical (IT security, compliance, quality) and cutting creates disproportionate risk. Others are truly wasteful. Surgical approach: zero-base the overhead, identify what drives value, protect that, eliminate waste.

> **Cạm bẫy:** ABC complexity. Activity-based costing can be overly complex for SME — implementing ABC with 50 cost pools and 30 activities in a 50-person company is overkill. Match the sophistication of cost system to the complexity of the business and the decisions it needs to support. Simple, consistent, and used beats sophisticated and ignored.
