### Các Chi Phí Không Liên Quan (Non-Relevant Costs)

Nhận diện chi phí KHÔNG liên quan quan trọng không kém nhận diện chi phí liên quan. Đưa non-relevant costs vào analysis → distorted decisions.

> **Bài học:** Nhiều quyết định sai trong business xảy ra vì người ta include "cost" của thứ họ đã có (sunk) hoặc sẽ xảy ra dù thế nào (unavoidable fixed). Result: reject profitable opportunities hoặc accept bad ones. Discipline in cost relevance filtering = better decisions.

---

**Hai loại chi phí không liên quan quan trọng nhất:**

**1. Sunk Costs:**
Đã phát sinh, không thể recover. Không liên quan đến future decisions.

| Ví dụ Sunk Cost | Tại sao không relevant |
|---|---|
| Research cost đã bỏ ra cho project bị cancel | Money đã chi — cannot be recovered |
| Giá mua NVL đã mua | Purchase price is past; current decision only affects future use |
| Training cost của nhân viên vừa trained | Training already occurred — cannot undo |
| Depreciation của máy móc đã mua | Capital outflow already happened |

**Common sunk cost traps:**
```
"Chúng ta đã invest 2 tỷ vào hệ thống này — phải tiếp tục dùng"
→ Wrong: 2 tỷ is gone. Question: Is continuing FUTURE investment worthwhile?

"Chúng ta đã mua NVL với giá cao — phải bán sản phẩm với giá cao hơn"
→ Wrong: Sunk cost doesn't affect optimal selling price. Market determines price.
   If can't sell at price that covers FUTURE variable costs → sell at whatever recovers some value.
```

**2. Allocated/Absorbed Fixed Overhead:**
Chi phí gián tiếp cố định được phân bổ cho sản phẩm/dịch vụ — không thay đổi khi quyết định short-term.

```
Ví dụ: Factory overhead absorbed = 50,000/đv

Order analysis:
  Revenue: 180,000
  Variable cost: 80,000
  Allocated overhead: 50,000 (allocated, not additional)
  "Profit" = 50,000

BUT relevant analysis:
  Revenue: 180,000
  Variable cost: 80,000
  CONTRIBUTION = 100,000 (positive!)

  Allocated overhead 50,000 sẽ xảy ra dù accept hay reject order
  → NOT relevant to this decision
  → ACCEPT the order (CM positive = contributes to covering fixed costs)
```

---

**Non-cash costs — thường không relevant trong short-term:**

| Non-cash cost | Relevant? | Context |
|---|---|---|
| Depreciation | Usually NOT | Cash outflow already happened (capex) |
| Amortization | Usually NOT | Same as depreciation |
| Provisions/Accruals | Usually NOT | Non-cash accounting estimate |
| Impairment charges | Usually NOT | Writing down asset value |

**Exception:** Depreciation CAN be relevant when:
- The decision involves whether to use or sell/scrap the asset (different treatment)
- For long-term strategic decisions, opportunity cost of asset tied up in production

---

**Committed vs Discretionary Fixed Costs:**

| | Committed Fixed | Discretionary Fixed |
|---|---|---|
| **Thay đổi được không?** | Short-term: No; Long-term: Maybe | Yes — management choice |
| **Ví dụ** | Rent (contracted), bank loan payments | Advertising, training, R&D |
| **Relevant for?** | Not relevant for short-term decisions | Relevant if decision changes spending |

**Áp dụng:**
- Cut advertising budget → CM from reduced brand awareness impact (indirect effect)
- Advertising itself: discretionary → relevant IF you're deciding whether to run the campaign
- Factory rent (committed): NOT relevant to whether to accept a one-off order

---

**Common mistakes in relevant cost analysis:**

```
MISTAKE 1: Include depreciation in special order pricing
→ "Product costs 90K including 30K depreciation → need 90K minimum price"
→ Wrong: Depreciation not relevant. Minimum price = variable cost only for idle capacity.

MISTAKE 2: Reject profitable order because it doesn't "cover full cost"
→ "Full cost is 120K, order price is 100K → reject"
→ Wrong if: CM > 0, capacity idle, no better alternative
→ Order contributes 100K − variable cost (say 60K) = 40K toward fixed costs

MISTAKE 3: Accept order that seems profitable but ignores opportunity cost
→ "Order price 100K, variable cost 60K → CM 40K → accept"
→ Wrong if: displaces regular sales with CM 70K → real cost = 60K + 70K opp. cost = 130K > 100K
```

---

**Decision framework — short-term relevant cost:**

```
Step 1: Identify the decision
Step 2: For each cost — ask: "Would this cost CHANGE if we make this decision?"
  YES → RELEVANT
  NO  → NOT RELEVANT
Step 3: Include opportunity costs of alternatives foregone
Step 4: Make decision based on relevant costs only
Step 5: Sanity check: does this make business sense?
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Amazon Web Services (AWS) pricing strategy is a masterclass in relevant cost thinking. AWS prices at incremental cost of serving additional customers (server marginal cost is near-zero for capacity already built). This lets AWS dominate on price while covering fixed costs across massive volume. Traditional IT companies pricing at "full cost" (including overhead) couldn't compete. Cost relevance is competitive strategy.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Use "full costing" for all decisions. Full cost allocation is important for pricing strategy and profitability analysis, but it's wrong for incremental decisions (special orders, short-term capacity utilization, make vs buy). Different decisions need different cost frameworks.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** When rejecting a project due to sunk costs, forget the opportunity cost of NOT doing something new. "We've invested so much in our current approach, can't change." This sunk cost reasoning prevents pivoting to better opportunities. Sunk cost should be ignored; opportunity cost of staying on current path must be assessed.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Confuse "unavoidable cost" with "irrelevant cost." Some fixed costs are avoidable IF you close a division or exit a market — then they ARE relevant to that long-term decision. "Factory rent is fixed, so irrelevant" — true for short-term incremental decisions; NOT true if decision is "close the factory." Context determines relevance.
