### KPI và Dữ Liệu Chi Phí

KPI (Key Performance Indicator) chi phí là những chỉ số cụ thể, đo lường được, giúp track tiến độ của cost management objectives. Không phải mọi metric đều là KPI — KPI là những gì truly matter nhất cho business outcomes.

> **Bài học:** 5 KPI được theo dõi và acted upon hàng tuần tốt hơn 50 KPI trong dashboard không ai xem. KPI phải be actionable: khi metric đỏ, phải có người biết mình cần làm gì.

---

**Leading vs Lagging KPIs trong cost management:**

| | Leading KPI (Input/Process) | Lagging KPI (Output/Outcome) |
|---|---|---|
| **Timing** | Predict future cost | Report past cost |
| **Controllability** | High — có thể act now | Low — đã xảy ra rồi |
| **Ví dụ** | Utilization rate, OT hours, defect rate | Cost per unit, Gross Margin, OpEx/Revenue |
| **Value** | Early warning | Track achievement |

**Nguyên tắc:** Balance leading và lagging. Chỉ có lagging → reactive. Chỉ có leading → no accountability for outcomes.

---

**5 KPI chi phí cần thiết nhất cho SME VN:**

**1. Gross Margin (%) — tháng và YTD**
```
= (Revenue − COGS) / Revenue × 100%
Target: Không dưới ngưỡng chiến lược (ví dụ: không <35%)
Owner: CFO + COO
```

**2. Operating Expense Ratio (%)**
```
= (SG&A + Other OpEx) / Revenue × 100%
Target: Tương quan với revenue growth (có leverage không?)
Owner: CFO
```

**3. Cost per Unit (sản xuất) hoặc Cost per Transaction (dịch vụ)**
```
Sản xuất: Total production cost / Units produced
Dịch vụ: Total service cost / Tickets/Orders served
Target: Not above standard cost; ideally trending down
Owner: Operations Manager
```

**4. Variance vs Budget (%)**
```
= (Actual − Budget) / Budget × 100%
Flag khi: > ±5% cho material items
Owner: Department heads
```

**5. Cash Burn Rate (nếu pre-profitability)**
```
= Monthly cash outflow − Monthly cash inflow
Runway = Current cash / Monthly net burn
Target: Runway > 12 months
Owner: CFO, CEO
```

**Data infrastructure cho cost KPIs:**

```
Source data:
  ERP → Trial balance, cost center reports
  HR system → Headcount, labour hours
  Production system → Units produced, defects, downtime
  CRM → Revenue, orders, customer metrics

Data flow:
  Source systems → ETL/Integration → Data warehouse → Reporting tool

Tools phổ biến VN:
  Basic: Excel + MISA exports (sufficient for <50 tỷ revenue)
  Mid: Power BI + MISA/SAP data
  Advanced: Custom analytics platform
```

**Review cadence:**

| Nhóm | Metrics | Frequency | Format |
|---|---|---|---|
| C-suite | Strategic: GM, EBITDA, OpEx ratio | Monthly | 1-page dashboard |
| Dept Heads | Variance vs budget, key cost drivers | Monthly | Dept-level P&L |
| Supervisors | Unit cost, OT, defects, utilization | Weekly | Operational report |

> **Phân tích sâu:** Google's OKR (Objectives and Key Results) framework applied to cost: "Reduce COGS ratio from 65% to 60% by end of year" is the Objective; "Reduce material waste from 8% to 4%" and "Achieve 92% equipment utilization" are the Key Results — leading indicators that drive the objective. This cascades accountability throughout organization. Each team knows how their work connects to the financial outcome.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** KPI dashboard chỉ có màu đỏ/xanh mà không có commentary. "Màu đỏ" tells you there's a problem. Commentary tells you WHY and WHAT's being done. Without commentary, dashboard is just an alarm without instructions.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Change KPIs frequently because they're uncomfortable. If gross margin is red → manager suggests removing it from dashboard or changing target. KPIs must be stable and honest. If a metric is consistently red → address the underlying problem, not the metric.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Vanity metrics — look good but don't correlate with outcomes. "Revenue per employee" sounds impressive but depends on industry and business model. "Website visits" for an offline business. Always ask: "If this metric improved, would we be confident our cost management is actually better?" If no → it may be a vanity metric.
