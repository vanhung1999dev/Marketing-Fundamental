### Mô Hình BSC Trong Lập Ngân Sách Chiến Lược

Balanced Scorecard (BSC) được sử dụng như cầu nối giữa chiến lược và ngân sách — đảm bảo rằng nguồn lực được phân bổ cho các hoạt động thực sự drive strategic objectives, không chỉ cho những gì dễ đo hoặc dễ được phê duyệt.

> **Bài học:** BSC không phải chỉ là một reporting framework — là resource allocation framework. Khi mỗi budget item được liên kết với một BSC objective, bạn có thể hỏi: "Nếu chúng ta cắt budget này, objective nào sẽ bị ảnh hưởng?" Điều đó biến budget cut từ "tùy tiện" thành "có căn cứ chiến lược".

---

**BSC-Budget Linkage — cơ chế:**

```
STEP 1: Strategy Map với BSC objectives
  Financial: Tăng EBITDA 20%; Revenue +25%
  Customer: NPS >55; Retention >85%
  Process: Launch 4 new SKUs; OTIF >95%
  L&G: ERP go-live; Sales training 40h/rep

STEP 2: Identify Strategic Initiatives for each objective
  Financial (Revenue +25%):
    → Initiative A: Enter Northern market — requires sales headcount + marketing
    → Initiative B: Launch e-commerce channel — requires tech + content investment

  Customer (NPS >55):
    → Initiative C: Customer success program — 2 FTEs + CRM module
    → Initiative D: Product quality improvement — QC upgrade + lab equipment

  L&G (ERP go-live):
    → Initiative E: ERP implementation — implementation partner + internal time
    → Initiative F: Sales training program — training budget + trainer cost

STEP 3: Cost each initiative
  Initiative A: 3 tỷ (headcount 2T + marketing 1T)
  Initiative B: 1.5 tỷ (tech 1T + content 0.5T)
  Initiative C: 0.8 tỷ (2 FTEs 0.6T + CRM 0.2T)
  ...

STEP 4: Prioritize within resource envelope
  Total available: 8 tỷ for strategic initiatives
  Ranked by impact × feasibility → select highest-return portfolio
```

---

**Strategic Initiative Portfolio Management:**

```
For each strategic initiative, document:
  □ Which BSC objective it supports
  □ Expected outcome (measurable)
  □ Budget required (one-time + ongoing)
  □ Timeline to realize benefit
  □ Owner (accountable person)
  □ Key dependencies (what must happen first?)
  □ Risk level (high/medium/low)

Portfolio view:
  Quick wins (low cost, fast payback): do these first
  Strategic bets (high investment, longer horizon): balance carefully
  Mandatory spend (compliance, maintenance): non-negotiable
  Low value spend (nice to have, weak strategic link): candidates for cut
```

**Balanced Portfolio Across BSC Perspectives:**

```
Common trap: Over-invest in Financial/Process initiatives;
             Under-invest in Learning & Growth

Why L&G gets underfunded:
  → Benefits are longer-term and less tangible
  → Easier to cut training budget than production budget
  → Short-term P&L pressure → cut what doesn't show immediately

Why L&G underfunding is dangerous:
  → L&G enables all other perspectives (it's the foundation)
  → Companies that consistently cut L&G fall behind on capability
  → Creates "capability debt" that becomes expensive to repay later

Healthy balance: ensure L&G initiatives are not all the first to be cut
                when budget is tight
```

---

**BSC Review Cadence in Budget Cycle:**

```
Annual: Set BSC objectives for year; allocate budget to initiatives
Quarterly: Review BSC scorecard; are initiatives on track?
  → Green: on track; no budget change needed
  → Yellow: at risk; may need reallocation or intervention
  → Red: off track; investigate; may need to reallocate budget

Mid-year reforecast: Adjust budget based on H1 actuals and H2 outlook
  → If H1 outperformed: are there upside investments to accelerate?
  → If H1 underperformed: which initiatives are deprioritized?
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Research by Kaplan & Norton on BSC implementation: organizations that explicitly link their budget to their Strategy Map and BSC achieve 35% higher strategy execution success rates. The mechanism: budget linkage forces clarity on priorities, exposes conflicts (too many initiatives, not enough resources), and creates accountability (if budget was allocated, results should follow).

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Build BSC separately from budget. "Here's our BSC scorecard (strategy team's work). Here's our budget (Finance's work)." Two separate documents with no explicit link → budget funds what's familiar, not what's strategic. Solution: in the budget template, require each major budget line to state which BSC objective it supports.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Too many strategic initiatives. "We have 47 strategic initiatives." With 47 initiatives, nothing is truly strategic — just a list of everything the company does. Discipline: 10–15 strategic initiatives maximum for a mid-size company. If initiative doesn't make the top 15, it's operational, not strategic.

> **Cạm bẫy:** BSC objectives set aspirationally but not funded realistically. "NPS target 70" but no customer success investment in budget. "Revenue +30%" but sales headcount stays flat. BSC without budget to back it is just wishful thinking. Every objective must have identifiable investment; every investment must connect to an objective.
