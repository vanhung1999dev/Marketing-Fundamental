### Tổng Quát Quy Trình Phân Tích Chiến Lược

Lập ngân sách chiến lược (Strategic Budgeting) khác về bản chất so với lập ngân sách hành chính truyền thống. Thay vì hỏi "Năm ngoái chi bao nhiêu, năm nay tăng bao nhiêu phần trăm?", ngân sách chiến lược hỏi "Mục tiêu chiến lược của chúng ta là gì, và cần phân bổ nguồn lực như thế nào để đạt được?"

> **Bài học:** Ngân sách là công cụ thực thi chiến lược — không phải công cụ kiểm soát chi tiêu. Khi ngân sách được lập bottom-up từ "đây là những gì tôi cần" thay vì top-down từ "đây là chiến lược chúng ta đang thực thi", ngân sách sẽ perpetuate status quo thay vì drive change.

---

**Strategic Budgeting vs Traditional Budgeting:**

| | Traditional | Strategic |
|---|---|---|
| **Điểm khởi đầu** | Năm ngoái + X% | Mục tiêu chiến lược |
| **Câu hỏi chính** | Chi phí này hợp lý không? | Investment này có giúp đạt chiến lược không? |
| **Horizon** | 1 năm | 3–5 năm với 1-năm chi tiết |
| **Flexibility** | Cứng nhắc sau khi duyệt | Rolling forecast; adjust mid-year |
| **Accountability** | Spend vs budget | Results vs strategic objectives |
| **Link to strategy** | Yếu — separate exercises | Mạnh — budget is operationalized strategy |

---

**Strategic Analysis Framework — 3 tầng:**

```
EXTERNAL ANALYSIS
  PESTEL: Political, Economic, Social, Tech, Environmental, Legal
  Porter's 5 Forces: competitive intensity; bargaining power; substitutes
  Market sizing: TAM, SAM, SOM
  Competitor benchmarking: capabilities, cost structure, positioning
        ↓
INTERNAL ANALYSIS
  SWOT synthesis: Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats
  Capability assessment: what do we do well vs competitors?
  Cost structure analysis: where is money being spent vs where it creates value?
  Financial baseline: where are we starting from?
        ↓
STRATEGIC DIRECTION
  Where to play: which markets, segments, geographies
  How to win: value proposition; competitive advantage
  What to invest: resource allocation to strategic priorities
  What to stop: activities not aligned with strategy (fund reallocation)
```

**Quy trình Strategic Analysis cho lập ngân sách:**

```
Step 1 (Tháng 8–9): External scan
  → Collect market data, competitor intelligence
  → Update macro assumptions (FX, inflation, labour cost)
  → Identify key uncertainties for scenario planning

Step 2 (Tháng 9): Internal review
  → Performance vs current-year budget: what worked, what didn't?
  → Capability gaps vs strategy requirements
  → Cost structure analysis: where is money going?

Step 3 (Tháng 9–10): Strategic priority setting
  → CEO/leadership team: reaffirm or adjust strategic direction
  → Set 3–5 strategic priorities for next year
  → Resource envelope: what is available to allocate?

Step 4 (Tháng 10–11): Budget build
  → Departments build from strategic priorities downward
  → Finance consolidates and tests for consistency
  → Scenario models: base, upside, downside

Step 5 (Tháng 11–12): Review and approval
  → Challenge sessions: are allocations driving right activities?
  → Board/CEO approval
  → Communication to all teams
```

---

**Scenario Planning trong Strategic Analysis:**

```
3 scenarios minimum:
  BASE CASE: Most likely outcome; central planning assumption
    → GDP growth on track; FX stable; market grows as expected
  
  UPSIDE CASE: Favorable conditions
    → Market grew faster than expected; new channel outperformed
    → What additional investments do we make? How fast?
  
  DOWNSIDE CASE: Adverse conditions
    → Economic slowdown; key customer lost; raw material spike
    → Where do we cut? What is protected? What is deferred?

Budget for base case; test downside resilience; identify upside optionality
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** McKinsey research on high-performing companies: they allocate resources significantly differently from the rest. Top performers are 2.6× more likely to reallocate resources actively (shifting >30% of capex between units over 10 years) than low performers. Low performers keep the same resource allocation year after year — funding the same activities regardless of strategic relevance. The budgeting process is where this active reallocation happens — or doesn't.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Treat strategic analysis as a separate exercise from budget building. "We did our 3-year strategy in June; now in October we build next year's budget." If the budget process doesn't reference the strategy document, the budget will not reflect the strategy. Strategy and budget must be explicitly linked: each budget line must connect to a strategic priority.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Overdo the analysis, underdo the decision. PESTEL, SWOT, 5 Forces, market sizing — producing 200-page strategy documents that gather dust. Strategic analysis should end in decisions: "We will invest here, disinvest from there, and this is how it shows in the budget." Analysis without decision is just expensive entertainment.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Single-point forecast without scenarios. "We forecast 15% revenue growth." Based on what assumptions? What if one key customer leaves? What if a competitor undercuts? Scenarios reveal the range of outcomes and allow contingency planning. Presenting only base case gives false certainty.
