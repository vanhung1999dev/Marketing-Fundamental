### Từ Chiến Lược Tới Kế Hoạch Hành Động

Khoảng cách lớn nhất trong quản trị không phải giữa "biết" và "không biết" — mà giữa "biết" và "làm". Strategy-to-execution gap là lý do 67% well-formulated strategies fail to achieve their objectives (theo Harvard Business Review). Kế hoạch hành động (Action Plan) với ngân sách cụ thể là cầu nối quan trọng nhất.

> **Bài học:** Chiến lược không được thực thi bởi vì nó hay — mà vì nó được translate thành hành động cụ thể, với người chịu trách nhiệm cụ thể, ngày deadline cụ thể, và ngân sách cụ thể. Thiếu bất kỳ yếu tố nào → chiến lược sẽ remain aspirational.

---

**Strategy → Initiative → Action Plan → Budget:**

```
STRATEGY LEVEL:
  "Trở thành top 3 công ty trong ngành trong 3 năm"

STRATEGIC OBJECTIVE LEVEL (BSC):
  "Tăng market share từ 8% lên 15% trong 3 năm"
  "Cải thiện customer retention từ 72% lên 85%"

INITIATIVE LEVEL (Year 1):
  Initiative 1: Enter 2 new provinces (market share driver)
  Initiative 2: Launch loyalty program (retention driver)
  Initiative 3: Upgrade product quality (both drivers)

ACTION PLAN LEVEL (Quarterly milestones):
  Initiative 1 — Q1:
    □ Hire 2 regional sales managers (Owner: HR + Sales Director)
    □ Identify top 50 target accounts in new provinces (Owner: Sales)
    □ Establish distributor partnerships in each province (Owner: CCO)
  
  Initiative 1 — Q2:
    □ First 10 new accounts signed (Owner: Regional Managers)
    □ Revenue from new provinces: 2 tỷ (Owner: Regional Managers)

BUDGET LEVEL:
  Initiative 1 total budget: 3.2 tỷ
    Q1: 1.2 tỷ (hiring + setup cost)
    Q2–Q4: 2.0 tỷ (salaries + marketing + travel)
```

---

**OKR Framework — objectives and key results:**

```
OKR (popular in tech, increasingly adopted in VN corporates):

OBJECTIVE: Qualitative, ambitious, inspiring direction
  "Become the most trusted F&B supplier in HCM"

KEY RESULTS: Quantitative, time-bound, measurable
  KR1: Revenue from HCM grows from 15 tỷ to 22 tỷ by Dec 31
  KR2: NPS from HCM customers increases from 35 to 52 by Dec 31
  KR3: # of enterprise accounts in HCM grows from 12 to 25 by Dec 31

OKR vs KPI:
  KPI: ongoing metric to track performance (always on)
  OKR: time-bound goal for specific improvement (annual/quarterly)
  Budget ties to OKRs: each KR needs investment to achieve it
```

---

**Cascading to Individual Action Plans:**

```
Company OKR → Department OKR → Individual OKR

Company: Revenue HCM grows to 22 tỷ

Sales Dept OKR:
  O: Close enterprise accounts in HCM
  KR: 13 new enterprise accounts signed (from 12 to 25)
  KR: Pipeline coverage 4× target at all times
  Budget: 2 reps × 1.2 tỷ = 2.4 tỷ headcount + 0.6 tỷ marketing

Individual Sales Rep (each):
  Target: 6–7 new enterprise accounts
  Activity: 20 meetings/month; 8 demos/month; 3 proposals/month
  Support: company provides marketing materials, product demos, pricing authority

Individual has no direct budget ownership but their activity
feeds the budget allocation justification for the department
```

---

**Accountability Framework:**

```
Every action plan item needs:
  WHAT: specific deliverable (not "improve customer service")
  WHO: one person accountable (not "the team")
  BY WHEN: specific date (not "Q1" alone; "March 31")
  WITH WHAT RESOURCES: budget allocated
  HOW TO MEASURE: what indicates success or failure
  DEPENDENCIES: what must happen first or in parallel

Review cadence:
  Weekly: Task completion; blockers surfaced
  Monthly: Initiative progress vs plan; budget vs actual
  Quarterly: Full initiative review; go/no-go on continued investment
```

---

**RACI Matrix for Budget Execution:**

```
R = Responsible (does the work)
A = Accountable (owns the result; single person)
C = Consulted (input needed)
I = Informed (kept in loop)

Example for "Launch loyalty program":
  Activity                    CEO  CFO  CMO  IT   Sales
  Define program design         I    C    A    C    C
  Build technology              I    C    C    A    I
  Set budget and approve        A    R    C    I    I
  Run marketing campaign        I    I    A    I    R
  Measure KPIs and ROI          I    C    A    I    C
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Bain & Company research: the number one predictor of successful strategy execution is clear accountability — specifically, one named person for each major initiative, not a committee. "Collective responsibility" typically means no one feels personally accountable. When a strategy initiative has one named owner with clear metrics and budget, execution probability is substantially higher.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Action plans too vague. "Improve marketing effectiveness" is not an action plan. "Reduce CAC from 800K to 500K by December by shifting 30% of Facebook budget to Google Search and referral program" is. The more specific the action plan, the more checkable progress is.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** No milestone checkpoints until year-end. Annual initiative with no quarterly milestones → problem discovered in December, too late to correct. Quarterly milestones + monthly budget tracking gives early warning. "By end of Q2, we should have X% of our annual target" — is this on track?

> **Cạm bẫy:** Strategy cascaded but budget not cascaded. CEO communicates strategy initiatives, but individual budgets aren't updated to reflect them. Sales rep's Q1 plan doesn't include the HCM expansion targets; their activities stay the same. Cascade must include budget cascade: each person whose activity is needed must have the resources to do it.
