### Khung Bản Đồ Chiến Lược Trong Thực Thi Ngân Sách

Strategy Map là công cụ trực quan hóa logic nhân quả của chiến lược — và khi được tích hợp vào quy trình lập ngân sách, nó đảm bảo rằng tiền được chi vào nơi thực sự tạo ra giá trị chiến lược chứ không phải chỉ vào nơi có lịch sử chi tiêu.

> **Bài học:** "Tell me how you measure me, and I will tell you how I behave." Tương tự: "Tell me where you allocate budget, and I will tell you what your real strategy is." Budget allocation là strategy in action — hoặc strategy in aspiration. Khi allocation không match với strategy, strategy là fiction.

---

**Từ Strategy Map đến Budget Map:**

```
Strategy Map (mục tiêu):
  "Tăng market share tại phân khúc SME lên 18%"
  "Cải thiện NPS từ 38 lên 55 trong 12 tháng"
  "Giảm COGS từ 65% xuống 60% qua efficiency"

Budget Map (nguồn lực):
  → Tăng market share SME:
     - 3 sales reps chuyên SME: 1.8 tỷ lương + BHXH
     - SME-specific marketing: 0.8 tỷ
     - SME product bundling (product team time): 0.3 tỷ
     Total: 2.9 tỷ để đạt objective này
  
  → Cải thiện NPS:
     - Customer Success Manager: 0.6 tỷ
     - CRM upgrade (customer service module): 0.4 tỷ
     - Product quality improvement project: 1.2 tỷ
     Total: 2.2 tỷ để đạt objective này
  
  → Giảm COGS:
     - Lean manufacturing consultant: 0.5 tỷ
     - Equipment upgrade (yield improvement): 2.0 tỷ
     - Procurement team training: 0.2 tỷ
     Total: 2.7 tỷ để đạt objective này
```

---

**Cause-Effect Validation — ngân sách có logic không:**

```
For every major budget allocation, test the causal chain:

"We are spending 2.9 tỷ on SME sales team and marketing"
  → If we invest this: # SME-qualified meetings per month = ?
  → If meetings happen: Win rate at SME segment = ?
  → If wins happen: Revenue from SME segment = ?
  → Revenue translates to: market share improvement = ?
  → Market share improvement delivers: Financial objective met?

If you can't trace the causal chain, the budget item is suspect.
If you can trace it but numbers don't add up → budget is insufficient or objective unrealistic.
```

---

**Resource Alignment Test:**

```
Red flags that strategy and budget are misaligned:

1. "Cost leadership" strategy with high SG&A and no lean investment
   → Strategy says operate efficiently; budget says invest in overhead

2. "Innovation" strategy with no R&D or product development budget
   → Strategy says differentiate through product; budget funds nothing new

3. "Customer intimacy" strategy with no customer success investment
   → Strategy says retain customers through relationships; budget doesn't fund this

4. "Geographic expansion" strategy with no new market investment
   → Strategy says enter 3 new provinces; budget has no market development spend

When you find these mismatches: either the strategy needs to change OR the budget does.
Don't maintain both a strategy and a budget that contradict each other.
```

---

**Strategic Spending vs Operational Spending:**

```
Two types of budget:
  
  OPERATIONAL (Run the business):
    → Keeps current business running at current performance level
    → Salaries for existing headcount, rent, utilities, maintenance
    → These are largely fixed and non-negotiable short-term
  
  STRATEGIC (Change the business):
    → Invests in moving from current state to desired future state
    → New headcount, new systems, new capabilities, new markets
    → These are the investments that execute the strategy

Common mistake: Treat all spending as operational → no strategic investment
Better approach: Explicitly identify and protect strategic spending
  → Ring-fence strategic budget: make it hard to cut in tactical tradeoffs
  → Track strategic investment separately from operational cost
  → Measure strategic ROI: did the investment achieve its objective?
```

---

**Budget Governance — who owns what:**

```
CEO:
  → Owns strategic direction; approves strategic initiative portfolio
  → Ensures budget reflects strategy; challenges misalignment

CFO:
  → Owns budget process, consolidation, and financial modeling
  → Ensures budget is financially sound; stress-tests assumptions

Department Heads:
  → Own their budget; accountable for results vs budget
  → Propose strategic initiatives for their domain

Finance Business Partners:
  → Support each department in building budget
  → Challenge from financial rigor perspective
  → Ensure linkage to strategic objectives is explicit
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Amazon's "working backwards" principle applied to budgeting: start from the customer outcome you want to achieve, work backward to identify what you need to invest in. This is opposite of traditional budgeting (start with what you spent last year). It's also the Strategy Map approach: start from the financial outcome, work backward through customer → process → L&G to identify investments needed at each level.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Allow operational budgets to crowd out strategic investment. "We don't have budget for that strategic initiative because operational costs consumed everything." Solution: set strategic investment envelope at top of budget process (e.g., "10% of total budget is reserved for strategic initiatives"), then build operational budget within the remaining 90%.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Budget by department silo without cross-functional initiatives. Many strategic objectives require investment across multiple departments — no one department can fund alone. Cross-functional initiative budgets need explicit ownership (typically CEO/COO sponsors) to avoid falling through the cracks.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Update strategy but not budget. "We just decided to pivot our go-to-market to focus on enterprise rather than SME." If budget isn't updated to reflect this shift (more enterprise sales reps, longer sales cycles = more pipeline investment), the strategy pivot is just words. Budget must follow strategy changes within the same or next planning cycle.
