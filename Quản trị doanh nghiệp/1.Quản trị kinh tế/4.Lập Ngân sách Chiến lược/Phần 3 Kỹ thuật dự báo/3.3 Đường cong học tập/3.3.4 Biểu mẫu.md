### Biểu Mẫu Ứng Dụng Đường Cong Học Tập

Phần này cung cấp các templates cụ thể để áp dụng learning curve trong lập ngân sách — từ ước tính chi phí đơn giản đến phân tích hợp đồng phức tạp.

> **Bài học:** A learning curve template turns a theoretical concept into a practical budget tool. Sau khi xác định learning rate từ historical data, template cho phép bạn project chi phí tương lai với precision — và present to management with credibility because the logic is transparent and testable.

---

**Template 1: Basic Unit Cost Projection:**

```
Inputs (highlighted cells in Excel):
  First unit cost (a):        ____________ VNĐ
  Learning rate (r):          ____________ %  (e.g., 0.85 for 85%)
  Starting cumulative units:  ____________
  Production plan by period:  (table below)

Formula:
  b = LOG(r) / LOG(2)
  Unit cost for unit X = a × X^b  [Excel: =a*POWER(X,b)]

Period | New Units | End Cum. | Unit Cost (last unit) | Period Cost | Cumulative Cost
Q1     | 100       | 100      | =a*POWER(100,b)       | =sum of all Q1 unit costs | —
Q2     | 150       | 250      | =a*POWER(250,b)       | =sum of all Q2 unit costs | —
Q3     | 200       | 450      | =a*POWER(450,b)       | =sum of all Q3 unit costs | —
Q4     | 250       | 700      | =a*POWER(700,b)       | =sum of all Q4 unit costs | —

Shortcut for period cost (using cumulative average method):
  Period cost = Total cost at end cumulative - Total cost at beginning cumulative
  Total cost at X units = X × (a × X^b)  [= a × X^(1+b)]
  
  Q2 period cost = [250 × a × 250^b] - [100 × a × 100^b]
```

**Template 2: Contract Pricing with Learning Curve:**

```
Scenario: Bid on contract to deliver 500 units over 24 months
  First unit estimated cost: 8,000,000 VNĐ
  Learning rate: 85%
  Required profit margin: 15%

Step 1: Estimate total cost using learning curve
  b = LOG(0.85)/LOG(2) = -0.2345
  Total cost for 500 units = a × X^(1+b) = 8,000,000 × 500^(0.7655)
  = 8,000,000 × 500^0.7655
  = 8,000,000 × 113.4
  = 907,200,000 VNĐ (approx. 0.907 tỷ)

Step 2: Add required margin
  Bid price = Total cost / (1 - margin) = 0.907 tỷ / (1 - 0.15) = 1.067 tỷ

Step 3: Calculate per-unit bid price
  Average bid price = 1.067 tỷ / 500 units = 2,134,000 VNĐ/unit

Interpretation:
  First unit: costs 8M, price 2.13M → lose money on unit 1
  Unit 100: costs ~4M, price 2.13M → still losing
  Unit 200: costs ~3.3M, price 2.13M → breakeven around here
  Unit 500: costs ~2.2M, price 2.13M → slight loss on last unit (expected)
  
  BUT: Total revenue 1.067 tỷ > Total cost 0.907 tỷ → profitable contract overall
  
  This is the economic logic of competitive contracts with learning curves:
  early units are loss-leaders; later units generate profit
```

**Template 3: Budget for New Product Launch:**

```
New product launch: Year 1 production plan 1,200 units
  Learning rate: 80% (highly labour-intensive product)
  First unit direct labour: 4 hours @ 70,000 VNĐ/hour = 280,000 VNĐ
  Direct materials: stable at 150,000 VNĐ/unit (no learning for materials)
  Overhead: fixed 500 triệu/year

Month  | Cum. Units | DL Hours/unit | DL Cost/unit | Matl/unit | Total/unit | Month Cost
Jan    | 50         | =4×50^b       | ×70,000      | 150,000   | DL+Mat    | ×50
Feb    | 120        | =4×120^b      | ×70,000      | 150,000   | DL+Mat    | ×70
Mar    | 220        | =4×220^b      | ×70,000      | 150,000   | DL+Mat    | ×100
...    | ...        | ...           | ...          | ...        | ...        | ...
Dec    | 1200       | =4×1200^b     | ×70,000      | 150,000   | DL+Mat    | ×100

Annual:
  Total direct labour cost: sum of monthly DL
  Total materials: 1,200 × 150,000 = 180M
  Total overhead: 500M (fixed)
  Total COGS: DL + Materials + Overhead
```

**Template 4: Workforce Planning with Learning:**

```
Implication: if learning reduces time per unit,
             the same workforce can produce more units over time

Time per unit (hours): Y(X) = 4 × X^(-0.3219)  [80% curve]

Month | Cum. Units | Hours/unit | Units if 10 workers × 160h/month
  1   | 50         | 2.48       | 645
  3   | 200        | 1.74       | 920
  6   | 500        | 1.37       | 1,168
  12  | 1200       | 1.03       | 1,553
  24  | 2400       | 0.82       | 1,951

Interpretation:
  Same 10 workers, working same 160 hours each
  Can produce 645 units/month in Month 1 → 1,951 units/month by Month 24
  Effectively = 3× throughput without hiring → all due to learning

Budget implication:
  Labour cost stays flat (same headcount)
  Production volume grows → lower labour cost per unit
  → Build this efficiency gain into COGS budget explicitly
```

**Model Validation — checking assumptions:**

```
After first 3–6 months of production, validate:

  Actual cumulative units: ____
  Actual unit cost (latest): ____
  Model predicted cost: ____
  
  If actual > model by >15%: learning slower than expected → revisit rate assumption
  If actual < model by >15%: learning faster than expected → update to better rate
  
  Revised rate calculation:
    r_actual = (cost at doubled volume) / (cost at half volume)
    Update b: b = log(r_actual) / log(2)
    Reforecast remaining budget with revised rate
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Japan's manufacturing excellence post-WW2 was in large part a mastery of the learning curve at the national level. MITI (Ministry of International Trade and Industry) directed investment into industries where Japan could build cumulative production volume → ride learning curves down → achieve cost leadership → dominate export markets. This was explicit industrial policy based on learning curve economics. South Korea (Samsung, Hyundai, POSCO) repeated this playbook with semiconductors and steel. Learning curve is not just a cost accounting tool — it's the engine of national competitive advantage in manufacturing.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Calculate total contract cost using only last-unit cost. "By the end of the contract, unit cost will be X, so total cost = X × total units." This underestimates cost because early units are much more expensive. Always use cumulative total cost calculation: integrate the curve from unit 1 to unit N.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Share learning curve analysis externally without protecting IP. Your learning curve rate tells customers and competitors: (a) how fast you're improving, (b) what your target cost structure is. In some negotiations, this information weakens your position. Use internally for budgeting; share externally only in generalities.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Overestimate learning rate to win a contract, then fail to realize it. "We'll achieve 75% learning curve." Optimistic projection → low bid price → win contract → actual learning is 88% → lose money. Better to win at realistic margins than win at unrealistic cost projections. Validate rate with pilot production before committing to contract price.
