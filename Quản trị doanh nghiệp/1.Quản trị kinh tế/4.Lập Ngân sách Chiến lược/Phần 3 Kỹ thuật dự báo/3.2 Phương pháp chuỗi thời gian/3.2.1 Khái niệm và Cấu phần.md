### Phương Pháp Chuỗi Thời Gian — Khái Niệm và Cấu Phần

Time Series Analysis phân tích dữ liệu theo trình tự thời gian để identify patterns và dự báo giá trị tương lai. Khác với hồi quy (tìm quan hệ với biến ngoài), time series tìm patterns trong chính chuỗi dữ liệu — trend, seasonality, cycle, và random variation.

> **Bài học:** Hầu hết dữ liệu kinh doanh là time series — doanh thu hàng tháng, lượng đơn hàng hàng tuần, tồn kho hàng ngày. Time series analysis là kỹ năng nền tảng của financial planning. Nếu không decompose trend và seasonality đúng, forecast của bạn sẽ systematically over hoặc under-predict.

---

**4 Cấu Phần của Time Series:**

```
1. TREND (T) — xu hướng dài hạn:
   Upward trend: revenue growing year over year
   Downward trend: declining market, churning customer base
   Flat: mature, stable business
   
   Detect: plot data; fit linear or polynomial trend line
   Example: Revenue trend slope = +2 tỷ/quarter = systematic long-term growth

2. SEASONALITY (S) — biến động theo chu kỳ cố định:
   Regular, predictable fluctuations within a year
   
   VN business examples:
     F&B: Tết spike (Jan/Feb) + Q4 gift season + summer beverages
     Retail: Tết + Ngày Thương mại Điện tử 11/11, 12/12
     Construction: monsoon slowdown (May–Sep) in South VN
     Education: back-to-school (Sep) + exam season (May/Jun)
   
   Seasonal Index: month's average / overall average
   Example: December seasonal index = 1.35 → Dec is typically 35% above average

3. CYCLICAL (C) — biến động kinh tế dài hơn 1 năm:
   Business cycle fluctuations (expansion, peak, recession, trough)
   Usually 3–7 years; harder to separate from trend
   Relevant for: construction, banking, manufacturing capital goods
   Less visible in consumer goods (more driven by seasonality)

4. IRREGULAR/RANDOM (I) — biến động ngẫu nhiên:
   Unpredictable: COVID shock, one-off events, data errors
   Cannot be forecast; but should be identified and removed when analyzing
   
   Additive model: Y = T + S + C + I
   Multiplicative model: Y = T × S × C × I
   (Multiplicative more common when seasonal variation grows with trend)
```

---

**Seasonal Decomposition — practical method:**

```
Step 1: Calculate moving average to isolate trend
  12-month moving average (for monthly data) smooths out seasonality
  Each value = average of preceding 6 months + current + following 5 months
  This is the trend estimate (T)

Step 2: Calculate seasonal component
  Seasonal = Actual / Trend (multiplicative) or Actual - Trend (additive)
  Average the seasonal values for each month across years
  → Seasonal indices for each of 12 months

Step 3: Deseasonalize the data
  Deseasonalized = Actual / Seasonal Index
  This shows the underlying trend without seasonal noise

Step 4: Forecast
  Forecast Trend for future period (extend trend line)
  Multiply by seasonal index for that period
  Forecast = Trend × Seasonal Index

Example:
  Month 13 (January) trend estimate = 10 tỷ
  January seasonal index = 1.35 (Tết effect)
  January forecast = 10 × 1.35 = 13.5 tỷ
```

**Autocorrelation — đặc tính quan trọng của time series:**

```
Autocorrelation: this month's value correlates with last month's value

Lag-1 autocorrelation: correlation between Y_t and Y_(t-1)
  High positive → strong momentum (growth begets growth; decline begets decline)
  
Implication for budgeting:
  If autocorrelation is high, recent trend is very predictive of near future
  Simple forecast: next month ≈ this month × (1 + trend rate)
  
Seasonal autocorrelation: Y_t correlates with Y_(t-12) (same month last year)
  Seasonal businesses: this January is best predicted by last January (adjusted for trend)
  
Tools: ACF (Autocorrelation Function) plot in Excel or Python/R
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Companies with strong seasonal patterns must explicitly model seasonality in their budgets — otherwise they'll budget flat monthly numbers and show "bad" performance in low season, "great" performance in peak season, when both are simply seasonal patterns. This leads to wrong resource allocation (overstaffing in low season, understaffing in peak) and wrong bonus calculations (penalizing people for seasonal dips they can't control). Seasonal decomposition makes the underlying trend visible independent of the seasonal noise.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Budget as equal 1/12 of annual target each month. "Revenue target is 120 tỷ, so 10 tỷ per month." But if December is 35% above average → December target should be 13.5 tỷ, not 10 tỷ. Flat monthly budgets create misleading variance analysis throughout the year.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Confuse seasonality with trend. "Revenue was 9.5 tỷ last October; this October it's 10.2 tỷ — growth is strong." But if you compare October to October and see +7% — that's meaningful trend growth. Don't compare October to September (different seasonal pattern) to infer trend.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Assume seasonality is stable year-over-year. COVID permanently changed some seasonal patterns (e.g., Chinese New Year travel fell; domestic tourism rose). Post-COVID, online shopping seasons shifted. Always check whether recent seasonal patterns match historical seasonal indices before applying mechanically.
