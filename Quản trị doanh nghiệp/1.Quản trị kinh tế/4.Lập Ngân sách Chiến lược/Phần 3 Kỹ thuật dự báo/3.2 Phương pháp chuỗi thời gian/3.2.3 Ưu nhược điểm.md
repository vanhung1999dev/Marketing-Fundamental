### Ưu Nhược Điểm Của Phương Pháp Chuỗi Thời Gian

Hiểu đầy đủ strengths và limitations của time series methods giúp forecast team biết khi nào tin vào model, khi nào cần override bằng judgment, và khi nào cần combine với methods khác.

> **Bài học:** The best forecast is not the most technically sophisticated one — it's the one that gives you the best basis for decision-making, with honest uncertainty estimates, và được cập nhật kịp thời khi reality deviates from plan.

---

**Ưu Điểm Của Time Series Methods:**

```
1. USES ONLY INTERNAL DATA:
   Doesn't require external predictor variables
   Useful when GDP data, market data unavailable or unreliable
   → Self-contained; works with just your own historical sales data

2. CAPTURES PATTERNS WITHIN THE DATA:
   Trend, seasonality, cycles all extracted from actual business data
   → Reflects your actual business dynamics, not theoretical relationships

3. FAST TO BUILD AND UPDATE:
   Excel FORECAST.ETS builds in minutes
   Monthly update: add new data point; forecasts update automatically
   → Low maintenance overhead once model is built

4. GOOD FOR SHORT-TERM FORECASTING:
   1–12 months ahead: time series typically outperforms regression
   Autocorrelation means "recent past is best predictor of near future"
   → Budget year forecasting: time series very appropriate

5. HANDLES IRREGULAR DATA AUTOMATICALLY:
   Exponential smoothing adjusts weights over time
   Less sensitive to one-off outliers than regression if α is low
   → Robust to data quality issues in short-term forecasting

6. PROVIDES PREDICTION INTERVALS:
   FORECAST.ETS.CONFINT gives built-in uncertainty bounds
   → Budget communication: "Revenue forecast 128 tỷ ± 8 tỷ (95% CI)"
```

---

**Nhược Điểm và Giới Hạn:**

```
1. PURELY BACKWARD-LOOKING:
   Only uses historical patterns — cannot capture structural changes
   "The past predicts the future" — true only if conditions are similar
   
   Failure modes:
     New competitor entering market
     Regulatory change (e.g., new tax on category)
     Major macro shift (COVID; financial crisis)
     → Model keeps predicting based on pre-change patterns

2. CANNOT INCORPORATE STRATEGIC DECISIONS:
   "We're launching a major new product line in Q2"
   "We're expanding into 3 new provinces"
   → Time series model doesn't know about these changes
   → Must manually adjust forecasts for known strategic changes

3. REQUIRES SUFFICIENT HISTORY:
   Seasonal model needs at least 2 complete seasonal cycles (2 years)
   Trend model needs at least 12–18 months
   → New businesses or new product lines: insufficient history

4. STRUGGLES WITH LONG-TERM FORECASTING:
   12+ months ahead: uncertainty grows rapidly
   Time series forecasts become wide intervals → less useful
   → For strategic planning (3–5 years): regression with scenario analysis better

5. DOESN'T EXPLAIN "WHY":
   "Revenue will be 130 tỷ" — but why? What is driving it?
   Time series provides forecast without causal understanding
   → Management can't understand what levers to pull to change the forecast

6. CAN OVER-SMOOTH REAL CHANGES:
   High α exponential smoothing: too reactive (noise amplified)
   Low α: too stable (misses real trend changes)
   Finding optimal α requires technical judgment or optimization
```

---

**Combining Time Series với Other Methods:**

```
Best practice: Combine methods; use judgment to reconcile

QUANTITATIVE BASE:
  Time series (FORECAST.ETS): statistical baseline from historical patterns
  Regression: driver-based forecast (GDP, marketing, headcount)
  
  If both agree within 5% → high confidence in base forecast
  If they diverge → investigate: which assumption is more credible now?

MANAGEMENT OVERLAY:
  Add/subtract management judgment adjustments:
    + New product launch: add incremental revenue estimate
    + New market entry: add new region revenue
    - Key customer loss: subtract expected revenue impact
    ± Competitive dynamics: adjust for known competitive moves

FINAL BUDGET:
  = Quantitative base ± Management overlays
  Document each overlay: who decided, why, what evidence

Output:
  Point estimate: management-adjusted consensus
  Range: from quantitative confidence interval ± management judgment uncertainty
```

---

**When to Choose Time Series vs Regression:**

| Factor | Time Series | Regression |
|---|---|---|
| Historical data availability | ≥2 years own data | ≥2 years both X and Y data |
| External driver data available | Not needed | Required |
| Forecast horizon | 1–12 months | 1–36 months (if predictor forecastable) |
| Explanatory need | Low ("just tell me the number") | High ("tell me what drives it") |
| Business stability | Stable patterns | Can handle structural changes if captured in X |
| Seasonal complexity | Handles naturally | Needs dummy variables |

---

**Decision Tree for Forecast Method Selection:**

```
Q1: Do you have 24+ months of historical data?
  No → Use simpler methods (naive + management judgment)
  Yes → Continue
  
Q2: Is there a strong causal relationship with external variables?
  Yes → Consider regression (with time series as check)
  No → Use time series primarily

Q3: Are major strategic changes planned that break historical patterns?
  Yes → Heavy management overlay required on any quantitative method
  No → Trust quantitative methods more

Q4: How important is understanding the "why"?
  Important → Use regression (explains drivers)
  Not critical → Time series sufficient
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** The art of good forecasting is knowing the limitations of your methods and communicating them honestly. A forecaster who says "I'm 65% confident revenue will be 120–135 tỷ" is more valuable than one who says "revenue will be exactly 128.6 tỷ." The former can plan around uncertainty; the latter will be blindsided when reality deviates. Nassim Taleb's critique of forecasters: they tend to underestimate the frequency and magnitude of unexpected events. Good forecast processes explicitly account for "what could surprise us" and prepare contingencies.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Build time series model and never update it. Markets evolve; seasonal patterns shift; COVID permanently altered many industries. Model should be retrained with new data at least annually; checked for accuracy quarterly. "Our 2019 seasonal model" is likely outdated for 2025 planning.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Over-optimize smoothing parameters for historical fit without checking out-of-sample performance. "Our model has MAPE = 2% on historical data!" But if that was achieved by tuning α to fit historical perfectly, it may overfit and perform poorly on new data. Always hold out 20–30% of data as "test set" and evaluate accuracy on unseen data.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Use time series for capital investment decisions. For "should we build a new factory?" — this is a 10-year horizon decision with major strategic uncertainty. Time series is designed for short-term operational forecasting. For capital decisions, use scenario planning, DCF analysis, and strategic judgment — not mechanical extrapolation of historical trends.
