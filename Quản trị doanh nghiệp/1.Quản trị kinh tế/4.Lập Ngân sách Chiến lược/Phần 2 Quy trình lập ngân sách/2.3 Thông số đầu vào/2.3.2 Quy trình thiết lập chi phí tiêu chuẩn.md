### Quy Trình Thiết Lập Chi Phí Tiêu Chuẩn (Standard Costing)

Standard Costing là hệ thống xác định trước chi phí "nên là" cho từng đơn vị sản phẩm hoặc dịch vụ — sau đó so sánh với chi phí thực tế để xác định variances và tìm nguyên nhân.

> **Bài học:** Standard Costing không phải là kỹ thuật kế toán — là công cụ quản lý. Khi bạn biết "một đơn vị sản phẩm nên tốn 185,000 VNĐ" và thực tế tốn 210,000 VNĐ, bạn có một câu hỏi cụ thể cần trả lời và một cơ hội cải thiện cụ thể để theo đuổi.

---

**Standard Cost Components:**

```
Standard Cost per Unit = Direct Materials Standard + Direct Labour Standard + Overhead Standard

DIRECT MATERIALS STANDARD:
  Standard quantity × Standard price per unit of material
  
  Example — Product A:
    Material X: 2.5 kg × 80,000 VNĐ/kg = 200,000 VNĐ
    Material Y: 0.3 lít × 120,000 VNĐ/lít = 36,000 VNĐ
    Packaging: 15,000 VNĐ (standard pack)
    Total DM standard: 251,000 VNĐ/unit

DIRECT LABOUR STANDARD:
  Standard hours × Standard rate per hour
  
  Example:
    Skilled worker: 0.5 giờ × 80,000 VNĐ/giờ = 40,000 VNĐ
    Assembly: 0.3 giờ × 55,000 VNĐ/giờ = 16,500 VNĐ
    Total DL standard: 56,500 VNĐ/unit

MANUFACTURING OVERHEAD STANDARD:
  Standard overhead rate × Standard activity level
  
  Fixed overhead rate: Total budgeted fixed overhead / Budgeted production volume
  Variable overhead rate: Variable overhead per unit of activity driver
  
  Example: Budgeted fixed overhead 5 tỷ; budgeted production 100,000 units
    Fixed overhead rate: 5,000,000,000 / 100,000 = 50,000 VNĐ/unit
    Variable overhead: 12,000 VNĐ/unit (per historical data)
    Total overhead standard: 62,000 VNĐ/unit

TOTAL STANDARD COST: 251,000 + 56,500 + 62,000 = 369,500 VNĐ/unit
```

---

**Setting Standard Costs — methodologies:**

```
IDEAL STANDARDS (perfection-based):
  Assume zero waste, zero defects, perfect efficiency
  → Motivating but unachievable → demotivating if never met
  → Not recommended for budgeting

ATTAINABLE STANDARDS (realistic stretch):
  Based on efficient but achievable performance
  Allow for normal waste, learning curves, minor inefficiencies
  → Most appropriate for budgeting and performance management

HISTORICAL AVERAGE STANDARDS:
  Based on past actual performance
  Easy to set; doesn't encourage improvement
  → May perpetuate inefficiencies

INDUSTRY BENCHMARKS:
  Based on what best companies in your industry achieve
  Useful reference; context-dependent; may not be achievable without same scale

RECOMMENDATION: Set attainable standards using:
  Historical average as baseline
  Adjust for known improvement initiatives (lean projects, new equipment)
  Benchmark against industry best to identify improvement potential
```

**Variance Analysis Framework — review Module 2 concepts:**

```
MATERIAL VARIANCES:
  Total MV = (Standard cost - Actual cost) of actual production
  
  Price Variance (MPV):
    (Standard price - Actual price) × Actual quantity purchased
    Who owns: Procurement (they control price through supplier negotiation)
  
  Usage Variance (MUV):
    (Standard quantity - Actual quantity used) × Standard price
    Who owns: Production/Operations (they control consumption)

LABOUR VARIANCES:
  Rate Variance (LRV):
    (Standard rate - Actual rate) × Actual hours worked
    Who owns: HR (they control pay rates)
  
  Efficiency Variance (LEV):
    (Standard hours - Actual hours) × Standard rate
    Who owns: Production manager (they control labour productivity)

OVERHEAD VARIANCES:
  Fixed Overhead Expenditure Variance: Budgeted FOH - Actual FOH
  Fixed Overhead Volume Variance: (Actual production - Budgeted production) × Standard FOH rate
```

**Standard Cost Review — annual update process:**

```
Standards must be reviewed at least annually (ideally at budget time):

Step 1: Gather input data
  □ Updated material specifications (BOM changes, formula changes)
  □ Current and forecast material prices (from Procurement)
  □ Updated labour rates (post salary review, BHXH rate changes)
  □ Updated overhead budget (from Finance)
  □ Revised production volumes for overhead rate calculation

Step 2: Compare to prior standards
  □ Calculate % change in each standard cost component
  □ Investigate changes >5%: is it genuine or data error?
  □ Verify BOM accuracy with production team

Step 3: Set new standards
  □ Use attainable level for production volumes
  □ Price standards: use forward-looking prices, not just current
  □ Document rationale for major changes

Step 4: Communicate
  □ Brief production/operations team on new standards
  □ Explain how variances will be measured
  □ Set variance tolerance: investigate variances >3% of standard
```

**Standard Costing Integration với Budget:**

```
Budget COGS = Standard Cost per unit × Budgeted production volume

Monthly management reporting:
  Actual COGS: from accounting records
  Standard COGS: Standard cost × actual production volume
  Total variance: Actual - Standard = Total over/underspend vs standard
  
  Decompose variance:
    Material price variance: better/worse purchasing
    Material usage variance: better/worse production efficiency
    Labour rate variance: HR issue (pay rates)
    Labour efficiency variance: Production issue (productivity)
    Overhead spending variance: overhead cost control
    Overhead volume variance: production volume vs budget

Report format (monthly):
  Variance waterfall chart: start from budgeted COGS, show each variance,
  arrive at actual COGS. Makes cost story clear at a glance.
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Toyota Production System (TPS) và Standard Costing have a deep connection. TPS uses takt time (standard time per unit) to define the production rhythm. Any deviation is immediately visible and triggers a "stop and fix" response. This makes standard costing not just a financial tool but an operational control system — deviations flag problems in real-time, not months later in a management report.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Set standards too tight (ideal) → variances always unfavorable → team feels they always fail → standards become meaningless. Set attainable standards: based on efficient but realistic operations. A small favorable variance (2–3%) is healthy — shows team is performing at or above standard.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Don't update standards when processes change significantly. New machine installed that changes productivity and material yield → standards should be updated. Using outdated standards produces variances that don't reflect real performance vs current standard — they just reflect that the standard hasn't been updated.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Spend more time on variance analysis than on fixing problems. "Here is our 20-page variance analysis report." What actions were taken? Variance analysis is only valuable if it drives action. Simplify: identify top 3 variances; assign owner; define corrective action; track resolution. Monthly review: last month's corrective actions — did they work?
