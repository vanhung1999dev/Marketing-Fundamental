### Các Tiêu Chí Của Thông Số Đầu Vào Chuẩn

Budget assumptions (thông số đầu vào) là nền tảng của mọi budget model. Nếu assumptions sai — dù model có đúng đến đâu — output cũng sẽ sai. GIGO: Garbage In, Garbage Out.

> **Bài học:** Spend more time validating assumptions than perfecting the model. A simple model với excellent assumptions beats a complex model với poor assumptions. The most important skill in budgeting is not Excel — là judgment về which assumptions are credible, which are wishful, và which are dangerous.

---

**Categories of Budget Assumptions:**

```
MACRO ASSUMPTIONS (external; cannot control):
  GDP growth rate: VN target 6.5% (2024 direction)
  Inflation/CPI: NHNN target 4–4.5%
  USD/VNĐ exchange rate: 24,500–25,500 range (2024 context)
  Labour market: wage inflation 8–12%/year; minimum wage increases per NĐ
  Interest rates: base rate of SBV; floating loan rate (typically SBV + 2–4%)
  Raw material prices: commodities relevant to business (oil, steel, coffee, etc.)

INDUSTRY ASSUMPTIONS (sector-specific; research-based):
  Market growth rate: from VIRAC, GfK, Nielsen, BMI, industry associations
  Competitive intensity: new entrants, pricing pressure trend
  Customer behaviour: payment terms trends; online vs offline shift

COMPANY ASSUMPTIONS (internal; management decision):
  Pricing: price increase/decrease for products
  Volume: new customers, churn rate, organic growth
  Productivity: efficiency improvements planned
  New initiatives: timing and revenue/cost assumptions for new projects
  Headcount: hiring plan, attrition assumptions
```

---

**Assumption Documentation Standard:**

```
For each key assumption, document:
  
  WHAT: The assumption itself
    "USD/VNĐ exchange rate: 25,200 for FY2025"
  
  WHY: Rationale and source
    "Bloomberg consensus forecast as of November 2024; within range of
    SBV's implied managed float band; consistent with last 6-month trend"
  
  SENSITIVITY: Impact if assumption is wrong
    "+1,000 VNĐ/USD (rate 26,200) → COGS increases 3.2% → EBITDA -1.5pp"
  
  RISK: Probability of variance and direction
    "Moderate risk of VNĐ weakening further if USD strengthens globally"
  
  TRIGGER: When to update mid-year
    "If actual rate deviates >5% from assumption → reforecast required"
```

**Revenue Assumptions — rigorous approach:**

```
Weak assumption: "Revenue grows 15% because we expect good market growth"

Strong assumption: "Revenue grows 15%:
  Price: +5% (planned price increase across all SKUs, effective April 1)
  Volume — existing customers: +6% (based on account planning with top 20 accounts)
  Volume — new customers: +4% (2 new sales reps × 2 tỷ/rep quota)
  
  Risk: +5% price may reduce volume at price-sensitive accounts (10% of base)
  Offset: If price backfires, volume scenario -2% but price already +5% → net +3%
  Minimum scenario: +8% revenue growth (conservative)
  Maximum scenario: +20% (if new reps outperform)"

The difference: first version has no structure to challenge or verify.
Second version: every component can be tracked and actuals compared monthly.
```

**Cost Assumptions — key categories:**

```
LABOUR:
  Base salary inflation: 8% (market rate; recent trend; HCMC survey data)
  New hires: by role, start date, salary
  BHXH employer cost: 22.5% on top of gross (mandatory, non-negotiable)
  Bonus provision: 1.5 months salary (company policy; may vary)
  Turnover assumption: 15% → budget for replacement recruitment

RAW MATERIALS / COGS:
  Commodity prices: specific to each material type
  FX impact: for USD-denominated imports, use FX assumption
  Yield/waste: based on historical data + improvement target
  Supplier price changes: known increases from supplier notice letters

OVERHEAD:
  Rent: per contract (escalation clause? Annual step-up?)
  Utilities: based on volume + utility price change forecast
  Insurance: renewal quotes
  IT licenses: renewal pricing (typically CPI-indexed)
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** McKinsey research on forecast accuracy: companies that explicitly document and track their budget assumptions (and update them when market changes) produce forecasts 35% more accurate than companies that treat assumptions as implicit. The discipline of assumption documentation isn't bureaucracy — it's what allows you to know, when budget is wrong, whether it's because your model was wrong or your assumptions were wrong. That distinction determines the right corrective action.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Single point estimates without range. "Revenue will be 100 tỷ." Better: "Base 100 tỷ; Downside 88 tỷ (key customer lost); Upside 112 tỷ (new channel outperforms)." Range estimates reflect genuine uncertainty; single points create false precision and damage credibility when missed.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Assume past trends continue without examining why. "We've grown 20% for 3 years, so budget 20% again." But: (a) market may be saturating; (b) a large one-off customer inflated past results; (c) a competitor is entering. Always examine whether the conditions that drove past growth still apply.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Use inconsistent assumptions across departments. Sales budget assumes FX 25,200 for pricing. Procurement budget assumes FX 25,000 for costs. Finance builds P&L that shows higher margin than actually achievable. CFO must issue one set of macro assumptions used by all departments — enforced in budget guidelines memo, not left to each team to choose.
