### Ngân Sách Đầu Tư (Capital Budget)

Capital Budget (Capex) là kế hoạch cho các khoản đầu tư dài hạn — máy móc thiết bị, bất động sản, hệ thống công nghệ, và các tài sản khác tạo ra giá trị trong nhiều năm. Khác với operating expense (ghi nhận ngay vào P&L), capex được capitalize và depreciate dần.

> **Bài học:** Capex quyết định năng lực tương lai của doanh nghiệp — capacity để sản xuất, technology để vận hành, real estate để phục vụ. Budget cho capex không phải là "có tiền thì mua" — là portfolio investment decisions với risk-return tradeoffs phải được evaluate rigorously.

---

**Capex Categories:**

```
MAINTENANCE CAPEX (giữ business hoạt động):
  Replace worn equipment; repair infrastructure; software renewal
  Non-discretionary in most cases
  Budget: Historical replacement rate + age-of-asset adjustment
  Benchmark: 2–5% of asset base annually for maintenance

GROWTH CAPEX (mở rộng năng lực):
  New production lines, new locations, new technology systems
  Discretionary; must be justified by business case
  Budget: Project-by-project, each with IRR/NPV analysis

STRATEGIC CAPEX (build capabilities):
  R&D equipment, ERP systems, brand investments, M&A
  Often hard to quantify directly but strategically necessary
  Budget: Explicit strategic justification required
```

---

**Capital Investment Appraisal — business case requirements:**

```
For any capex request >500 triệu, require:

1. Project description
   What is being invested? What problem does it solve?
   What is the alternative of not investing?

2. Financial analysis:
   Initial investment amount (full cost including installation, training)
   Expected annual benefits (revenue increase OR cost savings)
   Project life (how many years before replacement?)
   Terminal value (residual value at end of life?)

3. NPV Analysis:
   NPV = Sum of discounted cash flows - Initial investment
   Discount rate = WACC (typically 9–12% for VN mid-market companies)
   Decision rule: NPV > 0 → invest; NPV < 0 → do not invest

4. IRR Analysis:
   IRR = discount rate at which NPV = 0
   Decision rule: IRR > WACC → invest
   Typically require IRR > 15% for growth projects (risk premium)

5. Payback Period:
   How many years to recover initial investment from cash flows?
   Rule of thumb: <3 years for IT; <5 years for manufacturing equipment; <10 years for real estate
   Simple and intuitive; but ignores time value

6. Risk assessment:
   What are key assumptions? What could go wrong?
   Sensitivity: if revenue benefit is 20% lower, does project still make sense?
```

**NPV Example — ERP Implementation:**

```
Project: ERP implementation
Initial investment: 4 tỷ (software license 2T + implementation 1.5T + training 0.5T)
Project life: 7 years (until next major upgrade)
WACC: 10%

Annual benefits:
  Year 1: 0.5 tỷ (low benefit; learning curve)
  Year 2: 1.0 tỷ (productivity improving)
  Year 3–7: 1.5 tỷ/year (full benefits: time saved, errors reduced, better decisions)

Annual costs:
  License maintenance: 0.3 tỷ/year
  Net annual benefit: Year 1: 0.2T; Year 2: 0.7T; Year 3–7: 1.2T

NPV calculation:
  PV of net benefits:
    Year 1: 0.2/(1.10)^1 = 0.182
    Year 2: 0.7/(1.10)^2 = 0.579
    Year 3: 1.2/(1.10)^3 = 0.901
    Year 4: 1.2/(1.10)^4 = 0.819
    Year 5: 1.2/(1.10)^5 = 0.745
    Year 6: 1.2/(1.10)^6 = 0.677
    Year 7: 1.2/(1.10)^7 = 0.616
    Total PV of benefits: 4.519 tỷ

  NPV = 4.519 - 4.000 = +0.519 tỷ → Marginally positive; worth doing
  IRR: approximately 14% (above WACC of 10%)
  Payback: ~4.5 years
```

**Depreciation Methods và Budget Impact:**

```
Straight-line (phổ biến nhất VN, per VAS):
  Annual depreciation = (Cost - Salvage Value) / Useful life
  
  Example: Machine cost 2 tỷ; salvage value 0.1 tỷ; life 10 years
  Annual depreciation = (2 - 0.1) / 10 = 0.19 tỷ/year

Accelerated (declining balance):
  Higher depreciation in early years
  Tax benefit: defers tax payment (cash flow advantage)
  Less common under VAS but available

Thông tư 45/2013/TT-BTC: quy định về khấu hao tài sản cố định
  → Defines minimum useful lives by asset category
  → Determines maximum annual depreciation allowable for tax purposes
  → Land use rights: not depreciated (unless finite use right)
  → Buildings: 25–50 years typical
  → Machinery: 5–15 years typical
  → IT equipment: 3–8 years typical
```

**Capex Prioritization — portfolio approach:**

```
Rank all capex requests by:
  Strategic alignment (high/medium/low)
  NPV (positive or negative)
  Payback period (short or long)
  Risk level (low risk vs high uncertainty)
  Mandatory vs discretionary

Total capex available (budget envelope): e.g., 20 tỷ
Fund from top of ranked list until envelope exhausted
Defer or cancel below the line

Priority matrix:
  Mandatory (compliance, safety): Fund regardless
  Strategic high NPV: Fund first from discretionary envelope
  Strategic medium NPV: Fund if envelope allows
  Nice-to-have, low NPV: Defer to next year
  Negative NPV projects: Do not fund unless strategically critical
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Capital allocation is CEO's most important job. Warren Buffett has written extensively that most CEOs are good operators but often mediocre capital allocators — because operating skill is built over careers, but capital allocation is rarely explicitly taught. Poor capital allocation (investing in low-return projects, over-investing in declining businesses, under-investing in growing ones) destroys shareholder value slowly but surely. The capex budget process is where capital allocation discipline is institutionalized.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Approve capex based on "we need it" without financial analysis. "We need a new production line." At what cost? What return? How long to break even? Without financial analysis, capex approvals become based on seniority and persuasion, not economic merit. Every capex request >500M must have NPV/IRR/payback calculation, even if rough.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Post-investment review never happens. "We approved 5 tỷ for new equipment promising 2 tỷ/year savings. Did we achieve it?" Few companies track whether approved projects actually delivered promised returns. Post-implementation review (PIR) closes the learning loop and improves future capital allocation quality.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Capex creep — project cost increases after approval. "We approved 3 tỷ, now they're saying 4.5 tỷ." Without strong project management and change control, capex budgets inflate. Solutions: require detailed cost estimates before approval (not ballpark); set contingency explicitly (typically 10–15%); require re-approval for increases >20% of original budget.
