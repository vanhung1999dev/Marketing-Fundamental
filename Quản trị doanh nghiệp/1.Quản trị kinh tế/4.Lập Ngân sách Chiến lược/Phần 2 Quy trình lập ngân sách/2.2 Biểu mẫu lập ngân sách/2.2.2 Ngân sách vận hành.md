### Ngân Sách Vận Hành (Operating Budget)

Operating Budget là phần lớn nhất của Master Budget — bao gồm toàn bộ chi phí và doanh thu từ hoạt động kinh doanh thường xuyên. Xây dựng operating budget chặt chẽ đòi hỏi hiểu sâu về cost drivers và revenue dynamics của business.

> **Bài học:** Operating Budget không phải là list of numbers — là theory of business: "Nếu chúng ta có X customers mua Y volume tại Z price, thì COGS sẽ là A, nhân công sẽ là B, và chúng ta sẽ earn C lợi nhuận." Khi budget sai, thường vì theory sai — không phải vì Excel sai.

---

**Revenue Budget — xây dựng từ drivers:**

```
Driver-based revenue model:

METHOD 1: Bottom-up từ sales team
  Sales rep 1 quota: 12 tỷ
  Sales rep 2 quota: 10 tỷ
  Sales rep 3 quota: 11 tỷ
  ...
  Total sales budget: sum of all quotas + management adjustment

METHOD 2: Market-based top-down
  Total addressable market: 500 tỷ
  Current market share: 8% = 40 tỷ
  Target market share end of year: 10% = 50 tỷ
  Revenue budget: 50 tỷ (+25%)

METHOD 3: Account-by-account (B2B)
  List all existing accounts + expected renewal/growth
  List expected new accounts with probability-weighted value
  Sum = base revenue + new revenue pipeline

Best practice: Start method 2 (top-down) to validate method 1 (bottom-up)
  If bottom-up sum < top-down target: gap must be explained or filled
  If bottom-up sum > top-down: validate — is the market big enough?
```

**COGS Budget — materials, labour, overhead:**

```
For manufacturing company:

DIRECT MATERIALS:
  For each product: BOM (Bill of Materials) × planned production volume
  Factor in: waste/yield rate, price escalation assumption, FX if imported
  
  Product A: 10,000 units × BOM cost 250,000 VNĐ = 2.5 tỷ
  Product B: 8,000 units × BOM cost 380,000 VNĐ = 3.04 tỷ
  Waste allowance (+3%): 0.17 tỷ
  Total direct materials: 5.71 tỷ

DIRECT LABOUR:
  Workers needed by production volume × hours × hourly rate
  Include BHXH/BHYT/BHTN employer cost (22.5% additional)
  Overtime estimate for peak months
  
  Example: 50 production workers × avg 8M/month gross × 12.225 (incl BHXH)
  = 50 × 8M × 12 months × 1.225 = 5.88 tỷ/year

MANUFACTURING OVERHEAD:
  Fixed: Factory rent, depreciation, insurance, supervision
  Variable: Utilities, consumables, maintenance
  
  Apply overhead rate: as % of direct labour or machine hours
  Common: (Total overhead budget) / (Total direct labour hours) = rate per DL hour
```

**SG&A Budget by Department:**

```
SELLING EXPENSES:
  Sales team salaries + BHXH
  Sales commissions (variable; tied to revenue)
  Marketing: digital, events, PR, creative
  Customer service costs
  Logistics/distribution (if included in selling, not COGS)
  CRM software, sales tools

GENERAL & ADMINISTRATIVE:
  Finance/Accounting team: salaries, audit fees, software
  HR team: salaries, recruitment, training
  IT: infrastructure, licenses, support
  Legal/compliance: retainers, filings
  Office: rent, utilities, supplies
  CEO/Management: salaries, travel, professional development
  Board/governance costs

Template per department:
  Personnel: List roles × salary × BHXH multiplier
  Non-personnel direct: itemized by category
  Allocated costs: IT, facilities shared costs
  Total: sum of above
```

**Headcount Budget — the most sensitive budget:**

```
For each role:
  Gross salary (based on market rate + internal equity)
  BHXH employer: 17.5%
  BHYT employer: 3.0%
  BHTN employer: 1.0%
  BHTNLĐ employer: 1.0%
  Total employer cost: 122.5% × gross salary
  
  Bonus provision: typically 1–3 months salary as provision
  Recruitment cost: typically 15–25% of annual salary for new hires
  Onboarding/training: 0.5–1 month productive capacity lost

Start timing:
  New hire in March → 10 months cost in year (not 12)
  Track start dates carefully in headcount model

Attrition:
  Budget should factor in expected turnover
  If 15% annual turnover with 30-day gap to rehire:
    Per departure: 30/365 × 12 = approximately 1 month salary saved
    But recruitment cost incurred: 15–25% of salary
    Net effect: roughly cost-neutral to slight positive for high-salary roles
```

**Budget Sensitivity Analysis:**

```
Key sensitivities to test for operating budget:

Revenue sensitivity:
  Base: 100 tỷ
  -10% scenario: 90 tỷ → what happens to EBITDA? (variable cost flex down)
  +10% scenario: 110 tỷ → incremental contribution

Cost sensitivity:
  Labour cost +8% → EBITDA impact (depends on labour % of revenue)
  Raw material +10% → COGS impact
  FX depreciation 5% (for USD-denominated inputs) → COGS impact

Break-even analysis:
  At what revenue level does the company break even on EBITDA?
  Fixed costs / Contribution Margin % = Break-even Revenue
  
  Example: Fixed costs 20 tỷ; CM% 40% → BE Revenue = 50 tỷ
  If budget revenue = 80 tỷ → Margin of Safety = (80-50)/80 = 37.5%
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Driver-based budgeting reduces the "black box" problem of budgeting. When Finance just says "marketing budget is 10 tỷ", nobody can challenge it effectively. When Finance says "marketing budget is 10 tỷ = 2,000 new customers × CAC 5M + 0.5 tỷ retention campaigns + 1.5 tỷ brand events", every component is challengeable: "Is CAC 5M achievable? Can we reduce retention to 0.3 tỷ?" Driver transparency makes budget conversations smarter.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Budget gross revenue without netting out discounts. "Revenue budget is 100 tỷ." But if average discount is 8% and return rate is 2%, net revenue is only 90 tỷ. Planning gross vs net creates disconnect between Sales team's "revenue" and Finance's "revenue." Always define clearly: gross vs net; and budget both.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Build operating budget without linking to capacity constraints. "We'll produce 50,000 units." Does the factory have capacity? Do we have materials? Do we have people? Budget must be tested against operational constraints or production shortfall will surprise in Q2.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Personnel budget includes approved headcount that doesn't get hired. "We budgeted 8 new hires but only hired 5 → we're under budget on salary." But the 3 unfilled positions mean planned work isn't being done → output suffers. Variance analysis should track not just cost variance but also output variance — "we saved 600M on salary but missed 3 tỷ in revenue because the sales team was understaffed."
