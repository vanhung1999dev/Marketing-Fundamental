### Bộ Báo Cáo Tài Chính Dự Phóng (Pro Forma Financial Statements)

Pro Forma Financial Statements là bộ ba báo cáo tài chính được dự phóng cho tương lai — P&L, Balance Sheet, và Cash Flow Statement — hoàn toàn tích hợp và nhất quán với nhau. Đây là đầu ra cuối cùng và quan trọng nhất của quy trình lập ngân sách chiến lược.

> **Bài học:** Có P&L budget mà không có Cash Flow budget giống như biết mình sẽ kiếm bao nhiêu nhưng không biết tiền đâu ra để trả nhân viên vào ngày 5 hàng tháng. P&L đo profitability; Cash Flow đo survival. Một công ty profitable có thể fail vì cash flow issues — "profit is an opinion, cash is a fact."

---

**Tích hợp Ba Báo Cáo:**

```
P&L (Income Statement):
  Revenue → COGS → Gross Profit → SG&A → EBITDA → D&A → EBIT → Interest → Tax → Net Income
  
  Feeds into:
    Net Income → Equity (retained earnings) in Balance Sheet
    Depreciation → add back to reconcile to Cash Flow
    Revenue × DSO/365 → Accounts Receivable in Balance Sheet
    COGS × DIO/365 → Inventory in Balance Sheet
    COGS × DPO/365 → Accounts Payable in Balance Sheet

Balance Sheet:
  Assets = Liabilities + Equity (must balance every period)
  
  Changes in Balance Sheet items → Cash Flow:
    AR increases → cash outflow (more receivables = cash tied up)
    Inventory increases → cash outflow
    AP increases → cash inflow (delay payment = cash preserved)
    PP&E increases (capex) → cash outflow
    Debt increases → cash inflow (financing)
    Equity increase from retained earnings → from P&L Net Income

Cash Flow Statement:
  Operating Cash Flow = Net Income + Depreciation ± Working Capital changes
  Investing Cash Flow = Capex + Acquisitions ± Disposals
  Financing Cash Flow = Debt drawn/repaid + Equity raised - Dividends paid
  Net Change in Cash = Sum of all three
  Ending Cash = Opening Cash + Net Change
```

---

**Projected P&L — key line items:**

```
Revenue: From Sales Budget (volume × price, by product/channel)

COGS: From Production Budget
  Materials: BOM × volume, with commodity price assumptions
  Direct Labour: FTEs × cost per FTE × production volume
  Manufacturing Overhead: fixed + variable components

Gross Profit = Revenue - COGS
Gross Margin % — key metric to track trend

SG&A: From department budgets
  Selling: Sales team + commission + marketing
  G&A: Finance + HR + IT + Admin + Legal

EBITDA = Gross Profit - SG&A (Earnings Before Interest, Tax, D&A)
EBITDA Margin % — most watched profitability metric by management and lenders

Depreciation & Amortization: From asset register + capex schedule

EBIT = EBITDA - D&A

Interest: From debt schedule (loan balance × interest rate)
  Beware: floating rate debt in rising rate environment
  VN: Most corporate debt is at floating or annually-reset rates

EBT = EBIT - Interest

Tax: EBT × 20% (standard CIT rate VN, per Luật Thuế TNDN)
  Adjustments: non-deductible expenses; tax incentives; prior year adjustments
  Always involve tax advisor for accuracy

Net Income: EBT - Tax
  Dividends: from dividend policy
  Retained Earnings: Net Income - Dividends → flows to Equity in Balance Sheet
```

**Projected Cash Flow — monthly is critical:**

```
Monthly Cash Flow Budget:

Operating:
  Cash receipts from customers:
    Revenue × collection timing = (% collected in month of sale × Revenue)
                                + (% collected in following month × prev month Revenue)
    Example: 70% collected same month; 25% in month+1; 5% in month+2
  
  Cash payments to suppliers:
    COGS × payment timing (DPO = 45 days → roughly paid 1.5 months after purchase)
  
  Operating expense payments: mostly paid in month incurred (salaries, utilities)
  
  Tax payments: quarterly installments (typically 20% of estimated annual tax)

Investing:
  Capex payments: per project schedule (may lump in one month or spread over months)

Financing:
  Loan drawdowns: when needed based on cash shortfall
  Loan repayments: per debt amortization schedule
  Dividends: per board decision timing

Net Cash Change each month:
  Positive: cash surplus (can repay debt or invest)
  Negative: cash deficit (need to draw on credit facility)

Minimum cash buffer: typically 1.5–2 months of operating expenses
  → Below this threshold: trigger action (draw on credit, delay capex, accelerate collections)
```

**Projected Balance Sheet — quarterly:**

```
Key working capital calculations:

Accounts Receivable = Revenue (quarterly) × DSO / 90
  Example: Q1 Revenue 25 tỷ; DSO 45 days
  Q1 AR = 25 × 45/90 = 12.5 tỷ

Inventory = COGS (quarterly) × DIO / 90
  Example: Q1 COGS 16 tỷ; DIO 60 days
  Q1 Inventory = 16 × 60/90 = 10.7 tỷ

Accounts Payable = COGS (quarterly) × DPO / 90
  Example: Q1 COGS 16 tỷ; DPO 45 days
  Q1 AP = 16 × 45/90 = 8.0 tỷ

PP&E = Opening PP&E + Capex additions - Depreciation
  Track separately: Gross cost vs Accumulated Depreciation vs Net Book Value

Balance Sheet must balance:
  Total Assets = Total Liabilities + Total Equity
  If doesn't balance → error in model (check retained earnings, check AR/AP timing)
```

**Variance Analysis Framework:**

```
Monthly actual vs budget review:

REVENUE VARIANCE:
  Volume variance: (Actual volume - Budget volume) × Budget price
  Price variance: (Actual price - Budget price) × Actual volume

COST VARIANCE:
  Volume variance: (Actual volume - Budget volume) × Budget unit cost
  Rate variance: (Actual unit cost - Budget unit cost) × Actual volume

Example:
  Budget: 1,000 units × 100,000 VNĐ/unit = 100 triệu revenue
  Actual: 900 units × 108,000 VNĐ/unit = 97.2 triệu revenue

  Volume variance: (900-1,000) × 100,000 = -10 triệu (unfavorable)
  Price variance: (108,000-100,000) × 900 = +7.2 triệu (favorable)
  Net variance: -2.8 triệu (unfavorable)

  Interpretation: Sold fewer units (missed volume) but at higher price (mix/pricing better)
  Action: Investigate why volume was lower; protect pricing discipline
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** The three-statement model is the foundation of all corporate finance analysis — from budgeting to M&A valuation to IPO preparation. A financial professional who can build a fully integrated three-statement model from scratch is valuable because it requires deep understanding of how accounting works (how transactions flow through all three statements). Excel-based models should be built with clear sections for assumptions, income statement, balance sheet, cash flow, and checks — the "check" cell that shows if balance sheet balances being the most important.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Build each statement independently without ensuring they reconcile. P&L looks good; Balance Sheet looks good; Cash Flow looks good — but they don't actually link together. If Net Income in P&L ≠ the source of retained earnings growth in Balance Sheet, model is broken. Always build integration checks.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Assume all revenue is collected on time. "Revenue budget is 100 tỷ." Cash receipts budget assumes 100% collected. But if DSO is 45 days, cash receipts in any month ≠ revenue in that month. Collection timing assumptions are one of the most important and most often wrong inputs in cash flow modeling.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Project financial statements without stress-testing. "Here's our forecast." Is this the best case, base case, or worst case? A single-scenario projection gives false precision. Run at minimum: base (most likely), downside (plausible bad scenario), and liquidity stress (when does cash run out under downside?). The liquidity stress test is most critical for operational management.
