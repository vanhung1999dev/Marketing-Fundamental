### Cấu Trúc Ngân Sách Tổng Thể (Master Budget)

Master Budget là tập hợp tất cả các ngân sách bộ phận được tổng hợp thành bức tranh tài chính toàn diện — bao gồm P&L dự phóng, Balance Sheet dự phóng, và Cash Flow dự phóng. Đây là đầu ra cuối cùng của quy trình lập ngân sách.

> **Bài học:** Master Budget không phải là Excel mà Finance dùng riêng — là ngôn ngữ chung của toàn bộ tổ chức. Khi CEO hỏi "chúng ta có đủ tiền để làm X không?", CFO nên có thể chạy X qua Master Budget model và trả lời trong vài giờ, không phải vài tuần.

---

**Cấu trúc Master Budget:**

```
OPERATING BUDGET:
  Revenue Budget          → Sales volume × price by product/channel
  Production Budget       → Units to produce = Sales + Ending Inv - Beginning Inv
  COGS Budget             → Materials + Labour + Overhead
  SG&A Budget             → Selling + General & Administrative expenses
  Operating Income Budget → Revenue - COGS - SG&A

CAPITAL BUDGET:
  Capex by project with business case
  Depreciation schedule (feeding into COGS and SG&A)
  Financing plan (debt, equity, retained earnings)

FINANCIAL STATEMENTS (Projected):
  P&L (Income Statement): monthly for year; quarterly trend
  Balance Sheet: quarterly snapshots
  Cash Flow Statement: monthly; critical for liquidity management

SUPPLEMENTARY SCHEDULES:
  Headcount plan: by department, by month
  Working capital model: inventory, AR, AP days
  Debt schedule: repayment, interest
  Tax calculation
```

**Balance Sheet Budget — quarterly:**

```
Key items to project quarterly:

Assets:
  Cash: Opening + Net Cash Flow from Cash Budget
  Accounts Receivable: Revenue × DSO/365
  Inventory: COGS × DIO/365
  PP&E: Opening + Capex - Depreciation
  
Liabilities:
  Accounts Payable: COGS × DPO/365
  Short-term debt: per financing plan
  Long-term debt: per repayment schedule
  
Equity:
  Opening equity + Net Income - Dividends

Balance check: Assets = Liabilities + Equity (must balance every period)
```

---

**Integration Architecture:**

```
How sub-budgets feed Master Budget:

Sales Budget
    ↓ Revenue
Revenue → Accounts Receivable, Cash receipts

Production Budget
    ↓ Production volume
Production → Materials Budget, Labour Budget, Overhead Budget
    ↓ COGS
COGS → Inventory, Cost of Sales in P&L

SG&A Budgets (each department)
    ↓ Operating expenses
SG&A → P&L

Capex Budget
    ↓ PP&E additions, Depreciation
Capex → Balance Sheet, P&L (depreciation)

Cash Budget (pulls from all above):
  Cash receipts from Revenue budget
  Cash payments from COGS, SG&A, Capex budgets
  Financing activities from debt schedule
    ↓
  Projected cash position each month
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** The discipline of building a fully integrated financial model (P&L + BS + CF all linked) is what separates sophisticated financial planning from simple forecasting. When you change one assumption (e.g., payment terms with customers shift from 30 to 45 days), a properly integrated model automatically flows that change through to accounts receivable, cash flow, and potentially financing needs. This integration is what allows "what-if" scenario analysis to work reliably.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Build P&L budget but not cash flow budget. Many SMEs plan P&L (profitability) without planning cash flow (liquidity). A profitable company can still run out of cash if receivables are slow and payables are fast. The cash budget is often more important than the P&L budget for operational management.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Budget at too aggregated level. "Total SG&A: 15 tỷ" — this doesn't help manage. Budget must be detailed enough to be manageable: by department, by major cost category, by month. But not so detailed that it takes 3 months to build and nobody maintains it during the year.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Master Budget model not updated when assumptions change. "We built the budget in November with USD/VNĐ 25,000, now it's 26,200 — our COGS is 5% higher than budget automatically." If the model is not updated with revised assumptions, actual vs budget comparison becomes meaningless. Monthly: update macro assumptions that have materially changed; track actuals against revised forecast, not just original budget.
