### Quy Trình Lập Ngân Sách Theo Mô Hình RACI

RACI (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) là framework để clarify roles trong bất kỳ quy trình phức tạp nào — và lập ngân sách là một trong những quy trình phức tạp nhất trong doanh nghiệp vì nó liên quan đến nhiều bộ phận, nhiều cấp quyết định, và nhiều conflicts về priorities.

> **Bài học:** Budget process không thể được run bởi Finance alone và cũng không thể bị dominated bởi operations alone. Finance là process owner; business units là content owners; CEO là decision-maker. Khi RACI không rõ ràng, ngân sách trở thành cuộc đấu tranh quyền lực thay vì allocation of resources to best strategic use.

---

**RACI Matrix cho Budget Process:**

| Activity | CEO | CFO/Finance | Dept Heads | Board |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Set strategic direction & priorities | **A** | C | C | C |
| Define budget guidelines (envelope, assumptions) | C | **A** | I | I |
| Build department budget proposals | I | C | **A** | I |
| Challenge and validate proposals | C | **A** | R | I |
| Consolidate into master budget | I | **A** | C | I |
| Review and approve master budget | **A** | R | C | C |
| Board ratification (if required) | R | R | I | **A** |
| Communicate approved budget | I | **A** | R | I |
| Monitor actuals vs budget (monthly) | C | **A** | R | I |
| Mid-year reforecast | C | **A** | R | I |

**Budget Calendar — VN Corporate Standard:**

```
YEAR N (current year):
  August:   CFO issues Budget Guidelines memo to all dept heads
              → Top-down envelope (total spend available)
              → Key assumptions (FX, inflation, salary increase %)
              → Strategic priorities for Year N+1
              → Template and deadlines

  September: Departments begin building bottom-up proposals
              → Revenue budget (Sales)
              → COGS budget (Operations/Procurement)
              → OpEx by department (HR, Marketing, IT, etc.)
              → Capex requests with business cases

  October:  First submission to Finance
              → Finance reviews, challenges, requests revisions
              → Cross-department consistency check
              → First consolidated P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow projection

  November: Iteration rounds
              → Budget challenges: "Why do you need this? What is the expected return?"
              → Trade-off discussions between departments
              → Scenario analysis (base/upside/downside)

  November 30: Draft master budget to CEO/Leadership team

  December: Board review and approval
              → Year N+1 budget approved before December 31
              → Communication to all departments

YEAR N+1 (budget year):
  January 15: Individual performance targets communicated to all
  Monthly:    Actual vs budget variance analysis and review
```

---

**Budget Guidelines Memo — what Finance must communicate:**

```
Top-down envelope:
  Total operating cost envelope: e.g., max 10% increase vs this year
  Capex ceiling: e.g., 15 tỷ total; require business case >500M per item
  Headcount: e.g., net additions ≤15 FTEs; must justify each new role

Economic assumptions (use consistently):
  USD/VNĐ rate: 25,200 (for planning)
  CPI/inflation: 4.5%
  Salary increase: 8% (applied to existing headcount)
  Minimum wage: per Nghị định in effect for Jan 1
  BHXH employer rate: 22.5% (17.5% BHXH + 3% BHYT + 1% BHTN + 1% BHTNLĐ)
  Corporate income tax rate: 20%

Strategic priorities to budget against:
  Priority 1: Enter Northern market — sales and marketing investment
  Priority 2: ERP implementation — IT and process change investment
  Priority 3: Product quality improvement — R&D and QC investment

Required outputs:
  □ Monthly P&L (12 months)
  □ Quarterly cash flow
  □ Headcount plan by month
  □ Capex schedule with business cases
  □ Key assumptions and sensitivities
```

---

**Budget Challenge Process:**

```
Finance's role in challenge sessions:
  NOT: rubber-stamp whatever department submits
  NOT: cut everything 20% arbitrarily
  YES: ask smart questions

Challenge questions:
  "This 20% marketing increase — what is the expected revenue return?"
  "You're requesting 2 new headcount — what will they do that existing team can't?"
  "This IT investment of 3 tỷ — what is the business problem being solved?"
  "Your salary costs are up 35% vs last year — please explain."
  "If we cut this 15%, what objective gets impaired?"

Finance must:
  □ Check mathematical accuracy of submissions
  □ Check consistency with strategic guidelines
  □ Check for double-counting or missing costs
  □ Stress-test key assumptions
  □ Ensure downside scenario is modeled
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Best-in-class budgeting organizations separate the "should" from the "can." Finance focuses on "can we afford this mathematically?" while strategic leadership focuses on "should we invest here vs somewhere else?" Confusing these roles leads to budgets that are financially precise but strategically incoherent, or strategically ambitious but financially unsound.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Start budget building without top-down guidance. Departments build whatever they want → Finance consolidates → Total is 150% of what's available → Painful cuts begin → Process takes 6 months, morale damaged. Fix: CFO issues envelope (maximum spend by category) before departments build. Departments work within envelope from day one.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Budget calendar too compressed. "We need next year's budget approved by November 15 — start September 15." Two months for a robust strategic budgeting process is insufficient. 4 months minimum (August to November) allows proper strategic analysis, proposal development, challenge, iteration, and approval.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Budget as negotiation game. Departments over-request knowing Finance will cut → Finance cuts 20% across the board → Departments "win" by over-requesting enough. This adversarial dynamic wastes enormous energy and produces a budget that neither reflects strategy nor realistic cost. Fix: require business cases for all increases; penalize gaming by remembering it next year.
