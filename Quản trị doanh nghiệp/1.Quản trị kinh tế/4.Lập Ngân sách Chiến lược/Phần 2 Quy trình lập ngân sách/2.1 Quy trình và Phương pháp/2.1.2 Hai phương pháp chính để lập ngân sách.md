### Hai Phương Pháp Chính Lập Ngân Sách: Incremental và Zero-Based

Incremental Budgeting (IB) và Zero-Based Budgeting (ZBB) đại diện cho hai triết lý đối lập trong lập ngân sách. Thực tế, hầu hết các doanh nghiệp hiệu quả dùng hybrid — ZBB cho strategic spending, incremental cho stable operational spending.

> **Bài học:** ZBB không phải là phương pháp kỳ diệu để cắt giảm chi phí — là phương pháp để justify mọi đồng chi tiêu từ đầu, đảm bảo rằng historical spending habits không perpetuate themselves. Nhưng ZBB cũng tốn kém và mệt mỏi nếu áp dụng cho toàn bộ ngân sách — chọn đúng nơi để dùng nó.

---

**Incremental Budgeting (IB):**

```
Cách thức:
  Ngân sách năm N+1 = Ngân sách năm N × (1 + adjustment%)
  
  Ví dụ:
    Marketing budget năm nay: 3 tỷ
    Adjustment: +10% (inflation + volume growth)
    Budget năm tới: 3 tỷ × 1.10 = 3.3 tỷ

Advantages:
  ✓ Fast and simple
  ✓ Predictable (departments know roughly what they'll get)
  ✓ Low administrative burden
  ✓ Suitable for stable, well-understood cost categories

Disadvantages:
  ✗ Perpetuates past spending patterns (good and bad)
  ✗ No challenge to fundamental necessity of existing spend
  ✗ Rewards spending all of budget to protect next year's allocation
  ✗ Does not reallocate based on strategic priority shifts
  ✗ "Baseline entitlement" culture
```

---

**Zero-Based Budgeting (ZBB):**

```
Cách thức:
  Every budget line starts from ZERO
  Each expense must be justified from scratch — không có "we always do this"
  
  Process:
    1. Identify "decision packages": activities that could be funded/not funded
    2. Rank decision packages by value and strategic alignment
    3. Fund from top until budget envelope is exhausted
    4. Everything below the line is unfunded

Advantages:
  ✓ Forces examination of every expense
  ✓ Eliminates zombie spending (activities that continue without value)
  ✓ Reallocates to strategic priorities each year
  ✓ Reveals cost structure clearly

Disadvantages:
  ✗ Very time-intensive (3–4× more effort than incremental)
  ✗ Can create uncertainty and stress for departments
  ✗ Risk of cutting stable, valuable activities for lack of documentation
  ✗ Difficult to implement fully in large, complex organizations
  ✗ Can damage long-term investments if annual ZBB over-optimizes for short-term
```

---

**Hybrid Approach — practical recommendation:**

```
Apply ZBB to:
  → Discretionary spending (marketing, training, travel, events)
  → New initiative requests
  → Indirect functions (HR, IT, Finance overhead itself)
  → Areas where cost has been growing without clear ROI evidence

Apply Incremental to:
  → Core operational costs (facilities, utilities, mandatory compliance)
  → Cost of goods sold (better to model from volume + unit cost)
  → Headcount for existing roles (incremental for pay increases)
  → Regulatory and contractual commitments

Calendar:
  Full ZBB every 3 years (complete ground-up justification)
  Modified incremental + challenge in interim years
  This balances rigor with administrative efficiency
```

---

**Rolling Forecast vs Annual Budget:**

```
Annual Budget (traditional):
  Built once, locked for the year
  Comparison: Actuals vs budget (fixed target)
  
  Problem: Business changes; budget becomes irrelevant by Q3
  "We're beating budget but missing market opportunity because budget was set when market was different"

Rolling Forecast (modern):
  Always-current forward look: e.g., 12-month rolling forecast, updated quarterly
  Budget is strategic anchor; rolling forecast is operational guide
  
  Process:
    → Q1 ends: add Q1 next year, drop Q1 this year → always 12 months forward
    → Update based on YTD actuals + revised outlook
    → More accurate; more work to maintain
  
  Driver-Based Forecasting:
    → Don't forecast every line item; forecast key drivers
    → Revenue driver: price × volume by product
    → COGS driver: volume × unit cost (from procurement)
    → Headcount driver: FTE × average cost
    → When drivers updated → P&L updates automatically
```

**Comparative Example:**

| Scenario | IB Result | ZBB Result | Winner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stable ops, well-justified costs | 3.3 tỷ (+10%) | 3.2 tỷ (justified same) | IB (less effort, same result) |
| Department with historical bloat | 3.3 tỷ (+10%) | 2.4 tỷ (justified only needed) | ZBB (found 0.9 tỷ of savings) |
| Shifting strategic priorities | +10% to old priority | 0% to old, fund new priority | ZBB (better reallocation) |
| New initiative | Not naturally accommodated | Explicit decision package | ZBB (clearer decision) |

> **Phân tích sâu:** Kraft Heinz famously applied aggressive ZBB after its 3G Capital and Berkshire Hathaway acquisition, cutting SG&A dramatically. Short-term: impressive margin expansion. Long-term: brand underinvestment led to revenue deterioration and massive goodwill writedowns. ZBB that prioritizes short-term cost efficiency over long-term brand health is a cautionary tale. ZBB works when decision-makers truly understand which "cuts" will harm long-term value and protect those investments even under ZBB rigor.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Apply ZBB across entire budget simultaneously. Enormous administrative burden + organizational stress → process fails. Start with one department or one cost category (e.g., marketing discretionary spend), build capability, then expand. ZBB is a muscle that must be built gradually.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Use incremental for strategic initiatives. "This new initiative starts with a 10% increase from last year's similar spend." But if last year's spend was on a different initiative, there's no baseline — this is a new investment decision requiring ZBB logic: justify from scratch, what return, what if not funded?

> **Cạm bẫy:** Rolling forecast replaces budget discipline. "We have rolling forecast now, so annual budget is less important." Budget is accountability anchor: what did we commit to achieve with what resources. Rolling forecast is the latest view of where we're going. Both serve different purposes. Remove the annual budget → remove accountability. Remove rolling forecast → lose current visibility. Need both.
