### Liên Kết Mục Tiêu Tăng Trưởng Với Nhu Cầu Vốn

Mỗi mục tiêu tăng trưởng đều có chi phí vốn tương ứng. Nhà quản trị giỏi không chỉ đặt mục tiêu doanh thu và lợi nhuận — mà còn lập kế hoạch vốn để đạt mục tiêu đó, hiểu rõ tỷ lệ giữa tăng trưởng và nhu cầu tài chính bổ sung, và đưa ra quyết định huy động vốn dựa trên kế hoạch kinh doanh cụ thể.

> **Bài học:** Tăng trưởng luôn tốn tiền. Doanh nghiệp tăng trưởng 30%/năm không có kế hoạch vốn rõ ràng = đang cầm bom nổ chậm. Nhiều doanh nghiệp thành công đã gần phá sản vì tăng trưởng quá nhanh mà vốn không theo kịp — "overtrading" hay "growing broke."

---

**Sustainable Growth Rate — Tốc Độ Tăng Trưởng Bền Vững:**

```
SGR = ROE × Retention Rate = ROE × (1 - Dividend Payout)

Where:
  ROE = Net Income / Equity
  Retention Rate = % of net income reinvested (1 - % paid as dividend)

INTERPRETATION:
  SGR = Maximum growth rate achievable WITHOUT needing external financing
  
  If actual growth > SGR: Company MUST get external capital (debt or equity)
  If actual growth < SGR: Company can self-finance growth; may accumulate excess cash

EXAMPLE:
  Net Income: 10 tỷ; Equity: 60 tỷ; ROE = 16.7%
  Dividend payout: 30%; Retention: 70%
  SGR = 16.7% × 70% = 11.7%
  
  If planned growth = 25% → Need external capital = 25% - 11.7% = 13.3% of asset base
  Asset base: 130 tỷ; External capital needed: 130 × 13.3% = 17.3 tỷ/year

SGR LEVERS (ways to increase SGR without external capital):
  1. Improve ROE (higher margin; better asset turnover; same leverage)
  2. Reduce dividend payout (reinvest more)
  3. Improve asset efficiency (grow revenue without proportional asset growth)
  4. Optimize working capital (reduce NWC/Revenue ratio = need less capital per unit growth)
```

**Capital Requirements by Growth Strategy:**

```
SCENARIO A: Organic Growth (Tăng trưởng hữu cơ) — Revenue +25%

  Starting position: Revenue 100 tỷ; Assets 130 tỷ; Equity 50 tỷ; Debt 80 tỷ
  
  Asset Growth Need:
    Assume assets grow proportionally with revenue: Asset/Revenue = 1.3×
    Target revenue: 125 tỷ; Target assets: 125 × 1.3 = 162.5 tỷ
    Asset increase needed: 32.5 tỷ
  
  Self-funded from retained earnings:
    Net Income: 100 × 15% net margin = 15 tỷ; Retain 70% = 10.5 tỷ
  
  External capital needed: 32.5 - 10.5 = 22 tỷ
  Can come from: Debt (22 tỷ additional); Equity injection; Mix
  
  Post-financing D/E check:
    New debt: 80 + 22 = 102; New equity: 50 + 10.5 = 60.5
    D/E = 102/60.5 = 1.69× (manageable for manufacturing)

SCENARIO B: Acquisition Growth — Buy competitor for 30 tỷ EV

  Financing structure:
    Cash: 5 tỷ (from existing cash reserves)
    Debt: 20 tỷ (acquisition loan, 5-year term, 12%)
    Equity raise: 5 tỷ (new investor round)
  
  Post-acquisition leverage:
    Old debt: 80 tỷ; Acquisition debt: 20 tỷ; Total: 100 tỷ
    EBITDA (combined): 20 tỷ + 5 tỷ (acquired) = 25 tỷ
    Net Debt/EBITDA: 100/25 = 4.0× (high; acceptable for 2–3 years with deleveraging plan)
    
  Deleveraging plan (critical):
    Year 1–3: Focus on FCF generation; pay down acquisition debt
    Target: Reduce to 2.5× Net Debt/EBITDA within 3 years
    Required FCF for deleveraging: (100 - 62.5) = 37.5 tỷ over 3 years → 12.5 tỷ FCF/year
    Feasibility: With 25 tỷ EBITDA; 5 tỷ tax; 3 tỷ capex → FCF ≈ 17 tỷ; achievable

SCENARIO C: Asset-Light Growth — License model; minimal capex

  Key feature: Growth does not require proportional asset increase
  Asset/Revenue ratio improves over time (more efficient capital use)
  
  Capital need dramatically lower:
    Revenue +25%: Only NWC grows (not fixed assets)
    NWC/Revenue: 15%; Incremental NWC: 25 × 15% = 3.75 tỷ
    SGR easily covers this
    → No external capital needed for 25% growth if asset-light model
```

**Capital Planning Template:**

```
3-YEAR CAPITAL PLAN (to accompany business plan for investors/banks):

                        Year 1    Year 2    Year 3
Revenue                100 tỷ   130 tỷ   169 tỷ
EBITDA (20%)            20 tỷ    26 tỷ    33.8 tỷ
Net Income (12%)        12 tỷ    15.6 tỷ  20.3 tỷ

Capital deployed:
  Beginning equity       50        56.4     67.3
  + Retained earnings   +6.4      +10.9    +14.2 (70% retention)
  + New equity raise    +0        +0       +0
  = Ending equity        56.4      67.3     81.5

  Beginning debt         80        95       102
  + New borrowing       +20       +12      +5
  - Repayments          (5)       (5)      (10)
  = Ending debt          95        102      97

Total capital deployed  151.4     169.3    178.5

Capital efficiency:
  Revenue/Assets         0.66×     0.77×    0.95×  (improving)
  ROIC = EBIT(1-T)/IC    12.5%     14.2%    17.1%  (improving)
  D/E                    1.68×     1.52×    1.19×  (deleveraging)
  
Note: This 3-year capital plan demonstrates the funding requirement,
      the sources, and the trajectory of key financial metrics.
      Every bank and investor will want to see this analysis.
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Capital planning is the synthesis of strategy and finance. Without it, growth targets are aspirations without grounding. With it, targets have a specific resource requirement attached: "To grow 30%/year for 3 years, we need X tỷ of capital, split Y% debt and Z% equity, with the debt fully repaid from operations within W years." This level of specificity builds credibility with lenders and investors while forcing management discipline.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Set growth targets without calculating capital requirements first. "We want to grow 50%/year for 5 years." Great. Do you have the capital? Where will it come from? What's the D/E impact? Can you service the debt at peak leverage? Growth targets without capital analysis = wishful thinking.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Underestimate working capital requirements in growth plans. Fast-growing companies often find that working capital consumes all their cash generation — leaving nothing for debt service or distributions. The NWC/Revenue ratio is the key parameter; if it's 30% and you're growing revenue by 50 tỷ, that's 15 tỷ of cash consumed by WC before any capex.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Assume that equity is "free money" while debt is "expensive." Both have cost. Equity investors expect ROE; if ROE expectations (20–30%) > after-tax cost of debt (8–10%), equity is actually more expensive than debt. The optimal capital structure balances the tax advantage of debt (deductible interest) against financial risk (leverage). Don't avoid debt because it "feels risky"; use it strategically within sustainable limits.
