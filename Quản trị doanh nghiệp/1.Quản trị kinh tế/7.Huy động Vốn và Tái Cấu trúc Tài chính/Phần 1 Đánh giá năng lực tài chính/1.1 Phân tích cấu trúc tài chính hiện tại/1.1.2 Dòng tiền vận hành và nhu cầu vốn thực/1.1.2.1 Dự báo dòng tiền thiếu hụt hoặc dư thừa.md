### Dự Báo Dòng Tiền Thiếu Hụt Hoặc Dư Thừa

Nhu cầu vốn thực sự của doanh nghiệp được xác định bởi dòng tiền — không phải lợi nhuận kế toán. Trước khi đi vay hoặc gọi vốn, phải dự báo chính xác khi nào thiếu tiền, bao nhiêu, và bao lâu. Vay sai thời điểm hoặc sai số lượng đều gây ra chi phí không cần thiết hoặc rủi ro thanh khoản.

> **Bài học:** Có hai loại doanh nghiệp đi vay: loại biết mình cần bao nhiêu và khi nào và loại đoán đại. Ngân hàng và nhà đầu tư dễ dàng phân biệt hai loại này qua chất lượng dự báo dòng tiền. Một kế hoạch vay với cash flow model chi tiết và cơ sở giả định rõ ràng = tín hiệu mạnh về năng lực quản trị tài chính.

---

**Cấu Trúc Dự Báo Dòng Tiền Vốn:**

```
CAPITAL NEEDS FORECASTING FRAMEWORK:

Step 1: Operating Cash Flow Forecast (Dòng tiền từ hoạt động kinh doanh)
  OCF = Net Income + Depreciation ± ΔNWC
  
  For 3-year forecast:
    Revenue forecast (by product/segment)
    - COGS (cost structure)
    = Gross Profit
    - SG&A
    = EBIT
    - Interest (based on current + planned debt)
    - CIT
    = Net Income
    + Depreciation (scheduled)
    ± Working Capital Change (ΔAR + ΔInventory - ΔAP)
    = Operating Cash Flow

Step 2: Investing Cash Flow (Dòng tiền từ đầu tư)
  - Planned Capex (maintenance + expansion)
  + Asset disposals (if any)
  = Investing Cash Flow (typically negative for growing company)

Step 3: Financing Cash Flow (Dòng tiền từ tài chính) — where the gap is filled
  Identified gap = OCF + ICF (if negative, need to fund)
  
  Sources:
    Existing debt service: -Principal payments - Interest
    New borrowing: +Drawdowns planned
    Equity: +Capital injection from owners/investors
    = Net Financing Cash Flow

Step 4: Net Cash Position
  Beginning Cash + OCF + ICF + FCF = Ending Cash
  
  MINIMUM CASH BUFFER: ≥ 1 month operating costs (safety reserve)
  Any period where projected ending cash < minimum = FUNDING GAP to fill
```

**Xác Định Nhu Cầu Vốn Theo Loại:**

```
TYPE 1: WORKING CAPITAL GAP (Thiếu hụt vốn lưu động)

  Cause: Revenue growth requires more AR + Inventory before AP can fund it
  
  Calculation:
    Incremental Revenue: +50 tỷ
    NWC/Revenue ratio: 25% (current)
    Additional NWC needed: 50 × 25% = 12.5 tỷ
    
    Timeline: Need 12.5 tỷ over next 12 months as revenue grows
    Source: Short-term revolving credit line (hạn mức tín dụng)
    Note: This is NOT a term loan; a revolving line is appropriate (fluctuates with business)
    
TYPE 2: EXPANSION CAPEX GAP (Thiếu hụt vốn đầu tư)

  Cause: Capital expenditure for factory, equipment, or technology
  
  Calculation:
    Planned factory investment: 20 tỷ
    Available FCF for next 12 months: 8 tỷ
    Gap: 12 tỷ
    
    Timeline: Need 12 tỷ before construction begins
    Source: Medium/long-term term loan (vay trung/dài hạn)
    Repayment: From incremental EBITDA generated by the new capacity
    
TYPE 3: SEASONAL WORKING CAPITAL (Vốn theo mùa vụ)

  Cause: Business with seasonal peaks needs extra WC at specific times
  
  Example: Retail company with 60% of annual revenue in Q4 (Oct–Dec)
    Peak inventory build: September; need 15 tỷ extra inventory
    Cash collections: October–January (after sales)
    Gap period: September – October (1–2 months)
    Amount: 15 tỷ peak WC
    
    Source: Seasonal credit facility; drawndown September; repaid November–December
    
TYPE 4: BRIDGE FINANCING (Vốn cầu nối)

  Cause: Specific temporary gap (e.g., waiting for large customer payment)
    Customer owes 10 tỷ; expected in 45 days; current obligations need 8 tỷ today
    Gap: 8 tỷ for 45 days
    Source: Short-term bridge; overdraft facility; factoring
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** The most important insight in capital needs assessment is distinguishing between structural gaps (permanent capital requirements for growth) and cyclical gaps (temporary WC needs from seasonality). Structural gaps require equity or long-term debt. Cyclical gaps require revolving credit. Funding a cyclical WC gap with a 5-year term loan is over-borrowing: you'll be paying interest on principal you don't need after the cycle turns. Banks that understand your business will structure the facility appropriately; those that don't will default to term loans. Know the difference and specify your needs precisely.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Build a 12-month cash flow forecast and present only the annual total — hiding months with cash shortfalls behind annual profitability. The bank will see through this. Present month-by-month; identify the peak gap; explain why it's temporary. Hiding cash flow seasonality = undermining credibility.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Build overly optimistic revenue forecasts in the capital needs model. Banks will haircut your projections by 20–30% and rerun their own model. If your base case requires 10 tỷ borrowing and the bank's haircut model shows you need 15 tỷ, you've lost credibility. Present a realistic base case with scenarios; acknowledge the risks.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Request more capital than needed "for safety margin." "Better to have more than we need." This logic leads to borrowing more than you can service, paying interest on idle cash, and reducing DSCR unnecessarily. Instead: build the appropriate minimum cash buffer into the model; request the amount that maintains that buffer in the downside scenario.
