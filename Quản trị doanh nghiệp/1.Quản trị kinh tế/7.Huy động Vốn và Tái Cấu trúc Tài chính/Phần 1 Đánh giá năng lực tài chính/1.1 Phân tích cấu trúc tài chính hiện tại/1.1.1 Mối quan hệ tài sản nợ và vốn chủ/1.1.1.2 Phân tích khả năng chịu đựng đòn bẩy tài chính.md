### Phân Tích Khả Năng Chịu Đựng Đòn Bẩy Tài Chính

Đòn bẩy tài chính (financial leverage) là con dao hai lưỡi: khi kinh doanh tốt, nó khuếch đại lợi nhuận cho cổ đông; khi kinh doanh xấu, nó khuếch đại tổn thất và có thể đẩy doanh nghiệp vào mất khả năng thanh toán. Phân tích khả năng chịu đựng đòn bẩy — còn gọi là "stress testing tài chính" — là bước bắt buộc trước khi quyết định vay thêm.

> **Bài học:** Đòn bẩy không sai — đòn bẩy quá mức mới là vấn đề. Mức đòn bẩy hợp lý phụ thuộc vào tính ổn định của dòng tiền kinh doanh: doanh nghiệp với dòng tiền ổn định (điện, nước, viễn thông) có thể chịu đòn bẩy cao hơn nhiều so với doanh nghiệp có dòng tiền biến động (bất động sản, xuất khẩu, xây dựng).

---

**Cơ Chế Đòn Bẩy Tài Chính:**

```
LEVERAGE EFFECT:

Company A (No debt): Equity 100 tỷ; No debt
  EBIT: 20 tỷ; Interest: 0; EBT: 20 tỷ; CIT (20%): 4 tỷ; Net Income: 16 tỷ
  ROE = 16/100 = 16%

Company B (With debt): Equity 50 tỷ; Debt 50 tỷ @ 12%
  EBIT: 20 tỷ; Interest: 6 tỷ; EBT: 14 tỷ; CIT: 2.8 tỷ; Net Income: 11.2 tỷ
  ROE = 11.2/50 = 22.4% → Higher ROE from leverage!

GOOD SCENARIO — Leverage amplifies returns:
  Base return on assets (EBIT/Total Assets) = 20/100 = 20%
  Cost of debt (after-tax) = 12% × (1-20%) = 9.6%
  Since ROA (20%) > after-tax cost of debt (9.6%): Leverage HELPS equity holders
  
BAD SCENARIO — What if EBIT drops 50% (to 10 tỷ)?:
  Company A: EBT 10; CIT 2; Net Income 8 → ROE = 8%
  Company B: EBIT 10; Interest 6; EBT 4; CIT 0.8; Net Income 3.2 → ROE = 6.4%
  
  MORE EXTREME — EBIT drops to 6 tỷ:
  Company B: EBIT 6; Interest 6; EBT 0; CIT 0; Net Income 0 → ROE = 0%
  Company A: EBT 6; CIT 1.2; Net Income 4.8 → ROE = 4.8%
  
  EVEN WORSE — EBIT drops to 4 tỷ:
  Company B: EBIT 4; Interest 6; EBT = -2 tỷ; Burning equity!
  → Bank may accelerate loan repayment; covenant breach
  → Company A: EBT 4; NI 3.2; Still profitable
```

**Debt Capacity Framework:**

```
MAXIMUM SUSTAINABLE DEBT (Ngưỡng nợ bền vững):

Step 1: Determine "normalized" EBITDA (loại bỏ one-off items)
  3-year average EBITDA or base-case forward EBITDA
  Don't use peak-year EBITDA; use through-the-cycle estimate

Step 2: Apply leverage multiple based on business risk
  Low risk business (stable revenue, essential product): 3.0–4.0× EBITDA
  Medium risk (moderate cyclicality): 2.0–3.0× EBITDA
  High risk (cyclical, project-based): 1.0–2.0× EBITDA
  
Step 3: Calculate maximum debt
  Max Debt = EBITDA × Multiple × (1 - current leverage buffer)
  
  Example: EBITDA 15 tỷ; manufacturing business (3.0× cap); current debt 20 tỷ
    Max debt: 15 × 3.0 = 45 tỷ
    Current debt: 20 tỷ
    Available headroom: 25 tỷ additional debt capacity
    (But practical: leave 20% buffer → available 25 × 80% = 20 tỷ practical headroom)

Step 4: Check against DSCR
  DSCR = (EBITDA - Taxes - Capex) / (Principal + Interest)
  Minimum acceptable: ≥1.25× (most banks require ≥1.2–1.5×)
  
  Example: EBITDA 15 tỷ; Taxes 2 tỷ; Maintenance Capex 1 tỷ → Free cash 12 tỷ
  Current debt service: Principal 5 tỷ + Interest 4 tỷ = 9 tỷ
  DSCR = 12/9 = 1.33× (just above minimum)
  
  New loan: 20 tỷ more at 12% for 5 years → additional service 4+2.4 = 6.4 tỷ/year
  New total service: 9 + 6.4 = 15.4 tỷ
  New DSCR = 12/15.4 = 0.78× → Below 1.0×; CANNOT SERVICE → reject this loan
```

**Stress Test — Kiểm Tra Sức Chịu Đựng:**

```
STRESS TEST SCENARIOS FOR LEVERAGE DECISION:

Before taking on new debt, run 3 scenarios:

Base Case: Current plan
  Revenue: 100 tỷ; EBITDA: 20%; D/EBITDA after new loan: 3.5×
  ICR: 2.5×; DSCR: 1.4× → Viable

Downside (–20% revenue; –5% margin):
  Revenue: 80 tỷ; EBITDA margin: 15% = 12 tỷ EBITDA
  D/EBITDA: 52.5/12 = 4.4× → High
  ICR: 12 × 60% / 6.3 tỷ interest = 1.14× → Very tight
  DSCR: (12 × 60% - 1.2 tax - 0.8 capex) / (4.2+6.3) = 5.2/10.5 = 0.5× → DISTRESS

Severe (–35% revenue; –8% margin):
  Revenue: 65 tỷ; EBITDA: 7% = 4.6 tỷ
  Interest: 6.3 tỷ > EBITDA → Cannot even cover interest from operations
  → Company in financial distress

DECISION FRAMEWORK:
  Base case viable: Yes (minimum condition; necessary but not sufficient)
  Downside viable: Ideally yes; at minimum DSCR ≥1.0× and ICR ≥1.5×
  Severe: Company can survive (not forced default); cash reserves to weather
  
  If downside scenario fails DSCR: Reduce planned borrowing; strengthen equity first
  If severe scenario leads to forced default: Borrowing is reckless; don't proceed

RESULT: The stress test should determine the MAXIMUM NEW DEBT amount
        that keeps the business viable in the downside scenario
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** VN banks apply their own stress tests in credit review, but their models are often backward-looking (based on historical financials) rather than forward-looking (scenario analysis). Smart borrowers prepare their own scenario analysis and present it to the bank proactively. This demonstrates financial sophistication and pre-empts the banker's concerns. A company that can articulate: "In our downside scenario, DSCR is 1.2× — we've stress-tested this loan at these parameters" is far more credible than one that just presents optimistic base-case projections.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Run stress tests only on revenue (what if revenue drops?) without stressing margin simultaneously. The most dangerous scenarios combine revenue decline with margin compression — and these often occur together (volume falls = operating leverage kicks in = margin falls). Stress revenue AND margin together.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Compare DSCR to just the new loan, ignoring existing debt service. Total DSCR must account for ALL debt service obligations. Adding a new loan to an already stretched debt profile can push total DSCR below threshold even if the new loan in isolation looks serviceable.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Take on maximum available debt capacity just because it's available. Lenders will often offer more than you should borrow. The right question isn't "how much will the bank lend?" but "how much can the business sustainably service in a realistic downside scenario?" Borrow within sustainable capacity; leave headroom for unexpected events.
