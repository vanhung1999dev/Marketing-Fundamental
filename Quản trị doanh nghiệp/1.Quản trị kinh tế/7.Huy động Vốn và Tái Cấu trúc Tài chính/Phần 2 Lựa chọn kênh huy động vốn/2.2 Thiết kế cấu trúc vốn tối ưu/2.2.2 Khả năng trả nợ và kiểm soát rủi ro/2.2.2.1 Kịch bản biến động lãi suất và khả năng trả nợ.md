### Kịch Bản Biến Động Lãi Suất và Khả Năng Trả Nợ

Lãi suất VN biến động đáng kể theo chu kỳ kinh tế và chính sách tiền tệ. Doanh nghiệp vay vốn lãi suất thả nổi phải lập kế hoạch cho kịch bản lãi suất tăng — điều này có thể chuyển một khoản vay khả thi thành gánh nặng không thể trả được nếu không có kế hoạch phòng ngừa.

> **Bài học:** Lãi suất VN đã biến động từ 6%/năm (2021) lên 13–14%/năm (2023) rồi về 8–10% (2024–2025). Doanh nghiệp vay lãi thả nổi với DSCR 1.3× ở lãi suất 8% có thể rơi xuống DSCR 0.9× khi lãi suất tăng lên 13% — mà không có gì thay đổi trong kinh doanh. Quản lý rủi ro lãi suất là yêu cầu bắt buộc, không phải tùy chọn.

---

**Lịch Sử Lãi Suất VN và Bài Học:**

```
VNIBOR + BANK LENDING RATE CYCLE:

2020: COVID; SBV cuts rates; lending rate 7–9% (lowest in decade)
2021: Continued low; 6–8%; aggressive borrowing; real estate boom
2022: Inflation; global rate hikes; VN starts tightening
2023 Q1: Lending rate peaks 12–14% for SME borrowers
2023 Q2–Q4: SBV starts cutting; rates ease
2024–2025: Lending rate 8–11% for well-rated SMEs

LESSONS FROM COMPANIES HIT BY 2022–2023 RATE SURGE:
  Companies with DSCR 1.3× at 8% interest:
    Original: EBITDA 15 tỷ; Debt 50 tỷ; Interest 4 tỷ (8%); Principal 5 tỷ
    DSCR = (15-3-2)/(5+4) = 10/9 = 1.11× at 8%
    
    Rate rises to 13%: Interest = 50 × 13% = 6.5 tỷ
    DSCR = (15-3-2)/(5+6.5) = 10/11.5 = 0.87× → BELOW 1.0×!
    
    → Company cannot service debt from operations at 13% rate
    → Must either sell assets; inject equity; or renegotiate with bank
    → Many VN companies found themselves in this position in H1/2023
    
PREVENTION:
  1. Stress test at +3–5% interest rate when deciding on new debt
  2. Keep DSCR ≥ 1.5× at current rates (buffer for rate increase)
  3. Lock in fixed rates for 2–3 years on long-term loans when rates are low
  4. Maintain credit line headroom (don't draw 100% of available credit)
```

---

**Interest Rate Risk — Kiểm Soát Rủi Ro Lãi Suất:**

```
TYPES OF INTEREST RATE RISK:

1. REPRICING RISK:
   Floating rate debt reprices with market rate changes
   → Monthly/quarterly repricing date → new rate applies
   → Impact: Monthly interest payment changes immediately
   
   Measurement: Duration of floating rate debt exposure
   Example: 50 tỷ floating rate; +1% rate increase = 500 triệu more annual interest

2. ROLLOVER RISK:
   Short-term loans must be refinanced at maturity at whatever rate prevails
   → Borrow at 8% today; refinance at 13% next year
   → Cash flow planning based on current rate is wrong
   
   Measurement: % of debt maturing in next 12 months
   Management: Stagger maturities; don't let >40% of debt mature in any single year

INTEREST RATE MANAGEMENT TOOLS:
   
   Fixed rate loans:
     Bank locks in rate for 1–5 years
     VN: Banks often reluctant to offer long-term fixed; may charge premium
     Benefit: Certainty; can plan cash flows exactly
     Risk: If rates fall, you're locked in at higher rate (opportunity cost)
     
   Rate cap agreement (rarely available for VN SME):
     Pay a premium to limit maximum interest rate
     If rate rises above cap: bank compensates the excess
     
   Conservative approach (most practical for VN SME):
     Mix fixed and floating rate debt (50/50 or 60/40 fixed/float)
     Maintain DSCR ≥ 1.5× at current floating rate (buffer = protection against rate rise)
     Keep some debt short-term to benefit when rates fall
```

**Debt Service Coverage — Chi Tiết Tính Toán:**

```
DSCR CALCULATION (Vietnamese banking standard):

DSCR = (Revenue from Project - Operating Costs excl. Depreciation) / (Principal + Interest)
     = (Project EBITDA - Taxes) / Annual Debt Service

OR for corporate loan:
DSCR = (EBITDA - Tax - Maintenance Capex) / (Annual Principal Repayment + Interest)

VN bank benchmark:
  ≥ 1.5×: Strong; easily approvable
  1.25–1.5×: Adequate; approvable with conditions
  1.0–1.25×: Tight; potential covenant breach; bank may require additional collateral
  < 1.0×: Insufficient; loan not serviceable from operations

SENSITIVITY TABLE (changes in DSCR with different scenarios):

Base: EBITDA 15 tỷ; Tax 2 tỷ; Capex 1 tỷ; Debt service 9 tỷ (5 principal + 4 interest)
DSCR = (15-2-1)/9 = 12/9 = 1.33×

Scenario               EBITDA  Tax  Capex  DS     DSCR   Status
-----------------------|-------|-----|-------|-------|-------|-------
Base case              | 15    | 2.0 | 1.0   | 9.0   | 1.33× | OK
Revenue -15%           | 12    | 1.5 | 1.0   | 9.0   | 1.06× | ⚠️
Rate +3% (interest +1.5)| 15   | 2.0 | 1.0   | 10.5  | 1.14× | ⚠️
Both shocks together   | 12    | 1.5 | 1.0   | 10.5  | 0.90× | 🔴
EBITDA -30%            | 10.5  | 1.1 | 1.0   | 9.0   | 0.93× | 🔴

INSIGHT: Combined stress (revenue down + rate up) = DSCR below 1.0
         → Must either (a) improve EBITDA baseline; or
                       (b) reduce debt; or
                       (c) have explicit plan for navigating this scenario
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Banks in VN increasingly conduct their own stress testing on borrower DSCR, particularly for corporate loans above 50 tỷ. They typically apply a +2–3% rate shock and a 10–20% EBITDA haircut to the base case. Smart borrowers present their own pre-stressed DSCR alongside the base case, demonstrating they've thought through the risks. This proactive disclosure often improves credit terms because it reduces the bank's uncertainty.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Calculate DSCR based on forecast-year EBITDA (optimistic) rather than through-the-cycle EBITDA (conservative). Banks will use historical 3-year average EBITDA as the base. If your forecast EBITDA is 15 tỷ but historical average is 10 tỷ, the bank calculates DSCR at 10 tỷ — potentially killing the deal. Present historical performance credibly alongside forward projections.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Ignore the fixed charge coverage ratio (FCCR) which includes lease obligations. With IFRS 16 / VAS changes, operating lease obligations may appear on the balance sheet. Banks increasingly look at FCCR = (EBITDA - Capex) / (Debt Service + Lease Obligations) — which can be significantly lower than pure DSCR for companies with large lease portfolios.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Borrow at floating rate for 5–7 year projects because "rates are low now." The project evaluation should be done at a rate that reflects the THROUGH-THE-CYCLE expectation, not the current rate trough. If your project only works at 8% interest and VN historical average lending rate is 11%, the project may not be economically sound at normalized rates.
