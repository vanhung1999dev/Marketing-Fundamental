### Tỷ Trọng Vốn Vay và Vốn Chủ Theo Chiến Lược

Không có cấu trúc vốn nào tối ưu cho mọi doanh nghiệp — nhưng có cấu trúc vốn phù hợp với từng giai đoạn, ngành, và chiến lược. Bài học từ lý thuyết tài chính doanh nghiệp là: cân đối giữa lợi ích của đòn bẩy (tax shield từ lãi vay) và chi phí của đòn bẩy (financial distress risk; agency cost) để tìm điểm tối ưu.

> **Bài học:** Trade-Off Theory cho rằng cấu trúc vốn tối ưu là điểm mà lợi ích biên của thêm 1 đồng nợ (tax shield) bằng chi phí biên (distress cost). Dưới điểm này: vay thêm tạo giá trị. Vượt quá điểm này: vay thêm phá hủy giá trị. Mỗi doanh nghiệp phải ước lượng điểm cân bằng này cho ngành và giai đoạn của mình.

**Yếu Tố Quyết Định Cấu Trúc Vốn Tối Ưu:**

```
FACTOR 1: ĐỘ ỔN ĐỊNH DÒNG TIỀN (Cash Flow Stability)

High stability → Can support higher leverage:
  □ Utility, infrastructure (concession model): D/E 4–8× (very high leverage OK)
  □ Consumer staples, food & beverage: D/E 2–4×
  □ Manufacturing with long-term contracts: D/E 1.5–3×

High volatility → Must maintain lower leverage:
  □ Commodity businesses (prices fluctuate): D/E ≤ 1.5×
  □ Real estate development: D/E 1–2× (project-specific)
  □ Startup / pre-revenue: D/E ≈ 0 (equity only)
  □ Seasonal businesses: D/E must account for off-season cash flow trough

FACTOR 2: TÀI SẢN THẾ CHẤP (Asset Tangibility)

High tangible assets (property, machinery) → More debt available:
  Lenders can foreclose on tangible assets; lower risk → more willing to lend
  Manufacturing, real estate, mining: High tangibility → high debt capacity

Low tangible assets (brand, relationships, IP) → Less debt:
  Service businesses, software, consulting: Low tangibility → lower debt capacity
  → Must rely more on equity; banks won't lend against intangibles easily

FACTOR 3: TỐC ĐỘ TĂNG TRƯỞNG (Growth Rate)

High growth → More equity, less debt:
  Growth options create value but are uncertain; debt creates cash flow obligations
  Fast-growing company with large capex = needs equity to fund without debt overhang
  
Mature/stable → More debt acceptable:
  Predictable cash flow; limited reinvestment; debt efficiently returns capital to owners
  "Cash cow" business: Maximize leverage within comfort range

FACTOR 4: THUẾ (Tax Efficiency)

If profitable (paying CIT): Debt has tax advantage (interest deductible)
  → Moderate leverage is tax-efficient
  
If loss-making: No CIT to offset → No tax benefit from debt
  → Pure financial risk; debt serves no tax purpose
  → Reduce leverage; seek equity
```

**Capital Structure by Life Stage:**

```
LIFECYCLE CAPITAL STRUCTURE FRAMEWORK:

STARTUP (Year 0–3):
  Typical: 90–100% equity
  Why: No track record; no assets; unpredictable cash flow
  Banks won't lend; equity (founders + angel) is primary source
  Key metric: Runway = Cash / Monthly Burn Rate

EARLY GROWTH (Year 3–7; Revenue 5–50 tỷ):
  Typical: 60–80% equity; 20–40% debt
  Debt: Working capital loans; equipment loans (collateralized)
  Equity: Retained earnings; possibly Angel/Series A round
  Key metric: ICR ≥ 3.0×; maintain DSCR comfortably above 1.25×

GROWTH (Year 7–15; Revenue 50–500 tỷ):
  Typical: 40–60% equity; 40–60% debt
  Debt: Mix of revolving + term loans; possibly corporate bond
  Equity: Retained earnings + possible PE round
  Key metric: Net Debt/EBITDA 2.0–3.5×; DSCR 1.3–1.8×

MATURE/SCALE (Revenue >500 tỷ):
  Typical: 30–50% equity; 50–70% debt
  Leverage closer to optimal range; capital returns to shareholders
  Key metric: Optimize WACC; target credit rating (if applicable)

IPO PREPARATION:
  Pre-IPO: Clean up balance sheet; reduce related-party debt; audit financials
  Post-IPO: Equity base grows; D/E improves; credit rating possible
  New source: Public equity markets (HoSE, HNX, UPCoM)
```

**WACC Optimization — Finding the Sweet Spot:**

```
IMPACT OF CAPITAL STRUCTURE ON WACC:

Illustrative example: Company with 10 tỷ EBIT; Ke = 18%; Kd (pre-tax) = 12%; CIT = 20%

D/(D+E)  Ke    Kd(AT)   WACC    EV       Notes
0%       18%   N/A      18.0%   44.4 tỷ  All equity; high WACC
20%      18%   9.6%     16.3%   49.1 tỷ  Start to benefit from debt tax shield
40%      19%   9.6%     14.2%   56.3 tỷ  Benefit continues; slight Ke increase
60%      21%   9.6%     12.6%   63.5 tỷ  Optimal zone; WACC minimized
70%      24%   9.6%     13.9%   57.6 tỷ  Ke rises fast; distress risk appearing
80%      30%   9.6%     15.7%   51.0 tỷ  Too much debt; WACC rising again

NOTE: Ke increases as leverage rises because equity holders demand more return
      for the increased financial risk of higher leverage
      
OPTIMAL: ~60% debt in this example (company- and industry-specific)
         WACC = 12.6%; EV = 63.5 tỷ (vs 44.4 tỷ all-equity)
         Debt creates ~43% more value in this scenario (through tax shield)

CAUTION: This is theoretical; real world also requires:
  □ Maintaining DSCR ≥ 1.25× in downside scenario
  □ Never exceeding lender covenant thresholds
  □ Always having refinancing plan for maturing debt
  □ Keeping enough credit headroom for opportunistic moves (acquisition, market downturn)
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** For VN SMEs, the biggest capital structure mistake is not over-leveraging — it's under-leveraging from excessive conservatism. Many founder-owned businesses have D/E < 0.5× when they could safely operate at 1.5–2.0×, meaning they're passing up significant value creation from the debt tax shield and leverage amplification. This conservatism comes from personal risk aversion (because personal assets are at risk via personal guarantees), not rational financial analysis. Separating the "what is optimal for the business" question from "what is comfortable for the owner" is important.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Use a fixed target D/E without considering DSCR. D/E measures the stock of debt vs equity; DSCR measures the flow (ability to service debt from operating cash flow). Both matter: a company can have "acceptable" D/E but failing DSCR if EBITDA is thin. Always check both ratios.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Rebalance capital structure only at major decision points (expansion, refinancing). Optimal capital structure should be a live target that evolves with the business. As profitability grows and retained earnings build up equity, D/E naturally improves — potentially below optimal. That's the right time to take on more debt (for investment or return of capital) rather than waiting for a crisis.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Copy the capital structure of a listed peer without adjusting for private company premium. Listed companies have access to public equity markets, lower cost of capital, and greater lender confidence. Private SMEs face higher Ke (illiquidity premium), more restrictive covenants, and less refinancing flexibility. The optimal leverage for a private SME is typically lower than for an equivalent listed company.
