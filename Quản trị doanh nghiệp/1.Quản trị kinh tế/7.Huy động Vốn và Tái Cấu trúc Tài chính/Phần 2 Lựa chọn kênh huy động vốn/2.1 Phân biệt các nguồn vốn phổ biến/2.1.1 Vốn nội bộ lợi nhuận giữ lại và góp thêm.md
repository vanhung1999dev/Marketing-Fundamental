### Vốn Nội Bộ — Lợi Nhuận Giữ Lại và Góp Thêm Vốn

Nguồn vốn rẻ nhất và linh hoạt nhất luôn là vốn từ bên trong: lợi nhuận giữ lại và vốn góp thêm từ chủ sở hữu hiện tại. Không có chi phí huy động, không có giao ước (covenant) từ bên ngoài, không pha loãng quyền kiểm soát. Tuy nhiên, vốn nội bộ bị giới hạn bởi khả năng tạo lợi nhuận và ý chí của cổ đông.

> **Bài học:** Pecking Order Theory (Myers, 1984) cho rằng doanh nghiệp ưu tiên nguồn vốn theo thứ tự: (1) Lợi nhuận giữ lại → (2) Nợ → (3) Phát hành cổ phần mới. Lý do: vốn nội bộ không tạo ra vấn đề thông tin bất đối xứng — không cần phải thuyết phục ai về giá trị công ty. Mọi doanh nghiệp nên khai thác tối đa vốn nội bộ trước khi huy động ngoại lực.

**Lợi Nhuận Giữ Lại — Retained Earnings:**

```
RETAINED EARNINGS CAPACITY:

Annual Net Income × Retention Rate = Annual Capital Addition from Profits

Example:
  Year 1: Net Income 12 tỷ; Retain 80% = 9.6 tỷ added to equity
  Year 2: Net Income 15 tỷ; Retain 80% = 12 tỷ added to equity
  Year 3: Net Income 18 tỷ; Retain 80% = 14.4 tỷ added to equity
  3-year total: 36 tỷ of capital from retained earnings alone

OPTIMAL RETENTION RATE DECISION:
  Pay dividend vs reinvest: Depends on comparison
  
  If ROIC > Cost of equity (investor's required return):
    → Reinvest more; don't distribute; company creates more value by keeping it
    
  If ROIC < Cost of equity:
    → Return capital to owners; they can invest elsewhere at higher return
    
  VN SME typical situation:
    ROIC: 15–25% (profitable growth phase)
    Owner's cost of equity: 12–18% (opportunity cost)
    → Usually should reinvest aggressively; high-growth phase
    → Reduce retention only when ROIC approaching cost of equity (mature phase)

WORKING CAPITAL IMPROVEMENT AS CAPITAL GENERATION:
  Reducing DSO by 15 days: On 100 tỷ revenue = 100/365 × 15 = 4.1 tỷ cash released
  Reducing DIO by 10 days: On 60 tỷ COGS = 60/365 × 10 = 1.6 tỷ released
  Total: 5.7 tỷ — without borrowing or equity injection
  
  This is "found capital" — just as valuable as a bank loan; better because no cost
```

**Góp Thêm Vốn Từ Cổ Đông Hiện Tại:**

```
OWNER/SHAREHOLDER CAPITAL INJECTION:

Process for VN companies:
  
  LLC (TNHH): Điều lệ quy định; thành viên góp thêm theo tỷ lệ vốn góp
              Thủ tục: Quyết định thành viên; điều chỉnh giấy phép kinh doanh
              
  JSC (Cổ phần): Tổng giám đốc + HĐQT phê duyệt; cổ đông hiện tại có quyền ưu tiên
              Chào bán cổ phần mới cho cổ đông hiện hữu
              Đăng ký với Sở KHĐT (nếu thay đổi vốn điều lệ)

CONSIDERATIONS FOR OWNER INJECTION:
  Advantage:
    No interest cost; no covenants; no third-party involvement
    Demonstrates owner confidence → positive signal to banks and other partners
    Improves D/E ratio → enables more debt capacity afterwards
    Quick (can be done in days if owner has capital)
  
  Disadvantage:
    Depletes owner's personal liquidity
    Opportunity cost (owner could invest elsewhere)
    If business fails → owner loses personal capital
    Limited by owner's personal wealth
  
  DECISION FRAMEWORK:
    If ROIC > personal alternative investment return AND company fundamentally viable:
    → Owner injection is rational; company worth personal capital
    
    If owner injecting capital to "save" a structurally broken business:
    → May be throwing good money after bad; assess fundamentals first

EQUITY RECAPITALIZATION (Tăng vốn điều lệ):
  Purpose: Formally increase charter capital; improves balance sheet appearance
  Requirements: Actual cash must flow in; not just accounting entry
  Documentation: Bank transfer evidence; registration update
  
  Benefit: Improved D/E ratio → better credit score → more bank lending capacity
  Cost: Dilutes existing shareholders if new equity issued (vs injection by existing shareholders)
```

**Lợi Nhuận Chưa Phân Phối — Sử Dụng Đúng Cách:**

```
RETAINED EARNINGS ON BALANCE SHEET:

Retained earnings represent cumulative profits kept in the business
They are EQUITY — not cash (common misconception)

Retained Earnings Balance = Σ(Net Income) - Σ(Dividends paid)

DOES NOT equal Cash Available:
  Retained earnings may be invested in:
    - Fixed assets (machinery, buildings)
    - Accounts receivable (extended to customers)
    - Inventory (stocked products)
    - Any other business asset
  
  The CASH sitting idle = Cash account on balance sheet (may be small)

CONVERTING RETAINED EARNINGS TO CASH (to fund new investment):
  Option 1: Generate new free cash flow from operations (OCF - Capex)
  Option 2: Reduce working capital (collect AR faster; reduce inventory; extend AP)
  Option 3: Sell non-core assets (convert fixed assets to cash)
  
  → Retained earnings are the foundation; converting them to deployable capital
    requires cash flow planning

DIVIDEND POLICY FRAMEWORK:
  SME in growth phase: 0–30% payout; reinvest aggressively
  SME in mature phase: 40–70% payout; return to owners; modest reinvestment
  SME in distress: 0% payout; retain all cash; preserve liquidity
  
  Consistency matters: Investors and banks expect consistent policy
  Don't pay large dividend in year when cash flow is tight
  Don't withhold dividend when excessive cash accumulates (diminishes ROE)
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Owner capital injection is the highest-confidence signal available to external capital providers. When the owner invests their own money in the business, it demonstrates alignment of interests and conviction about the company's prospects. Banks and investors always notice owner skin-in-the-game. A company asking for 20 tỷ in bank credit while the owners are distributing 5 tỷ as dividends sends a contradictory signal: "We need capital but we're distributing it." The right sequencing: reduce dividends, reinvest, demonstrate financial discipline — then ask for external capital from a position of strength.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Confuse "equity" on the balance sheet with "available cash." Equity is a residual claim, not a bank account. A company with 50 tỷ equity doesn't have 50 tỷ available to invest — it has 50 tỷ worth of net assets, most of which may be tied up in machinery and receivables. Assess cash availability separately from equity position.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Ignore working capital optimization as a capital source. For most SMEs, improving DSO from 75 to 55 days and DIO from 60 to 45 days releases enough capital to fund 1–2 quarters of growth without any external borrowing. Working capital efficiency is the most underutilized internal capital source.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Pay high dividends to owners in a growth phase because "the business is doing well." A 30% growth business that pays 50% of earnings as dividends is starving its own growth. The sustainable math: if the business earns 20% ROE and can grow at 30%, you need to retain at least 30/20 = 1.5× as much as you earn — meaning you should be injecting capital, not distributing it. High dividends in the growth phase often mean slower growth, more debt dependency, or dilutive equity raises later.
