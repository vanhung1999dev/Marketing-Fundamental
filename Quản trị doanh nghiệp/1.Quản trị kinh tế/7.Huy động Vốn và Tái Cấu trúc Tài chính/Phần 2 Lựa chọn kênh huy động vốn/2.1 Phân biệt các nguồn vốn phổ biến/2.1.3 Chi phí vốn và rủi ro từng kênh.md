### Chi Phí Vốn và Rủi Ro Từng Kênh

Mọi nguồn vốn đều có chi phí — kể cả vốn chủ sở hữu tưởng như "miễn phí." Hiểu và so sánh chi phí vốn thực tế của từng kênh là nền tảng để ra quyết định cấu trúc vốn tối ưu. Chi phí vốn bình quân gia quyền (WACC) là thước đo tổng hợp mà mọi dự án đầu tư phải vượt qua để tạo giá trị.

> **Bài học:** Nếu một dự án đầu tư tạo ra ROIC thấp hơn WACC, nó đang phá hủy giá trị doanh nghiệp — dù báo cáo lợi nhuận dương. WACC là "cost of doing business" — mức sinh lời tối thiểu mà mọi đồng vốn phải kiếm được để xứng đáng với chi phí của nó.

---

**Chi Phí Từng Kênh Vốn:**

```
1. CHI PHÍ NỢ VAY (Cost of Debt — Kd):

   Pre-tax Kd = Nominal interest rate + Hidden costs
   
   Hidden costs of bank borrowing:
     Arrangement fees: 0.5–1.5% of loan value (one-time)
     Collateral valuation fees: 0.3–0.5% of property value
     Mandatory insurance: Fire insurance; credit insurance
     Compensating balance requirements: Bank may require X% of loan kept in account
     
   True pre-tax Kd calculation:
     Loan: 20 tỷ; Nominal rate: 12%; Arrangement fee: 0.5% = 100M; Term: 3 years
     True pre-tax Kd = 12% + 100M/(20 tỷ × 3 years) = 12% + 0.17% = 12.17%
     
   After-tax Kd = Pre-tax Kd × (1 - CIT rate) = 12.17% × (1 - 20%) = 9.73%
   
   NOTE: Interest deductibility creates "tax shield" — effective cost lower than nominal rate
   
   COMPARISON: Bank loan vs bond (if applicable in future):
     Bank loan: 12–14%; flexible; negotiable; requires relationship
     Corporate bond: 10–12% (large companies); rigid; wider distribution; public

2. CHI PHÍ VỐN CHỦ SỞ HỮU (Cost of Equity — Ke):

   CAPM (Capital Asset Pricing Model):
   Ke = Rf + β × (Rm - Rf) + Country Risk Premium
   
   VN parameters (approximate 2025):
     Rf = 4.5% (10-year G-bond yield)
     Rm - Rf = 7–9% (equity risk premium for emerging market)
     β for SME: 1.2–1.6 (higher than listed companies; illiquidity premium)
     Country Risk Premium (CRP): 3–4% (VN sovereign risk above risk-free)
     
   Example: β = 1.3; Ke = 4.5% + 1.3 × 8% + 3.5% = 4.5% + 10.4% + 3.5% = 18.4%
   
   Alternative: Owner's opportunity cost:
     If owner can earn 15% in real estate or listed stocks: Ke = 15%
     Any retained earnings that earn <15% ROIC = below hurdle rate
     
   PE/VC expected return for private company:
     Series A: 25–35% IRR expectation
     Growth PE: 20–30% IRR expectation
     = These represent the "cost" to the company of PE capital

3. CHI PHÍ VỐN BÌNH QUÂN GIA QUYỀN (WACC):

   WACC = (E/V) × Ke + (D/V) × Kd × (1-T)
   
   Where: E = Equity value; D = Debt value; V = E + D; T = CIT rate
   
   Example:
     Equity: 50 tỷ (38% of total capital)
     Debt: 80 tỷ (62% of total capital)
     Ke = 18%; Kd (pre-tax) = 12%; CIT = 20%
     
     WACC = (38% × 18%) + (62% × 12% × 80%)
          = 6.84% + 5.95% = 12.79%
     
     INTERPRETATION: Every project must generate at least 12.79% ROIC to create value
     A project generating 10% ROIC: Destroys value (10% < 12.79% WACC)
     A project generating 20% ROIC: Creates value (20% > 12.79% WACC)
```

**Rủi Ro Từng Kênh Vốn:**

```
RISK PROFILE COMPARISON:

Channel         | Cost    | Dilution | Control | Speed  | Risk to Company
----------------|---------|----------|---------|--------|------------------
Retained EPS    | 0% direct| None    | Full    | High   | None (internal)
Owner injection | 0% direct| Possible| Full    | High   | Owner risk
Bank debt       | 9–14%AT | None     | Some*   | Medium | Covenant breach; collateral loss
PE/VC           | 20–35%  | Yes      | Shared  | Slow   | Loss of control; exit pressure
Strategic       | Varies  | Yes      | Shared  | Slow   | Conflict of interest; know-how loss

*Bank covenants may restrict dividends, additional debt, asset disposals

DEBT-SPECIFIC RISKS:
  Covenant breach risk:
    Financial covenants (e.g., D/E ≤ 3.0×; DSCR ≥ 1.25×) → breach = default
    Behavioral covenants (no additional debt without consent; no major asset sale)
    Solution: Negotiate realistic covenants; build buffer into projections
  
  Collateral risk:
    Personal guarantee = personal assets at risk if company defaults
    Property collateral = property can be seized; business disruption
    Consider: Is this the right project to put personal assets at risk?
  
  Refinancing risk:
    Short-term debt that needs rollover → bank may not renew → forced repayment
    Especially dangerous for long-term projects funded with short-term loans

EQUITY-SPECIFIC RISKS:
  Dilution math:
    Company value: 100 tỷ; New investor: 20 tỷ for 20% stake
    Post-money valuation = 100/80% = 125 tỷ (if pre-money 100 tỷ)
    Existing shareholders: 100 tỷ value × 80% = 80 tỷ (vs 100 tỷ before) but now own 80% of 125 = 100 tỷ
    Dilution only hurts if new capital doesn't create sufficient additional value
    
  Control risk:
    Cumulative dilution: Seed (10%) + Series A (25%) + Series B (20%) = founders own 45%
    At 45%, founders may lose voting control depending on structure
    Protective provisions in shareholder agreement become critical
```

**Decision Framework — Chọn Kênh Vốn Phù Hợp:**

```
DECISION TREE:

Is the capital for working capital or short-lived purpose (<2 years)?
  YES → Bank short-term / revolving credit
  NO → Continue

Is the company profitable and have tangible collateral?
  YES → Bank medium/long-term loan (preserve equity)
  NO → Continue

Does the company have high-growth potential and can share control?
  YES → PE/VC equity
  NO → Continue

Is there a strategic partner who adds more than money?
  YES → Strategic investment (negotiate carefully on control)
  NO → Consider alternative: asset sale; joint venture; franchise

GOLDEN RULES:
  1. Use cheapest capital available first (retained earnings > debt > equity)
  2. Match capital structure to business risk (risky business = more equity, less debt)
  3. Never let debt terms endanger business continuity
  4. Dilute equity only when value creation justifies it
  5. Always have Plan B for refinancing (don't be fully dependent on one lender)
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** The WACC concept is critical because it creates a unified hurdle rate for investment decisions. Without it, management may approve all projects that are "profitable" — including ones earning 8% return while the capital costs 13%. These projects destroy value while looking productive on the income statement. Calculating and using WACC as an investment filter is one of the most impactful financial discipline upgrades an SME can make.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Think of equity as "free" because there's no contractual interest payment. Equity has an opportunity cost — what the owner could earn elsewhere with that capital. If ROE consistently falls below the owner's opportunity cost, the business is underperforming vs alternatives. Recognizing this cost drives better capital allocation decisions.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Evaluate PE/VC investment only by headline valuation, ignoring IRR expectations. A PE fund at "200 tỷ valuation" wants 3× return in 5 years = 25% IRR required. Every year, the company must generate enough value to compound at 25% to meet fund expectations. If the business can't reliably generate 25% value growth, the PE investor will push for aggressive actions (faster growth, cost cutting, early exit) that may not align with founder goals.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Take the highest-valuation offer from an investor without considering the terms. A 150 tỷ valuation offer with 2× liquidation preference + full anti-dilution protection can be economically worse than a 120 tỷ offer with clean terms. The economic value to founders depends on the full deal structure, not just the valuation.
