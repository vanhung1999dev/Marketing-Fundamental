### Dự Báo Ảnh Hưởng Đến Dòng Tiền Sau Thuế

Thuế không chỉ ảnh hưởng đến lợi nhuận — ảnh hưởng trực tiếp đến dòng tiền theo những cách phức tạp hơn nhiều. Dự báo dòng tiền sau thuế chính xác đòi hỏi hiểu rõ timing của nghĩa vụ thuế, cấu trúc khấu hao, và sự khác biệt giữa lợi nhuận kế toán và dòng tiền thực tế.

> **Bài học:** Lợi nhuận sau thuế ≠ Dòng tiền. Một công ty có thể báo cáo lợi nhuận ròng 5 tỷ nhưng dòng tiền âm 3 tỷ trong cùng kỳ — vì doanh thu chưa thu tiền, vì phải đầu tư vốn lưu động, vì trả trước CIT quý. Hiểu sự khác biệt này là kỹ năng tài chính thiết yếu.

---

**Từ Lợi Nhuận Đến Dòng Tiền Tự Do:**

```
FREE CASH FLOW BRIDGE:

Net Income (Lợi nhuận ròng)                        14,400 triệu
(+) Depreciation & Amortization (Khấu hao)          2,000 triệu
  → Non-cash expense; adds back to get cash flow
(+/-) Changes in Working Capital (ΔVốn lưu động)   (1,500 triệu)
  → AR increased (customer hasn't paid yet): negative
  → AP increased (we haven't paid yet): positive
  → Inventory increased: negative
= Operating Cash Flow (OCF)                        14,900 triệu

(-) Capital Expenditure (Đầu tư TSCĐ)              (3,000 triệu)
  → Cash out now; depreciation is the tax benefit over useful life
= Free Cash Flow (FCF)                             11,900 triệu

KEY INSIGHT: Depreciation is a non-cash tax shield
  2,000 triệu depreciation × 20% CIT rate = 400 triệu annual cash tax saving
  → Depreciation doesn't cost cash; it saves cash via tax reduction
  → This is why accelerated depreciation improves cash flow
```

**Tax Timing Effects on Cash Flow:**

```
TAX PAYMENT TIMING vs INCOME RECOGNITION:

Quarter 1: Revenue 30 tỷ; EBT 7 tỷ; CIT provisional due April 30
  Cash collected from customers: 25 tỷ (5 tỷ still AR)
  Actual cash available for CIT payment: operating + collection
  CIT provisional payment: 7 tỷ × 20% = 1.4 tỷ due April 30

Quarter 4 (seasonal high): Revenue 50 tỷ; EBT 14 tỷ
  Annual quyết toán due March 31 next year
  Full-year CIT: 28 tỷ × 20% = 5.6 tỷ
  Provisional already paid (Q1–Q3): 4.2 tỷ
  Q4 balance due March 31: 1.4 tỷ (but this is paid 5 months after Q4)
  
  Cash flow timing benefit: Q4 CIT liability (2.8 tỷ) paid in March = deferred ~90 days
  At 10% working capital rate: 90/365 × 10% × 2.8 tỷ = 69 triệu free float benefit
  Small but real; don't pay early; meet deadlines exactly

VAT CASH FLOW TIMING:
  Month end large invoice: 10 tỷ sale on Dec 30 → 1 tỷ output VAT → due Jan 20
  Cash collection: Expected Feb 28
  Gap: Pay 1 tỷ VAT on Jan 20; collect 11 tỷ on Feb 28 → 39-day cash gap
  Required action: Plan short-term credit line drawdown for Jan 20 payment
                   Repay when customer pays Feb 28
```

**After-Tax Cash Flow Modeling — Capex Decision:**

```
Investment decision: Buy machine for 10 tỷ

WITHOUT TAX:
  Cost: 10 tỷ
  Annual operating savings: 3 tỷ/year × 5 years = 15 tỷ
  Simple payback: 3.3 years
  NPV (10%): 3 tỷ × PV annuity factor (5yr, 10%) - 10 tỷ = 3 × 3.791 - 10 = 1.37 tỷ

WITH TAX (CIT 20%; straight-line 5-year depreciation):
  After-tax operating savings: 3 tỷ × (1 - 20%) = 2.4 tỷ/year
  Annual depreciation: 2 tỷ/year → tax shield = 400 triệu/year
  Total annual after-tax cash benefit: 2.4 + 0.4 = 2.8 tỷ/year
  
  NPV WITH TAX:
    2.8 tỷ × PV annuity (5yr, 10%) = 2.8 × 3.791 = 10.61 tỷ
    Less cost: 10 tỷ
    NPV after tax: +0.61 tỷ (still positive; proceed with investment)
    
  NOTE: If depreciation were 10-year instead:
    Annual depreciation: 1 tỷ → tax shield = 200 triệu/year
    After-tax cash benefit: 2.4 + 0.2 = 2.6 tỷ/year
    NPV = 2.6 × 3.791 - 10 = -1.4 tỷ (negative! project rejected!)
    
    → SAME project; different depreciation assumption → different decision!
    → ALWAYS use minimum depreciation period in capex NPV models
    → Using minimum useful life (max depreciation) improves NPV by 1.37 tỷ in this example

LESSON: Tax assumptions critically affect capex decisions. Standard in VN SME practice
        is to use minimum TT 45 useful life in all capex NPV models.
```

**13-Week Rolling Cash Flow — Tax Integration:**

```
Week | Description          | Tax Items                        | Amount
-----|----------------------|----------------------------------|----------
W1   | Regular operations   | BHXH payroll (employee) withheld | -78M
W2   | Regular operations   | BHXH employer contribution       | -168M (due W2-W3)
W3   | VAT month end        | VAT Day-20 payment               | -850M
W4   | CIT quarter end      | (End Q: CIT provisional due W8)  | Plan
W5   | Operations           |                                  |
W6   | Payroll              | PIT withholding payment (Day 10) | -220M
...
W8   | CIT Q provisional    | CIT quarterly payment (Day 30)   | -1,200M
W9   | Operations           |                                  |
...
W12  | Next month VAT       | Day-20 upcoming VAT payment      | -900M (estimate)
W13  | BHXH next month      | BHXH Day-10                      | -246M

RULES FOR TAX IN 13-WEEK MODEL:
□ Every VAT payment: Enter exact amount and date (Day 20 of month)
□ Every BHXH payment: Enter estimated amount (Day 10 of month)  
□ Every CIT provisional: Enter based on cumulative estimate (Day 30 of quarter)
□ Annual quyết toán: Enter best estimate in W13 if within quarter of March 31
□ PIT withholding: Enter monthly (Day 10)
□ Never leave a tax payment as "TBD" — estimate and flag for update when precise
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** After-tax cash flow modeling is the most important financial skill for evaluating capital allocation decisions. The combination of (1) after-tax operating savings, (2) depreciation tax shield, and (3) timing of tax payments determines the true financial return of any investment. Presenting capex decisions on a pre-tax basis — common in SME environments — systematically overstates returns (because tax cost is ignored) while also missequencing cash flows (because tax payment timing is ignored). Build after-tax models as the default; flag pre-tax analysis as an approximation only.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Ignore depreciation tax shield in NPV calculations. Depreciation costs no cash but creates a real tax saving each year. Missing this in the model understates project returns. For capital-intensive businesses, the depreciation tax shield can represent 15–25% of total project NPV.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Plan Q4 cash flow without accounting for the year-end CIT "top-up" payment. If the year has been unusually profitable (above Q1–Q3 provisional payments), the March 31 settlement can be a significant cash outflow. Build this into the cash model by November once full-year EBT estimate is available.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Use the same after-tax model for GAAP reporting and for management decision-making without distinguishing between the two. Management should use after-tax free cash flow for decisions. GAAP net income for external reporting. The numbers will be different (due to timing); that's expected. What matters is using the right metric for the right purpose.
