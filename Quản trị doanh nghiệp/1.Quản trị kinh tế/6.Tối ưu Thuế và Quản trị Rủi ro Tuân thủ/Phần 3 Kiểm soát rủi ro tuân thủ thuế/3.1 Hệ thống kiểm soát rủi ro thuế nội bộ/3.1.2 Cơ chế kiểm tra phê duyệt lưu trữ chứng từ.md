### Cơ Chế Kiểm Tra, Phê Duyệt và Lưu Trữ Chứng Từ

Hệ thống kiểm soát nội bộ về chứng từ không chỉ bảo vệ doanh nghiệp trong kiểm tra thuế — còn ngăn chặn gian lận nội bộ, sai sót kế toán, và mất mát tài sản. Cơ chế này phải được thiết kế vào quy trình — không thể dựa vào ý thức cá nhân.

> **Bài học:** Kiểm soát nội bộ hiệu quả không phải là kiểm soát phức tạp — là kiểm soát đúng điểm. Nguyên tắc phân quyền (segregation of duties): người phê duyệt không được là người thanh toán; người nhận hàng không được là người tạo PO; người lập bảng lương không được là người phê duyệt bảng lương. Ba điểm kiểm soát này loại bỏ phần lớn rủi ro gian lận và sai sót.

**Ma Trận Phê Duyệt Chi Phí (Approval Authority Matrix):**

```
EXPENSE APPROVAL MATRIX (example for 20–100 tỷ revenue company):

Transaction Type          | <5M       | 5M–20M    | 20M–100M  | >100M
--------------------------|-----------|-----------|-----------|----------
Operating expenses        | Dept Head | CFO       | CEO       | Board
Capital expenditure       | N/A       | CFO       | CEO       | Board
Payroll (monthly)         | HR Manager| CFO       | CEO       | N/A
Vendor payment (new)      | Dept Head | CFO       | CEO       | N/A
Related-party transactions| N/A       | N/A       | CEO       | Board
Tax payments              | CFO       | CFO       | CEO       | N/A

SEPARATION OF DUTIES (critical controls):

1. Payment function separated from approval function:
   → Person approving invoice ≠ person signing payment
   
2. Cash handling separated from accounting:
   → Cashier ≠ accountant recording the transaction
   
3. Procurement separated from accounts payable:
   → Person who creates PO ≠ person who approves invoice payment
   
4. Payroll calculation separated from payroll approval:
   → Accountant calculates ≠ HR/CFO approves
   
5. Journal entries approved by senior reviewer:
   → All journal entries above 5M must have second reviewer sign-off
```

**Document Flow and Control:**

```
PURCHASE-TO-PAY (P2P) CONTROL FLOW:

Purchase Request (PR):
  Created by: Requesting department
  Approved by: Dept Head (per authority matrix)
  
Purchase Order (PO):
  Created by: Procurement / Requesting dept
  Approved by: CFO/CEO per authority matrix
  MST verification: Supplier tax code verified at this stage
  
Goods Receipt Note (GRN) / Acceptance Protocol:
  Created by: Receiving staff (warehouse / project manager)
  MUST be physically signed before invoice is processed
  
Invoice Matching:
  Accounting team: 3-way match PO + GRN + Invoice
  Any variance >5%: Held for investigation; not paid until resolved
  
Payment Approval:
  Prepared by: AP accountant
  Approved by: CFO + CEO (per authority matrix)
  Two-person rule: Both authorizations required for bank transfer
  
Payment Execution:
  Bank transfer with proper payment description referencing invoice number
  Bank statement reconciled weekly
  
Documentation Archive:
  Full package (PR, PO, GRN, Invoice, Bank transfer) filed together
  Digital scan + physical original (or digital original for e-invoice)

ORDER-TO-CASH (O2C) CONTROL FLOW (for revenue):
  Sales Order → Delivery Note (signed by customer) → Invoice → 
  Cash Collection Confirmation → Revenue Recognition
  
  Critical: Revenue cannot be recognized without signed customer delivery note
  VAT invoice must be issued timely (no accumulation of uninvoiced deliveries)
```

**Document Retention System:**

```
PHYSICAL DOCUMENTS (for older records / originals still required):
  Location: Dedicated archive room; locked; access controlled
  Organization: By year, by month, by type (AP / AR / payroll / tax returns)
  Index: Master spreadsheet of all files; box number; location
  Review: Annual review of files >10 years → can destroy (per VN law)

DIGITAL DOCUMENTS:
  Primary storage: Company server or cloud (with proper backup)
  E-invoices: Also stored in GDT portal (einvoice.gdt.gov.vn); always accessible
  Backup protocol: Daily backup to cloud; weekly test restore
  Naming convention: YYYY-MM-DD_[Type]_[VendorName]_[Amount]_[Invoice#]
  Access control: Role-based access; read-only for most; edit rights only for accounting
  Audit trail: Any modification logged with timestamp and user ID

RETENTION SCHEDULE:
  Tax returns + supporting workpapers: 10 years
  Fixed asset records: Life of asset + 10 years
  Employment contracts: Duration + 10 years
  Payroll records: 10 years
  VAT invoices: 10 years (e-invoice on GDT portal: 10 years)
  Bank statements: 10 years
  Contracts (commercial): 10 years after expiry
  Corporate resolutions: Permanent (never destroy)
  
  IMPORTANT: When destroying records past retention, keep an index that shows
             they existed and were properly destroyed
```

**Tax Return Review Checklist:**

```
BEFORE SUBMITTING ANY TAX RETURN:

VAT Monthly/Quarterly Return:
  □ Total output VAT per return = Sum of all output invoices issued in period
  □ Total input VAT per return = Sum of all input invoices (with bank payment for ≥20M)
  □ Beginning VAT credit balance matches prior period ending balance
  □ Any input invoices from suppliers flagged by tax authority as irregular? Remove.
  □ Net VAT payable matches general ledger VAT payable account
  □ Amount matches prior period provisional payment on file

CIT Annual Return:
  □ Revenue per CIT return = Revenue per audited financial statements
  □ All add-backs properly documented (list attached)
  □ All deductions and incentives have legal basis (list and citation)
  □ Loss carryforward correctly calculated and applied
  □ Balance of CIT payable matches general ledger
  □ Transfer pricing documentation attached (if required)
  □ CFO and CEO review and sign

PIT Annual Reconciliation:
  □ Total PIT withheld per return = Sum of monthly withholdings per payroll
  □ Each employee's income and PIT matches individual records
  □ Dependent registrations up to date
  □ Any employees with multiple income sources flagged (may need individual filing)
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Document control and approval matrices are not bureaucratic overhead — they are insurance against the most expensive corporate risk: undiscovered error or fraud in financial reporting. The ROI calculation is straightforward: the cost of a good internal control system (extra accounting staff time, system setup) is a small fraction of the potential cost of a tax assessment with penalties, a fraud discovery, or a bank loan refusal due to unreliable financial statements. For companies seeking investment or bank credit, strong internal controls are a prerequisite.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Have approval processes on paper but bypass them in practice for speed. "We'll get the approval tomorrow." The bypass is precisely when fraud and errors occur. If the approval process is genuinely too slow for operational needs, redesign it — don't bypass it. A faster approval path (e.g., WhatsApp approval documented and ratified) is better than no approval.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Archive documents only at year-end. By December, documents from January may already be lost, misfiled, or incomplete. Daily processing and same-week archiving is the standard. Document control is a daily habit, not an annual event.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Believe that digital storage alone (scanned PDFs on a laptop) is sufficient backup. Hardware failure, ransomware, accidental deletion — a laptop is a single point of failure. Minimum: local storage + cloud backup with versioning. Better: dedicated accounting software with cloud sync + separate backup service. The cost is minimal; the protection is significant.
