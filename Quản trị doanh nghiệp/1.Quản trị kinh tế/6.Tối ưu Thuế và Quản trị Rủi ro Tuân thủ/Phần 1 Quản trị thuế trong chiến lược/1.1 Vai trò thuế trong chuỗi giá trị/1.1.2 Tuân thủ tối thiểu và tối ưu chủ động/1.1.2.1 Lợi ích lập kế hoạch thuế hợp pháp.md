### Lợi Ích Lập Kế Hoạch Thuế Hợp Pháp

Lập kế hoạch thuế (tax planning) hợp pháp là quyền của mọi doanh nghiệp. Đây là việc sắp xếp hoạt động kinh doanh trong giới hạn pháp luật để tối thiểu hóa nghĩa vụ thuế một cách minh bạch — không phải trốn thuế, không phải gian lận, mà là sử dụng đúng các quy định và ưu đãi mà luật pháp cho phép.

> **Bài học:** Lord Tomlin (Anh, 1936) phán quyết: "Mọi người có quyền sắp xếp công việc của mình để thuế phải nộp theo quy định của pháp luật là thấp hơn mức sẽ phải nộp nếu không có sự sắp xếp đó." Nguyên tắc này được hầu hết các hệ thống pháp luật công nhận, bao gồm VN. Không tận dụng các ưu đãi thuế hợp pháp là bỏ tiền trên bàn.

**5 Lĩnh Vực Lập Kế Hoạch Thuế Chính Cho SME VN:**

```
1. CIT INCENTIVES — Tận Dụng Ưu Đãi CIT:
   
   Đầu tư vào địa bàn ưu đãi:
     Địa bàn kinh tế-xã hội khó khăn: CIT 10% trong 15 năm + miễn 4 năm + giảm 50% 9 năm
     KCN, KCX: CIT 10–17% tùy loại
     
   Ngành nghề ưu đãi:
     Phần mềm, công nghệ cao: 10% trong 15 năm
     Giáo dục, y tế, nông nghiệp: 10%
     Dự án đầu tư lớn (>6,000 tỷ): 10% 15 năm; miễn 4 năm; giảm 9 năm
   
   Ví dụ tính toán:
     Standard CIT on 20 tỷ EBT: 4 tỷ
     KCN incentive (10%): 2 tỷ → saving 2 tỷ/year
     15-year NPV at 10% discount: 15.2 tỷ → significant in investment decision

2. DEDUCTIBLE EXPENSE MAXIMIZATION:
   
   Ensure all legitimate costs are properly documented and deductible:
     R&D expenses: Standard 100% deductible; additionally can apply for 150% R&D incentive
     Training and education: Fully deductible with proper documentation
     Business entertainment: Up to 15% of total deductible expenses (Điều 9 CIT Law)
     Depreciation: Use maximum allowable rates (Thông tư 45/2013)
     Provision for bad debt: Deductible when properly classified and documented
   
   Timing of deductions:
     Prepaid expenses: Must amortize; can't deduct all in one year
     Depreciation timing: Straight-line vs accelerated
     End-of-year bonuses: Deductible in year accrued if paid before filing deadline

3. VAT MANAGEMENT:
   
   Input VAT credits: Every business purchase with proper 10% VAT invoice
   creates a credit against output VAT → reduces net VAT payable
   
   VAT refund: If input > output for 12 consecutive months → eligible for refund
               If exporting: 0% on exports; input VAT fully refundable
   
   Timing: Claim input VAT immediately (don't leave credits sitting)
            Submit refund claim promptly; don't let large credits build up without action

4. DEPRECIATION AND CAPITAL ALLOWANCES:
   
   Thông tư 45/2013: Three methods allowed (straight-line, declining balance, unit of production)
   Minimum and maximum useful lives specified by asset category
   
   Strategy: Use minimum useful life (= maximum depreciation per year) to pull forward tax deductions
   
   Example:
     Equipment cost: 5 tỷ
     Tax useful life: 5–10 years (straight-line)
     Using 5-year life: Depreciation = 1 tỷ/year → Tax saving = 200 triệu/year
     Using 10-year life: Depreciation = 500 triệu/year → Tax saving = 100 triệu/year
     Benefit of shorter life: 100 triệu/year × 5 years (earlier) = time value benefit

5. TIMING OF INCOME AND EXPENSE:
   
   Accelerate deductible expenses into current year:
     Year-end bonuses (accrued; pay before filing deadline)
     Deferred maintenance: advance to Dec; get deduction this year not next
     Bulk purchases of consumables before year-end (if material)
   
   Defer income where possible:
     Milestone billing: Don't invoice Dec 31 if Jan 1 achieves same outcome
     NOTE: Must be economically real deferral; artificial deferral = evasion risk
```

**Transfer Pricing — Giao Dịch Nội Bộ Liên Kết:**

```
Nghị định 132/2020/NĐ-CP: Transfer Pricing rules

Applicable when:
  Related party transactions: Parent-subsidiary; sister companies; ≥25% cross-ownership
  
  Common related party structures in VN:
    Holding company → Operating subsidiaries
    Family group companies (ông A owns multiple companies)
    Foreign parent → VN subsidiary

Arm's Length Principle:
  Prices must be as if transacted with independent third parties
  Methods: CUP (comparable uncontrolled price); Cost Plus; TNMM; Profit Split
  
  Planning considerations:
    Intragroup service fees (management fees): must be arm's length; not excessive
    Intragroup loans: interest rate must be arm's length (bank rate reference)
    Property transactions: independent valuation required
    
  Documentation requirement:
    If total related-party transactions > 20% of revenue OR > 20 tỷ:
    Must prepare Transfer Pricing Documentation file (Country File + Local File)
    Submit with annual CIT return
    
  Penalty for non-compliance: Tax authority can re-determine taxable income based on
  arm's-length reconstruction → potential large additional tax + penalties
```

**Tax Planning Calendar:**

```
YEAR-ROUND:
  □ Every invoice: verify VAT rate correct; verify deductibility
  □ Monthly: claim all input VAT credits; no carryover of claimable credits
  □ Quarterly: estimate CIT position; ensure provisional payments adequate

OCTOBER–NOVEMBER (Year-End Planning):
  □ Review year-to-date P&L: estimate full-year taxable income
  □ Identify deductible expenses to maximize before year-end
  □ Bonus accruals: Document and accrue year-end bonuses
  □ Bad debt review: Provision qualifying overdue receivables
  □ Fixed asset additions: Commission before year-end for full-year depreciation
  □ Review CIT provisional vs estimate → top up if needed

DECEMBER:
  □ Final review: all deductions properly documented
  □ Year-end journal entries for provisions, accruals
  □ Transfer pricing documentation updated

JANUARY–MARCH (Annual Filing Season):
  □ Finalize financial statements
  □ Prepare CIT return; reconcile to financial statements
  □ File by March 31; pay any balance
  □ Prepare TNCN annual reconciliation
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** The most valuable tax planning for VN SMEs is often not sophisticated transfer pricing or exotic structures — it's proper documentation of everyday business expenses. Research by VN tax practitioners suggests that the average SME has 10–20% of operating expenses that are legitimate but improperly documented — missing invoices, wrong invoice content, payments to informal suppliers without invoices. Fixing this alone typically saves 2–4% of revenue in additional tax costs (equivalent to 0.4–0.8% of revenue in CIT at 20% rate). For a 50 tỷ revenue company: 200–400 triệu/year of pure saving from documentation discipline.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Confuse "reducing tax" with "hiding income." Tax planning optimizes deductions and timing — it works with actual income and actual expenses. Any arrangement that involves not reporting actual income or fabricating expenses = illegal. The test: would you be comfortable if the tax authority saw exactly what you did and why?

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Engage in tax planning only at year-end. Year-end planning can only adjust the deduction side (accelerate expenses, maximize provisions). The income side (revenue, major transactions) is already done. True tax planning is integrated into business decisions throughout the year: location of new facility, structure of major contracts, timing of asset investments.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Rely entirely on accountant/tax advisor for tax planning without management involvement. Tax advisor can identify opportunities — but the decision to invest in KCN, structure R&D activities, or adjust compensation mix requires management judgment and business strategy alignment. Tax planning is a management responsibility enabled by tax expertise, not the other way around.
