### Tăng Trưởng và Nhu Cầu Vốn Lưu Động

Tăng trưởng doanh thu tiêu thụ vốn lưu động. Đây là cái bẫy phổ biến nhất của startup và SME VN: doanh thu tăng 50%, lợi nhuận tăng, nhưng tiền mặt cạn dần. Hiểu cơ chế này là điều kiện tiên quyết để lập kế hoạch tài chính cho giai đoạn tăng trưởng.

> **Bài học:** Tăng trưởng không "tự tài trợ" được — nó tiêu thụ vốn. Mỗi 1 tỷ doanh thu mới cần một lượng vốn lưu động nhất định để tài trợ. Nếu không tính trước nhu cầu này, doanh nghiệp tăng trưởng nhanh nhưng chết vì khát tiền.

---

**Cơ Chế: Tại Sao Tăng Trưởng Cần Vốn:**

```
Working Capital Cycle trong ngành phân phối:
  Day 0: Mua hàng từ nhà cung cấp → AP tăng (nhà cung cấp tài trợ)
  Day 30: Trả tiền nhà cung cấp → Cash out
  Day 15: Giao hàng cho khách hàng → AR phát sinh (khách hàng "nợ")
  Day 70: Khách hàng trả tiền → Cash in

Net cash out period = Day 70 (cash in) - Day 30 (cash out) = 40 days
  During these 40 days, company must finance the gap from own cash or bank debt

When revenue doubles:
  If DSO, DPO, DIO all unchanged:
  Working capital needed DOUBLES
  → Company must find additional financing to support growth
```

---

**Công Thức Tính Nhu Cầu Vốn Lưu Động Tăng Thêm:**

```
Additional NWC needed = ΔRevenue × (NWC / Revenue ratio)

Example:
  Current Revenue: 100 tỷ
  Current NWC: 25 tỷ (NWC/Revenue = 25%)
  Plan: Grow revenue to 130 tỷ (+30 tỷ)
  
  Additional NWC needed = 30 tỷ × 25% = 7.5 tỷ
  → Need to fund 7.5 tỷ additional working capital to support 30 tỷ revenue growth

NWC/Revenue ratio varies by business model:
  Distribution/FMCG: 15–25%
  Manufacturing: 25–40%
  Services (low AR): 5–15%
  Construction: 30–50%+ (long cycles, high AR)
  Retail (fast turnover): 5–15%
  E-commerce (prepaid): negative (collect before fulfilling)
```

---

**Mô Hình Self-Funding Growth:**

```
Can growth be funded from operating cash flow alone?

Sustainable Growth Rate (SGR):
  SGR = ROE × Retention Rate
  
  Example:
    ROE = 20%; Retention Rate (không chia cổ tức) = 80%
    SGR = 20% × 80% = 16%
  
  Interpretation: Company can grow 16%/year without external financing
  Growth faster than SGR → needs external capital (debt or equity)
  
  VN startup/SME typically want 30–50% growth → far exceeds SGR → need external funding

Cash Conversion Efficiency:
  If CCC can be reduced during growth phase → frees capital to fund growth
  Reducing CCC by 10 days on 100 tỷ revenue = 100/365 × 10 = 2.7 tỷ released
  → Partially funds growth without external capital
```

---

**Kế Hoạch Vốn Lưu Động Khi Tăng Trưởng:**

```
Step 1: Dự báo doanh thu theo quý (từ budget)
  Q1: 28 tỷ | Q2: 30 tỷ | Q3: 32 tỷ | Q4: 35 tỷ

Step 2: Tính NWC cần thiết theo doanh thu
  NWC/Rev ratio = 22% (based on current operations)
  Q1: 6.2 tỷ | Q2: 6.6 tỷ | Q3: 7.0 tỷ | Q4: 7.7 tỷ

Step 3: Tính NWC gap (nhu cầu tăng thêm vs đầu kỳ)
  If starting NWC = 5.5 tỷ:
  Q1 gap: 0.7 tỷ | Q2 gap: 0.4 tỷ | Q3 gap: 0.4 tỷ | Q4 gap: 0.7 tỷ
  Total annual additional NWC needed: ~2.2 tỷ

Step 4: Nguồn tài trợ NWC
  Option A: Short-term bank line (working capital facility)
  Option B: Improve CCC (reduce DSO / increase DPO)
  Option C: Equity injection
  Option D: Reduce growth rate to match available capital
```

**Seasonal Working Capital — Đặc Thù VN:**

```
Seasonal businesses face peak WC demand at specific periods:

F&B/Retail (Tết season — Jan/Feb):
  Revenue peak: 150–200% of average month
  Inventory buildup: Start Oct/Nov for Tết production
  Cash need: 3–4 months before Tết peak → WC need peaks Nov/Dec
  
  Planning: Arrange credit facility by September; don't wait until December

Construction (dry season — Nov–Mar in South VN):
  Revenue peak: Q1 (dry season construction pushes)
  AR peak: follows revenue by 60–90 days
  Cash need: Q1–Q2
  
  Planning: Ensure credit facility covers Q1 AR buildup

Fashion/Apparel (pre-Tết + back to school):
  Two peaks: Dec–Jan (Tết) + Aug–Sep (back to school)
  Inventory commitment: 3–4 months before each peak
  Cash timing: Critical to not run short during inventory buildup phases

Template:
  Month | Rev Forecast | AR Build | Inventory | AP Offset | Net WC Need | Cash Gap
  Oct   | 20 tỷ        | +5 tỷ    | +8 tỷ     | -4 tỷ     | +9 tỷ       | Fund from line
  Nov   | 22 tỷ        | +3 tỷ    | +5 tỷ     | -2 tỷ     | +6 tỷ       | Fund from line
  Jan   | 38 tỷ (Tết)  | +12 tỷ   | -10 tỷ    | -3 tỷ     | -1 tỷ       | Flush; repay line
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** The "growth trap" — where a profitable growing company runs out of cash — is one of the most common causes of SME failure globally, and particularly acute in VN where bank credit is tight and expensive. The mathematical inevitability: if NWC/Revenue = 25% and you grow 40%/year, you need 10% of current revenue as new WC every year. If OCF margin is 8%, you can only self-fund 8% growth in WC from operations. The 32% gap must be financed externally. Board and founder alignment on this math early prevents panic later.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Budget for P&L only; forget to budget for balance sheet changes. A revenue plan showing 30% growth with healthy profit margins looks great — until the CFO realizes the company needs to raise 15 tỷ in working capital to execute it. Integrated financial planning (P&L + BS + CF together, as covered in Module 4) prevents this surprise.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Assume working capital ratios stay constant when scaling. In early-stage growth, WC efficiency often deteriorates: new customers get longer credit terms; inventory errors increase; supplier terms worsen as you're still small. Budget for WC/Revenue ratio to worsen 2–5% during rapid scaling, not remain flat.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Fund long-term working capital needs with short-term credit. Rolling over a 90-day bank line to fund what is effectively a permanent WC increase creates refinancing risk. If the bank doesn't renew the line, the company is in immediate trouble. Structural WC increases should be funded by medium-term loans or equity, not revolving short-term credit.
