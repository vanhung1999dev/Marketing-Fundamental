### Công Nợ Phải Trả và Quan Hệ Nhà Cung Cấp

Khoản phải trả (Accounts Payable) là nguồn tài trợ miễn phí từ nhà cung cấp. Tối ưu DPO (Days Payable Outstanding) — trả tiền muộn nhất có thể mà không phá vỡ quan hệ hoặc mất chiết khấu — là một trong những cách hiệu quả nhất để cải thiện vốn lưu động mà không tốn chi phí tài chính.

> **Bài học:** DPO là đòn bẩy vốn lưu động rẻ nhất mà hầu hết SME chưa khai thác tốt. Mỗi ngày tăng DPO từ 30 lên 45 ngày = thêm 15 ngày tín dụng thương mại miễn phí. Với công ty có COGS 80 tỷ/năm, đó là 80/365×15 = 3.3 tỷ vốn giải phóng — không mất một đồng lãi.

---

**DPO — Chỉ Số Cốt Lõi:**

```
DPO (Days Payable Outstanding) = (AP Balance / COGS) × 365

Example:
  AP Balance: 8 tỷ
  Annual COGS: 72 tỷ
  DPO = (8 / 72) × 365 = 40.6 days ≈ 41 days

Interpretation:
  Company takes on average 41 days to pay suppliers after receiving goods
  
  Industry benchmarks (VN):
    SME retail/distribution: 30–45 days (limited bargaining power)
    Large retail chain (Vinmart, MM Mega): 60–90 days
    Manufacturing (large): 45–75 days
    Construction: 60–120 days (but often forced by cash flow, not choice)
    
  Higher DPO = better for buyer (more supplier credit used)
  Too high DPO = damages supplier relationships; may trigger COD requirement
```

---

**Tối Ưu DPO — Chiến Lược Phân Tầng Nhà Cung Cấp:**

```
TIER 1 — Strategic suppliers (top 20% by spend/criticality):
  Strategy: Pay on time or early for relationship and continuity
  Target: Negotiate better payment terms (Net 45 → Net 60) in exchange for volume commitment
  Tactic: Offer early payment discount in cash-flush periods
  
TIER 2 — Important suppliers (middle 30%):
  Strategy: Pay at exact due date; maximize term length
  Target: Extend terms to Net 45 if possible
  Tactic: Consistent on-time payment builds trust for term extension negotiation

TIER 3 — Commodity suppliers (bottom 50% by criticality):
  Strategy: Maximize DPO; push for longest possible terms
  Target: Net 60 or longer; multiple sources to maintain leverage
  Tactic: Price competition between suppliers to maintain favorable terms

EARLY PAYMENT DISCOUNT evaluation:
  If supplier offers: 2/10 Net 30
  Cost of NOT taking discount: 37.2% annualized (calculated in AR section)
  If company's cost of capital < 37.2%: take the discount
  If cash is tight: skip discount; use the 20 extra days as free financing
```

---

**Cân Bằng DPO và Quan Hệ Nhà Cung Cấp:**

```
DPO Optimization Matrix:

                      | High Supplier Power | Low Supplier Power
----------------------|---------------------|-------------------
High Criticality      | Negotiate carefully | Push for Net 60+
                      | Relationship first  | But maintain relation
Low Criticality       | Find alternatives   | Maximize DPO
                      | Reduce dependency   | COD/spot market OK

Supplier Relationship Investment:
  □ Pay on time consistently (even if terms not maximized) → builds trust
  □ Give forecast visibility → supplier plans better → rewards with better terms
  □ Commit volume → in exchange for extended payment terms
  □ Strategic partnership → exclusive arrangements + extended payment

Warning: DPO > industry norm may signal:
  (a) Strong buyer (positive) OR
  (b) Cash flow difficulty/inability to pay (negative — suppliers will eventually react)
  Context is critical
```

**Supply Chain Finance (SCF) — Công Cụ Hiện Đại:**

```
Traditional problem:
  Buyer wants: long payment terms (DPO 60 days)
  Supplier wants: early payment (DSO 15 days)
  → Zero-sum: one side wins, one side loses

SCF solution:
  Bank/fintech intermediary buys approved invoices from supplier at slight discount
  Supplier gets cash immediately (DSO 1–3 days)
  Buyer pays bank at original due date (DPO maintained or extended)
  Both sides win; bank earns spread

SCF economics (example):
  Invoice value: 1 tỷ, Due in 60 days
  Bank discount rate: 8% per annum = 8%/365 × 60 = 1.32%
  Supplier receives: 986.8 triệu immediately (vs waiting 60 days)
  Buyer pays: 1 tỷ at Day 60 (no change)
  
  Cost to supplier: 13.2 triệu (cost of early liquidity)
  Buyer can offer: to absorb some/all of the discount → win-win negotiation

VN context: VietinBank, BIDV offer basic SCF; fintechs like Finhay, KiotViet
           are developing supply chain finance products for SME ecosystem
```

**AP Process — Kiểm Soát Nội Bộ:**

```
Invoice-to-Payment Process:
  Step 1: Receive goods → GRN (Goods Received Note) signed
  Step 2: Receive invoice → Match to PO and GRN (3-way match)
  Step 3: Approval routing:
    <50 triệu: Department head approves
    50–200 triệu: CFO approves
    >200 triệu: CEO + CFO approve
  Step 4: Enter in accounting system; set payment due date
  Step 5: Payment run: batch on fixed days (e.g., every Tuesday)
  Step 6: Bank transfer + record in AP ledger

Controls to prevent fraud:
  □ Segregation: different people approve vs pay
  □ Regular AP reconciliation vs supplier statements
  □ Vendor master file access restricted; new vendor addition requires dual approval
  □ Periodic audit of AP aging vs actual payments
  □ Alert for duplicate invoices (same amount + same vendor + same date)
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Walmart has historically been the benchmark for DPO management. By using its massive buying power to extend payment terms to 60–90 days across thousands of suppliers, Walmart essentially gets suppliers to fund its operations. This "supplier float" was estimated at billions of dollars — free financing that competitors couldn't replicate. However, this created controversy: large retailers using buying power to delay payments to small suppliers has been a policy issue in many countries. VN government has considered regulations limiting payment delays from large retailers to small suppliers, similar to EU regulations capping B2B payment terms at 60 days.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Pay all suppliers early because "it's the right thing to do." Suppliers have factored the payment terms into their pricing. If terms are Net 30 and you pay in 5 days, you're giving away cash for free. Either negotiate early payment discount in return, or pay at the stated due date. Cash in your bank earns opportunity returns; cash in supplier's bank earns them opportunity returns.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Ignore DPO as a lever and focus only on AR. Most SME focus exclusively on "collect faster" (reducing DSO) but ignore "pay later" (increasing DPO). Both have symmetric effects on CCC. Moving DPO from 30 to 45 days has the same cash impact as moving DSO from 60 to 45 days — but DPO is often easier to control.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Damage Tier 1 supplier relationships by pushing DPO too aggressively. A key supplier who switches you to COD because you've been 45 days overdue for 6 months is a supply chain risk. The cash gain from delayed payment must be weighed against supply security risk. Tier 1 strategic suppliers should be treated as partners, not ATMs.
