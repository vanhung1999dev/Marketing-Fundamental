### Công Nợ Phải Thu và Quản Lý Rủi Ro Khách Hàng

Khoản phải thu (Accounts Receivable) đại diện cho doanh thu đã ghi nhận nhưng chưa thu được tiền — vốn của công ty đang bị khách hàng giữ. Quản lý AR là cân bằng giữa hai mục tiêu mâu thuẫn: nới lỏng tín dụng để tăng doanh thu vs thắt chặt để giảm rủi ro và cải thiện dòng tiền.

> **Bài học:** AR không phải là tài sản an toàn — là tài sản có rủi ro. Một khoản phải thu 90 ngày từ khách hàng đang khó khăn có thể có giá trị thực chỉ bằng 50–60% giá trị sổ sách. Quản lý AR phải là quản lý rủi ro tín dụng thực sự, không chỉ là theo dõi con số.

---

**DSO — Chỉ Số Cốt Lõi:**

```
DSO (Days Sales Outstanding) = (AR Balance / Revenue) × 365

Example:
  AR Balance: 18 tỷ
  Annual Revenue: 120 tỷ
  DSO = (18 / 120) × 365 = 54.75 days ≈ 55 days

Interpretation:
  It takes on average 55 days to collect payment after sale
  
  Industry benchmarks (VN):
    Modern trade (siêu thị): 30–45 days
    B2B distribution: 45–75 days
    Construction/project: 90–120+ days
    Government contracts: 90–180 days (notoriously slow)
    
  Trend is more important than absolute: DSO increasing by 5+ days QoQ → warning

AR Turnover = Revenue / AR Balance = 365 / DSO
  AR Turnover = 365 / 55 = 6.6 times/year
  → Receivables cycle through 6.6 times per year
```

**Phân Tích Aging AR — Công Cụ Giám Sát:**

```
AR Aging Report (snapshot at month-end):

Customer     | Total AR | 0–30d  | 31–60d | 61–90d | 91–120d | >120d
-------------|----------|--------|--------|--------|---------|------
Company A    | 2.5 tỷ  | 2.5 tỷ |        |        |         |
Company B    | 1.8 tỷ  | 0.8 tỷ | 1.0 tỷ |        |         |
Company C    | 3.2 tỷ  | 0.5 tỷ | 0.7 tỷ | 1.5 tỷ | 0.5 tỷ  |
Company D    | 0.6 tỷ  |        |        |        | 0.1 tỷ  | 0.5 tỷ
TOTAL        | 8.1 tỷ  | 3.8 tỷ | 1.7 tỷ | 1.5 tỷ | 0.6 tỷ  | 0.5 tỷ
             | 100%    | 47%    | 21%    | 19%    | 7%      | 6%

Action triggers:
  31–60 days: send polite reminder; confirm invoice receipt
  61–90 days: escalate to account manager; investigate dispute/issue
  91–120 days: senior escalation; discuss payment plan
  >120 days: legal/collections action; provision for bad debt
  
  Provision rate (typical VN):
    31–60d: 5–10% provision
    61–90d: 20–30% provision
    91–120d: 50% provision
    >120d: 70–100% provision
```

**Phân Loại Khách Hàng Theo Rủi Ro:**

```
Credit Score Model (simplified):
  Criteria                | Weight | Score range
  Financial strength      | 30%    | 1–5
  Payment history         | 30%    | 1–5
  Business tenure         | 20%    | 1–5
  Order volume/loyalty    | 20%    | 1–5

Risk Tiers:
  Tier A (4.0–5.0): Premium customers → Net 60 terms; high credit limit
  Tier B (3.0–3.9): Standard customers → Net 30–45 terms; standard limit
  Tier C (2.0–2.9): Risky customers → Net 15 or COD; low limit; requires deposit
  Tier D (<2.0): Refused credit → COD only or require bank guarantee

Review frequency:
  Tier A: Annual review
  Tier B: Semi-annual review
  Tier C: Quarterly review; any late payment triggers immediate re-tier
  New customers: Start at Tier C; promote after 3 on-time payments
```

**Chính Sách Chiết Khấu Thanh Toán Sớm:**

```
Standard notation: "2/10 Net 30"
  2% discount if paid within 10 days
  Otherwise full amount due within 30 days

Cost of NOT taking the discount (annualized):
  = (Discount % / (1 - Discount %)) × (365 / (Net days - Discount days))
  = (0.02 / 0.98) × (365 / 20)
  = 2.04% × 18.25
  = 37.2% annualized

Interpretation: Declining 2/10 Net 30 is equivalent to borrowing at 37.2% per annum.
  If customer's bank loan rate < 37.2% → customer SHOULD pay early and take discount.
  If customer's financing rate > 37.2% → they'll use supplier credit instead.

VN context:
  Bank loan rates: 8–12% for good customers; 15–20% for SME unsecured
  → Most VN businesses should take early payment discounts if available
  Offering 1/10 Net 30: cost = (0.01/0.99) × (365/20) = 18.4% → attractive for most
```

**Chiến Lược Thu Hồi Công Nợ:**

```
PROACTIVE (before due date):
  □ Confirm invoice receipt within 2 days of sending
  □ 5 days before due: courtesy reminder via email/Zalo
  □ Ensure no dispute or query outstanding

REACTIVE (after due date):
  Day 1–7 overdue: Automated reminder; account manager phone call
  Day 8–15 overdue: Senior escalation; investigate root cause
  Day 16–30 overdue: Management-to-management call; payment plan discussion
  Day 31–60 overdue: Formal demand letter; suspend new shipments
  Day 61–90 overdue: Final demand; legal notice; credit reporting
  >90 days: Hand to collections/legal; full provision

Key principle: Act early. Late-stage collection is exponentially more expensive.
  Collection cost at 30 days overdue: ~5% of debt
  Collection cost at 90 days overdue: ~25% of debt
  Collection cost at 180 days overdue: ~50%+ of debt (if recoverable at all)
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** In B2B distribution in Vietnam, AR management is often the difference between profitable and unprofitable distributors. A distributor with 2,000 customers and average AR 5 triệu per customer = 10 tỷ in AR. Moving DSO from 60 to 45 days frees up 4.1 tỷ in cash (= 10 tỷ × 15/365... more precisely: at DSO=60, AR = revenue/365×60; at DSO=45, AR = revenue/365×45; difference = revenue/365×15). At 12% cost of capital, that's 490 triệu/year in freed financing cost — pure profit improvement from better AR management without any sales increase.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Track only total AR balance, not aging distribution. AR balance can stay constant while quality deteriorates (old receivables replace paid ones). Always review aging distribution monthly. If the ">90 days" bucket is growing as % of total AR, it's a leading indicator of future bad debt write-offs.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Give the same credit terms to all customers. Tier A customers who always pay early deserve better terms (higher limit, longer net days) to deepen the relationship. Tier C/D customers with poor history deserve tighter terms (deposit, COD) regardless of order size. One-size-fits-all credit policy either over-restricts good customers or under-protects against risky ones.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Allow sales team to override credit decisions. "This customer is important — give them more time." Sales compensation linked to revenue (not cash collected) creates perverse incentives: sales team wants to ship to everyone; credit team wants to protect cash. Resolution: tie part of sales commission to cash collection, not just revenue booking; give CFO final say on credit overrides.
