### Tiền Mặt và Khả Năng Thanh Khoản

Tiền mặt là huyết mạch của doanh nghiệp. Một công ty có thể thua lỗ trên báo cáo kết quả kinh doanh nhưng vẫn tồn tại nếu có đủ tiền mặt — nhưng một công ty sinh lời cao vẫn có thể phá sản nếu cạn kiệt thanh khoản. Đây là nghịch lý phổ biến nhất mà các nhà sáng lập VN gặp khi tăng trưởng nhanh.

> **Bài học:** "Profit is an opinion; cash is a fact." Hầu hết startup và SME VN sụp đổ không phải vì kinh doanh thua lỗ — mà vì hết tiền mặt giữa lúc đang tăng trưởng. Quản trị thanh khoản phải là ưu tiên số một của CFO và CEO, không phải chỉ là việc của kế toán.

---

**Vốn Lưu Động — Định Nghĩa Nền Tảng:**

```
Net Working Capital (NWC) = Current Assets - Current Liabilities

Current Assets (Tài sản ngắn hạn):
  + Tiền mặt và tương đương tiền (Cash & equivalents)
  + Các khoản phải thu (Accounts Receivable — AR)
  + Hàng tồn kho (Inventory)
  + Trả trước ngắn hạn (Prepaid expenses)

Current Liabilities (Nợ ngắn hạn):
  - Phải trả nhà cung cấp (Accounts Payable — AP)
  - Vay ngắn hạn ngân hàng (Short-term bank loans)
  - Thuế và các khoản phải nộp (Tax payable)
  - Chi phí dồn tích (Accrued expenses)

NWC dương: Tài sản ngắn hạn > Nợ ngắn hạn → cushion for operations
NWC âm: có thể là hiệu quả (siêu thị, e-commerce) hoặc là rủi ro (manufacturer)
```

---

**Ba Cấu Phần Vốn Lưu Động:**

```
TIỀN MẶT:
  Vai trò kép:
    (1) Operational buffer: thanh toán chi phí hàng ngày không kịp thu
    (2) Strategic reserve: cơ hội đầu tư, xử lý khủng hoảng, đàm phán vị thế

  Minimum cash target (rule of thumb):
    Conservative: 2–3 tháng fixed costs
    Aggressive: 0.5–1 tháng fixed costs
    
  VN context: Banks often tighten credit at year-end (Tết) → hold higher buffer Q4

KHOẢN PHẢI THU (AR):
  Capital tied up in goods/services delivered but not yet paid
  Every day of DSO = Revenue/365 đồng locked up in AR
  Example: Revenue 120 tỷ/năm, DSO 45 days → AR balance = 120/365 × 45 = 14.8 tỷ
  → 14.8 tỷ could be reinvested if collected faster

HÀNG TỒN KHO (Inventory):
  Capital locked in goods not yet sold
  Every day of DIO = COGS/365 đồng locked up in inventory
  Example: COGS 80 tỷ/năm, DIO 60 days → Inventory = 80/365 × 60 = 13.1 tỷ
```

**Thanh Khoản — Chỉ Số Đo Lường:**

| Chỉ số | Công thức | Ngưỡng tốt | Ngưỡng nguy hiểm |
|---|---|---|---|
| Current Ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | 1.5–2.5 | < 1.0 |
| Quick Ratio | (CA - Inventory) / CL | > 1.0 | < 0.5 |
| Cash Ratio | Cash / CL | > 0.2 | < 0.1 |
| Operating CF Ratio | Operating CF / CL | > 0.4 | < 0.2 |

```
Interpretation:
  Current Ratio = 1.8: For every 1đ current debt, have 1.8đ current assets
  Quick Ratio = 0.8: After removing inventory, slightly less than 1:1 cover
  → Suggest: inventory is significant; risk if inventory can't be liquidated quickly

  Industry benchmarks vary significantly:
    Manufacturing: CR target 1.5–2.5 (holds inventory)
    Retail: CR target 1.2–1.8 (fast inventory turnover)
    Services: CR target 1.0–1.5 (little inventory)
    Supermarket/E-commerce: CR can be <1.0 (negative WC model — paid before suppliers)
```

**Cash Flow Statement — Ba Dòng Tiền Quan Trọng:**

```
OCF (Operating Cash Flow):
  = Net Income + Depreciation ± Changes in Working Capital
  
  OCF > Net Income: company is converting profit to cash efficiently
  OCF < Net Income: profit is being tied up in AR or inventory — warning sign
  
  Healthy benchmark: OCF/Net Income > 0.8 (80% of profit converts to cash)

FCF (Free Cash Flow):
  = OCF - Capex
  
  FCF measures cash generated after maintaining/growing asset base
  Positive FCF = company generates surplus cash → can pay debt, dividends, expand
  Negative FCF during growth phase = normal (investing for future)

Cash Burn Rate (for early-stage or distressed companies):
  = Monthly fixed costs + variable costs - monthly collections
  Runway = Cash balance / Monthly burn rate
  
  Example: Cash 5 tỷ; burn rate 800 triệu/month → Runway = 6.25 months
```

**Dấu Hiệu Cảnh Báo Sớm Thanh Khoản:**

```
🔴 Critical (immediate action needed):
  Bank balance drops below 1 month of payroll + rent
  Bounced checks or missed payroll
  Suppliers switching to cash-on-delivery (COD)
  Bank demands collateral top-up or accelerates loan repayment

🟡 Warning (monitor closely):
  OCF negative for 2+ consecutive quarters
  DSO increasing more than 5 days quarter-over-quarter
  Inventory increasing faster than revenue
  Overdraft facility used >80% of limit consistently
  Tax payments consistently delayed

🟢 Healthy signals:
  Cash balance covers 2+ months fixed costs
  OCF consistently > Net Income
  DSO trending down or stable
  Supplier payments on time without strain
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Amazon's negative working capital model is one of the most studied in business. Amazon collects cash from customers within days, but pays suppliers in 30–60 days. This creates a structural funding subsidy: suppliers effectively fund Amazon's operations for free. In VN, VinCommerce (Vinmart) and Tiki apply similar logic. The lesson: for consumer businesses with bargaining power over suppliers, negative working capital is not a weakness — it's a competitive advantage and a form of free financing.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Confuse profitability with liquidity. "We're profitable — why don't we have cash?" Because profit is accrual-based (recognizes revenue when delivered, not when collected) while cash is flow-based. A company growing 50% per year can be very profitable and cash-flow-negative simultaneously — because every new sale requires financing inventory and AR before cash is collected.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Maintain a single "cash in bank" figure without separating operating cash from strategic reserve. Mix operating and strategic cash together → either draw down strategic reserve for operations (reducing safety buffer) or hold excessive idle cash (opportunity cost). Maintain separate mental (or actual) accounts: operating cash, tax reserve, strategic reserve.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Allow AR to grow unchecked during rapid growth phase. "Revenue is up 80% — the AR growth is just proportional." But if DSO also increases (e.g., from 30 to 55 days), that's a double compounding: more revenue AND slower collection = exponential cash strain. Monitor DSO separately from AR balance.
