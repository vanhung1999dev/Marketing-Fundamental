### Ngưỡng Thanh Khoản Tối Thiểu An Toàn

Ngưỡng thanh khoản tối thiểu là lượng tiền mặt và tài sản thanh khoản tối thiểu mà doanh nghiệp phải duy trì để không rơi vào khủng hoảng thanh toán — ngay cả trong điều kiện bất lợi. Đây không phải là mục tiêu tối ưu mà là sàn an toàn.

> **Bài học:** Thanh khoản tối thiểu không phải là con số kế toán trên giấy tờ — là ranh giới sinh tồn của doanh nghiệp. Mọi quyết định phân bổ vốn đều phải đặt câu hỏi: "Sau khi thực hiện quyết định này, chúng ta vẫn còn trên ngưỡng tối thiểu không?" Nếu câu trả lời là không — dừng lại.

---

**Xác Định Ngưỡng Thanh Khoản Tối Thiểu:**

```
Method 1: Fixed Cost Coverage
  Minimum Cash = N months of fixed costs
  
  Fixed costs include:
    Payroll (including BHXH employer contribution 22.5%)
    Rent (office, warehouse, stores)
    Loan repayments (principal + interest)
    Utilities and basic operating expenses
    Tax obligations (VAT, CIT instalments)
  
  Example:
    Monthly fixed costs: 2.5 tỷ
    Conservative target (3 months): 7.5 tỷ minimum
    Standard target (2 months): 5.0 tỷ minimum
    Aggressive target (1 month): 2.5 tỷ minimum

Method 2: Stress Test Based
  Run downside scenario: What if revenue drops 30% for 3 months?
  Calculate cumulative cash need in that scenario
  Set minimum as cash needed to survive that scenario

Method 3: Debt Obligation Based
  Minimum = largest single debt repayment in next 30 days × 1.5
  (Never get below 1.5× of your largest upcoming payment)

Best practice: Use Method 1 as baseline; validate with Method 2 stress test
```

---

**Liquidity Ratios — Ngưỡng Tham Chiếu:**

```
Current Ratio (CR) = Current Assets / Current Liabilities:
  Crisis: CR < 1.0 (can't cover short-term obligations with short-term assets)
  Warning: 1.0 ≤ CR < 1.2
  Minimum safe: 1.2 ≤ CR < 1.5
  Comfortable: 1.5 ≤ CR < 2.5
  Excess: CR > 2.5 (holding too much idle capital)

Quick Ratio (QR) = (Cash + AR) / Current Liabilities:
  Crisis: QR < 0.5
  Warning: 0.5 ≤ QR < 0.8
  Minimum safe: QR ≥ 1.0

Cash Ratio = Cash / Current Liabilities:
  Crisis: < 0.1
  Warning: 0.1–0.2
  Minimum safe: > 0.2
  
Industry adjustments:
  Retail (fast inventory): CR 1.0–1.5 acceptable (inventory liquidates quickly)
  Manufacturing: CR 1.5–2.5 (inventory takes longer to liquidate)
  Services: CR 1.0–1.5 (little inventory, fast AR collection)
  Construction: CR 1.5–2.5+ (long cycles; large AR balances)
```

**Liquidity Reserve Structure:**

```
Layer 1 — Operational Float (immediate access):
  Purpose: Day-to-day payroll, suppliers, VAT, rent
  Target: 30–45 days of daily cash outflows
  Location: Main operating bank account
  Trigger for replenishment: drops below 15-day coverage

Layer 2 — Tactical Reserve (3–5 days access):
  Purpose: Unexpected payments, collection shortfalls, small opportunities
  Target: 1–2 months of fixed costs
  Location: Separate current account or short-term deposit (overnight)
  Trigger: Draw when Layer 1 drops below 15 days

Layer 3 — Strategic Reserve (5–10 days access):
  Purpose: Major disruption (customer default, credit line cancelled, recession)
  Target: 2–3 months of fixed costs OR value of largest single credit exposure
  Location: Short-term deposit + undrawn credit line
  Trigger: Only draw in genuine crisis; approval by CEO + Board
  
  Combined target: Layer 1 + 2 + 3 = 3–4 months fixed costs
  Minimum safe: Layer 1 + 2 = 2 months fixed costs
```

---

**Stress Testing — Kịch Bản Rủi Ro:**

```
Build a stress test matrix annually (update quarterly):

Scenario A (Mild — 60% probability):
  Revenue -10%, AR collection on schedule
  → Cash flow impact: -X tỷ; safety level maintained

Scenario B (Moderate — 30% probability):
  Revenue -25%, DSO extends 15 days, one major customer defaults
  → Cash flow impact: -Y tỷ; dip below Layer 2 for 2 months; recover by month 4

Scenario C (Severe — 10% probability):
  Revenue -40%, bank line cancelled, major customer (20% revenue) goes bankrupt
  → Cash flow impact: -Z tỷ; must draw strategic reserve; may need emergency capital

Response plan for each scenario:
  Scenario A: Monitor weekly; no action needed
  Scenario B: Freeze non-critical capex; accelerate collections; draw credit line
  Scenario C: Emergency cost cuts; explore bridge financing; board notification
  
Testing question: "In Scenario C, how many months of runway do we have?"
Target: Minimum 3 months runway even in Scenario C
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Warren Buffett's "elephant gun" philosophy: always hold substantial cash reserves — not because you earn good returns on cash, but because cash is options. When markets dislocate (Lehman 2008, COVID 2020), companies with cash can acquire assets at distressed prices, snap up talent, or take market share from cash-strapped competitors. In VN context: companies that held cash reserves going into COVID-19 (2020–2021) could maintain inventory, support suppliers, and even acquire competitors. Cash is insurance + optionality.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Set minimum cash target once and never review it. Business scale changes; fixed cost structure changes; risk profile changes. Minimum liquidity targets should be reviewed quarterly against current fixed cost base and balance sheet structure. A target set at 10 tỷ two years ago may be 20 tỷ now if the business has doubled.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Count undrawn credit line as "cash equivalent" for minimum target. An undrawn credit line is valuable contingent liquidity — but it's not the same as cash. Banks can cancel or reduce lines during stress (precisely when you need them most). Minimum cash target should be in actual cash/near-cash assets; credit line is an additional buffer, not a substitute.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Mistake seasonal cash build-up for structural improvement. Cash balance peaks in January (post-Tết collection) or Q4 (year-end payments). "We have 15 tỷ cash!" — but next month fixed costs + supplier payments will draw it down significantly. Always evaluate cash position on a 13-week rolling cash flow basis, not just current balance.
