### Thời Gian Thu Tiền và Tốc Độ Luân Chuyển Công Nợ

DSO (Days Sales Outstanding) đo lường tốc độ công ty chuyển doanh thu thành tiền mặt. Giảm DSO là đòn bẩy mạnh nhất để cải thiện Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) và giải phóng vốn bị khóa trong khoản phải thu.

> **Bài học:** DSO là nhiệt kế của sức khỏe thu tiền. Khi DSO tăng, đó là dấu hiệu đầu tiên của một trong ba vấn đề: khách hàng đang gặp khó khăn tài chính, quy trình thu tiền đang yếu, hoặc chính sách tín dụng đang quá lỏng lẻo. Hành động sớm khi DSO tăng — đừng chờ đến khi nợ xấu phát sinh.

---

**Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) — Công Thức Nền Tảng:**

```
CCC = DSO + DIO - DPO

Where:
  DSO = Days Sales Outstanding (thời gian thu tiền từ khách)
  DIO = Days Inventory Outstanding (thời gian hàng nằm kho)
  DPO = Days Payable Outstanding (thời gian trả tiền nhà cung cấp)

Example:
  DSO = 55 days (thu tiền từ khách mất 55 ngày)
  DIO = 45 days (hàng nằm kho trung bình 45 ngày)
  DPO = 35 days (trả nhà cung cấp sau 35 ngày)
  
  CCC = 55 + 45 - 35 = 65 days
  
  Meaning: Company must finance 65 days of operating cycle from own cash or bank debt
  
  If Annual COGS = 72 tỷ:
  Capital tied up in CCC = 72 tỷ / 365 × 65 = 12.8 tỷ
  
  Goal: Reduce CCC → reduce capital tied up → less need for external financing
```

---

**DSO Calculation và Monitoring:**

```
Standard DSO:
  DSO = (AR Balance / Revenue) × 365
  
  Monthly version: DSO = (AR Balance at month end) / (Revenue in month) × 30
  
  Rolling 3-month: DSO = (AR at month end) / (Average monthly revenue over 3 months) × 30
  → More stable; avoids distortion from seasonal peaks

Best-Possible DSO:
  = (Current (not yet due) AR / Revenue) × 365
  Represents DSO if all past-due AR were collected immediately
  
  Gap: Actual DSO - Best-Possible DSO = "Delinquency days"
  Delinquency days = how many days of revenue are in overdue AR
  Example: Actual DSO = 55, Best-Possible DSO = 38 → 17 days of delinquency
  At Revenue 120 tỷ: 17 days × (120/365) = 5.6 tỷ in overdue AR

Weighted Average DSO (by customer):
  More accurate; weights large customers more heavily
  Large B2B customer with 90-day DSO can dominate the average
  → Segment DSO analysis: top 10 customers + rest separately
```

---

**AR Turnover — Góc Nhìn Bổ Sung:**

```
AR Turnover Ratio = Revenue / Average AR Balance

Example:
  Revenue: 120 tỷ
  Average AR (Q1–Q4 average): 16.5 tỷ
  AR Turnover = 120 / 16.5 = 7.27 times/year
  = AR cycles through approximately every 50 days (365/7.27)

Interpretation:
  High AR Turnover (>10×): Fast collection; tight credit terms; or short credit period
  Low AR Turnover (<6×): Slow collection; loose credit; or long credit terms industry norm
  
Trend analysis (more important than absolute):
  If AR Turnover declining QoQ: collections slowing → investigate why
  If AR Turnover improving: credit tightening working OR mix shifting to faster payers

Peer comparison:
  Company AR Turnover: 7.27×
  Industry median: 8.50×
  → Company is slightly slower than peers; opportunity to improve
```

**Chiến Lược Rút Ngắn DSO:**

```
1. INVOICE QUICKLY:
   Same-day invoicing after delivery (not week-end batch)
   E-invoice (required per Nghị định 123/2020 for most businesses)
   → Customer's payment clock starts immediately
   Impact: 3–5 days DSO reduction typically

2. PAYMENT TERMS CLARITY:
   State payment terms on every invoice (Net 30; due date in bold)
   Include bank details on invoice (reduce payment friction)
   → Reduce payment delays due to confusion/missing information

3. PROACTIVE COLLECTIONS:
   Automated reminder at day 0 (due), day +3, day +7
   Phone call at day +10 for invoices >5 triệu
   Escalation to senior at day +15
   → Each touchpoint recovers 20–30% of overdue balance

4. INCENTIVIZE EARLY PAYMENT:
   Offer 1–2% discount for payment within 10 days
   Only offer to cash-generative customers (not those who struggle to pay anyway)
   → Selective early payment discount; not blanket policy

5. DEPOSIT / PARTIAL PREPAYMENT:
   New customers: 30–50% deposit before delivery
   Large orders: 30% upfront + 70% on delivery
   → Reduces AR from day one; also qualifies customer commitment

6. FACTORING (when bank credit is tight):
   Sell AR to factoring company at discount (typically 2–5%)
   Get 85–95% of invoice value immediately
   Factor collects from customer
   → Trade off discount for immediate liquidity
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Amazon's AR management offers a counter-intuitive insight: Amazon collects payment from customers instantly (credit card or Amazon Pay) but its retail business still shows DSO of ~20 days due to marketplace seller settlements. The real lesson is about designing the business model for cash flow: subscription (AWS, Prime) — cash collected upfront; marketplace — minimal AR; direct retail — cards settle within days. Business model design is the most powerful lever on DSO. For VN businesses: moving toward subscription, prepayment, or platform models structurally improves DSO without requiring more collection effort.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Reduce DSO by cutting all credit terms — then lose customers to competitors offering better terms. DSO optimization is not "collect faster at all costs" — it's "collect as fast as the market allows while maintaining competitive customer terms." Know your market's standard terms; aim to be at or better than standard, not outlier.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Celebrate DSO reduction without checking whether it was achieved by mix shift (losing large, slow-paying customers) vs genuine improvement. If DSO drops from 55 to 40 because you lost two customers who paid in 90 days, that's a revenue problem, not a WC improvement.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Apply same collection intensity to all customers regardless of relationship. A key strategic customer who is temporarily 30 days late deserves a different conversation than a commodity customer with no strategic value. Segmented collection approach: firm on commodity accounts; flexible and relationship-first on strategic accounts.
