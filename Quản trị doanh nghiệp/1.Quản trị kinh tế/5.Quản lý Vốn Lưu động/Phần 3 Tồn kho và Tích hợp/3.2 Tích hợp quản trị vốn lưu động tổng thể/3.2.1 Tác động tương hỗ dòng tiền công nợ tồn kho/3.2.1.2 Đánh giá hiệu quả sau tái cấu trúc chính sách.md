### Đánh Giá Hiệu Quả Sau Tái Cấu Trúc Chính Sách Vốn Lưu Động

Sau khi thực thi thay đổi chính sách vốn lưu động, đánh giá kết quả thực tế vs kỳ vọng là bước học hỏi quan trọng nhất. Đây không phải là bước tùy chọn — là cơ chế phản hồi biến quản trị WC từ một lần thực hiện thành năng lực tổ chức liên tục cải thiện.

> **Bài học:** Execution without measurement is just hope. Mọi thay đổi chính sách WC phải có hypothesis rõ ràng TRƯỚC khi thực thi và evaluation rõ ràng SAU. Không đánh giá → không học hỏi → mắc lại lỗi tương tự → không có cải tiến tích lũy.

---

**Post-Implementation Review Framework:**

```
Timeline:
  Week 4: First check — are early indicators tracking?
  Month 3: Mid-term review — are primary effects materializing?
  Month 12: Full annual review — sustained impact assessment

Three Questions:
  1. Did the change achieve its intended primary effect? (e.g., DSO actually declined?)
  2. Were secondary effects as modeled? (e.g., revenue impact within estimated range?)
  3. Were there unmodeled surprises? (positive or negative)

Answer each with data, not narrative:
  "DSO declined from 55 to 44 days" ✓ (vs target 40 — partially achieved; model said 40)
  "Revenue impact: -2.1%, within -3% estimate" ✓
  "Unexpected: One strategic customer switched to competitor — not captured in model" ✗
```

**KPI Measurement After WC Policy Change:**

```
BEFORE/AFTER COMPARISON (same period, prior year):

Metric              | Pre-Change | Target  | Month 3 | Month 12 | vs Target
--------------------|------------|---------|---------|----------|----------
DSO (days)          | 55         | 40      | 46      | 42       | -13 days (vs -15)
DPO (days)          | 35         | 43      | 39      | 41       | +6 (vs +8)
DIO (days)          | 50         | 42      | 47      | 44       | -6 (vs -8)
CCC (days)          | 70         | 54      | 64      | 65       | -5 (vs -16)
Cash released (tỷ)  | —          | 5.3     | 2.1     | 3.8      | 72% of target
Bad debt (% rev)    | 0.8%       | 0.4%    | 0.6%    | 0.45%    | Near target
AR overdue (%)      | 18%        | 10%     | 14%     | 11%      | Near target
Revenue change      | baseline   | -1%     | -2.1%   | -1.3%    | Slightly worse
COGS change         | baseline   | +0.3%   | +0.5%   | +0.4%    | Slightly worse
Net P&L impact (tỷ) | —          | +1.1    | estimate| +0.85    | 77% of target

Analysis:
  Primary effects: Partially achieved (DSO, DIO, DPO all improved but less than target)
  Revenue impact: Slightly worse than modeled (2.1% vs 1% expected)
  Overall: 72–77% of projected benefit achieved
  
  Actions:
    Investigate: why DSO target not fully achieved (which customer segments are lagging?)
    Revise: revenue impact assumption upward for future models (model underestimated sensitivity)
    Continue: trends are positive; full benefit may take 6–12 more months to materialize
```

**Variance Attribution — Lý Do Sai Lệch:**

```
When actual ≠ projected, classify variance:

MODEL ERROR:
  Assumption was wrong (revenue sensitivity underestimated)
  → Update model for future projections

EXECUTION FAILURE:
  Policy decided correctly but not implemented consistently
  (e.g., sales team ignoring credit policy; warehouse not following new reorder rules)
  → Fix implementation; training; accountability

EXTERNAL CHANGE:
  Market conditions changed after model was built
  (e.g., competitor suddenly offered better credit terms, reducing pricing power)
  → Revise strategy; original model was right for original conditions

DATA QUALITY:
  AR aging data was inaccurate; DSO calculation had errors
  → Fix data quality; don't make decisions on bad data

TIMING:
  Change takes longer to implement than modeled (e.g., renegotiating with 200 suppliers takes 9 months not 3)
  → Adjust timing assumptions; results will come; be patient

Example attribution:
  "DSO reduction target: -15 days; actual: -13 days.
  Variance -2 days: Execution failure (30% of Tier C customers still on old terms — sales override);
                    Timing lag (new credit policy not applied to existing contracts until renewal)"
  Action: Enforce credit policy on existing customers at next annual review; no override without CFO
```

**Learning Loop — Capture Institutional Knowledge:**

```
After each major WC policy change, document:

POLICY CHANGE LOG:
  Change: [what changed]
  Date: [when implemented]
  Rationale: [why the change was made]
  Model assumptions: [key assumptions at time of decision]
  
  Month 3 actual: [data]
  Month 12 actual: [data]
  
  What worked: [lessons for next time]
  What didn't work: [failure modes to avoid]
  Model improvement: [which assumptions to update]
  
  Reviewer: [CFO name]
  
Storage: Internal finance wiki or project management system
Review: Annually, when making similar decisions, during CFO transitions

Value of log:
  New CFO takes over → reads log → understands WC history → avoids repeating mistakes
  Next time similar decision faces → reference log → better assumptions → better outcome
  Over 3–5 years → institutional knowledge > any individual's memory
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** The PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) cycle, developed by Deming and embedded in Japanese manufacturing quality culture, applies perfectly to WC management. Most companies are good at Plan (model projections) and Do (implement the change) but weak at Check (rigorous measurement) and Act (update approach based on learning). Without Check and Act, the cycle never tightens: each WC project starts from scratch rather than building on accumulated learning. Companies that consistently run Post-Implementation Reviews become progressively better at WC management — it's a compounding skill.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Define success as "cash released" only. Cash released is a one-time measure. True success includes: cash released + ongoing P&L improvement + no unexpected damage to customer/supplier relationships. A policy that released 4 tỷ but cost 2 tỷ/year in ongoing margin and damaged 3 key supplier relationships is a failure, not a success.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Compare results to budget rather than to pre-change baseline. "We hit our cash flow budget this year." But the budget might have been set poorly. Compare to: pre-change baseline adjusted for business growth + vs modeled projection. This reveals whether the WC change itself created value, independent of overall business performance.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Let urgency kill the post-implementation review. "We're too busy with the next project to document lessons from the last one." This is the most common failure mode. Schedule the Month 3 and Month 12 review in the calendar before starting the project. Make it a non-negotiable deliverable, not optional.
