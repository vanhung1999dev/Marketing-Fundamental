### Mô Phỏng Kịch Bản Thay Đổi Một Cấu Phần Vốn Lưu Động

Thay đổi bất kỳ cấu phần nào của working capital — AR, AP, hoặc Inventory — đều tạo ra phản ứng dây chuyền trên toàn hệ thống. Mô phỏng kịch bản (scenario modeling) cho phép định lượng tác động toàn diện trước khi ra quyết định, thay vì khám phá hậu quả sau khi đã thực thi.

> **Bài học:** Working capital model là công cụ "nếu-thì" (if-then simulator). Trước khi thay đổi bất kỳ chính sách nào — tín dụng khách hàng, tồn kho, điều khoản nhà cung cấp — hãy chạy qua mô hình để thấy tác động tổng thể lên dòng tiền, P&L, và balance sheet. Điều này chuyển quản trị từ phản ứng sang chủ động.

---

**Integrated WC Model — Cấu Trúc:**

```
Three linked statements:

INCOME STATEMENT Impact:
  Revenue change → affects AR buildup
  COGS change → affects inventory and AP
  Carrying cost change → affects overhead (hidden in COGS or G&A)
  Bad debt provision → affects SG&A
  Early payment discount expense → affects SG&A or COGS

BALANCE SHEET Impact:
  AR: changes with revenue × DSO
  Inventory: changes with COGS × DIO
  AP: changes with COGS × DPO
  Cash: net of all above changes

CASH FLOW Impact:
  Operating CF = Net Income + Depreciation - ΔNWC
  ΔNWC = ΔAR + ΔInventory - ΔAP
  Positive ΔNWC = cash absorbed (bad for cash)
  Negative ΔNWC = cash released (good for cash)
```

---

**Scenario 1: Tighten AR (reduce DSO from 55 to 40 days):**

```
Assumptions: Revenue 120 tỷ/year; COGS 80 tỷ; current DIO 50, DPO 35

Step 1: Calculate AR change
  Old AR = 120/365 × 55 = 18.1 tỷ
  New AR = 120/365 × 40 = 13.2 tỷ
  Cash released (one-time): 18.1 - 13.2 = 4.9 tỷ

Step 2: Estimate revenue impact
  Assume: 8% of revenue comes from customers who need Net 45–60
  These customers reduce orders by 40% → revenue impact: 120 × 8% × 40% = -3.8 tỷ/year
  Gross margin impact (30%): -1.14 tỷ/year

Step 3: Bad debt reduction
  DSO reduction → fewer overdue AR → less bad debt
  Estimated bad debt reduction: from 0.8% to 0.4% of revenue
  Saving: 0.4% × 120 tỷ = 480 triệu/year

Step 4: Interest saving on released capital
  4.9 tỷ × 10% (borrowing rate) = 490 triệu/year saved

Step 5: Net P&L Impact:
  Gross margin loss: -1,140 triệu/year
  Bad debt saving: +480 triệu/year
  Interest saving: +490 triệu/year
  NET ANNUAL P&L IMPACT: -170 triệu/year (slightly negative)

Step 6: Cash impact:
  Year 1: One-time cash inflow +4.9 tỷ (AR reduction)
  Year 2+: Annual P&L slightly negative (-170 triệu/year)

DECISION: The one-time 4.9 tỷ cash benefit comes at -170 triệu/year ongoing cost.
  If company is cash-constrained: may be worth it (immediate relief)
  If company is well-funded: the trade-off doesn't justify the revenue risk
  Better approach: Tighten only for Tier C/D customers (reduce bad debt, minimal revenue impact)
```

---

**Scenario 2: Reduce Inventory (DIO from 50 to 35 days):**

```
Assumptions: Same as above

Step 1: Inventory change
  Old Inventory = 80/365 × 50 = 10.96 tỷ
  New Inventory = 80/365 × 35 = 7.67 tỷ
  Cash released: 10.96 - 7.67 = 3.29 tỷ

Step 2: Service level impact
  Lower inventory → stockout risk increases
  Estimate: service level drops from 97% to 92% (5% stockout rate increase)
  Revenue lost to stockouts: 120 tỷ × 5% × (1 - fill rate) = complex; estimate 1% revenue
  Gross margin lost: 120 × 1% × 30% = -360 triệu/year

Step 3: Carrying cost saving
  3.29 tỷ × 25% = 823 triệu/year saved

Step 4: Interest saving
  3.29 tỷ × 10% = 329 triệu/year

Step 5: Net Impact:
  Revenue/margin lost to stockout: -360 triệu/year
  Carrying cost saving: +823 triệu/year
  Interest saving: +329 triệu/year
  NET ANNUAL P&L IMPACT: +792 triệu/year (positive!)
  One-time cash: +3.29 tỷ

DECISION: This scenario is clearly positive — provided the service level assumption is correct.
  Key risk: If stockout impact is higher than estimated (closer to 3–4% revenue loss) → recalculate
  Mitigant: Don't cut uniformly; reduce only C-item inventory; maintain A-item safety stock
```

---

**Scenario 3: Extend AP (DPO from 35 to 50 days):**

```
Step 1: AP change
  Old AP = 80/365 × 35 = 7.67 tỷ
  New AP = 80/365 × 50 = 10.96 tỷ
  Cash retained (one-time benefit): 3.29 tỷ

Step 2: Supplier cost impact
  Assume: 30% of suppliers will price in extended terms (1.5% cost increase)
  COGS impact: 80 tỷ × 30% × 1.5% = -360 triệu/year more COGS

Step 3: Supply reliability risk
  Tier 1 suppliers might deprioritize orders → service risk premium
  Estimate: 0.3% stockout increase = 120 × 0.3% × 30% GM = -108 triệu/year

Step 4: Interest saving
  3.29 tỷ × 10% = 329 triệu/year

Step 5: Net Impact:
  COGS increase: -360 triệu/year
  Supply risk: -108 triệu/year
  Interest saving: +329 triệu/year
  NET: -139 triệu/year (marginally negative)
  One-time cash: +3.29 tỷ

DECISION: Marginal negative ongoing but significant one-time cash benefit.
  Better approach: Extend DPO selectively (Tier 2/3 only); maintain Tier 1 terms
  Revised scenario with selective application: net positive with minimal supply risk
```

---

**Combined Scenario — Applying All Three Improvements:**

```
Apply selectively (best of all three scenarios):
  AR: Tighten only for Tier C/D (5% of revenue) → minimal revenue impact
  Inventory: Reduce C-item inventory; maintain A-item safety stock → DIO 50 → 42
  AP: Extend Tier 2/3 only (40% of COGS) → DPO 35 → 43

One-time cash released:
  AR (partial): +1.5 tỷ
  Inventory (partial): +1.8 tỷ  
  AP (partial): +2.0 tỷ
  TOTAL ONE-TIME CASH: +5.3 tỷ

Annual ongoing benefit:
  Bad debt reduction: +300 triệu
  Carrying cost saving: +450 triệu
  Interest saving: +530 triệu
  Cost/supply risk: -150 triệu
  NET ANNUAL: +1,130 triệu/year

This is the "best of all three" approach — significant cash released; positive ongoing P&L
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** McKinsey research on working capital optimization in consumer goods companies found that best-in-class WC management generates 2–4% of revenue in annual cash flow benefit vs median companies — and this gap compounds over time. A 100 tỷ revenue company with 3% WC improvement gap = 3 tỷ/year in extra cash. Over 5 years = 15+ tỷ in cumulative value (before compounding). WC optimization is not a one-time project; it's an ongoing competitive advantage that compounds annually.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Model only the primary effect of a WC change, not second-order effects. "Tighten AR → 4.9 tỷ cash released." Full story: -170 triệu/year ongoing cost from revenue impact + bad debt saving + interest saving → net negative ongoing. Without full model, decision seems clearly positive; with full model, nuance emerges.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Optimize each component separately without checking system-level impact. Best AR + Best Inventory + Best AP managed independently might collectively stress customer and supplier relationships in ways that the combined model reveals. Always model the combination.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Model with overly optimistic assumptions to justify a desired outcome. "Revenue impact will be minimal (2%); carrying cost saving will be maximum." If every assumption skews optimistic, the model validates any decision. Use base + bear case assumptions; only proceed if bear case is still acceptable.
