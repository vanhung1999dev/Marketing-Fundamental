### Cân Bằng Rủi Ro Đứt Gãy Cung Ứng và Chi Phí Lưu Kho

Safety stock (tồn kho an toàn) là buffer giữa sự không chắc chắn của cung và cầu với yêu cầu không để hết hàng. Tính toán safety stock tối ưu là bài toán cân bằng: tồn kho quá ít → stockout và mất doanh thu; tồn kho quá nhiều → vốn chết và chi phí lưu kho cao.

> **Bài học:** Safety stock không phải là "cứ trữ nhiều cho chắc" — là kết quả của tính toán dựa trên dữ liệu. Cùng một mức service level có thể đạt được với safety stock rất khác nhau tùy vào chất lượng forecast và tính ổn định của supply chain. Đầu tư vào forecast accuracy và supplier reliability giảm nhu cầu safety stock mà không tăng stockout risk.

---

**Stockout Cost — Chi Phí Hết Hàng:**

```
Types of stockout cost:
  
  1. Direct lost sales:
     Customer orders but you're out of stock → they buy elsewhere
     Cost = Gross margin × lost sales volume
     
  2. Emergency replenishment cost:
     Rush shipping, premium pricing from spot market, overtime production
     Can be 2–5× normal replenishment cost
     
  3. Customer relationship damage:
     Repeat stockout → customer reduces reliance on you → long-term revenue loss
     Hard to quantify but very real; especially in B2B relationships
     
  4. Lost volume discounts:
     Missing volume targets due to stockout → lose quantity discount tier
     
  5. Brand/reputation damage:
     For consumer brands: "out of stock" experience → negative brand perception

Example:
  A-item with 500 triệu revenue/month
  Estimated stockout frequency without safety stock: 2×/year
  Average stockout duration: 3 days; avg daily sales: 1.67 tỷ/30 = 55 triệu/day
  Gross margin: 30%
  
  Annual stockout cost = 2 × 3 days × 55 triệu × 30% = 99 triệu
  PLUS: emergency replenishment premium, relationship damage → total likely 200+ triệu
```

**Safety Stock Calculation — Công Thức:**

```
Basic Safety Stock Formula:
  SS = Z × σ_demand × √Lead Time
  
  Where:
    Z = service level factor (from normal distribution)
    σ_demand = standard deviation of daily/weekly demand
    Lead Time = supplier lead time (in same units as demand)

Service Level → Z Factor:
  90% service level: Z = 1.28
  95% service level: Z = 1.65
  97% service level: Z = 1.88
  99% service level: Z = 2.33
  99.9% service level: Z = 3.09

Example:
  Product: A-item
  Average daily demand: 100 units
  Std dev of daily demand: 20 units (high variability)
  Lead time: 14 days (fixed)
  Target service level: 97% (A-item)
  
  SS = 1.88 × 20 × √14 = 1.88 × 20 × 3.74 = 140.7 → 141 units
  
  At average cost 50,000 VNĐ/unit:
  Safety stock value = 141 × 50,000 = 7.05 triệu
  Annual carrying cost = 7.05 triệu × 25% = 1.76 triệu
  
  For a product generating 500 triệu/month, 1.76 triệu/year is trivial → justified

When both demand AND lead time are variable:
  SS = Z × √(LT_avg × σ²_demand + D_avg² × σ²_LT)
  (More complex but more accurate for high-variability environments)
```

---

**Reorder Point (ROP) — Điểm Đặt Hàng:**

```
ROP = Average demand during lead time + Safety Stock
    = (Average daily demand × Lead time) + Safety stock

Example (continued from above):
  ROP = (100 units/day × 14 days) + 141 units = 1,400 + 141 = 1,541 units
  
  When inventory drops to 1,541 units → place new order
  New order should arrive just as inventory reaches safety stock level (141 units)
  If demand was average and lead time was average

Variability buffers:
  Above formula covers demand variability only (lead time fixed)
  If lead time is also variable: need to add lead time variability buffer
  → Use the full formula with both σ_demand and σ_LT
```

**Sensitivity Analysis — Safety Stock vs Service Level:**

```
How service level impacts safety stock (same example as above):

Service Level | Z     | Safety Stock | Carrying Cost/yr | Stockout Risk
90%           | 1.28  | 96 units     | 1.20 triệu       | 10% of cycles
95%           | 1.65  | 124 units    | 1.55 triệu       | 5% of cycles
97%           | 1.88  | 141 units    | 1.76 triệu       | 3% of cycles
99%           | 2.33  | 175 units    | 2.19 triệu       | 1% of cycles
99.9%         | 3.09  | 232 units    | 2.90 triệu       | 0.1% of cycles

The 90% → 99% jump costs: 2.19 - 1.20 = 0.99 triệu/year extra carrying
But reduces stockouts from 10% → 1% of replenishment cycles
For A-item with 200 triệu/year at risk from stockout: 0.99 triệu cost easily justified

For C-item with 5 triệu/year at risk: maybe 90–95% service level is sufficient
→ Service level target should be calibrated to the stockout cost, not applied uniformly
```

**Reducing Safety Stock Without Increasing Stockout Risk:**

```
Safety stock can be reduced by improving supply chain reliability:

1. REDUCE LEAD TIME:
   Shorter lead time → less exposure during replenishment → less SS needed
   SS with 14-day LT: 141 units
   SS with 7-day LT: 141/√2 = 100 units (29% reduction)
   
   How: Qualify local or regional suppliers; invest in supplier relationships;
        negotiate consignment or VMI (Vendor-Managed Inventory) arrangements

2. REDUCE LEAD TIME VARIABILITY:
   More reliable supplier → less buffer needed
   Example: Supplier A (LT 14d ± 7d) vs Supplier B (LT 14d ± 2d)
   → Switching to Supplier B significantly reduces SS requirement
   
3. IMPROVE DEMAND FORECAST ACCURACY:
   SS = Z × σ_demand × √LT
   If σ_demand reduces by 30% (through better forecasting): SS reduces by 30%
   → Invest in demand forecasting as an inventory reduction lever

4. VENDOR-MANAGED INVENTORY (VMI):
   Supplier monitors your inventory levels and ships automatically
   → Lead time effectively = 0 for replenishment decisions
   → Safety stock can be minimal
   → Works best for commodity inputs with reliable suppliers
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** The mathematical optimization of safety stock was developed in operations research in the 1950s-60s. But the real innovation came from lean manufacturing practitioners who showed that the right answer isn't "calculate the right safety stock" — it's "reduce variability so less safety stock is needed." Toyota's system doesn't aim for perfect safety stock calculation; it aims to reduce demand variability (through production smoothing) and supply variability (through supplier development) to the point where very little safety stock is needed. The insight: safety stock is a band-aid on variability; reducing variability is the cure.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Set one safety stock level for all SKUs (e.g., "30 days of supply for everything"). A-items with high variability need data-driven safety stock. C-items with stable demand might need only 5–10 days. Uniform policy either over-stocks stable items or under-stocks variable ones.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Calculate safety stock once and never update. Demand patterns change seasonally; lead times change when suppliers change; variability changes with market conditions. Safety stock parameters should be recalculated quarterly for A-items; semi-annually for B-items.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Confuse average stock and safety stock in financial reporting. Average inventory = cycle stock/2 + safety stock. When reporting inventory reduction progress, ensure both cycle stock AND safety stock are tracked separately. Reducing cycle stock (buy less each time, more often) is good. Reducing safety stock below the calculated floor is risky.
