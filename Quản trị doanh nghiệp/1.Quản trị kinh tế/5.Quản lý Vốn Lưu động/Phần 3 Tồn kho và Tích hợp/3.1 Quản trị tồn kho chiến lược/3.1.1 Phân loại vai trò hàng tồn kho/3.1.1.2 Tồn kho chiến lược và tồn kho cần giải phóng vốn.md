### Tồn Kho Chiến Lược và Tồn Kho Cần Giải Phóng Vốn

Không phải tất cả tồn kho đều nên bị cắt giảm. Một số tồn kho là chiến lược — giúp bảo vệ doanh thu, tận dụng cơ hội, hoặc giảm rủi ro chuỗi cung ứng. Số còn lại là vốn chết — không tạo ra giá trị, chỉ giam giữ tiền mặt. Phân biệt hai loại này là bước đầu tiên để đưa ra quyết định tồn kho đúng đắn.

> **Bài học:** Mục tiêu không phải là "giảm tồn kho tối đa" hay "tăng tồn kho để an toàn" — mà là giữ đúng lượng tồn kho cho đúng mục đích chiến lược. Strategic inventory is an asset; excess inventory is a liability. Phân biệt được hai loại là nền tảng của quản trị tồn kho thông minh.

---

**Tồn Kho Chiến Lược — Khi Nào Cần Giữ Nhiều Hơn:**

```
1. SAFETY STOCK FOR SUPPLY CHAIN RISK:
   When: Single-source supplier with long lead time; geopolitically risky supply
   How much: Calculate based on lead time variability + demand variability
   Example: Imported component, 45-day lead time, ±15 days variability
   → Safety stock = average demand × safety factor × lead time variability
   → Might require 30–45 days of extra stock vs 5–10 days for domestic supplier
   
   VN context: COVID-19 exposed single-source dependency on China for many manufacturers
   → Post-COVID, smart companies hold 30–60 days strategic buffer on critical imports

2. PRE-SEASONAL BUILDUP:
   When: Predictable seasonal demand spike (Tết, back-to-school, rainy season)
   How much: Forecast peak demand × weeks of peak × service level target - regular stock
   Example: Tết sales = 3× normal month; production lead time = 6 weeks
   → Need to start building inventory in October/November for Jan/Feb Tết
   
   Financing: Plan credit line drawdown for pre-seasonal inventory buildup
   → Repay credit line from Tết season collections

3. OPPORTUNISTIC PURCHASE:
   When: Supplier offers significant price discount for bulk purchase
   Calculation: Compare discount value vs carrying cost of extra inventory
   
   Formula: Buy more if: Discount % > Carrying cost rate × Extra holding period
   Example: Supplier offers 8% discount for 3 months of demand (vs normal 1 month)
   Carrying cost: 25%/year × 2 extra months = 4.2%
   Decision: 8% discount > 4.2% carrying cost → buy the bulk quantity
   
   Caution: Only for commodities with stable demand and no obsolescence risk

4. COMPETITIVE AVAILABILITY ADVANTAGE:
   When: Your category is demand-driven; first to fill order wins the sale
   Example: Distributors, spare parts dealers, industrial supplies
   → Holding premium inventory levels is part of the value proposition
   → Cost is a deliberate competitive investment; not an inefficiency
```

**Tồn Kho Cần Giải Phóng — Identifying Dead and Excess Stock:**

```
TYPES OF UNPRODUCTIVE INVENTORY:

Excess Stock (over-bought for current demand):
  Definition: Inventory > safety stock + expected demand for planning horizon
  Cause: Over-optimistic demand forecast; bulk purchase without demand
  Action: Stop replenishment; allow to draw down naturally; consider promotion

Slow-Moving Stock:
  Definition: DIO > 90 days; selling at <1/12 of annual demand per month
  Cause: Demand decline; wrong product-market fit; poor placement; poor promotion
  Action: Investigate root cause; promotional push; channel expansion; price reduction

Dead Stock (non-moving):
  Definition: No sales in >90 days (for FMCG) or >6 months (for industrial)
  Cause: Product discontinued; seasonal item not sold; customer cancelled; defective
  Action: Liquidate immediately at any price that recovers cash
    Priority: Sell at cost → sell at deep discount → donate/scrap → write off
    Rule: Any cash recovered > storage cost saved → sell it

Obsolete Stock:
  Definition: Can no longer be sold at any reasonable price (expired, outdated, recalled)
  Cause: Product expiry; technology obsolescence; regulatory change
  Action: Write off immediately; dispose appropriately; learn from the cause

Example Dead Stock Analysis:
  Item       | Last Sale | Value  | Action
  Product A  | 8 months  | 800 tỷ | List on B2B marketplace; offer 40% off
  Product B  | 14 months | 300 tỷ | Scrap/donate; write off
  Product C  | 4 months  | 200 tỷ | Bundle with fast-moving product; promote
```

---

**Dead Stock Liquidation Playbook:**

```
Order of preference (maximize cash recovery):

1. Sell through existing channel at discount (20–40% off):
   Fastest; highest recovery; preserves some brand
   Risk: cannibalizes regular sales; trains customers to wait for discount

2. Sell through secondary channels (distributor clearance, B2B marketplace):
   Good recovery (50–70 cents on dollar)
   Avoids brand cannibalisation in primary channel

3. Bundle with fast-moving products:
   Example: "Buy 2 Product A (fast mover), get Product C (dead stock) free"
   → Zero price received for dead stock but customer acquisition for fast mover

4. Return to supplier (if within return policy):
   Full credit recovery minus restocking fee
   Not always possible; depends on relationship and contract

5. Sell to jobbers/liquidators:
   20–40 cents on dollar; quick; removes problem
   Loss-generating but preserves cash vs zero from write-off

6. Donate to charity (if applicable):
   Tax benefit (in some jurisdictions); brand goodwill
   VN: Tax deductibility for charitable donations requires proper documentation

7. Write off and dispose:
   Zero cash recovery; but stops ongoing storage cost
   Tax deductible write-off in correct period

Rule: Make a decision within 30 days of identifying dead stock.
      Every month of delay = more carrying cost + less liquidation value
```

**Inventory Funding Decision:**

```
WHICH INVENTORY TO FUND WHEN CAPITAL IS CONSTRAINED:

Priority 1: A-item safety stock and active stock
  → Directly protects revenue; high service level commitment
  
Priority 2: Pre-seasonal strategic buildup (approved with seasonal credit plan)
  → Revenue-generating; temporary; funded by dedicated seasonal facility

Priority 3: Normal B-item replenishment
  → Maintains standard service level on important SKUs

Priority 4: Opportunistic purchases (only if explicit ROI > carrying cost)
  → Require CFO sign-off; ROI analysis before approval

DEFER/REJECT:
  C-item replenishment beyond 30 days of supply
  Speculative purchases without confirmed demand
  Any SKU on elimination list
  Items for projects/customers without confirmed PO
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Supply chain disruptions post-2020 (COVID, Suez Canal blockage, semiconductor shortage) forced a global reassessment of lean-vs-resilient inventory strategy. Companies that had aggressively pursued lean (near-zero safety stock) were severely damaged. Companies with strategic buffers (especially in critical components) had competitive advantage — they could keep shipping when competitors couldn't. The pendulum has swung: "resilient inventory" is now recognized as a strategic investment, not inefficiency. For VN manufacturers dependent on imported inputs: a 30–60 day strategic buffer in critical materials is cheap insurance relative to the cost of a production shutdown.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Value inventory at purchase cost when making decisions to liquidate. "We paid 500 triệu for this stock; we can't sell it for 350 triệu." Sunk cost fallacy. The 500 triệu is gone whether you sell or hold. The decision is: 350 triệu cash now (sell) vs continued carrying cost + hope of higher sale (hold). Often, take the cash now.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Fail to segment strategic vs excess when reporting DIO. "DIO is 75 days." If 15 days of that is strategic safety stock (approved) and 60 days is excess (problem), these need different actions. Reporting combined DIO masks the issue. Report: Strategic stock (days) + Operating stock (days) + Excess/Dead stock (days) = Total DIO.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Build strategic inventory without a drawdown plan. "We bought 90 days of Component X for supply security." But without a clear plan for when to use vs replenish the buffer, the 90 days of stock can become permanent excess inventory. Strategic stock must have: defined trigger conditions for drawdown, replenishment protocol, and maximum holding period.
