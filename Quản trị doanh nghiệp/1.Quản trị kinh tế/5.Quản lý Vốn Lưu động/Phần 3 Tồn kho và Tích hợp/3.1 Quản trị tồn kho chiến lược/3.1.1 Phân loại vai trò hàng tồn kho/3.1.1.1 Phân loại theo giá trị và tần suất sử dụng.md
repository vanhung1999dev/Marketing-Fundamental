### Phân Loại Hàng Tồn Kho Theo Giá Trị và Tần Suất Sử Dụng

Không phải tất cả hàng tồn kho đều cần được quản lý với mức độ chú ý như nhau. Phân loại ABC giúp tập trung nguồn lực quản lý vào những SKU quan trọng nhất — thay vì trải đều sự chú ý trên tất cả hàng nghìn mặt hàng và không làm tốt cái nào.

> **Bài học:** Pareto's 80/20 rule áp dụng rất rõ cho tồn kho. Top 20% SKU thường tạo ra 80% doanh thu và 80% lợi nhuận. Quản lý tốt 20% đó quyết định phần lớn kết quả tài chính. 80% còn lại là long tail — quan trọng để có mặt, nhưng không cần sự chú ý quản lý như nhau.

---

**ABC Classification — Phương Pháp Phân Loại:**

```
STEP 1: Collect data (12 months rolling)
  For each SKU: Annual Revenue (or COGS); Number of Transactions; Average Order Quantity

STEP 2: Rank by Annual Revenue (descending)
  Sort all SKUs from highest to lowest annual revenue contribution

STEP 3: Calculate cumulative % of total revenue

STEP 4: Assign categories
  Category A: SKUs in top 0–80% of cumulative revenue (typically 15–25% of SKU count)
  Category B: SKUs from 80–95% cumulative (typically 25–35% of SKU count)
  Category C: SKUs from 95–100% cumulative (typically 40–60% of SKU count)

Example (simplified, 10 SKUs):
  SKU    | Rev (tỷ) | % Rev | Cum%  | Category
  SKU-01 | 12.0     | 24.0% | 24%   | A
  SKU-02 | 9.5      | 19.0% | 43%   | A
  SKU-03 | 8.0      | 16.0% | 59%   | A
  SKU-04 | 6.5      | 13.0% | 72%   | A
  SKU-05 | 4.0      | 8.0%  | 80%   | A (80% threshold)
  SKU-06 | 3.5      | 7.0%  | 87%   | B
  SKU-07 | 2.5      | 5.0%  | 92%   | B
  SKU-08 | 1.5      | 3.0%  | 95%   | B (95% threshold)
  SKU-09 | 0.9      | 1.8%  | 96.8% | C
  SKU-10 | 0.6      | 1.2%  | 98%   | C
  ...    | ...      | ...   | ...   | C

Result: 5 SKUs (50%) = Category A → drive 80% of revenue
```

**Inventory Management Policy Per Category:**

```
CATEGORY A — Tight Control:
  Service level target: 99.5% (stockout almost never acceptable)
  Safety stock: Formula-based, data-driven (lower tolerance for over-stock)
  Review frequency: Weekly (at minimum)
  Reorder method: Continuous review (reorder when reaching ROP)
  Forecast accuracy target: <5% MAPE
  Responsibility: Senior buyer; escalate any stockout to management
  Physical count: Monthly cycle count
  
  Actions:
    □ Track demand daily for top A-items
    □ Build supplier relationships to reduce lead time
    □ Have at least 1 backup supplier identified
    □ Never let go below safety stock without senior sign-off

CATEGORY B — Moderate Control:
  Service level target: 97–98%
  Safety stock: Moderate buffer
  Review frequency: Bi-weekly or weekly for fast movers within B
  Reorder method: Min-max review or periodic review
  Forecast accuracy target: <10% MAPE
  Responsibility: Buyers
  Physical count: Quarterly cycle count

CATEGORY C — Simplified Control:
  Service level target: 90–95% (some stockouts acceptable — low revenue impact)
  Safety stock: Minimal or zero
  Review frequency: Monthly
  Reorder method: Periodic review; batch replenishment
  Forecast accuracy: Less critical
  Responsibility: Junior buyers or automated
  Physical count: Annual
  
  KEY QUESTION for each C item:
    Should this SKU still exist?
    Can it be consolidated with another SKU?
    Can it be moved to special-order (no stock held)?
```

**XYZ Classification — Tần Suất và Biến Động:**

```
Complement ABC with XYZ (demand variability):

X items: Stable, predictable demand (low variability, CoV < 0.5)
  → Suitable for lean inventory; JIT possible; good for algorithm-based reorder

Y items: Moderate variability (CoV 0.5–1.0)
  → Some safety stock; need to monitor demand pattern changes

Z items: Highly variable or sporadic demand (CoV > 1.0)
  → High safety stock OR special-order basis OR strategic stockpile
  → Difficult to forecast; judgment required

Combined Matrix (prioritized management attention):
  AX: Highest revenue + stable demand → automate reorder; tight but simple management
  AY: Highest revenue + moderate variability → active management + forecasting
  AZ: Highest revenue + erratic demand → very high attention; strategic safety stock
  CX: Low revenue + stable → minimize stock; automate
  CZ: Low revenue + erratic → question existence; special order or eliminate

CoV (Coefficient of Variation) = Standard Deviation of Demand / Average Demand
  Calculate using monthly demand data over 12 months per SKU
```

**SKU Rationalization — Hợp Lý Hóa Danh Mục:**

```
Process: Annual SKU review

Step 1: Identify candidates for elimination
  □ Revenue < threshold (e.g., < 50 triệu/year)
  □ DIO > 120 days (very slow moving)
  □ <3 customers buying in last 12 months
  □ Negative margin (price < cost after allocation)
  □ Customer/market segement being exited

Step 2: Assess impact of elimination
  □ Would eliminating this SKU lose any customers?
  □ Is it a "door opener" that brings other higher-margin SKUs?
  □ Is it required for regulatory/compliance reasons?
  □ Is it part of a bundle that customers value as a whole?

Step 3: Decision
  KEEP: strategic despite low revenue (relationship, compliance, bundle)
  ELIMINATE: announce 90-day notice; exhaust existing stock; remove from catalog
  SPECIAL ORDER: remove from stock; offer only on customer request (5–10 day lead time)

Typical result: 15–25% SKU reduction
  → Frees inventory capital (C items often = 20–30% of inventory value despite 5% of revenue)
  → Simplifies operations; reduces complexity cost
  → Re-focuses buyer attention on A items
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Toyota's product strategy at various points has been explicitly anti-complexity. Akio Toyoda famously said "I've been struggling to understand why we have so many variations." Product complexity is an inventory killer: each variant needs its own safety stock, forecasting, and supplier relationship. In VN retail and FMCG, SKU proliferation is a chronic problem. Companies that can resist the temptation to add every possible variant maintain cleaner, more capital-efficient inventory portfolios. The lesson: every new SKU comes with a hidden cost — safety stock, forecast error, management attention.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Run ABC analysis once and never update it. Demand shifts over time: old A-items become C-items as market changes; new products launch as C-items and grow to A-items. ABC classification should be recalculated quarterly (or at least semi-annually) to reflect current demand reality.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Use only ABC without considering demand variability. Two A-items with same annual revenue but one has smooth demand (predictable) and one has erratic demand (unpredictable) require very different safety stock policies. Without XYZ analysis, the erratic-demand A-item will either stockout frequently or carry excessive safety stock.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Eliminate C-items mechanically without understanding their strategic role. Some low-revenue items are "KVI" (Known Value Items) — items customers use as a price reference. Some are "door openers" that lead to high-margin purchases. Some are required by contract. Never eliminate based solely on revenue/profitability without understanding the strategic context.
