### Đàm Phán và Tái Cấu Trúc Điều Khoản Thanh Toán

Điều khoản thanh toán không phải là cố định vĩnh viễn — là kết quả của đàm phán và có thể tái cấu trúc khi hoàn cảnh thay đổi. Kỹ năng đàm phán điều khoản thanh toán — với cả khách hàng lẫn nhà cung cấp — là một trong những kỹ năng tài chính thực chiến có giá trị nhất.

> **Bài học:** Mọi điều khoản thanh toán đều có thể đàm phán — câu hỏi là bạn mang gì đến bàn đàm phán. Volume commitment, dự báo nhu cầu, chia sẻ data, loyalty, early payment — đều là "đồng tiền" để trao đổi lấy điều khoản tốt hơn. Đừng chấp nhận điều khoản mặc định khi bạn có giá trị trao đổi.

---

**Đàm Phán Điều Khoản Với Nhà Cung Cấp — Tăng DPO:**

```
PREPARATION (trước đàm phán):
  □ Know your current terms and payment history
  □ Research: what terms do competitors get from this supplier?
  □ Know your leverage: % of supplier's total revenue you represent
  □ Calculate the value of better terms: DPO 30 → 45 = X tỷ freed capital
  □ Know your BATNA (Best Alternative): can you find another supplier?
  □ Prepare what you can offer in exchange

OPENING:
  "We've been a reliable partner for 3 years — paying on time, growing volume.
  We're planning to increase orders by 25% next year. As we scale together,
  we'd like to discuss aligning payment terms with our growth trajectory."
  
  → Frame as partnership, not extraction
  → Lead with your track record and future value
  → Ask for terms that reflect the long-term relationship

NEGOTIATION:
  Scenario A: Supplier has capacity; want your volume growth
  → Offer volume commitment letter in exchange for Net 60 terms
  → "Lock in" minimum purchase volumes; supplier accepts extended terms as quid pro quo
  
  Scenario B: Supplier is tight on cash; doesn't want to extend terms
  → Propose Supply Chain Finance: bank pays supplier immediately; you pay bank at Net 60
  → Both sides win; no relationship cost
  
  Scenario C: Supplier has pricing power; you need the product
  → Accept existing terms; focus DPO extension on Tier 2/3 suppliers instead
  → Don't damage critical supplier relationships for small cash benefit

CLOSE:
  □ Confirm new terms in writing (amendment to purchase agreement or email confirmation)
  □ Share updated terms with treasury team to update cash flow model
  □ Honor new terms consistently — don't take extended terms then pay even later
```

**Đàm Phán Điều Khoản Với Khách Hàng — Giảm DSO:**

```
Approaching large customers about tightening terms (reducing DSO):

APPROACH 1: Offer incentive (less confrontational):
  "We're introducing an early payment program. Invoices paid within 10 days
  receive a 1.5% discount. We want to reward our best customers for helping
  our cash flow while providing you with a competitive return on your cash."
  
  → Framed as mutual benefit
  → Customers with good cash flow and high cost of capital will participate
  → DSO drops without any change to contract terms

APPROACH 2: New customer onboarding (starting right):
  All new customers start at Net 15 or 50% deposit
  "Our standard terms for new relationships are Net 15 until we establish
  a payment history. After 3 on-time payments, we can discuss Net 30."
  
  → Set right expectation from day one
  → Much easier than changing existing terms later

APPROACH 3: Tighten terms on slow payers (direct approach):
  After pattern of late payment: formal conversation
  "As you know, your recent invoices have been paid an average of 22 days
  after due date. To maintain our commercial relationship, we need to adjust
  your payment terms to Net 15 or require a 30% deposit on orders."
  
  Key: have data; be specific; have alternatives ready (COD option)
  
APPROACH 4: Business model shift (structural):
  Move from invoice-based to subscription/retainer model
  → Customer pays monthly fee in advance
  → DSO drops to 0; cash flow transforms
  Works best for: service businesses, software, maintenance contracts
```

**Tái Cấu Trúc Khi Khách Hàng Không Trả Được:**

```
When a customer genuinely cannot pay (cash flow difficulty):

Step 1: Assess sincerity
  Genuine difficulty: transparent communication; specific reason; cooperative
  Manufactured excuse: evasive; no explanation; history of manipulation
  → Different responses; don't apply same treatment

Step 2 (for genuine difficulty): Structured payment plan
  Principles:
    Get first payment immediately (tests commitment + reduces exposure)
    Shorter payment plan = better (3 months max before reassessing)
    Each installment must be specific date + amount + method
    No new shipments until plan is on track
  
  Example structure:
    Total overdue: 500 triệu
    Immediate: 150 triệu (30%) — TODAY
    Month 1: 150 triệu
    Month 2: 100 triệu
    Month 3: 100 triệu
    
    If any payment missed by >3 days: full amount immediately due;
    legal proceedings begin

Step 3: Document everything
  □ Written agreement (email minimum; formal letter if significant amount)
  □ Track each installment; acknowledge receipt
  □ Internal: provision remaining balance at 50% until fully paid

Step 4: Resume normal terms after plan completed
  If plan followed: restore to previous tier; note the incident in credit file
  If plan failed (any missed payment): permanent Tier D; COD only; legal for overdue
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Payment terms negotiation is a zero-sum game in the immediate sense (buyer gets longer terms = seller finances longer) but can be positive-sum when structured correctly. SCF arrangements, volume commitments, and demand forecast sharing create value for both sides beyond just "who holds cash longer." The companies that understand this — and negotiate accordingly — achieve better terms without damaging relationships. The companies that treat it purely as "make them hold cash" damage supply chain relationships in ways that manifest as poor service, quality issues, and supply disruptions.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Negotiate payment terms every year regardless of relationship quality. Constantly pushing for better terms from suppliers who are already at their limit signals adversarial intent — even if it's just procurement following a script. Know when to accept current terms and invest in relationship rather than extract another 5 days.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Offer blanket early payment discount to all customers without ROI calculation. "We'll offer 1.5% discount for payment within 10 days." At 10% cost of capital: 1.5% / (30-10 days) × 365 = 27.4% annualized. You're paying 27.4% to collect 20 days faster. If your cost of capital is 10%, that's destroying value. Only offer discounts when the cost of capital (or cash need) justifies the discount rate.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Fail to update internal systems after negotiating new terms. New terms agreed with Supplier A → accounts payable team doesn't know → pays on old terms → either pay too early (waste cash) or too late (breach new agreement). Every negotiated change must be immediately updated in: accounting system, purchase order template, payment run rules.
