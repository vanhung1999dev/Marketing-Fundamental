### Cấu Trúc Công Nợ Theo Rủi Ro và Lợi Nhuận

Không phải tất cả khoản phải thu đều có giá trị như nhau và không phải tất cả khoản phải trả đều có chi phí như nhau. Cấu trúc hóa danh mục AR/AP theo rủi ro và lợi nhuận cho phép đưa ra quyết định phân bổ vốn và chiến lược thu tiền thông minh hơn.

> **Bài học:** AR portfolio là một danh mục đầu tư. Quản lý nó như quản lý portfolio: đa dạng hóa (không để một khách hàng quá lớn), cân bằng rủi ro-lợi nhuận (khách hàng rủi ro cần biên lợi nhuận bù đắp), và liên tục tối ưu hóa (giảm tỷ trọng rủi ro; tăng chất lượng).

---

**AR Portfolio Analysis — Phân Tích Danh Mục Phải Thu:**

```
Customer Concentration Risk:
  Red flag: Single customer > 20% of total AR
  Severe: Single customer > 30% of total AR
  
  Why it matters: If that customer defaults → immediate liquidity crisis
  Mitigation: Actively grow revenue from other customers; reduce concentration
  
  Target portfolio structure:
    No single customer > 15% of AR
    Top 5 customers ≤ 50% of AR
    Diversified across industries/sectors (reduces correlated default risk)

Risk-Return Analysis per customer segment:
  
  Segment     | Revenue% | AR% | Bad Debt% | Margin% | Net Risk-Adj Return
  ------------|----------|-----|-----------|---------|--------------------
  Tier A (5)  | 40%      | 35% | 0.1%      | 18%     | High (17.9% adj)
  Tier B (20) | 40%      | 40% | 0.5%      | 20%     | Medium (19.5% adj)
  Tier C (50) | 15%      | 20% | 2.0%      | 22%     | Low (20% adj)
  Tier D (25) | 5%       | 5%  | 5.0%      | 25%     | Very Low (20% adj)
  
  Insight: Tier C/D customers have higher gross margin but high bad debt →
           net risk-adjusted return is similar or lower than Tier A/B
           → Grow Tier A/B volume; restrict Tier C/D
```

---

**Margining for Credit Risk — Risk-Adjusted Pricing:**

```
When to price in credit risk:

Standard customer (Tier B): 0% premium (standard price)
Risky customer (Tier C):
  Expected bad debt: 2% of revenue from this customer
  Collection cost premium: 0.5% (extra time chasing)
  Opportunity cost (slower payment): 1.5% (DSO 60 vs 30 = 30 extra days × 12%/365 × 30d)
  Total credit cost: 4%
  → Price Tier C at 4% premium vs Tier B OR require prepayment OR reduce credit

Government/project customers (very long DSO):
  DSO = 120 days vs standard 30 days = 90 extra days
  Financing cost = 12%/365 × 90 = 2.96% of invoice value
  → Build this cost into project pricing; don't absorb it in margin

Formula:
  Credit cost % = (DSO - Standard DSO) / 365 × WACC + Expected Bad Debt %
  Price adjustment needed = at least cover credit cost
```

**AP Portfolio Optimization — Cấu Trúc Phải Trả:**

```
Classify AP by:

1. Cost of Trade Credit:
  Free credit (no early payment discount offered): maximize DPO; no cost
  Expensive credit (high discount for early payment): calculate cost; pay early if worthwhile
  Penalized credit (late payment penalty): pay on time; penalty > cost of capital
  
2. Supplier Criticality:
  Critical single-source: minimize DPO risk; pay on time; relationship > cash optimization
  Strategic multi-source: negotiate best terms; optimize DPO within relationship limits
  Commodity: maximize DPO; easily replaced if relationship damaged

3. Invoice Size:
  Large invoices: manage precisely; call to confirm before due date
  Small invoices: batch payments; reduce transaction costs
  Recurring fixed: automate; standing orders; remove manual process

AP Portfolio Target:
  % paid exactly on due date: >80% (not early, not late)
  % paid early (not taking discount): <5% (waste of cash)
  % paid >15 days late (by choice): only on commodity suppliers
  % past due (missed deadline): <2% (relationship/legal risk)
```

**Working Capital Quality Indicators:**

```
Beyond the numbers — assessing WC quality:

AR Quality:
  GOOD: Diversified customer base; all Tier A/B; low bad debt history; improving DSO
  BAD: Concentrated in 1–2 customers; rising overdue %; write-offs increasing

Inventory Quality:
  GOOD: High A-item %; fast turnover; low obsolescence; DIO stable or improving
  BAD: High C-item %; slow turnover; growing >90-day aging; DIO worsening

AP Quality:
  GOOD: Clear payment terms; systematic process; good supplier relationships
  BAD: Many disputes; suppliers demanding COD; payment runs chaotic

WC quality indicators affect:
  Credit rating from banks (better quality = better loan terms)
  Company valuation (acquirers discount bad WC quality in M&A)
  Operational risk (poor WC = supply chain and customer relationship risk)
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Working capital quality matters as much as working capital quantity. Two companies can both have CCC = 50 days, but one has high-quality AR (Tier A/B customers, diversified, low bad debt) while the other has low-quality AR (concentrated, overdue, high bad debt risk). The first is fundable at low cost; the second faces higher financing costs and greater volatility. When VN SMEs seek bank financing, banks increasingly assess WC quality — not just the balance sheet numbers. Improving WC quality (customer mix, collection quality, inventory freshness) improves financing access and cost.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Measure AR only in total, not in quality-adjusted terms. "AR is 15 tỷ." But if 3 tỷ is >90 days old with questionable collectability, economic AR value might be 12–13 tỷ. Understanding the economic (risk-adjusted) value of AR gives a more honest picture of financial health.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Treat all bad debt as an operating cost rather than a credit policy failure. Some bad debt is unavoidable. But if bad debt is consistently >1% of revenue, it's a signal of systematic credit policy failure — either too many high-risk customers, inadequate collection processes, or both. Investigate: which segments are generating bad debt? What could have prevented it?

> **Cạm bẫy:** Focus exclusively on DSO as the AR metric; ignore concentration. DSO = 35 days looks excellent — but if 60% of AR is from one customer who has been reliable for 3 years, there's hidden concentration risk. That single customer hit by a recession, regulatory change, or internal crisis creates a sudden 9 tỷ bad debt that no DSO metric warned about.
