### Cơ Chế Giám Sát và Thu Hồi Công Nợ Hiệu Quả

Thu hồi công nợ là kỹ năng thực chiến, không phải lý thuyết. Hệ thống thu hồi hiệu quả kết hợp automation (không bỏ sót), escalation có cấu trúc (ai làm gì ở bước nào), và relationship intelligence (đối xử khác nhau với từng khách hàng).

> **Bài học:** The best collection is prevention. Khách hàng trả đúng hạn vì quy trình của bạn rõ ràng, invoicing nhanh, và nhắc nhở nhẹ nhàng đúng thời điểm — không phải vì bạn đủ "hung dữ" khi đòi tiền. Thiết kế quy trình thu tiền để thanh toán trở nên dễ dàng, không phải để đòi tiền trở nên đáng sợ.

**Collections Process — Step by Step:**

```
PRE-DUE PREPARATION (7 days before due date):
  □ Verify invoice was received and accepted (no disputes)
  □ Confirm payment method and banking details are correct
  □ If any pending dispute: resolve before due date; don't let dispute be excuse for late payment
  □ For large invoices (>200 triệu): call to confirm receipt and payment timeline
  Tool: Automated task created in CRM/accounting system 7 days before each due date

DUE DATE (Day 0):
  □ Automated email/Zalo reminder sent
  □ Include invoice PDF; bank details; exact amount; due date confirmation
  □ Friendly tone: "As a reminder, Invoice #XXX is due today..."
  □ Check if payment received by end of business day

DAY +3 (first soft follow-up):
  □ If not paid: personalized email + phone call from accounts receivable team
  □ Offer to help if there's any issue: "Is there anything blocking the payment?"
  □ Confirm new expected payment date; record in system
  □ If paying: thank and confirm receipt

DAY +7 (escalation within collections team):
  □ If still unpaid: account manager (sales) informed and involved
  □ Account manager calls their contact: peer-to-peer conversation
  □ Goal: understand root cause — dispute? cash flow issue? administrative delay?
  □ Agree on specific payment commitment (date + amount)

DAY +15 (senior escalation):
  □ CFO or senior finance manager calls financial counterpart (CFO/Finance of customer)
  □ Formal written demand letter via email (not yet legal notice)
  □ Discuss payment plan if customer has genuine difficulty
  □ Document all commitments

DAY +30 (formal notice):
  □ Formal demand letter (legal-format; via registered mail)
  □ Suspend all new shipments to this customer
  □ Internal: provision 50% of invoice value in accounts
  □ Tier downgrade: customer moved to Tier D immediately

DAY +60+ (collections/legal):
  □ Refer to external collections agency OR
  □ Begin legal proceedings (Khởi kiện tại Tòa án Kinh tế)
  □ Write off as bad debt; provision 100%
  □ Report to credit bureau (CIC) if applicable
```

**Dispute Management — Xử Lý Tranh Chấp:**

```
Common dispute types:
  (1) Invoice not received → resend; confirm delivery
  (2) Wrong amount on invoice → correct and reissue immediately (same day)
  (3) Goods quality issue → route to quality/sales team; separate from payment
  (4) Different terms than agreed → review contract; escalate to senior
  (5) "We didn't order this" → check PO; provide delivery proof
  (6) "We already paid" → check bank; investigate and reconcile

Dispute Resolution Rules:
  □ All disputes responded to within 2 business days
  □ Finance team resolves disputes — do NOT let disputes sit
  □ CRITICAL: separate legitimate dispute from "manufactured excuse to delay"
    Legitimate: first-time occurrence; specific identifiable issue
    Manufactured: pattern behavior; vague complaints; occurs only when payment is due
  □ If dispute appears manufactured: acknowledge; set 5-day resolution deadline; 
    inform that payment for undisputed portion is still due

Dispute Impact on Collections:
  Legitimate dispute → hold payment for disputed amount; collect undisputed portion
  Resolved dispute → payment due within original terms from resolution date
  Do NOT hold entire invoice when only a small portion is disputed
```

**Collections KPIs — Đo Lường Hiệu Quả:**

```
Metric               | Target    | Calculation
---------------------|-----------|-------------------------------------------
Collection Rate      | >98%      | Cash collected / Total invoiced (TTM)
DSO                  | <45 days  | AR / (Revenue/365)
Overdue AR %         | <10%      | AR > due date / Total AR
Bad Debt %           | <0.5%     | Write-offs / Revenue (TTM)
Dispute Rate         | <2%       | Disputed invoices / Total invoices
Days to Resolve      | <5 days   | Average dispute resolution time

Monthly Collections Report to CFO:
  Total AR balance: ____
  Collected this month: ____ (% of target)
  New overdue this month: ____
  Resolved disputes this month: ____
  Write-offs this month: ____
  Top 5 overdue accounts: [list]
  Actions in progress: [list]
  
Review monthly in AR meeting: Collections team + CFO + Sales team lead
```

**Difficult Conversations — Kỹ Năng Đàm Phán Thu Tiền:**

```
Scenario 1: "We have cash flow problems — can we pay in installments?"
  Response framework:
  □ Acknowledge their situation: "I understand this is a difficult time"
  □ Assess sincerity: Is this genuine or habitual?
  □ Negotiate payment plan: 30/30/30/10 over 4 months; get written commitment
  □ Require first installment immediately (tests sincerity)
  □ Put payment plan in writing (email confirmation minimum)
  □ If first installment not made: escalate to formal legal immediately

Scenario 2: "Our payment is stuck in approval" (administrative excuse)
  Response:
  □ "Who is the approver? When was it submitted?"
  □ "Can I speak directly with the approver to clarify anything?"
  □ Set clear deadline: "If not approved by [date], I need to escalate"
  □ Follow up precisely on the stated date

Scenario 3: Customer goes completely silent
  Response:
  □ Escalate immediately to account manager for relationship call
  □ Use multiple channels (email + phone + Zalo + visit if significant amount)
  □ Silence is a red flag: treat as potential default risk
  □ Provision and suspend shipments after 10 days of silence on overdue amount
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Research by Dun & Bradstreet shows that collection probability drops exponentially with time: an invoice 30 days overdue has ~90% collection probability; at 90 days, ~70%; at 180 days, ~50%; at 1 year, ~25%. This exponential decay is why early action is so valuable. The first 30 days after due date are the golden collection window. After 90 days, you're increasingly in distressed debt territory where collection costs escalate sharply.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Maintain silence on overdue accounts to preserve the relationship. "I don't want to pressure them; we have a good relationship." This is a false kindness. Professional, systematic follow-up is not aggressive — it's expected behavior in business. Most customers actually respect suppliers who manage their AR professionally. What damages relationships is inconsistent, sporadic, emotionally-charged collection — not consistent, professional follow-up.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Assign collection to the least experienced person because "it's just admin work." Collections requires excellent communication skills, negotiation ability, and business judgment (distinguish genuine difficulty from evasion). Put your best communicator on collections, especially for large accounts.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Provide a payment plan and then not monitor it. "Customer agreed to pay 30 triệu on the 15th." The 15th arrives; no payment; collections team doesn't follow up because "they have a plan." Payment plans are only valuable if every installment is monitored and any miss is escalated immediately. A missed installment on a payment plan is a more serious signal than the original overdue — treat it accordingly.
