### Quy Trình Rà Soát và Cập Nhật Dự Báo Dòng Tiền Định Kỳ

Dự báo dòng tiền không có giá trị nếu không được cập nhật và rà soát thường xuyên. Quy trình rà soát định kỳ biến cash forecast từ một bảng tính tĩnh thành một công cụ quản trị sống động — liên tục phản ánh thực tế và kịp thời cảnh báo rủi ro.

> **Bài học:** Cash forecast tốt nhất không phải là bảng tính phức tạp nhất — là bảng tính được cập nhật đều đặn nhất và được review bởi người có thẩm quyền hành động. Một mô hình đơn giản cập nhật hàng tuần thắng một mô hình tinh vi cập nhật hàng quý.

---

**Cadence Rà Soát — Theo Tần Suất:**

```
DAILY (5–10 phút — level: treasurer hoặc kế toán trưởng):
  □ Check bank balances (morning; all accounts aggregated)
  □ Update actual inflows vs forecast for previous day
  □ Note any unexpected large payments coming today
  □ Flag to CFO if any account approaches minimum threshold
  
  Tool: Banking app + morning email summary
  Output: 3-line daily cash flash: Opening | In | Out | Closing | vs Min Threshold

WEEKLY (30–45 phút — level: CFO):
  □ Update 13-week rolling forecast (roll forward by 1 week; add week 13)
  □ Compare actual last week vs forecast: explain variances > 10%
  □ Reforecast next 4 weeks based on new information
  □ Identify any weeks in next 13 where cash could breach minimum
  □ Take preemptive action if gap identified (accelerate collection; draw credit line)
  
  Output: Updated 13-week forecast + action items for the week

MONTHLY (60–90 phút — level: CFO + CEO):
  □ Full month actual vs budget cash flow reconciliation
  □ Cash flow variance attribution: volume (more/less activity) vs timing (same activity, shifted)
  □ Review CCC components: DSO, DPO, DIO trends
  □ Scenario update: refresh bear/stress scenarios for next quarter
  □ Decision: any policy changes needed (tighten AR, draw credit line, defer capex)?
  
  Output: Monthly cash management report to board/owners
```

---

**Update Process — Bước Cập Nhật Hàng Tuần:**

```
Monday morning ritual (30 minutes):

Step 1: Input last week actuals (10 min)
  Pull bank statement exports for all accounts
  Update forecast spreadsheet: actual collections vs forecast; actual payments vs forecast
  
Step 2: Identify variances (5 min)
  Highlight cells where actual > 15% off forecast
  Note root causes: "Customer A paid late"; "Emergency equipment repair"

Step 3: Roll forward 1 week (5 min)
  Remove completed week; add new week 13
  New week 13 = high-level estimate based on revenue forecast and payment schedule

Step 4: Reforecast weeks 2–4 (10 min)
  Based on: confirmed orders received; invoices outstanding; payment commitments
  This is the most valuable part: converts probability-based forecast to confirmed forecast
  
  Confirmed collections (high confidence): customer confirmed payment date
  Probable collections (medium confidence): within credit terms, customer is reliable
  Uncertain collections (low confidence): overdue; in dispute; new customer

Step 5: Identify cash gaps and plan response
  Any week where closing cash < minimum threshold → immediate action plan
  Typical responses: call top 3 debtors; draw credit line by X; defer payable Y
```

---

**Variance Analysis Framework:**

```
Monthly variance analysis: Actual CF vs Forecast CF

INFLOW variance:
  Volume effect: Revenue was higher/lower than forecast → proportional collection change
  Timing effect: Revenue was on plan but collection was faster/slower (DSO change)
  Mix effect: More cash sales than forecast → faster collection
  Bad debt effect: Provision realized → permanent shortfall
  
  Example:
    Forecast collections: 12 tỷ | Actual: 10.8 tỷ | Variance: -1.2 tỷ (10% short)
    Analysis: Revenue on plan; DSO extended 7 days (one large customer delayed)
    Action: Follow up specifically with the delayed customer; no systemic issue

OUTFLOW variance:
  Cost inflation: input prices higher than budget
  Timing shift: payment moved forward or backward
  Emergency spend: unplanned repair/maintenance
  DPO change: paying faster or slower than planned
  
  Example:
    Forecast payments: 9 tỷ | Actual: 10.2 tỷ | Variance: +1.2 tỷ overspend
    Analysis: Emergency HVAC repair 500 triệu; tax underpayment correction 700 triệu
    Action: Budget for maintenance reserves; ensure tax estimates are updated quarterly
```

**Escalation Protocol:**

```
Automatic escalation rules:

Level 1 (Daily — Treasurer to CFO):
  Trigger: Any account drops below 50% of minimum threshold
  Action: CFO reviews; approves drawing credit line if needed

Level 2 (Weekly — CFO to CEO):
  Trigger: 4-week forecast shows any week below minimum threshold
  Action: CEO + CFO review; agree on corrective actions
  Corrective options: accelerate AR; defer AP; draw credit line; defer capex; owner cash injection

Level 3 (Immediate — CEO to Board):
  Trigger: Cash will breach minimum within 2 weeks; no internal solution available
  Action: Emergency board meeting; consider bridge financing, equity injection, asset sale
  
Principle: Early escalation allows more options.
  2 weeks' notice: multiple options available (credit line, accelerated collections, AP deferral)
  2 days' notice: only emergency measures available (emergency loan, personal injection)
  0 days' notice: insolvency risk
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Private equity portfolio management provides the best model for rigorous cash forecasting cadence. PE-backed companies universally run 13-week rolling forecasts updated weekly, with daily cash sweeps and automated alerts. When a portfolio company shows cash declining toward minimum threshold, the PE firm's operating partner intervenes immediately — not because they're micromanaging, but because early intervention costs far less than late intervention. VN SME owners should adopt the same discipline: treat your company's cash like a PE firm treats a portfolio company's cash.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Stop updating forecast when things are going well. "Cash is strong; no need to forecast this week." The forecast is most valuable as an early warning system — and it can only warn early if it's maintained consistently. The one week you skip may be the week a problem first appeared in the forecast.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Confuse updating the forecast with managing cash. The forecast tells you what will happen; managing cash means taking actions to change what will happen. If forecast shows a cash gap in week 8, the response is not to revise the forecast — it's to take actions now to fill the gap before week 8 arrives.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Forecast only cash (bank accounts) without tracking undrawn credit. If you have 3 tỷ cash and 7 tỷ undrawn credit, your available liquidity is 10 tỷ — very different from 3 tỷ. Track both: actual cash + committed undrawn credit lines = total available liquidity. The minimum threshold should be expressed in total available liquidity, not just cash.
