### Các Biến Số Trọng Yếu Ảnh Hưởng Đến Dòng Tiền

Dự báo dòng tiền chính xác phụ thuộc vào việc xác định đúng các biến số then chốt — những yếu tố có tác động lớn nhất và có thể dự đoán được. Không phải mọi biến số đều quan trọng như nhau; quản lý có hạn năng lực nên tập trung vào 20% biến số tạo ra 80% sự biến động dòng tiền.

> **Bài học:** Cash flow forecasting không phải là việc điền vào một bảng Excel phức tạp với 200 dòng. Là việc xác định 5–8 biến số tạo ra 80% biến động dòng tiền và theo dõi chúng sát sao. Mô hình phức tạp nhưng theo dõi không nổi thì tệ hơn mô hình đơn giản nhưng được theo dõi nhất quán.

---

**Nhóm Biến Số 1: Doanh Thu và Thu Tiền:**

```
Collection Pattern (critical for cash timing):
  Not all revenue converts to cash at the same time
  
  Revenue mix by payment timing:
    Cash/card sales: collect same day (Day 0)
    Net 15 customers: collect Day 15
    Net 30 customers: collect Day 30
    Net 45 customers: collect Day 45
    Government/project: collect Day 60–120+
  
  Example: Monthly revenue 15 tỷ with collection pattern:
    20% cash (3 tỷ) → collect month 0
    50% Net 30 (7.5 tỷ) → collect month 1
    30% Net 60 (4.5 tỷ) → collect month 2
  
  January revenue 15 tỷ → cash inflow split: Jan 3 tỷ + Feb 7.5 tỷ + Mar 4.5 tỷ
  
  Key driver: DSO by customer segment; track monthly changes

Seasonal Revenue Pattern:
  For businesses with Tết, harvest, or academic seasonality:
  Revenue peaks create AR peaks → cash inflow peaks 30–60 days later
  Must forecast the LAG between revenue and cash, not just revenue
```

---

**Nhóm Biến Số 2: Chi Phí Và Thanh Toán:**

```
Fixed Cost Schedule (predictable, important to get right):
  Payroll: usually 1st–5th of month (must be funded)
  Rent: 1st or 15th depending on lease
  Bank repayment: usually consistent monthly schedule
  BHXH employer: paid 30th of following month (lag from payroll month)
  VAT: 20th of following month for most SMEs
  CIT quarterly instalment: 30th of Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4 months
  
  These are non-negotiable; forecast them exactly; flag in cash model

Variable Cost Payment Pattern:
  COGS payments: lag from receipt of goods
    Supplier Net 30: purchase month + 30 days
    Supplier Net 45: purchase month + 45 days
  
  Variable overhead: roughly proportional to revenue (with lag)
  Marketing/sales: varies by campaign; plan explicitly per activity
  
  Key driver: DPO by supplier; forecast payment dates precisely
```

**Nhóm Biến Số 3: Vốn Lưu Động Thay Đổi:**

```
ΔAR (Change in Accounts Receivable):
  Cash impact = -ΔAR (AR increasing = cash outflow equivalent)
  
  If revenue grows 10% and DSO unchanged:
    AR grows 10% → cash tied up increases proportionally
  If DSO also lengthens 5 days:
    Additional AR buildup = Revenue/365 × 5 days extra
  
  Forecast: predict revenue growth × collection pattern → monthly AR forecast
           → ΔAR = month-end AR minus prior month AR

ΔInventory (Change in Inventory):
  Cash impact = -ΔInventory (inventory increasing = cash outflow)
  
  Driven by:
    Planned production / purchasing schedule
    Seasonal buildup (pre-Tết inventory)
    Demand forecast accuracy
  
  Forecast: production/purchase plan → inventory build/draw schedule → ΔInventory

ΔAP (Change in Accounts Payable):
  Cash impact = +ΔAP (AP increasing = cash inflow equivalent — using supplier credit)
  
  Driven by:
    Purchasing volume (proportional to COGS)
    DPO terms with suppliers
  
  Forecast: purchase plan × DPO → AP balance forecast → ΔAP

Net WC cash impact = -ΔAR - ΔInventory + ΔAP
```

---

**Nhóm Biến Số 4: Đầu Tư và Tài Chính:**

```
Capex (Capital Expenditure):
  Most significant non-recurring cash outflow
  Usually lumpy: 0 for months → large outflow when equipment purchased
  Must be planned by project; exact timing matters for cash forecast
  
  Example: Factory equipment purchase 5 tỷ in March → large March cash outflow
           followed by depreciation (non-cash) but no further cash impact

Debt Service:
  Principal repayment: per loan amortization schedule
  Interest payment: monthly or quarterly
  Both are contractually fixed → highly forecastable
  
  New borrowing: positive cash inflow; plan draw timing

Dividend/Owner Distributions:
  For owner-operated businesses: must explicitly plan owner cash withdrawals
  Unplanned owner drawings are a major cause of SME cash crises
  Recommendation: fix monthly owner salary + annual dividend (board-approved)
```

**Building the Cash Flow Forecast Template:**

```
13-Week Rolling Cash Forecast (standard tool):

Week of: | W1    | W2    | W3    | W4    | ... W13
---------|-------|-------|-------|-------|--------
INFLOWS
AR Collections:
  - Cash/card | 850   | 920   | 780   | 850   |
  - Net 30 due| 1,200 | 1,100 | 1,300 | 1,150 |
  - Net 60 due| 400   | 450   | 380   | 420   |
Other Income  | 50    | 50    | 50    | 50    |
TOTAL IN      | 2,500 | 2,520 | 2,510 | 2,470 |

OUTFLOWS
Payroll       | 1,800 |       |       |       | (if bi-monthly)
Suppliers     | 800   | 750   | 900   | 650   |
Rent          |       |       |       | 300   | (1st of month)
Bank repayment|       | 500   |       |       | (bi-monthly)
Tax (VAT)     |       |       |       | 200   | (20th)
Other OpEx    | 150   | 120   | 180   | 140   |
TOTAL OUT     | 2,750 | 1,370 | 1,080 | 1,290 |

NET CASH FLOW | -250  | +1,150| +1,430| +1,180|
Opening Cash  | 3,200 | 2,950 | 4,100 | 5,530 |
Closing Cash  | 2,950 | 4,100 | 5,530 | 6,710 |
Min Threshold | 2,500 | 2,500 | 2,500 | 2,500 |
BUFFER        | +450  | +1,600| +3,030| +4,210|

Units: triệu VNĐ; update every week; roll forward
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** The 13-week rolling cash flow forecast (sometimes called the "13-week burn") is standard practice in corporate finance, distressed debt advisory, and private equity portfolio management. It became the standard precisely because 13 weeks (one quarter) gives enough visibility to act — while being short enough that forecasts remain reasonably accurate. Weekly granularity captures the lumpy nature of cash flows (payroll on the 1st; rent on the 5th; VAT on the 20th). Any cash manager who doesn't run a 13-week rolling forecast is flying blind.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Build monthly cash forecast instead of weekly. Monthly forecast misses the intra-month timing crisis. Cash might average 5 tỷ per month but drop to 500 triệu on the 2nd (after payroll) before collections come in on the 15th. Weekly forecast catches these "valley" periods before they become emergencies.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Forecast only point estimate with no scenario analysis. "We'll collect 2.5 tỷ next week." What if a major customer delays payment? Run three scenarios: Base (expected collection), Bear (10% shortfall), Stress (30% shortfall + bank payment due). Know in advance when cash would breach minimum threshold in each scenario — so response is planned, not panicked.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Build cash forecast but never compare actual vs forecast. The forecast is only useful if it's updated with actuals and the variance is analyzed. "Why did we collect 300 triệu less than forecast?" → identified a customer that consistently pays late → update credit terms. Without variance analysis, the forecast is just a document; with it, it's a learning tool.
