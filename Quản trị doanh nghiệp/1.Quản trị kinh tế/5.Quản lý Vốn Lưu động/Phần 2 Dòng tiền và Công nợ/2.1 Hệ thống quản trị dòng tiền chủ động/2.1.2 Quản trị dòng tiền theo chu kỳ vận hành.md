### Quản Trị Dòng Tiền Theo Chu Kỳ Vận Hành

Mỗi doanh nghiệp có chu kỳ vận hành đặc thù — chuỗi các hoạt động từ khi bỏ tiền ra đến khi thu tiền về. Hiểu và tối ưu chu kỳ này là cốt lõi của quản trị dòng tiền chủ động.

> **Bài học:** Quản trị dòng tiền theo chu kỳ vận hành nghĩa là không chờ vấn đề xảy ra rồi phản ứng — mà thiết kế các điểm kiểm soát (control points) tại từng giai đoạn của chu kỳ để tiền chảy nhanh hơn, dự đoán được hơn, và rủi ro thấp hơn.

---

**Chu Kỳ Vận Hành Theo Ngành:**

```
DISTRIBUTION / WHOLESALE:
  Day 0: Place purchase order to supplier
  Day 30: Receive goods; AP recorded
  Day 30–45: Goods in warehouse (DIO)
  Day 45–75: Deliver to customers; AR recorded
  Day 90–120: Collect from customers

  CCC = DSO(45) + DIO(30) - DPO(30) = 45 days
  Capital tied up: COGS/365 × 45 days
  Key levers: DSO reduction (negotiate shorter terms) + DPO extension

MANUFACTURING:
  Day 0: Buy raw materials; AP recorded
  Day 30: Pay supplier
  Day 15–45: Materials in production (WIP inventory)
  Day 45–60: Finished goods in warehouse
  Day 60–80: Ship and invoice customers; AR recorded
  Day 100–130: Collect from customers

  CCC = DSO(60) + DIO(45) - DPO(30) = 75 days
  Much longer cycle; more capital intensive
  Key levers: Production efficiency (reduce WIP time) + inventory optimization + AR management

RESTAURANT / F&B RETAIL:
  Day 0: Purchase ingredients; pay cash or Net 15
  Day 0–3: Use ingredients; deliver to customer
  Day 0: Customer pays (cash, card, or app) — immediate or same day!

  CCC = DSO(0) + DIO(2) - DPO(15) = -13 days
  NEGATIVE CCC: Collect before paying suppliers → structural cash advantage
  
CONSTRUCTION / PROJECT:
  Day 0: Win contract; mobilize
  Day 1–30: Incur labor + material costs (cash out)
  Day 30–90: Milestone completion → invoice customer
  Day 90–150: Customer certifies; pays (often slow)
  
  CCC can be 60–120+ days; high capital intensity
  Key levers: Advance payment in contract (30% mobilization); tight milestone invoicing
```

**Control Points — Điểm Kiểm Soát Tại Từng Giai Đoạn:**

```
PURCHASE ORDER stage:
  □ Is this supplier on approved vendor list with agreed terms?
  □ Has DPO term been confirmed (Net 30? Net 45?)
  □ Has purchase been approved per authority matrix?
  → Control: all POs require finance approval for payment terms

GOODS RECEIPT stage:
  □ 3-way match: PO + GRN + Invoice
  □ Quality acceptance before AP is recorded
  → Control: no AP recorded without signed GRN

CUSTOMER INVOICE stage:
  □ Invoice sent same day as delivery (e-invoice per NĐ 123/2020)
  □ Payment terms clearly stated; bank details included
  □ Confirm receipt within 24 hours
  → Control: automated invoice generation from delivery confirmation

COLLECTION stage:
  □ Day 0 of due: automated reminder sent
  □ Day +3 of due: manual follow-up call
  □ Day +7 of due: escalation to account manager
  → Control: AR aging reviewed in weekly team meeting

PAYMENT stage:
  □ All payments require approval per matrix (CFO for >50 triệu)
  □ Fixed payment run days (Tuesday + Friday only)
  □ No off-cycle payments without CFO sign-off
  → Control: bank requires dual authorization for payments >100 triệu
```

**Cash Flow Acceleration Techniques:**

```
1. ACCELERATE BILLING:
   Problem: Sales delivers; invoice sent 3 days later
   Solution: Mobile invoicing at point of delivery (ERP mobile app)
   Gain: 3 days earlier start of payment clock = revenue/365 × 3 days cash freed

2. CONCENTRATE CASH:
   Problem: Revenue in 8 different bank accounts; hard to deploy efficiently
   Solution: Daily automatic sweep to master account
   Gain: Better visibility; maximize interest; avoid idle cash in sub-accounts

3. SHORTEN PAYMENT TERMS FOR NEW CUSTOMERS:
   Problem: All customers get Net 30 by default
   Solution: New customers start at Net 15 or 50% deposit; upgrade after 3 on-time payments
   Gain: Reduces default risk on early-stage customers; faster collection from new accounts

4. MILESTONE BILLING (for project businesses):
   Problem: Invoice only at project completion (60+ days into project)
   Solution: Negotiate milestone payments: 30% at contract; 30% at midpoint; 40% at completion
   Gain: Dramatically reduces average DSO for project revenue

5. SUBSCRIPTION/RETAINER MODELS:
   Problem: One-time sales revenue → lumpy cash flow
   Solution: Convert to monthly retainer or subscription where possible
   Gain: Recurring, predictable cash inflows; often pre-collected; reduces AR
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** The operating cycle and cash conversion cycle are not just analytical tools — they're the levers that companies use to compete. Amazon compressed its CCC to below zero, meaning its business operations generate cash rather than consuming it. This structural advantage means Amazon can fund growth from operations, requires less external capital, and has more resilience against downturns. For VN businesses, systematically improving CCC by 10 days per year compounds into significant competitive advantage over 5–10 years: more cash available, lower financing costs, better supplier and customer terms.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Focus only on P&L metrics in weekly/monthly reviews; skip cash flow discussion. Revenue, margin, and cost are important — but they're lagging indicators of business health. Cash flow is the coincident indicator (reflects what's actually happening right now). Teams that don't review cash flow weekly will be surprised monthly.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Treat cash management as purely a finance function. Warehouse manager's decision to accept a large unplanned shipment affects inventory and cash. Sales manager's decision to extend credit affects AR and cash. Production manager's overtime decision affects cash by the 5th of next month. Every operational decision has cash implications — cash management is everyone's responsibility.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Optimize for average cash position while ignoring daily or weekly volatility. A company with average cash 8 tỷ/month might still face a payroll crisis on the 1st when cash drops to 1.5 tỷ before the month's collections arrive. Smooth averages mask intra-month volatility. The 13-week weekly forecast specifically addresses this; monthly averages alone do not.
