### Sai Lầm Về Exit Strategy — Bán Công Ty Sai Cách Và Sai Thời Điểm

Exit — bán công ty; IPO; acquisition — là mục tiêu cuối của nhiều founder. Nhưng là topic mà founder VN nghĩ về quá ít; quá muộn; và thường với những hiểu lầm cơ bản. Kết quả: Bán khi không muốn bán; không bán được khi muốn; hoặc bán với terms tệ vì không chuẩn bị trước. Mỗi outcome này có thể avoid với strategy đúng đắn.

> **Bài học:** Exit không phải một event — nó là một process kéo dài nhiều năm của intentional building. Company được built để be acquired (hay IPO-ready) là different animal từ company built to run. Biết sớm mình muốn gì → Build toward it from day 1.

---

**Nhóm 1 — Timing Mistakes:**

```
SAI 28.1 — Bán khi đang khó; không khi đang mạnh:
  Tình huống: "Chúng tôi cần bán vì không raise được round tiếp theo"
           Buyer knows you're distressed → All negotiating power shifts to them
           Valuation: 50-70% của what you'd get in a position of strength
           Terms: Earn-out heavy; escrow large; founder locked for 3-5 years
  
  Best time to sell = When you don't NEED to
  □ Revenue growing 30%+ YoY
  □ Multiple interested parties creating competition
  □ Healthy balance sheet; no immediate funding need
  □ Business momentum clearly positive
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Think about exit 2-3 years before you want to exit
  □ Build exit optionality continuously: Don't paint yourself into a corner
  □ If approached while in strong position: At minimum; understand the opportunity even if not selling

SAI 28.2 — Không chuẩn bị "exit readiness":
  Tình huống: Received acquisition interest → "Great; let's talk" → Due diligence reveals:
           □ Financial records: Mess; 3 years un-audited
           □ Contracts: Customer contracts unsigned; verbal agreements
           □ IP: Some code written by contractor with unclear IP assignment
           □ Employee: BHXH not paid properly; no formal offer letters for 30% of team
           □ Legal: Shareholder agreement issues; cap table mess
           Kết quả: Deal falls through; or price reduced 30% to account for "hair"
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ "Exit readiness audit" annually: As if you were about to receive due diligence
  □ Clean books: Audited or audit-ready; professional accounting from Year 2+
  □ Contracts: Everything in writing; properly signed
  □ IP hygiene: IP assignment agreements with all contributors; open source license review
  □ Cap table: Clean; all paperwork filed; all conversions documented
  □ HR: BHXH; offer letters; employment contracts → All proper

SAI 28.3 — Không build relationships với potential acquirers early:
  Tình huống: "Muốn bán công ty → Reach out to potential buyers → First conversation"
           Buyers: Don't know company; no relationship; cold conversation
           Time to trust: 6-18 months minimum
           Vs. companies that cultivated relationships: Buyer already knows; trusts; values them
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Map potential acquirers: Who would logically want to buy us? Top 5-10 companies
  □ Build relationships: Attend same conferences; contribute to same communities
  □ Strategic partnerships: Pilot integrations; joint customer projects → Buyer sees value firsthand
  □ Advisor board: Include someone from potential acquirer ecosystem
```

**Nhóm 2 — Valuation và Deal Structure:**

```
SAI 28.4 — Không hiểu valuation methodology:
  What acquirers use (M&A; PE; strategic):
  □ Revenue multiple: 3-8× ARR for SaaS; 1-2× for services; 0.5-1× for distribution
  □ EBITDA multiple: 6-12× EBITDA for profitable businesses
  □ Strategic value: Sometimes pays above formula for capability; market; or team
  
  What drives premium:
  □ Growth rate: 40%+ YoY → Premium multiple
  □ Profitability: High margin → Better multiple
  □ Revenue quality: Recurring > transactional; high NRR > low
  □ Strategic fit: Fills specific gap for buyer → Strategic premium
  □ Competition: Multiple interested parties → Auction dynamic → Higher price
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Understand your valuation range before entering negotiation
  □ Advisor: M&A advisor (investment banker) to run competitive process
    Fee: 2-5% of transaction value → Usually earns fee through better price
  □ Know your walk-away price: Below which → Not worth it

SAI 28.5 — Focus on headline price; ignore structure:
  Tình huống: Agreed: 50 tỷ exit → "Great!"
           Structure: 20 tỷ at close; 30 tỷ earn-out over 3 years
           Earn-out conditions: Revenue targets very ambitious; vague trigger conditions
           Year 2: Buyer changes priorities; earn-out not paid despite founder's best effort
           Effective exit: 20 tỷ; not 50 tỷ
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Earn-out skepticism: Should only accept if targets are reasonable and within your control
  □ Earn-out structure: Clear metrics; clear measurement; dispute resolution mechanism
  □ Preferred deal: Maximum cash at close; minimum earn-out
  □ Escrow: Understand how much held; for what purpose; release conditions; typical amounts

SAI 28.6 — Không negotiate founder protections post-acquisition:
  Tình huống: Sold company; become "employee" of acquirer
           Month 6: Acquirer "reorganizes"; founder's team disbanded; product shelved
           Earn-out impossible to achieve; original vision killed
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Employment agreement: Negotiate separately from acquisition agreement
    □ Role; responsibility; reporting structure post-acquisition
    □ Decision authority on your product/team
    □ Minimum resource commitment from acquirer
  □ Earn-out triggers: Define what "reasonable support" means from acquirer for earn-out
  □ Good leaver/bad leaver: Under what conditions can you leave and still receive earn-out?
  □ IP reversal: If acquirer doesn't develop within X years; do you get IP back? (Rare but possible)
```

**Nhóm 3 — Founder Considerations:**

```
SAI 28.7 — Không align co-founders on exit expectations:
  Tình huống: Founder A: "Sell at 30 tỷ; happy to exit"
           Founder B: "Hold until 200 tỷ; want to build long-term"
           Received offer: 40 tỷ → A wants to take it; B doesn't
           Deadlock; board conflict; deal falls through; relationship damaged
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Exit alignment conversation: Annual discussion among founders về exit horizon và threshold
  □ Drag-along rights: Majority shareholder can drag minority into exit → Negotiate early
  □ Tag-along rights: If majority sells; minority can sell on same terms
  □ Liquidity preferences: Understand order of payments in exit for each share class
  □ Early discussions avoid late-stage surprises

SAI 28.8 — No secondary sale consideration:
  Tình huống: Founder holds all equity; zero liquidity after 7 years of work
           Cannot take any money off table until exit event
           Personal financial pressure → Making decisions to force exit prematurely
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Secondary sale: Some portion of founder shares sold to new investor or existing investor
    Allows: Founder takes some money off table; reduces financial pressure
  □ When to consider: Series B onwards; if company has strong trajectory
  □ Investor permission: Typically requires investor approval; negotiate right from early rounds
  □ Balance: Personal liquidity vs. signaling (too large secondary = Loss of faith from market)

SAI 28.9 — Post-exit planning không đủ:
  Tình huống: Sell company → "Free at last!" → Month 3: Lost; depressed; no purpose
           Sold company đã là identity; now identity gone
           Locked into earn-out at acquirer but unhappy → "Golden handcuff" misery
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Pre-exit planning: What will you do after? (Before; không after you've signed)
  □ Emotional preparation: Company exit is emotional event; not just financial
  □ Lock-up period: Understand duration (typically 1-3 years); what activities are restricted
  □ What brings purpose: Advisor roles; next venture; philanthropy; investing → Have a plan
  □ Financial planning: What to do with exit proceeds → Tax planning; investment strategy
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** The best exits don't happen by accident — they happen because founder built a company that was genuinely worth acquiring AND prepared the business for a transaction AND cultivated the right relationships AND understood what they wanted from an exit. All four are necessary. Missing any one: Either the deal doesn't happen; or it happens on bad terms; or it happens at the wrong time.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Treat exit as single event; không multi-year process. By the time a serious acquirer makes an offer; the value has already been created hoặc destroyed. Your deal outcome is largely determined by what you did 2-3 years before the offer; not by the negotiation itself. Plan early.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** DIY acquisition negotiation. "Tôi biết business của mình nhất; tôi sẽ negotiate." But: You don't know the buyer's alternatives; their internal politics; market comparables; or deal structure tricks. M&A advisor pays for itself. At minimum: Experienced lawyer với M&A background on every deal.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Emotional attachment blocking rational decision-making. "This company is my baby; I can't sell for less than 200 tỷ." Maybe justified. But: If offer is 60 tỷ today; and path to 200 tỷ requires 5 more years of uncertain work with significant downside risk — the math may favor taking 60. Decision should be based on risk-adjusted expected value; không on emotional anchor.
