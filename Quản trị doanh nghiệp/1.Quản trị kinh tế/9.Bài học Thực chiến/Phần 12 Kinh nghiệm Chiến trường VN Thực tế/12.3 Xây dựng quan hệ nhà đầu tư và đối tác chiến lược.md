### Xây Dựng Quan Hệ Nhà Đầu Tư Và Đối Tác Chiến Lược

Quan hệ với investor và strategic partners không bắt đầu khi bạn cần tiền hoặc cần partnership — nó bắt đầu từ trước đó rất lâu. Founder thành công nhất nhìn investor relationship như một long-term game; không phải transaction. "Warm introduction từ người quen" thường convert gấp 10× "cold pitch deck." Và strategic partnerships khi done right là compound returns; khi done wrong là distraction plus liability.

> **Bài học:** Raise capital từ investor đúng còn quan trọng hơn raise capital đủ. Wrong investor với bad terms và wrong expectation có thể destroy company dù bạn có good product và good team. Do your diligence on investors như investors do diligence on you.

  Size: 50–500+ tỷ VND
  Active players: Sequoia; Monk's Hill; Jungle; KB Investment; regional funds

**Investor Relations — Long Game:**

```
TRƯỚC KHI RAISE: BẮT ĐẦU XÂY RELATIONSHIP 12–18 THÁNG TRƯỚC
  
  Step 1: Map out target investors (20–30 names)
  □ Who has invested in similar companies?
  □ Who has deep expertise in your sector?
  □ Who in your network can intro you?
  
  Step 2: Get intros; have exploratory conversations (NOT pitching yet)
  "Tôi đang build trong [sector]; biết anh/chị expert — có thể xin 20 phút share feedback?"
  → Updates every 2–3 months: "Này; update tiến độ; không cần gì; chỉ muốn share"
  
  Step 3: When you're ready to raise → They know you; your progress; your team
  Decision is lower risk for them → Higher conversion; better terms

DURING RAISE:
  □ Create process: Set timeline; multiple investors in parallel; create urgency naturally
  □ Due diligence prep: Cap table; financials; contracts; team bios; references
  □ Know your numbers cold: ARR; MoM growth; CAC; LTV; churn; runway; headcount
  □ Know your ask: How much; at what valuation; use of funds (specific; not "working capital")
  
  Term sheet negotiation (đã cover ở Phần 7.3):
  □ Pre-money valuation; option pool dilution
  □ Liquidation preferences: 1× non-participating preferred = fair; 2× or participating = watch out
  □ Pro-rata rights; board composition; information rights; veto rights

AFTER RAISE:
  □ Board management: Prepare; don't waste board time; come with problems AND options
  □ Regular updates: Monthly investor letter (2 pages max): Wins; losses; asks
  □ Bad news early: Investors hate surprises. Good news they'll hear eventually anyway.
  □ Build 1:1 relationships with each board member; not just formal board meetings
  
  RULE: Treat investors like senior advisors; but remember: YOU are CEO; you make decisions
  Don't be paralyzed by board; but don't ignore board
```

**Strategic Partnerships — Khi Nào và Làm Sao:**

```
TYPES OF STRATEGIC PARTNERSHIPS:

1. DISTRIBUTION PARTNERSHIP:
  Partner has your target customers; you have product they need
  VD: Software vendor partnering với accounting firms to sell to their clients
  Structure: Referral fee (5–20%); or reseller discount (20–40%)
  Watch: Partner commitment level; conflict of interest with their other partners; exclusivity

2. TECHNOLOGY PARTNERSHIP:
  Integrate với partner's platform/product → Deeper value for shared customers
  VD: HR software integrating với payroll provider
  Structure: API integration; data sharing agreement; joint marketing
  Watch: Data security; liability; platform risk (nếu partner changes API/pricing)

3. CO-MARKETING PARTNERSHIP:
  Access each other's audiences
  VD: B2B tool co-hosting webinar với complementary tool
  Structure: Split costs; cross-promotion; shared leads
  Watch: Brand fit; audience overlap; execution commitment from both sides

4. INVESTMENT + PARTNERSHIP (Strategic investor):
  Partner also becomes investor
  Pros: Aligned incentive; capital + distribution
  Cons: Complexity; potential conflict if partnership underperforms
  
PARTNERSHIP NEGOTIATION:
  □ Value must flow BOTH ways — sustainable partnerships are win-win; not just win-lose
  □ Define success metrics upfront: What does "successful partnership" look like after 12 months?
  □ Review period: "Let's pilot for 6 months; then decide whether to formalize"
  □ Exit clause: How do we end partnership if it's not working?

VN-SPECIFIC PARTNERSHIP DYNAMICS:
  □ Enterprise partnerships often need executive-level blessing; not just operational agreement
  □ Exclusivity demands common — push back or time-limit ("6-month exclusive; then review")
  □ Follow-through gap: Partner excited to sign; less excited to execute → Check in regularly
  □ Government partnership: Slow; complex; but can provide legitimacy và access that's hard to get otherwise
```

**Red Flags Khi Chọn Investor và Partner:**

```
INVESTOR RED FLAGS:
  ❌ Vague về investment thesis hoặc portfolio track record
  ❌ No references available from previous portfolio founders
  ❌ Demanding excessive controls for small check (board seat cho 500M VND angel round?)
  ❌ Unresponsive during diligence (if slow now; will be slow when you need them later)
  ❌ "We invest in the founder; not the company" = No accountability; sounds nice; means little
  ❌ Negotiate aggressively on every term; no relationship spirit
  ❌ Liquidation preference > 1×; participating preferred = Extremely investor-friendly; founder loses
  
PARTNER RED FLAGS:
  ❌ All talk; no concrete commitment: "We'll definitely send you leads" với no structure
  ❌ More interest in your data/customer list than in mutual value creation
  ❌ Asking for exclusivity without clear value they're providing
  ❌ References từ previous partners negative; or unavailable
  ❌ Decision making takes forever → Not really committed
  ❌ "We'll figure out the details later" on key commercial terms → Figure out NOW
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Founder-investor relationship là long-term marriage; không phải one-night stand. Bạn sẽ làm việc với investor này qua khủng hoảng; qua thành công; qua hard decisions. Chemistry; values alignment; và communication style fit matters as much as check size. A smaller check from the right investor beats a larger check from the wrong one.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Treat every investor meeting như pitch; not like discovery conversation. Best investor meetings feel like collaborative problem-solving; not performance. Investors invest in people they trust; and trust builds in genuine conversation; not polished decks.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Accept first term sheet out of fear. "Chỉ có một term sheet; phải accept." Create competition — even artificial urgency through process management. First term sheet rarely the best term sheet. Take time (reasonable) to explore options.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Strategic partnership với competitor-adjacent companies. "They're in adjacent space; not direct competitor." But if they acquire competitor OR if their product evolves into your space — suddenly you've given them data; customer relationships; and access. Define exclusivity; data usage; and competitive use restrictions explicitly in any partnership agreement.
