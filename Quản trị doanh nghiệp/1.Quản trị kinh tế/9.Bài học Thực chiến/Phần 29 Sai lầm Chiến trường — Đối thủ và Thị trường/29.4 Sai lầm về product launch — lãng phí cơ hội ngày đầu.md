### Sai Lầm Về Product Launch — Lãng Phí Cơ Hội Ngày Đầu

Product launch là moment mà team đã work toward hàng tháng hoặc hàng năm — và là moment mà nhiều startup VN làm không tốt nhất. Launch không phải là "ngày product live" — launch là một coordinated event để tạo maximum awareness; maximum first impression; và maximum learning signal in minimum time. Làm sai ở đây: Waste momentum; miss first impression; và tạo ra patterns khó thay đổi sau.

> **Bài học:** Launch là chiến lược; không phải milestone. "Ship and hope" không phải launch. Launch là: Right audience + Right message + Right moment + Right follow-through = Compounding momentum.

---

**Nhóm 1 — Pre-Launch Failures:**

```
SAI 19.1 — Launch khi product chưa đủ stable:
  Tình huống: "Đã delay đủ rồi; launch đi; fix bugs sau"
           Launch → Bugs; crashes; data loss → First impression: "App tệ; bug nhiều"
           Reviews: 1-2 sao trên App Store → Hard to recover
           Customers who tried: Unlikely to give second chance
  
  VN market đặc biệt: Bad reviews spread fast via Zalo groups; Facebook communities
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Launch criteria: Defined minimum bar; không launch without passing
    □ Core flow: Works without critical bug 99% of time
    □ Load test: Handle expected Day 1 traffic (prepare for 5× normal)
    □ Support ready: Team and process for handling complaints
    □ Rollback plan: Can you revert if major issue?
  □ Soft launch first: 100-500 users; fix issues; then full launch
  □ Better: 2 weeks late with good product vs. on-time with broken product

SAI 19.2 — Không build pre-launch audience:
  Tình huống: Build product 12 months in secret → Launch day → Nobody knows about it
           Announce on Facebook → 200 likes → 5 signups
           "Marketing team didn't do their job"
  
  Thực tế: Marketing's job starts long before launch
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Build in public: Share progress updates; behind-the-scenes; early insights
    Audience builds curiosity và investment in your success
  □ Waitlist: Collect emails 2-3 months before launch
    Even 500 waitlist = 500 people who care enough to follow up
  □ Beta users: 20-50 power users who test; give feedback; become advocates
  □ Press: Reach out to tech journalists; startup bloggers 4-6 weeks before launch
    (Not day of launch — they have editorial calendar; need lead time)

SAI 19.3 — Không có launch day coordination:
  Tình huống: Launch day: Team send emails at different times; social posts scattered
           No coordinated moment of launch → No collective excitement
           One team pushes "live"; another team hasn't prepared customer support
           Sales team doesn't know what launch promotions to offer
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Launch war room: All hands on deck on launch day
  □ Countdown communication: What happens at each hour
  □ Launch checklist (T-7 days through T+7 days):
    □ T-7: Press embargo sent; email sequence ready; social content scheduled
    □ T-3: Final performance test; support team briefed; FAQs ready
    □ T-1: All team knows their role; final go/no-go check
    □ T-0: Coordinated push — email; social; press release; SMS; community posts
    □ T+1: Monitor; respond to all feedback; fix critical issues immediately
    □ T+7: First week review; what worked; what didn't; plan Week 2 push
```

**Nhóm 2 — Message và Positioning Failures:**

```
SAI 19.4 — Feature launch thay vì story launch:
  Tình huống: Launch announcement: "Ra mắt phiên bản 2.0 với 47 tính năng mới:
           Tính năng A; tính năng B; tính năng C; tính năng D..."
           Reader: "Oke; cool" → Scroll past → No action
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Launch a story; not a feature list
  □ "Who is this for? What problem does it solve? Why now?"
  □ One hero message: What is THE most important thing about this launch?
  □ Customer-centric: "You can now [do X] instead of [painful Y]"
  □ Evidence: Customer quote; data point; demo video showing transformation

SAI 19.5 — Launch message không clear về CTAs:
  Tình huống: Beautiful launch email → 5 CTAs: "Try now; Learn more; Watch video; Share; Follow us"
           User: Confused → Click nothing → Delete
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ One primary CTA per message
  □ Clear action: "Start free trial" → Not "Explore our platform"
  □ Lower friction: Fewer steps between "read launch email" và "using product"
    (Pre-filled signup; magic link; immediate access)

SAI 19.6 — Không segment launch message:
  Tình huống: One launch email to all: Prospects; current users; lapsed users; partners
           Same message → Not relevant for anyone perfectly
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Segment launch communications:
    □ Current users: "Here's what's new for you; how to use it"
    □ Lapsed users: "We've improved X; Y; Z — come back and try"
    □ Prospects: "Here's why now is the best time to try"
    □ Partners: "How does this affect our collaboration"
  □ Different channels for different segments:
    In-app notification; email; SMS; Zalo OA; push notification — each has its audience
```

**Nhóm 3 — Post-Launch Failures:**

```
SAI 19.7 — "Tung xong; quên":
  Tình huống: Launch week: Excited; active; monitoring
           Week 2: Team moves on to next feature
           Launch momentum: Dissipates; press follows up but nobody responds
           Review sites: Getting questions; nobody answers
           First users: Need support; nobody follows up → Churns
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Post-launch plan: Weeks 2-4 as important as Week 1
  □ Press follow-up: Reply to every media inquiry for 4 weeks post-launch
  □ User outreach: Personal email to every new user in first 2 weeks (if < 100)
  □ Feedback loop: Weekly synthesis of user feedback; quick fixes shipped in first 30 days
  □ Momentum reinforcement: Week 2 angle; Week 3 customer success story; Week 4 milestone

SAI 19.8 — Không capture learnings từ launch:
  Tình huống: Launch → Some metrics → Move on
           "We got 1,000 signups" → Not: "Of 1,000 signups; where did they come from?
           What messaging converted best? What channels worked? Who churned in Day 1?
           What questions did support get most? What bugs were reported most?"
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Launch post-mortem at Day 7 và Day 30:
    □ Acquisition: Channel breakdown; conversion rates; cost per acquisition
    □ Activation: % who completed onboarding; where drop-off happened
    □ Retention: Day 1; Day 7 retention
    □ Revenue: First paying users; trial-to-paid rate
    □ Qualitative: What did users say; what did press write; what support questions came
  □ This data feeds directly into: Product roadmap; marketing optimization; messaging improvement

SAI 19.9 — Launch trong thời điểm sai:
  Tình huống: Launch day before Tết holiday → Most decision makers on vacation
           Launch during competitor's big announcement → Media attention elsewhere
           Launch on Monday morning → Weekend burnout readership
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Calendar optimization: Tuesday-Thursday launches get most attention
  □ Avoid: Major holidays; competitor earnings; sector-wide conferences (conflict or opportunity?)
  □ VN-specific: Tránh: Dịp Tết; nghỉ lễ 30/4-1/5; họp Đảng; major political events
    (Media coverage; decision-maker availability both affected)
  □ Leverage: Post-Tết excitement (February); back-to-school (August); budget season (Q4)
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Trong 1-2 tuần đầu sau launch; mọi tín hiệu đều quantifiable: Từng channel; từng message; từng conversion step. Đây là information density cao nhất mà bạn sẽ có về product-market fit và positioning. Founders who treat launch as purely a "ship" event miss this intelligence gold mine. Treat launch as an intelligence-gathering operation as much as a growth event.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Benchmark launch success against vanity metrics: Total signups; press mentions; social shares. These feel good but don't tell you if you've launched something people will pay for. Better metrics: Trial-to-paid conversion; Day 7 retention; NPS từ first users; support ticket types.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Launch too many things simultaneously. "Version 2.0 with major redesign AND new mobile app AND new pricing." Three narratives = No clear narrative. Pick one story per launch. Save other announcements for their own moments.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Build for months; launch once; declare it failed. Many products need 2-3 launch attempts as positioning and messaging is refined. Gmail was "invitation only" for 2 years — not because of technical constraint; but to manage growth and message premium. Launching is a skill that improves; not a one-time event.
