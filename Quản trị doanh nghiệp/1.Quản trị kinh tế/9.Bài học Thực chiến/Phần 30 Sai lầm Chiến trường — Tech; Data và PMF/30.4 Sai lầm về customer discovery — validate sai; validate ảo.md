### Sai Lầm Về Customer Discovery — Validate Sai; Validate Ảo

Customer discovery là quá trình mà founder dành thời gian hiểu khách hàng trước khi build — và là quá trình được làm sai nhất trong startup ecosystem VN. Không phải vì không được làm — mà vì được làm sai cách. Kết quả: "Validated" nhưng thực ra là "confirmed your own assumptions." Product được build → Nobody wants it. Tiền và thời gian: Gone.

> **Bài học:** Customer discovery tốt là counter-intuitive: Mục tiêu là DIS-confirm your assumptions; không confirm chúng. Bạn phải cố để không bị validate — và nếu khách hàng vẫn validate → Đó mới là real signal.

---

**Nhóm 1 — Leading Questions và Confirmation Bias:**

```
SAI 23.1 — Hỏi leading questions:
  Phổ biến nhất:
  "Anh/chị có nghĩ sản phẩm này sẽ hữu ích không?" → "Có" (polite)
  "Anh/chị có muốn có tính năng X không?" → "Có" (hypothetical future)
  "Nếu có ứng dụng giúp làm X; anh/chị có dùng không?" → "Có" (hypothetical)
  
  These questions are WORTHLESS for validation
  
  Giải pháp — Better questions:
  "Hãy kể cho tôi nghe lần gần nhất bạn gặp vấn đề [X]?"
  "Bạn hiện tại đang làm gì để giải quyết [problem]?"
  "Bạn có thể show tôi quy trình hiện tại của bạn không?"
  "Điều gì frustrating nhất về cách bạn đang làm bây giờ?"
  
  Key: Ask about PAST behavior; không FUTURE hypotheticals
       People are good at describing what they've done; bad at predicting future behavior

SAI 23.2 — Presenter bias: Show product; ask for feedback:
  Tình huống: Show mockup hoặc working demo → "Anh/chị thấy thế nào?"
           Customer: See solution before problem is validated
           Their reaction: Evaluate solution; không confirm problem severity
           They may think "This is nice" about a problem they don't actually have severely
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Problem validation BEFORE solution validation
  □ Sequence: (1) Is this a real problem? How painful? How frequent? (2) Then: Is our solution a good fit?
  □ "The Mom Test" principle: Don't mention your solution until you've understood problem deeply
  □ Solution validation: "Here's what we're thinking. What would prevent you from using this?"
    (Forces criticism; không just politeness)

SAI 23.3 — Interview mostly users who will be positive:
  Tình huống: Interview: 5 customers who gave beta feedback; 3 team members; 2 investors
           10 interviews done → "Validated"
           Reality: These are the most supportive people in your ecosystem → Not representative
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Interview mix: 50% current/potential customers; 25% people who tried and said no; 25% non-users
  □ Target skeptics: People who likely would NOT buy → Their objections are most valuable
  □ Random sampling: Don't cherry-pick positive-leaning people
  □ "Null hypothesis" mindset: Default assumption = "This product is NOT needed"
    Try to disprove your own assumption
```

**Nhóm 2 — Scope và Depth Errors:**

```
SAI 23.4 — Quá ít interviews để conclude:
  Tình huống: 3-5 interviews → "Validated! Build it!"
           3 people is not statistical; không even anecdotal
           High variance: 3 friends all agree = No information
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Minimum: 20-30 interviews across 2-3 different customer segments
  □ Pattern emergence: Themes should repeat independently before drawing conclusions
  □ Stop when: Same patterns repeat without new insights (typically 15-25 interviews)
  □ "20 interviews saying the same thing" > "100 survey responses"

SAI 23.5 — Không phân biệt pain levels:
  Tình huống: Interviewing 20 people about problem → "Mọi người đều có vấn đề này"
           Không phân biệt: Person A: "Thỉnh thoảng annoying" vs. Person B: "Costs me 10 hours/week"
           Build product assuming all 20 are Person B → Actual market = mostly Person A
           Low WTP; low urgency; slow adoption
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Pain scoring: Explicitly ask: "How often does this happen? How much time/money does it cost?"
  □ JTBD (Jobs to be Done): "What are you actually trying to accomplish?"
  □ ICP identification: Which interviewees had severe pain? Build for THEM → not average

SAI 23.6 — Talk to wrong people:
  Tình huống: Product for CFOs → Interview: Junior finance staff
           "Everybody has this problem" → CFOs actually don't have same priority
           Build product → CFOs don't care → Can't sell
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Precisely define: Who will buy (economic buyer) + Who will use (end user) + Who influences (champion)
  □ These can be different people → Interview ALL three roles
  □ B2B: Interview at right seniority level for each decision role
  □ "Get to the economic buyer" in interviews same as in sales
```

**Nhóm 3 — Validation Vs. Exploration:**

```
SAI 23.7 — Validation stage không đúng thứ tự:
  Wrong order: Solution (build) → Problem (discover) → What happened?
  Right order: Problem (discover) → Solution (design) → Build
  
  Thực tế tại VN: Many founders have solution first (often from their own experience as user)
              Nothing wrong with this IF they then rigorously validate problem exists for market
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Even if your solution comes first: Separate problem validation từ solution pitch
  □ Before building: "I think the problem is [X]. Let me talk to 20 potential customers to confirm."
  □ Discovery questions: Do not pitch the solution; explore the problem space

SAI 23.8 — Không test willingness to pay:
  Tình huống: 30 interviews; all confirmed the problem is real và painful
           Build product → Launch → Charge 500k/tháng → Nobody buys
           "But everyone said they had this problem!"
           Đúng. Having problem ≠ Willing to pay that price to solve it.
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ WTP test during discovery: "Nếu có giải pháp tốt; bạn sẽ trả bao nhiêu?"
  □ Fake door test: Pricing page với payment option before product exists
    → Who clicks "Buy" / "Sign Up" = Real intent (not polite yes)
  □ Pre-sales: "We're launching next month. Want to pre-pay at discount?"
    → Yes with payment = Validated WTP

SAI 23.9 — Discovery kết thúc sau launch:
  Tình huống: Discovery in pre-product phase → Launch → Discovery stops
           "Chúng tôi đã validated; giờ build và sell"
           Reality: Market evolves; needs change; new segments emerge
           Customers who were good fit 12 months ago may no longer be
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Ongoing discovery: 5-10 customer conversations per month; permanently
  □ New customer interview: Every new significant customer → "Tell me about before you found us"
  □ Churned customer interview: "Tell me about why you stopped"
  □ "Voice of customer" as continuous process; không one-time phase
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Tất cả các mistake trong customer discovery đều xuất phát từ một bias chung: We want to be right. We want our product to be needed. This desire distorts every interaction — we ask questions that confirm; we interpret ambiguous signals positively; we talk to people most likely to agree. The antidote is artificial pessimism: "I'm going to assume this product is wrong until proven otherwise. I'm going to try my hardest to find the person who will tell me no." That mindset creates real learning.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Confuse "customer discovery" với "market research report." Reports tell you about averages và categories. Discovery tells you about specific people's specific pains và workflows. "The market is 50 billion" doesn't help you build a product. "Here's exactly what Maria does every Tuesday morning and why it's painful" does.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Stop discovery khi mọi thứ going well. Paradox: Discovery is most valuable when you think you already know the answer. "We know our customers" = Dangerous assumption for a product that evolves. Best companies do discovery continuously even post-PMF because they know understanding shifts.

> **Cạm bẫy:** 100% quantitative validation. "We did surveys; 73% said they want this." Surveys tell you what people think they want. Interviews tell you what they actually do and struggle with. Both have value — but for early validation; qualitative depth >> quantitative breadth. The richness of "tell me what you did last time" cannot be captured in a checkbox survey.
