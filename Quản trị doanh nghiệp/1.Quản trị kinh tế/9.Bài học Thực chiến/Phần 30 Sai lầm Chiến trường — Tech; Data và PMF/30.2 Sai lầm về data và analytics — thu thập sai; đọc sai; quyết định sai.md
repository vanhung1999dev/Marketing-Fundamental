### Sai Lầm Về Data Và Analytics — Thu Thập Sai; Đọc Sai; Quyết Định Sai

"Data-driven" là slogan của mọi startup hiện đại — nhưng nhiều startup VN "data-driven" theo nghĩa họ có dashboard đẹp và họ refer đến numbers trong meetings. Thực tế: Data họ track sai; metrics họ đo không connect với business outcomes; và decisions vẫn được đưa ra từ gut feeling nhưng được justified bằng cherry-picked data. Đây là bức tranh thực tế.

> **Bài học:** Bad data is worse than no data. Bad data tạo ra illusion of certainty; dẫn đến confident decisions trên false premises. No data ít nhất buộc bạn admit uncertainty. Good data practice: Understand what you're measuring; why; and what decisions it informs.

---

**Nhóm 1 — Vanity Metrics và Wrong Metrics:**

```
SAI 21.1 — Optimize cho vanity metrics:
  Tình huống: "Chúng tôi có 100,000 users!" (Monthly Active = 5,000)
           "Chúng tôi có 50,000 downloads!" (Daily Active = 800; paying = 120)
           "Chúng tôi có 30% MoM growth!" (Base was 10 users; growing by 3 users/month)
  
  Vanity metrics look good in press releases; không trong P&L
  
  Common vanity metrics in VN startup ecosystem:
  □ Registered users (vs. active users; paying users)
  □ Total downloads (vs. installs; active installs)
  □ Page views (vs. meaningful engagement)
  □ Social followers (vs. engaged followers; leads generated)
  □ GMV gross (vs. net revenue; margin)
  □ "Users" (vs. which type of user matters for your model?)
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ North Star Metric: ONE metric that best captures value delivered to customers
    (Not downloads; not signups — something that shows actual value in use)
    Ví dụ: Airbnb = Nights booked; Uber = Trips completed; Slack = DAU
  □ Metric selection: "Does improving this metric directly lead to better business outcomes?"

SAI 21.2 — Không phân biệt correlation và causation:
  Tình huống: "Ngày mưa; doanh số tăng → Bán thêm vào mùa mưa!"
           (Reality: Mưa → Người ở nhà → Shop online more)
           Scaled up rain-based promotions → No effect; lost money
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Correlation doesn't imply causation: Always ask "mechanism?"
  □ Controlled experiments: A/B test before scaling any insight
  □ Multi-variable confusion: In business; many things change simultaneously
    True causation requires controlling other variables
  □ Questions to ask: "What else changed at the same time?" "Is there another explanation?"

SAI 21.3 — Dashboard tốt; decision không đổi:
  Tình huống: Company builds beautiful Tableau dashboard
           Every Monday: Leadership looks at it; nods; discusses
           6 months later: Same decisions being made; same gut feelings
           "Có data nhưng không thay đổi gì"
  
  Data without decision change = Expensive decoration
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Data → Decision protocol: Every metric in dashboard: "What action do we take if this metric is [X]?"
  □ Pre-defined thresholds: "If churn > 5%/month → Trigger customer success investigation"
  □ Decision log: Track decisions made; what data informed them; what outcomes happened
  □ Data skeptics: Have someone whose job is to challenge data interpretations
```

**Nhóm 2 — Data Collection Mistakes:**

```
SAI 21.4 — Không track user behavior; chỉ track transactions:
  Tình huống: E-commerce: Track orders; revenue; GMV
           Not tracking: Where users drop off in checkout; which products browsed không mua;
                         how long they stay on pages; what they search for
           Kết quả: Know WHAT happens; không know WHY
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Behavior analytics: Mixpanel; Amplitude; Firebase Analytics
  □ Funnel tracking: Every step from acquisition to retention; with drop-off at each step
  □ Session recording: Hotjar; FullStory → Watch real user sessions (5-10/week) → More valuable than aggregates
  □ Event taxonomy: Define and track meaningful user actions; không just page views

SAI 21.5 — Sampling bias trong research:
  Tình huống: Customer survey → 200 responses; 45% positive
           Reality: Only currently happy customers responded; churned customers didn't
           "45% positive" → Actually: "45% of remaining customers are positive"
           True satisfaction across all customers (including churned): Much lower
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Survey design: Who responds? Survivorship bias? Self-selection bias?
  □ Churned customer interviews: Most valuable feedback you'll get; hardest to get
  □ Representative samples: Define who you want to hear from; engineer getting that sample
  □ Response rate: Low response rate → High selection bias

SAI 21.6 — Không có baseline measurements:
  Tình huống: Run marketing campaign → "Revenue went up 20% → Campaign worked!"
           No control: Was it seasonal? Was it the campaign? Was it something else?
           No pre-campaign baseline; no control group
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Baseline before any experiment: Measure 2-4 weeks of normal before changing anything
  □ A/B testing: Test vs. control; simultaneous; same period
  □ Holdout groups: Small % of customers who don't see change → True counterfactual
  □ Incrementality: Not just "did sales go up" but "how much sales came from THIS action"

SAI 21.7 — Data silos — marketing data không connect với sales data:
  Tình huống: Marketing: "Chúng tôi generated 500 leads this month"
           Sales: "Tôi closed 10 deals from 100 qualified leads"
           Finance: "Revenue 2 tỷ this month"
           Nobody knows: Of those 500 marketing leads → Which ones became the 10 deals?
                          What was marketing ROI by channel? What's LTV by acquisition channel?
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ CRM as single source of truth: Every lead; every touch point; every deal; all in one place
  □ UTM tracking: Every marketing link → Track source through to conversion
  □ Attribution model: Last touch; first touch; linear → Agree on one; apply consistently
  □ Weekly revenue operations review: Marketing + Sales + Finance data together
```

**Nhóm 3 — Analytical Mistakes:**

```
SAI 21.8 — Average hides the story:
  Tình huống: "Average order value: 500k" → Sounds stable
           Reality: 80% of orders are 100-200k; 20% of orders are 1.5-3M
           Average skewed by large outliers; median = 180k
           Decision based on average: Wrong product and pricing strategy
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Always check distribution; không just average
  □ Median often more informative than mean for skewed data
  □ Segmented analysis: AOV by customer segment; by channel; by time period
  □ P50/P90/P99 (percentiles): "50% of customers have LTV < X; top 10% have LTV > Y"

SAI 21.9 — Small sample size; big conclusions:
  Tình huống: "20 user tests confirmed our hypothesis" → "We have validated product-market fit"
           "3 enterprise deals show our sales model works" → "We've cracked enterprise sales"
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Sample size humility: Always state sample size; acknowledge limitations
  □ Statistical significance: For quantitative decisions; N needs to be sufficient
    (Rule of thumb: > 100 observations for percentage conclusions; > 30 for mean)
  □ Confidence interval: "We're 80% confident that..." rather than "data shows..."
  □ Replication: Does the pattern hold in different time periods? Different segments?

SAI 21.10 — Không học từ churned customers:
  Tình huống: Focus analytics entirely on retained; active; happy customers
           Churned customers: Analyzed only as "churn rate %"
           Miss: Why they churned; what alternative they moved to; what would have kept them
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Exit interviews: Every significant churned customer → 15-minute call
    Ask: "What drove the decision? What are you using instead? What could we have done?"
  □ Churn reasons taxonomy: Category buckets; track trends over time
  □ Cohort analysis: Which customer segments churn fastest? Common characteristics?
  □ Product-churn correlation: Which actions (or absence) predict churn? Build leading indicators.
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Data maturity at startups follows a predictable path: First, tracking nothing → then tracking everything → then drowning in data → eventually, tracking the right things. Most startups in VN are somewhere between "tracking nothing" and "tracking everything." The jump to "tracking the right things" requires asking: "What decision does this data inform?" For every metric you track. If there's no answer, that metric shouldn't be on your dashboard.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** "We'll set up analytics properly later." Later never comes. The cost of retroactively implementing analytics on historical data is enormous. Set up baseline analytics on Day 1. Even basic: Google Analytics; GA4; Mixpanel free tier; and a basic cohort tracking spreadsheet.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Data analyst không có business context. Analyst produces report; business reads it without understanding methodology. Then business misinterprets; makes bad decisions. Fix: Analyst presents context AND data. "This is what we measured; this is the limitation; this is what I think it means for [specific decision]."

> **Cạm bẫy:** Analysis paralysis. "We need more data before deciding." Sometimes true. Often: An excuse to delay a decision that's uncomfortable. Good framework: "What is the cost of being wrong if we decide now vs. 2 weeks from now?" If cost is low and delay is costly → Decide now. More data is only worth getting if it would meaningfully change the decision.
