### Sai Lầm Về PMF — Khi Nghĩ Mình Có Mà Thực Ra Chưa

Product-Market Fit (PMF) là khái niệm mà ai trong startup cũng nhắc đến nhưng rất ít người biết chính xác khi nào mình có nó. Nguy hiểm nhất: Founder nghĩ mình đã có PMF và bắt đầu scale — trong khi thực ra chỉ có "PMF illusion" từ vài users tốt; từ thị trường nhỏ chưa đủ representative; hoặc từ retention tốt cho lý do sai. Scale mà không có real PMF = Đốt tiền rất nhanh.

> **Bài học:** PMF không phải binary switch — "on" hoặc "off." Đó là spectrum từ "zero signal" đến "undeniable pull." Bạn biết mình chưa có PMF khi phải explain và convince. Bạn biết mình có khi bạn đang cố kìm hãm growth để không drown. Most startups sống ở middle — "some signal; unclear picture."

---

**Nhóm 1 — False PMF Signals:**

```
SAI 22.1 — Confuse politeness với validation:
  Tình huống: Show product to friends; colleagues; family → "Hay đấy; tôi sẽ dùng thử"
           Founder: "Validated! Everyone likes it!"
           Reality: People are polite; especially to people they like
           Real test: Will they pay? Will they change behavior for it? Will they tell others?
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Talk to strangers; không chỉ friends and family
  □ "Would you use it?" → Weak signal
  □ "Would you pay $X for it?" → Stronger signal
  □ "Would you sign up for beta waitlist?" → Even stronger
  □ "Here's link to sign up right now" → Strongest; reveals real commitment

SAI 22.2 — Early adopter enthusiasm ≠ Mass market PMF:
  Tình huống: First 100 users: Technology enthusiasts; early adopters; startup ecosystem people
           They love it; engagement great; NPS = 75
           Founder: "PMF achieved! Scale!"
           Scale to broader market: Conversion → Terrible; retention → Bad; CAC → 10×
  
  Thực tế: Early adopters và mainstream market có fundamentally different needs
  Early adopters: Love novelty; tolerant of rough edges; minimal hand-holding needed
  Mainstream: Need polish; need social proof; need clear ROI; need hand-holding
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Profile your early users: Are they representative of your target market?
  □ "Chasm" test: What % of your users would describe themselves as mainstream/conservative?
  □ Expand test: Before scaling; add 100 users from mainstream profile → What happens to metrics?

SAI 22.3 — Retention từ lock-in; không từ value:
  Tình huống: Company retention rate: 90% monthly
           "Strong PMF signal!"
           Reality: Customers stay because: High switching cost; data stored in platform;
                     contractual obligation; nobody uses it but nobody wants the process of migrating
           NPS: 12 (neutral/negative) → They stay but don't like it
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Test retention type: "If it were easy to switch; would you?" (Hypothetical)
  □ "Disappointed if product disappeared?" survey (Sean Ellis test)
    > 40% "very disappointed" → PMF. 25-40% → Weak signal. < 25% → No PMF.
  □ Referral rate: Are customers telling others? Organic referral = PMF signal
  □ Usage frequency: Are they using more over time? Or constant/declining?

SAI 22.4 — Confuse market conditions với product PMF:
  Tình huống: COVID-19 boom: Every e-commerce; delivery; online meeting tool grew 300%
           Founder: "Strong PMF → Scale!"
           Post-COVID: Growth flatlines; some products decline
           "Chúng tôi đã có PMF; rồi market changed"
  
  Thực tế: Rising tide lifted all boats. Product didn't have PMF; market had tailwind.
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ "Normalize" for market conditions: "Is our growth relative to market growing or shrinking?"
  □ Net Promoter Score doesn't care about market conditions → More stable signal
  □ Cohort retention normalized for macro conditions
  □ Post-boom test: Do users stay when alternatives emerge?
```

**Nhóm 2 — PMF in Wrong Segment:**

```
SAI 22.5 — PMF trong segment quá nhỏ để build company on:
  Tình huống: "Chúng tôi có incredible PMF với CFOs của công ty 50-200 người ở Hà Nội"
           NPS 80; net revenue retention 130%; churn 2% monthly
           Market size: ~2,000 companies → Total revenue ceiling = 20 tỷ/năm if 100% market share
           VC investment required? This business doesn't support VC returns
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ TAM validation BEFORE product development và fundraising
  □ PMF in a niche: Real and valuable IF: (a) Large enough → Build sustainable business
                                           (b) Wedge into larger adjacent market
  □ Expand adjacencies: Can this PMF be the wedge to reach a larger segment?

SAI 22.6 — PMF với wrong customers (high service; low willingness to pay):
  Tình huống: SME customers: Love product; engage daily; NPS 65
           But: WTP (willingness to pay) = 200-500k/tháng maximum
           CAC = 2 triệu; LTV = 3 triệu; margin thin
           Scale: Need 10,000 customers to be meaningful → Expensive to acquire + serve
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ WTP discovery: Ask early → "What would you pay for this?"
  □ Unit economics test before scale: "At this price; at this CAC; at this churn → Will business work?"
  □ Segment shift consideration: Same product; different segment → Different price point

SAI 22.7 — Không biết WHEN PMF shifts (product evolves; market shifts):
  Tình huống: Had PMF in 2020; product và market has shifted by 2023
           Team still operating as if 2020 PMF is valid
           Retention declining; CAC increasing → "Marketing execution problem"
           Actually: PMF degraded; needs re-validation
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ PMF re-assessment: Annual check; minimum
  □ "Disappointed" survey: Run quarterly; track trend over time
  □ Cohort retention trend: New cohorts retaining better or worse than old ones?
  □ Win rate on new deals: Improving or declining? Declining may signal product-market drift
```

**Nhóm 3 — Tìm PMF Đúng Cách:**

```
SAI 22.8 — Pivot liên tục mà không học từ signal:
  Tình huống: No PMF signal → "Pivot!"
           New product; new pivot → Still no signal → "Pivot again!"
           Year 2: 3 different products; no PMF in any; cash running out
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Pivot vs. persist decision framework:
    Persist: You have partial signal; you know what to improve; path is clear
    Pivot: No signal after genuine attempt; market evidence suggests different direction
  □ "Genuine attempt": At least 3-6 months; 100+ customers engaged with core value prop
  □ Document learnings from each product: What signal did we get? What did we learn?
  □ Pivot should be informed; không just "ran out of ideas on current path"

SAI 22.9 — Không measure core value delivery:
  Tình huống: Track signups; DAU; revenue — không track if product actually delivering core value
           Example (expense management app): Track users logging in; không track if expenses being managed faster
           Users: Using app but not experiencing core value → Churn inevitable
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Define core value metric: "What specific behavior indicates customer is getting value?"
  □ Time-to-value: How long từ signup đến first experience of core value?
  □ Value milestone: % of users reaching core value milestone trong first X days
  □ Correlation: Does core value metric correlate with retention? It should.
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** PMF validation is not a phase you complete and move on from — it's a continuous obligation. Market evolves; product evolves; competition changes; customer needs shift. The best companies treat PMF assessment as an ongoing practice: Regular customer conversations; cohort analysis; NPS tracking; win/loss review. They don't wait for declining metrics to tell them PMF has eroded.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** "Chúng tôi có revenue → Có PMF." Revenue is necessary but not sufficient for PMF. You can have revenue from customers who tolerate your product without loving it. PMF is about: Do customers love you? Would they miss you? Do they pull others in?

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** "Investors say we have PMF." Investors making investment decisions don't have visibility into your retention cohorts; your real NPS; or your churned customer interviews. They see a story và metrics in a pitch deck. Only you have the full picture. Trust your own data; not their perception of it.

> **Cạm bẫy:** PMF as excuse. "We don't have PMF yet → Don't need to optimize revenue; operations; team." Pre-PMF doesn't mean operational chaos is acceptable. Process; execution discipline; và financial management still matter. "Finding PMF" is not a blank check for inefficiency.
