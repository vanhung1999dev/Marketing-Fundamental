### Sai Lầm Về Technical Decisions — Stack; Architecture; Outsource

Technical decisions trong startup không chỉ là "engineer problem" — chúng là business decisions với long tails. Chọn sai tech stack: Lock in cho 5 năm. Outsource sai: Mất 6 tháng + 1 tỷ. Microservices quá sớm: 3x team complexity khi có 2 engineers. Những sai lầm này phổ biến nhất ở non-technical founders và founders với strong technical background nhưng enterprise mindset.

> **Bài học:** Good technical decisions at startup stage: Simple; boring; proven. Bad technical decisions: Clever; cutting-edge; complex. Optimize for speed-to-learn; not architectural purity. Refactor khi có evidence; không anticipate.

---

**Nhóm 1 — Stack và Architecture Mistakes:**

```
SAI 20.1 — Over-engineer sớm vì "cần scale":
  Tình huống: 2-engineer startup builds microservices architecture
           "Chúng tôi cần scale later → Build for scale now"
           Kết quả: 80% time on infrastructure; 20% on product
           Competitor with monolith: Ships 3× faster; learns 3× faster
           You: Still setting up Kubernetes while they're iterating on product
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Rule: Build for 10× current scale; not 100×
  □ Monolith first: Always. Microservices khi có strong evidence of need
    (Evidence: Specific service has clear independent scaling requirement)
  □ "Premature optimization is the root of all evil" — applies to architecture too
  □ Refactor when bottlenecks appear; don't pre-solve problems you don't have

SAI 20.2 — Chọn stack vì fashionable; không vì team knowledge:
  Tình huống: Team expertise: PHP; Java → CTO: "Chúng ta nên dùng Rust + GraphQL + Kubernetes"
           (Trending trên HackerNews; "industry future")
           Kết quả: Learning curve 3 months; productivity tanked; bugs from unfamiliarity
           + Hard to hire: Few developers know this stack in VN
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Stack selection criteria: (1) Team expertise; (2) Talent availability in VN market;
                              (3) Ecosystem/library support; (4) Business use case fit
  □ "Best technology" in theory ≠ Best technology for your team in practice
  □ VN tech market: Strong in PHP; Python; Java; Node.js — hiring in these easier
  □ New stack: Only if strong business reason AND clear learning plan AND team buy-in

SAI 20.3 — Không có technical debt strategy:
  Tình huống: Ship fast; accumulate technical debt → After 18 months:
           Every new feature takes 3× longer due to debt
           Team morale down; engineering estimates unreliable
           "Cần rewrite toàn bộ" → 6-month halt to shipping → Business impact
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Technical debt is normal and acceptable — unmanaged technical debt is not
  □ "Boy Scout rule": Leave code slightly better than you found it; every sprint
  □ Debt budget: 20% of engineering time dedicated to debt reduction (not "eventually")
  □ Debt register: Log known tech debt; prioritize by business impact
  □ Red flag: When estimates consistently 2-3× initial → Technical debt crisis

SAI 20.4 — Build vs. Buy analysis không thorough:
  Tình huống: Need authentication system → "Build from scratch; we need control"
           4 weeks of engineering time later → Mediocre auth system với bugs
           Auth0 cost: 300k VND/tháng → Would have done it better in 2 hours
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Build vs. Buy framework:
    Build khi: Core differentiator; competitive advantage; unique requirement
    Buy khi: Commodity; not differentiating; good solutions exist; fast setup
  □ Always check: Is there a good SaaS solution for this? What's cost vs. build cost?
  □ "Not invented here" syndrome: Common in technical founders → Fight it
  □ Build priority order: (1) Core value differentiators → (2) Everything else = Buy
```

**Nhóm 2 — Outsourcing Mistakes:**

```
SAI 20.5 — Outsource core product:
  Tình huống: "Thuê agency build app; sau đó tuyển team in-house maintain"
           Agency builds: Works for demo; technical debt extreme; no documentation
           Founder: "Chúng tôi nhận code → What is this? Nobody understands it"
           In-house engineers: Cannot maintain; rewrite required
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Rule: Never outsource product's core differentiating technology
  □ OK to outsource: UI/UX design; specific integrations; non-core services
  □ If outsource initial build: Require: Code ownership; documentation; knowledge transfer
  □ Code review: In-house technical person reviews every sprint; not after handover
  □ Transition plan: From day 1; with clear criteria for when to bring in-house

SAI 20.6 — Không có technical specification trước khi outsource:
  Tình huống: "Tôi cần làm một app giống TikTok nhưng cho cộng đồng B2B"
           Agency: "OK; được; 800 triệu; 4 tháng"
           Month 4: "Đây là sản phẩm" → Completely different from expectation
           Month 5: "Cần thêm 400 triệu và 2 tháng để sửa"
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Spec before contract: User stories; wireframes; technical requirements → All agreed
  □ Scope of work: What is IN and OUT of scope → Written; signed
  □ Change request process: Any scope change = Separate quote; separate approval
  □ Payment milestone tied to spec delivery; không time or effort

SAI 20.7 — Vendor management kém với offshore team:
  Tình huống: Offshore team VN-Philippines (or VN-India)
           Communication: Email; asynchronous; weekly update calls
           Problem: Context loss; priority confusion; quality issues discovered late
           Month 6: 3 months of rework required
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Overlap hours: At least 4 hours/day shared working time
  □ Definition of done: Every ticket/story has clear acceptance criteria
  □ Daily standup: Even async (written update) if timezone prevents live
  □ Code review: Not just QA; code review by in-house developer
  □ Sprint demo: Every 2 weeks; functional demo of what was built
```

**Nhóm 3 — Infrastructure và Security:**

```
SAI 20.8 — Security afterthought cho SaaS product:
  Tình huống: "Security là vấn đề của sau khi scale"
           Month 18: Customer discovers SQL injection vulnerability → Data breach
           VN market: Reputation damage severe; no PDPD compliance → Legal liability
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Security from Day 1: Not expensive; but requires mindset shift
  □ OWASP Top 10: Every developer knows and checks against
  □ Password storage: Bcrypt/Argon2 — never plain text or MD5
  □ SQL injection: Parameterized queries; ORMs as default protection
  □ HTTPS everywhere; no exceptions
  □ Dependency scanning: Automated check for known vulnerabilities (npm audit; safety)
  □ Penetration test: Annual for any product handling sensitive data

SAI 20.9 — Không có disaster recovery plan:
  Tình huống: Database server crashes; corrupted data; hosting provider incident
           No backup; no recovery procedure → "We lost everything"
           Or: Backup exists but never tested → Restore fails when needed
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ 3-2-1 backup: 3 copies; 2 different media; 1 offsite
  □ Daily automated backups; point-in-time recovery for databases
  □ ACTUALLY TEST recovery: Quarterly drill; "can we restore from backup?"
  □ RTO (Recovery Time Objective): How long until back to operational?
  □ RPO (Recovery Point Objective): Maximum data loss acceptable?
  □ Hosting redundancy: Multi-AZ deployment cho critical production

SAI 20.10 — Cloud cost management negligence:
  Tình huống: Dev environment running 24/7; unused instances; unoptimized queries
           Month 6: AWS bill 150 triệu → "Chờ; tại sao cao vậy?"
           Điều tra: 40% of spend on idle resources; 30% on unoptimized storage
  
  Giải pháp:
  □ Cloud cost review: Monthly; by service; by environment
  □ Tagging: Every resource tagged by environment; team; project
  □ Right-sizing: Regularly review if instance sizes match actual usage
  □ Dev environments: Auto-shutdown after hours; không 24/7
  □ Reserved instances: For stable workloads; 30-50% cost savings vs. on-demand
  □ Budget alerts: Auto-notify when spending exceeds threshold
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Technical decisions have compound effects: A good decision today reduces cognitive load; maintenance overhead; and future cost. A bad decision today creates compound interest in the form of bugs; slow feature velocity; và engineer frustration. The best technical leaders at startups are not the most technically sophisticated — they're the ones who make consistent; pragmatic; maintainable choices and keep the team shipping quickly.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** CTO/Tech lead không align với business priorities. Building technically impressive system mà business doesn't need yet → Resource waste. Best CTOs constantly ask "What does the business need from technology in the next 6 months?" và optimize for that.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Non-technical founders completely delegate technical decisions. You don't need to be an engineer to ask: "Why are we building this instead of buying?" "Why does this take 3 months?" "What is the risk if this fails?" These are business questions with technical answers. Stay engaged.

> **Cạm bẫy:** "Rewrite to fix all technical debt." The "big rewrite" that will fix everything almost always: Takes 2× estimated time; introduces new bugs; and doesn't actually fix the root causes (which are often process và culture; not code). Prefer continuous incremental improvement over big bang rewrites.
