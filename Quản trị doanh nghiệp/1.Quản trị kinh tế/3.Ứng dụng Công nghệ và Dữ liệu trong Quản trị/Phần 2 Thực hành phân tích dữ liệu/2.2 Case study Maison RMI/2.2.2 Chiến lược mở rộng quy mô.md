### Chiến Lược Mở Rộng Quy Mô — Scale-Up Strategy

Scale-up là giai đoạn chuyển từ một concept thành công sang một multi-location, multi-brand operation có hệ thống. Đây là giai đoạn nguy hiểm nhất trong lifecycle của một F&B brand vì growth pressure thường đi trước system readiness.

> **Bài học:** "Premature scaling is the #1 cause of startup death." Trong F&B, premature scaling = mở rộng trước khi có systems, processes, và talent để maintain quality. Growth that dilutes brand = brand damage that's hard to reverse.

---

**Scale-Up Framework cho Premium F&B:**

```
Stage 1 — Concept Validation (1–3 locations):
  Goal: Prove the concept is repeatable, not just lucky
  Metrics: Same-store sales growth, customer return rate, NPS
  Systems needed: Basic POS, simple inventory, COGS tracking
  Risk: Low; failure is recoverable

Stage 2 — Systematization (4–10 locations):
  Goal: Document and systematize everything that made Stage 1 work
  Metrics: Location-to-location consistency scores, training completion
  Systems needed: Central kitchen, standard operating procedures, HRMS
  Risk: Medium; this is where many brands stumble

Stage 3 — Acceleration (10+ locations):
  Goal: Efficient deployment of proven system at scale
  Metrics: New location ramp time, location contribution margin, brand NPS
  Systems needed: Full ERP, CRM, analytics, camera AI for operations
  Risk: High; speed and quality tension maximum

Maison RMI trajectory: navigating Stage 2 → Stage 3 challenges
```

---

**Standardization vs Customization:**

```
Premium F&B brands face a fundamental tension:
  Brand consistency requires: same recipes, same service standards, same ambience
  Local relevance requires: adapting to local customer preferences and location

Resolution framework:
  STANDARDIZE (non-negotiable):
    - Core recipes (within defined specs)
    - Service standards (timing, presentation, language)
    - Brand visual identity (interior design guidelines)
    - Food safety and hygiene protocols
    - Pricing (premium brands avoid location-based price variation)
  
  CUSTOMIZE (allowed within guardrails):
    - Local menu additions (within brand aesthetic)
    - Local events and community engagement
    - Minor interior adaptations (if brand guidelines allow)
```

**Location Selection — data-driven approach:**

```
Traditional approach: founder intuition + broker recommendation
Data-driven approach:

Demand indicators:
  □ Foot traffic data (phone location data, manual count)
  □ Catchment area demographics (income, age, lifestyle)
  □ Competitor density and performance (if observable)
  □ Anchor tenant quality (malls: which brands nearby)
  □ Digital demand signal: search trends for brand name in area

Supply factors:
  □ Rent per sqm vs revenue potential
  □ Lease terms: length, escalation clauses, early exit rights
  □ Visibility and accessibility (parking, public transport)
  □ Back-of-house adequacy (kitchen size, ventilation, loading)

Financial hurdle:
  □ Projected Year 1 revenue (conservative, base, optimistic)
  □ Break-even analysis: at what revenue does location become profitable?
  □ Payback period on capex: how many months to recover fit-out cost?
  □ Portfolio risk: how correlated is this location with others?
```

**Operations Playbook — the system of scale:**

```
For each new location, Maison RMI (and any scaling F&B) needs:

Pre-opening (60 days before):
  □ Hire location manager 60 days early (train at existing location)
  □ Hire full team 30 days early (extensive training at existing location)
  □ Equipment installation and testing
  □ Soft opening with invited guests (test run)

Go-live:
  □ First 30 days: senior team presence daily
  □ Daily KPI review: revenue vs forecast, ticket size, waste rate, labour cost
  □ Customer feedback loop: daily summary of complaints/compliments
  □ Mystery shopper week 2 and week 4

Ongoing monitoring:
  □ Same-store sales vs system average
  □ Customer satisfaction vs system average
  □ Labour productivity vs standard
  □ Food cost vs standard
```

**Talent at scale — the critical bottleneck:**

```
In premium F&B, talent is the product:
  A trained, motivated barista/chef is the quality
  An untrained or demotivated one is the complaint

Scale challenge:
  Finding and training quality staff at VN scale is hard:
  → High turnover in F&B (40–60%/year is common)
  → Training takes 2–4 weeks minimum for standards
  → Senior talent grows slower than location count
  
Maison RMI's solutions:
  → Internal promotion pipeline (grow managers from within)
  → Training academy (systematic, not ad-hoc)
  → Better-than-market compensation for key roles (reduce turnover)
  → Technology to reduce skill dependency (standardized equipment, recipes)
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Starbucks' success in international expansion comes from their "playbook" — a documented, tested system for opening every new location. Everything is specified: equipment layout, training curriculum, music volume, dialogue scripts for staff. This isn't killing creativity; it's protecting brand quality at scale. Without a playbook, every location manager makes their own decisions → inconsistency → brand dilution.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Scale marketing before scaling operations. "We'll grow the brand awareness then the locations will fill up." But if operations can't deliver consistent quality at new locations, marketing brings disappointed customers who post negative reviews. Operations readiness must precede marketing push.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Franchise too early to solve capital constraint. "We can scale faster if we franchise." Franchising before the system is documented and proven is exporting your problems to franchisees. Their failures damage your brand. Build the system first, franchise later.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Central kitchen investment timing. Central kitchen reduces per-unit food cost and improves consistency — but requires minimum volume to justify the fixed cost. Rule of thumb: viable central kitchen for F&B at ~8–12+ locations of similar menu. Below that, commissary or location-level production often more economical.
