### Phân Tích Lưu Chuyển Tiền Tệ — Cash Flow Vinamilk

Cash Flow analysis là bước quan trọng để validate chất lượng của earnings — một công ty có thể có high reported Net Income nhưng poor cash generation, đây là red flag đáng kể.

> **Bài học:** "Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity, cash is reality." Net Income trong P&L là kế toán-driven; Cash Flow from Operations (OCF) là thực tiễn. Khi OCF >> Net Income → tốt. Khi OCF << Net Income → investigate. Khi OCF âm liên tục trong profitable company → crisis signal.

---

**Vinamilk Cash Flow Pattern:**

```
Approximate illustration:
  Net Income: 9,000–10,000 tỷ/năm (approximate)
  Depreciation add-back: ~2,000–3,000 tỷ (significant PP&E)
  Working capital changes: variable
    - Inventory buildup in H1 (prep for peak seasons)
    - Drawdown in H2
  Operating Cash Flow: typically 100–120% of Net Income
    → Strong OCF quality; earnings well-backed by cash

FCF (Free Cash Flow) = OCF - Capex:
  Capex: ~2,000–4,000 tỷ/năm (farm expansion, plant upgrades)
  FCF: approximately 6,000–8,000 tỷ/năm
  FCF Yield: FCF/Market Cap — tracked by investors for dividend coverage
```

**Cash Flow Quality Metrics:**

| Metric | Formula | Vinamilk (approx) | Signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| OCF/Net Income | OCF / Net Income | ~110–120% | >100% = good earnings quality |
| FCF Margin | FCF / Revenue | ~10–13% | Positive = cash generative |
| Capex/Revenue | Capex / Revenue | ~5–6% | Maintenance capex; growth capex |
| Dividend Coverage | OCF / Dividends | ~1.5–2.0× | >1× = sustainable dividend |
| Cash Conversion | OCF / EBITDA | ~85–95% | >70% = healthy |

**Cash Allocation — kiểm tra quản lý tài chính:**

```
Vinamilk's cash priorities (historical):
  1. Maintain operations: working capital, capex for maintenance
  2. Growth investment: farm expansion, new capacity
  3. Dividend: very high payout ratio (consistent priority for shareholders)
  4. M&A/strategic: occasional; e.g., investments in overseas dairy

Implication: Vinamilk is NOT in growth reinvestment mode (high CAPEX, low dividend)
             Rather in mature cash cow mode (steady capex, return cash to shareholders)
             → Appropriate for a market-leading, mature consumer brand
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Warren Buffett uses OCF/Net Income ratio as a measure of earnings quality in his analyses. A company with OCF consistently exceeding Net Income is demonstrating that its accounting earnings are conservative — there aren't aggressive revenue recognition or cost capitalization tricks inflating reported profit. Vinamilk's strong OCF relative to reported earnings is a positive quality indicator.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Focus only on Net Income, ignore OCF. A company can show Net Income growth while OCF is declining — classic sign of accounting manipulation or business deterioration. For Vinamilk, checking OCF vs Net Income is the first quality check before trusting earnings trend.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Confuse Free Cash Flow with Operating Cash Flow. FCF = OCF - Capex. For capital-intensive businesses like Vinamilk (farms, manufacturing), Capex is substantial. OCF might be strong but FCF (after capex) is much lower. Dividend sustainability should be evaluated against FCF, not OCF.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Biological asset fair value changes distort P&L and Cash Flow. Under VAS, Vinamilk's dairy herd is classified as biological assets measured at fair value. Changes in fair value flow through P&L (non-cash). This must be stripped out when analyzing underlying cash generation from operations — it's an accounting adjustment, not cash. Cash Flow Statement add-back for non-cash items handles this, but requires awareness in analysis.
