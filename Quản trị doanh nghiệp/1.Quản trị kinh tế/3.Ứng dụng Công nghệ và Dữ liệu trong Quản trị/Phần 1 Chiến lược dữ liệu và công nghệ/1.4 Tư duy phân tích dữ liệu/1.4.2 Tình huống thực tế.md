### Tình Huống Thực Tế — Phân Tích Dữ Liệu Trong Quản Trị

Từ lý thuyết đến thực hành: case này dẫn dắt qua một tình huống quản trị điển hình, áp dụng các frameworks đã học — Strategy Map, BSC, funnel metrics, data quality, và analytical thinking.

> **Bài học:** Framework chỉ có giá trị khi chúng được áp dụng trong thực tế, với real data, real constraints, và real decisions. Không có case nào là "clean" — business decisions luôn có missing information, competing priorities, và time pressure.

---

**Case: Công ty Phân Phối Thực Phẩm B2B (SME 150 người, doanh thu ~80 tỷ/năm)**

**Tình huống:** CEO nhận thấy margin đang giảm dần trong 3 quý liên tiếp, dù revenue vẫn tăng 12% YoY. Ban giám đốc muốn hiểu tại sao và quyết định phải làm gì.

---

**Bước 1: Xây dựng Strategy Map để contextualize vấn đề:**

```
Financial:
  [✓] Revenue growing 12% YoY
  [!] Gross Margin: 28% → 24% → 21% (3 quý liên tiếp declining)
  [!] EBITDA: 12% → 9% → 6%

Customer:
  [✓] NPS stable at 42
  [?] Customer retention rate (data not available — need to get)
  [?] Average order size trend (not tracked currently)

Internal Process:
  [?] Order fulfillment cost per order (not tracked)
  [!] Delivery lead time: 2.1 days → 3.3 days (degraded)
  [?] Procurement cost savings (not tracked)

Learning & Growth:
  [?] Sales team tenure and experience (not in HRMS properly)
  [!] No formal training program for procurement team
```

Observation: Company tracks Financial metrics well but has significant gaps in Customer, Process, and L&G data. This itself is a finding.

---

**Bước 2: Hypothesis-Driven Analysis của margin decline:**

```
H1: COGS increased (raw material prices)
H2: Product mix shifted to lower-margin products
H3: Customer mix shifted to lower-margin customers (e.g., larger buyers with better terms)
H4: Operational costs increased (delivery, labor)
H5: Pricing not adjusted despite cost increases
H6: Sales team selling too much to discount-requiring customers
```

**Data collected to test each hypothesis:**

| Hypothesis | Data needed | Where to get | Finding |
|---|---|---|---|
| H1: COGS prices | Supplier invoices vs 9 months ago | Procurement + Finance | Raw material up 6% average |
| H2: Product mix | Revenue by product category, margin by category | ERP (if clean) | Premium products down from 45% to 31% of mix |
| H3: Customer mix | Revenue and margin by customer tier | ERP + manual analysis | Large customers grew from 30% to 47% of revenue |
| H4: Op costs | COGS detailed breakdown | Finance | Delivery cost per order up 22% |
| H5: Pricing | Price list dates vs cost changes | Finance + Sales | Last price increase was 14 months ago |
| H6: Discounting | Discount rates by deal by rep | CRM (manual review) | Average discount increased from 8% to 14% |

**Root causes identified:** Multiple compounding factors:
1. Premium product mix erosion (biggest driver)
2. Large customer concentration growing (price-sensitive segment)
3. No pricing adjustment in 14 months despite 6% COGS increase
4. Discounting discipline breakdown

---

**Bước 3: Cách data quality issues cản trở analysis:**

```
Issues discovered during analysis:
  □ Product margin by category: Finance had data but in Excel, took 3 days to get
  □ Customer margin by tier: required manual matching of ERP and separate spreadsheet
  □ Delivery cost per order: not captured in ERP — had to manually estimate from logistics data
  □ Discount rates: not logged in CRM consistently — only 60% of deals had discount data

Root: No SSOT for key operational metrics. Each analysis requires
      custom data extraction instead of pulling from dashboard.

Time to insight: 2 weeks vs should be 2 days with proper systems.
Cost: ~40 hours of Finance/Ops analyst time for this one analysis.
```

---

**Bước 4: Recommendations và projected impact:**

```
1. Pricing update:
   → Increase prices 5% on affected products (cover COGS + restore margin)
   → Expected impact: +3% on gross margin

2. Product mix incentives:
   → Sales incentive reweighted to premium products
   → Discount authority: only Sales Manager can approve >10% discount
   → Expected impact: +2% gross margin over 6 months

3. Large customer profitability review:
   → Calculate true profitability of top 5 customers (incl. delivery cost, returns, payment terms)
   → Renegotiate terms or reduce service intensity for loss-making accounts
   → Expected impact: +1% gross margin

4. Data infrastructure:
   → Connect ERP and sales data for ongoing margin-by-product and margin-by-customer tracking
   → Dashboard built so this analysis can be done monthly (not quarterly)
   → Investment: 40 hours setup + monthly maintenance
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** This case illustrates the "data flywheel": better data → faster insights → better decisions → better outcomes → more resources to invest in data. Many SMEs are stuck before the flywheel starts because the cost of getting the first good insight is so high (2 weeks, manual analysis) that they do it quarterly. With proper infrastructure, monthly margin review becomes routine — and problems are caught earlier.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Present findings without recommendations. "Margin đã giảm vì ba nguyên nhân..." and stopping there. Good analysis doesn't just diagnose — it prescribes: "Here are the 4 actions, with expected impact and priority sequence." The CEO's job becomes deciding, not problem-solving.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Make recommendations without acknowledging uncertainty. "These actions will restore margin to 28%." No — these actions are expected to restore margin based on our analysis; actual results depend on customer reaction to price increase, competitor response, etc. State confidence level and key assumptions.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Analysis paralysis — wait for perfect data before acting. 60% of deals with discount data is not perfect, but it's enough to confirm the pattern. "Ready, aim, aim, aim, aim, fire" vs "Ready, aim, fire, learn, adjust." Most business decisions need to be made with 70–80% of ideal information.
