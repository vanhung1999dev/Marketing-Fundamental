### Tư Duy Theo Mô Hình Logic

Analytical thinking không phải bẩm sinh — là kỹ năng có thể học và rèn luyện. Tư duy logic giúp phân tích problems một cách có cấu trúc, tránh cognitive biases, và ra quyết định dựa trên evidence thay vì intuition.

> **Bài học:** Analytical thinking là meta-skill: nó không chỉ apply cho data analysis — nó apply cho mọi business decision. Manager tư duy logic sẽ hỏi đúng câu hỏi, nghi ngờ correlation-causation, và demand evidence trước khi commit resources.

---

**MECE Framework — Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive:**

```
MECE = không overlap + không bỏ sót

Ví dụ tốt:
  Phân tích revenue decline theo:
  → By product category (MECE: mỗi revenue rơi vào đúng 1 category)
  → By customer segment (MECE: mỗi customer thuộc đúng 1 segment)
  → By geography (MECE: mỗi deal ở đúng 1 region)

Ví dụ không MECE:
  Phân tích revenue decline theo: "Sales issues" AND "Market issues"
  → Không rõ ràng: cùng vấn đề có thể thuộc cả hai → overlap
  → Không exhaustive: "Pricing issues"? "Product issues"? Bị bỏ sót
```

**MECE helps:**
- Problem decomposition: breakdown large problem to smaller, addressable pieces
- Hypothesis generation: ensure all possible explanations considered
- Communication: logical structure makes arguments clearer

---

**Hypothesis-Driven Analysis:**

```
Traditional approach:
  Collect data → Analyze everything → Hope insight emerges
  (slow, unfocused, often inconclusive)

Hypothesis-driven approach:
  Define problem clearly → Form specific hypotheses → Test each
  (focused, faster, more actionable)

Ví dụ:
  Problem: "Win rate giảm từ 28% xuống 19% trong Q3"
  
  Hypotheses:
    H1: Competition increased (more competitors bidding)
    H2: Proposal quality decreased (new sales reps without training)
    H3: Target market shifted (more SME customers, different buying behavior)
    H4: Pricing not competitive vs new market conditions
  
  Test each with data:
    H1: Track # competitors on each deal
    H2: Win rate by sales rep tenure
    H3: Win rate by customer size
    H4: Lost deal reason codes from CRM
  
  Data → confirm or reject each hypothesis → Root cause → Fix
```

**Correlation vs Causation — critical distinction:**

```
Correlation: Two variables move together
  "Doanh thu cao nhất vào tháng 12" — mùa Tết/Giáng Sinh correlated với sales

Causation: One variable causes the other
  "Marketing campaign in November caused December sales spike"

How to test causation:
  - A/B test: randomly assign intervention to some, not others
  - Time series: does change in A reliably precede change in B?
  - Control for confounding variables
  - Logical mechanism: is there a plausible causal pathway?

Classic examples in VN business:
  Correlation: "Sales reps who use CRM more have higher revenue"
    → Is CRM causing better sales? Or are better reps both using CRM AND selling more?
  
  Causation test: Randomly assign CRM training to some reps → measure revenue change
  
  Correlation: "Customers who receive more emails churn less"
    → Is email preventing churn? Or do engaged customers both read emails AND stay?
```

**Statistical Thinking — không cần PhD:**

**1. Sample size matters:**
- 3 customer interviews ≠ "customers want X"
- Survey với 50 responses từ 10,000 customers: margin of error cao
- Rule of thumb: 30+ for quantitative conclusions; recognize limitations

**2. Base rates:**
- "30% win rate increase" → from 2% to 2.6%? Or 25% to 32.5%?
- Always state absolute and relative change

**3. Averages can mislead:**
- Average deal size 500M nhưng 80% deals là <200M + một vài deals lớn
- Distribution matters more than average for many decisions
- Median often more useful than mean for skewed data

**5 Whys — root cause analysis:**

```
Problem: "Gross margin giảm 5% points in Q2"

Why 1: COGS tăng 8% while revenue tăng 3%
Why 2: Raw material costs increased significantly
Why 3: Supplier changed pricing mid-year (emergency)
Why 4: Original supplier had quality issue, forced switch to backup supplier
Why 5: No qualified backup supplier pre-approved — only one source for key material

Root cause: Single-source supplier dependency
Fix: Dual-source supplier policy; pre-qualify backup suppliers for all critical materials
Not fix: Negotiate harder with current supplier (symptom, not cause)
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** McKinsey's "structured thinking" approach: every analysis starts with a hypothesis, every hypothesis is tested with specific data, every conclusion is challenged. This discipline prevents the common trap of "data theater" — collecting data to confirm what you already believe. True analytical thinking is willing to kill your own hypothesis.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Data to support a conclusion already reached. CEO decided to launch new product → asks team to "do analysis" → team finds data that supports the decision (confirmation bias). True analytical thinking: start with "what would convince us NOT to do this?" and look for that evidence first.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Treat every data point as signal. "Sales dropped 3% this week" — is this signal or noise? Statistical significance, trend vs anomaly, seasonality — these matter. Making major decisions based on one week's data without context is reactive management, not data-driven management.

> **Cạm bẫy:** MECE analysis that's too granular. Breaking problem into 200 sub-questions when 5 would do. MECE is a framework for clarity, not exhaustiveness theater. Real constraint: analyst time is limited. Prioritize the 3–5 hypotheses most likely to explain the problem and most actionable if confirmed.
