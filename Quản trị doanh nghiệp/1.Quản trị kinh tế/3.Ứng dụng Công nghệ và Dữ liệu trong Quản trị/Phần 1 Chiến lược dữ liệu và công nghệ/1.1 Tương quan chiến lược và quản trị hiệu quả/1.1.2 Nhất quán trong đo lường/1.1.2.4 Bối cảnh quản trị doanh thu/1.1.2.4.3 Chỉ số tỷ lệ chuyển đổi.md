### Chỉ Số Tỷ Lệ Chuyển Đổi (Conversion Rates)

Conversion rate tại mỗi stage của funnel là dấu hiệu sức khỏe rõ ràng nhất của quy trình bán hàng. Khi conversion giảm ở một stage cụ thể, đó là nơi cần can thiệp — không phải nơi khác.

> **Bài học:** Toàn bộ revenue pipeline là tập hợp conversion rates nhân với nhau. Cải thiện một stage có tác động nhân: nếu cả 4 stages cải thiện 10%, tổng end-to-end conversion tăng (1.1)^4 = 46%. Biết stage nào weak nhất để ưu tiên cải thiện — đó là leverage point cao nhất.

---

**Funnel math — tác động nhân:**

```
1,000 Leads (top of funnel)
  × 25% MQL rate     = 250 MQLs
  × 40% SQL rate     = 100 SQLs
  × 70% Opp rate     = 70 Opportunities
  × 30% Win rate     = 21 Customers

End-to-end conversion: 21/1,000 = 2.1%

If Win Rate improves 30% → 40%:
  70 Opps × 40% = 28 customers = +33% revenue from same pipeline

If MQL→SQL improves 40% → 55%:
  250 MQL × 55% = 137 SQLs → 96 Opps → 29 customers = +38% revenue
```

---

**Benchmark theo channel:**

| Channel | Lead-to-Close Conversion | Đặc điểm VN |
|---|---|---|
| Inbound (website, content) | 3–7% | Growing với digital presence |
| Referral/Introduction | 10–20% | Rất mạnh trong B2B VN — trust-based buying |
| Outbound cold | 0.5–2% | Thấp; cần strong messaging |
| Existing customer upsell | 20–40% | Warm relationship = cao nhất |
| Event/conference | 2–5% | Moderate intent |

---

**Bottleneck Analysis — khi conversion thấp:**

```
Ví dụ: Win rate thấp ở Proposal → Close (18% vs 30% target)

Diagnose — hỏi từng nguyên nhân:
  □ Price: bị reject vì giá cao hơn competitor?
  □ Proposal quality: value proposition không compelling?
  □ Champion: người advocate nội bộ có đủ mạnh không?
  □ Decision process: đã hiểu đúng who approves chưa?
  □ Timing: deal timing không align với customer budget cycle?

Fix theo root cause:
  Price → Pricing strategy review; competitive positioning
  Proposal quality → Template redesign; storytelling training; case studies
  Champion → Coach champion to navigate internal selling
  Timing → Pipeline planning to align with Q4 budget approval cycles
```

---

**Time-in-Stage Analysis:**

```
Deals stuck in stage > 2× average time = likely stalled

Average time per stage (B2B mid-market VN benchmark):
  MQL → SQL: 3–7 ngày
  SQL → Opportunity: 7–14 ngày
  Opportunity → Proposal: 14–30 ngày
  Proposal → Close: 21–60 ngày

CRM alert: flag deals not moving stage in X days
  → Manager review triggered
  → Either accelerate with new approach, or disqualify
```

---

**Lost Deal Analysis — critical nhưng thường bị ignore:**

```
For every lost deal, capture:
  □ Stage lost at (early vs late)
  □ Reason: price / competitor / no decision / timing / wrong fit
  □ Which competitor won (if any)
  □ What would have changed the outcome?

Monthly lost deal review reveals:
  → Patterns in reasons → systemic issues to fix
  → Product gaps → roadmap input
  → Pricing feedback → strategy adjustment
  → Competitive intelligence → sales enablement

Most companies track won deals; only best companies systematically learn from losses.
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** HubSpot research: companies with a clear, defined sales process achieve 18% higher revenue than those without one. Companies that regularly analyze lost deals improve win rates 10–15% per year, compounding over time. The mechanism: systematic loss analysis reveals systemic issues (product gap, pricing, messaging) that management can fix. Random luck can't compound; systematic learning can.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Set one conversion rate target for entire team without segmenting by rep, channel, or product. Average hides bimodal distribution: some reps at 40%, others at 15%. Aggregate 27% looks acceptable but masks a critical coaching opportunity. Always look at distribution, not just average.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Measure conversion at close nhưng không check: are won customers satisfied? Win rate 35% với customers churn quickly là worse than 25% với high retention. Post-sale metrics (renewal rate, NPS, expansion) phải connect back to sales process quality.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Cherry-pick conversion data — report best months, suppress bad months. Consistent measurement, including during slumps, is the only way to identify genuine improvement vs noise. Trend analysis requires consistent methodology — not selective reporting to make performance look better than it is.
