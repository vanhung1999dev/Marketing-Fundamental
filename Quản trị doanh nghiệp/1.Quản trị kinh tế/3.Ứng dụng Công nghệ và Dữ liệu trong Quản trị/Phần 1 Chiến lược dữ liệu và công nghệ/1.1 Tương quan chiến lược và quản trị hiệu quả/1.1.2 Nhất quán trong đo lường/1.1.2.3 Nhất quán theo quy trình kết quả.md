### Nhất Quán Theo Quy Trình Kết Quả (Input–Process–Output–Outcome)

Framework Input → Process → Output → Outcome giúp thiết kế hệ thống đo lường logic từ gốc đến ngọn — không chỉ đo kết quả cuối mà còn đo các điểm kiểm soát trong quy trình để có thể can thiệp sớm.

> **Bài học:** Nếu chỉ đo Outcome (doanh thu cuối năm) — bạn biết kết quả nhưng không biết tại sao và không có thời gian để fix. Nếu đo cả Input và Process — bạn thấy vấn đề 3–6 tháng trước khi nó ảnh hưởng kết quả tài chính.

---

**Framework 4 tầng:**

| Tầng | Định nghĩa | Ví dụ Sales | Ai controls |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Input** | Resources và activities bạn control | # Sales reps, marketing budget | Sales Manager, Marketing |
| **Process** | Cách inputs được chuyển hóa | # Cuộc gọi/tuần, meetings booked | Sales Rep + Manager |
| **Output** | Kết quả ngay lập tức | # Deals won, contracts signed | Sales team |
| **Outcome** | Business impact cuối cùng | Revenue, Margin, NPS | Finance + CEO |

---

**Ví dụ Operations F&B — toàn bộ chuỗi:**

```
INPUT:   Raw material quality (supplier scorecard)
         Machine availability (maintenance schedule adherence)
         Labour skills (training completion rate)

PROCESS: Yield rate per production run
         Defect detection rate at QC checkpoint
         OEE = Availability × Performance × Quality

OUTPUT:  Units produced vs planned
         Defect rate of finished goods
         Batch pass rate (first-time-right %)

OUTCOME: COGS as % of revenue
         Customer complaint rate
         Product return rate
```

---

**Leading vs Lagging:**

```
LEADING (upstream, predictive):
  Sales: # Meetings booked this week predicts next month pipeline
  Ops: Machine uptime predicts production output next week
  HR: Training hours predicts next quarter productivity

LAGGING (downstream, confirmatory):
  Revenue, Profit, NPS, Customer churn

Management discipline:
  - Lead with LEADING indicators in weekly/daily management
  - Report LAGGING indicators in monthly/quarterly reviews
  - Investigate when leading doesn't translate to lagging as expected
```

---

**Building the measurement cascade:**

```
Step 1: Start with Outcome (what do we want to achieve?)
Step 2: Work backward: what Outputs drive that Outcome?
Step 3: Work backward: what Processes generate those Outputs?
Step 4: Work backward: what Inputs are needed for those Processes?
Step 5: Assign ownership and measurement frequency at each level
Step 6: Test the logic: "If we improve Input X, Process Y will improve,
        increasing Output Z, leading to Outcome A" — is this actually true?
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Theory of Change (phổ biến trong development sector, increasingly used in business) explicitly maps Activities → Outputs → Outcomes → Impact, với assumptions stated at each link. If an outcome isn't achieved, you can trace back: was it input failure? Process failure? Or was the assumption between Process and Output simply wrong? This diagnostic precision separates learning organizations from those that only react to bad numbers without understanding why.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Chỉ đo Outcomes, blame team khi outcome bad mà không diagnose. "Revenue missed" → frustrated meeting với finger-pointing. Better: "Revenue missed. Pipeline was 92% of needed. Win rate 18% vs 25% target. Root cause: proposal quality — invest in training." Structured diagnosis requires input/process metrics.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Quá nhiều metrics tại mỗi level — analysis paralysis. Limit to 2–3 key metrics per level. 5 inputs × 5 processes × 5 outputs × 5 outcomes = 20 metrics to track, which is already a lot for most teams. Priority discipline required.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Optimize Input metrics trong isolation mà không check Process impact. Ví dụ: tăng # sales calls per day (Input) mà không đảm bảo quality → more calls, same or fewer wins → efficiency theater. Always connect back: "Did improving this Input actually improve the downstream Process and Output?"
