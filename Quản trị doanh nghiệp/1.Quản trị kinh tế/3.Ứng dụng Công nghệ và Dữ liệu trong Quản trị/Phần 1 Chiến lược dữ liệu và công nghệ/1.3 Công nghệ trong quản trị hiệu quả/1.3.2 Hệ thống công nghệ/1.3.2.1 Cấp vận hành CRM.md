### Hệ Thống CRM — Cấp Vận Hành

CRM (Customer Relationship Management) là hệ thống quản lý toàn bộ vòng đời quan hệ khách hàng — từ lead đầu tiên đến loyal customer — trong một nền tảng thống nhất.

> **Bài học:** CRM không phải công cụ cho IT — là công cụ cho Sales, Marketing, và Customer Service. Nếu sales team không dùng CRM mỗi ngày, nó không deliver value dù tốt đến đâu. CRM adoption là culture problem trước khi là technology problem.

---

**Core CRM Modules:**

**Contact & Account Management:**
- Lưu trữ tất cả thông tin về prospects và customers
- Interaction history: mọi email, call, meeting được logged
- Account hierarchy: parent company → subsidiaries → contacts
- Segmentation: tags, custom fields để filter và target

**Pipeline & Opportunity Management:**
- Visual pipeline: drag-and-drop deals qua stages
- Deal value, probability, expected close date
- Activity reminders: follow-up tasks tự động tạo
- Forecast from pipeline stage probabilities

**Marketing Automation:**
- Email sequences: automated follow-up after form fill
- Lead nurturing: drip campaigns based on behavior
- Lead scoring: rank leads by engagement and fit
- Campaign ROI tracking: which campaigns generate best SQLs

**Customer Service:**
- Ticket management: customer issues tracked to resolution
- SLA management: response time commitments tracked
- Knowledge base: self-service articles reduce ticket volume
- Customer health scores: early warning of churn risk

**CRM Implementation — common steps:**

```
Phase 1 (Week 1–2): Setup & Configuration
  □ Define pipeline stages matching your actual sales process
  □ Create custom fields for your industry/product specifics
  □ Set up user accounts and access levels
  □ Import existing customer and contact data (clean first!)

Phase 2 (Week 3–4): Integration & Automation
  □ Connect email (Gmail/Outlook) for auto-logging
  □ Set up lead capture forms (website)
  □ Configure automated follow-up sequences
  □ Connect with accounting system if possible

Phase 3 (Month 2): Adoption & Habit
  □ Sales team training — hands-on, not slides
  □ Manager models CRM usage in every pipeline review
  □ Remove alternatives: stop accepting Excel pipeline updates
  □ Weekly check: is everyone logging activities?

Phase 4 (Month 3+): Optimization
  □ Review pipeline stages: do they match reality?
  □ Identify reporting most useful for decisions
  □ Add integrations as needs emerge
```

**CRM Adoption Metrics:**

```
Track weekly for first 3 months:
  □ # Deals created (is team entering new opportunities?)
  □ # Activities logged per rep (calls, emails, meetings)
  □ # Deals updated this week (is pipeline current?)
  □ % Deals with next action date set (discipline indicator)

Red flags:
  □ Rep submitting pipeline in Excel "for this week" → CRM bypass
  □ Deals sitting in same stage for 30+ days → stale pipeline
  □ No activities logged for active deals → not using CRM
```

> **Phân tích sâu:** Aberdeen Group research: companies with CRM achieve 14.6% improvement in sales productivity vs those without. But more importantly: only 47% of CRM implementations achieve successful adoption. The gap between buying CRM and realizing value = adoption. Companies with mandatory CRM usage (manager only reviews CRM data, not Excel) achieve significantly higher adoption rates.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Implement CRM without migrating existing customer data. Day 1: CRM is empty; team still refers to old Excel. Result: two systems, confusion, eventual abandonment. Before go-live: clean and import all existing contacts and deals. Starting with real data makes adoption much faster.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Buy most feature-rich CRM for current size. A 5-person sales team using Salesforce Enterprise is like driving a truck to buy groceries. Start with free or basic tier; upgrade when you hit clear limitations. HubSpot Free is genuinely excellent for early-stage teams.

> **Cạm bẫy:** CRM becomes a reporting tool, not a working tool. Sales team fills in CRM Friday afternoon to satisfy manager, not throughout the week because it helps them sell. Fix: make CRM genuinely useful for the rep (not just the manager). Features that help reps: email templates, call logging from mobile, activity reminders, email open tracking. When CRM helps them do their job better, adoption follows.
