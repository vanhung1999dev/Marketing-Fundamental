### Active Listening — Nghe Thực Sự

**Levels of listening:**

- **Level 1 — Cosmetic listening:** Appear to listen, actually thinking about your response
- **Level 2 — Conversational listening:** Hear words, process at surface, respond to content
- **Level 3 — Active listening:** Hear words + observe non-verbals + understand emotions + reflect back meaning

Leader cần: Level 3 trong important conversations.

**Active listening techniques:**
- **Paraphrasing:** "Vậy ý bạn là..." — reflect back what you heard, allow correction
- **Labeling emotions:** "Nghe như bạn frustrated với cách decision được made" — acknowledge emotional component
- **Silence:** Comfortable with silence after someone speaks — don't rush to fill space
- **Clarifying questions:** "Bạn có thể nói rõ hơn về..." — genuinely curious, not rhetorical

> **Bài học:** Người cảm thấy genuinely heard sẽ: (1) open up more information, (2) lower defensiveness, (3) more receptive to your message afterward. Active listening không là passive — nó là active investment trong quality of information you receive và relationship you build.

> **Phân tích sâu:** **Zeigarnik Effect** trong conversations — người có incomplete thoughts (interrupted, dismissed, not fully heard) carry those thoughts as "open loops" → they're distracted, resentful, và not fully present. When you fully hear someone out — close the loop — they can move forward. Leaders who are known as good listeners get more candid information from their team.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến:** Listening với intent to respond (formulating counter-argument while they're still talking) instead of listening với intent to understand. You can tell — và người talking cũng cảm nhận được.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Nodding và "uh-huh" while checking phone hoặc thinking về other things. Distracted listening is worse than honest "I don't have time right now" — vì it's deceptive. If you don't have bandwidth to truly listen: "Tôi muốn give conversation này full attention — có thể reschedule không?"
