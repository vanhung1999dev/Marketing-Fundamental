### Cialdini's 6 Principles of Influence

Robert Cialdini's research trên mechanisms của influence:

**1. Reciprocity** — Người tend to return favors
- Application: Give value first, genuinely. Help people without expectation → they want to help you when needed.

**2. Commitment và Consistency** — Người consistent với past commitments
- Application: Get small yes → leads to larger yes. "Bạn đồng ý rằng customer experience quan trọng?" → "Vậy bạn có muốn xem proposal về improving CX không?"

**3. Social Proof** — Người follow others, especially in uncertainty
- Application: Show who else has done this successfully. "3 teams trong region đã adopt process này với strong results."

**4. Authority** — Người defer to credible experts
- Application: Cite credible sources, reference expertise, present credentials relevant to the point.

**5. Liking** — Người easily influenced bởi người họ like
- Application: Genuine interest trong people, find common ground, mirror values. Manipulative nếu fake; powerful khi genuine.

**6. Scarcity** — Người value things more khi rare hoặc limited
- Application: "Chúng ta có window 2 tuần để act trước khi opportunity closes."

> **Bài học:** Cialdini principles được used ethically (help people make decisions aligned with their interests) hoặc manipulatively (exploit psychological shortcuts to get people to act against their interests). Ethical use: understand these principles để communicate more effectively, not to manipulate. If what you're selling isn't genuinely good for them, no principle should be used.

> **Phân tích sâu:** **Reciprocity** là most powerful principle cho long-term leadership influence. Nếu bạn consistently give help, share information, và support people — without keeping score — bạn build a bank of goodwill that enables future influence. Simon Sinek: "Leadership is not about being in charge. It is about taking care of those in your charge." Givers build influence over time; takers extract it và lose it.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến:** Trying to influence through logic alone when emotional buy-in is the barrier. People make decisions emotionally and justify them rationally. If someone isn't convinced by data, adding more data won't help — understand the underlying emotional concern and address that.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Using influence techniques transactionally — "tôi giúp anh hôm nay để anh giúp tôi mai." Reciprocity-as-transaction is obvious and trust-eroding. Genuine giving creates genuine reciprocity; calculated giving creates transactional dynamics.
