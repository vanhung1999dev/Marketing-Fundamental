### Phân Tích Attractiveness Của Industry

Michael Porter's Five Forces framework phân tích structural attractiveness của một industry — ultimately determines long-run profit potential available to participants.

**Force 1: Threat of New Entrants**

*Cao khi:* Barriers to entry thấp (low capital requirements, no regulation, easy tech access, no brand loyalty needed)

*Thấp khi:* Economies of scale, high switching costs, regulatory barriers, strong existing brand loyalty, access to distribution difficult

*Implication:* Industry với high threat of entry = profit gets competed away as new entrants flood in. Strategy: build barriers.

> **Bài học:** Nhiều tech startup industries có low entry barriers → rất nhiều competitors → race to bottom on margins. Companies survive long-term là những người successfully build barriers after initial entry: network effects (Facebook), switching costs (enterprise software), scale advantages (Amazon logistics).

**Force 2: Bargaining Power of Buyers**

*Cao khi:* Buyers large và few, product undifferentiated, buyers have low switching costs, buyers can backward integrate

*Thấp khi:* Many small buyers, product differentiated và important, high switching costs, buyers depend on seller's expertise

*Implication:* High buyer power → buyers dictate terms, compress margins. Strategy: differentiate, create switching costs, avoid concentration in few buyers.

> **Phân tích:** B2B businesses phải track buyer concentration. Nếu top 3 clients = 60%+ revenue → extremely high buyer power risk. Một client rút đi = business crisis. Diversify customer base như diversify investment portfolio.

**Force 3: Bargaining Power of Suppliers**

*Cao khi:* Few suppliers, no substitutes, suppliers can forward integrate, product is critical input

*Thấp khi:* Many suppliers, substitutes available, supplier needs the business, buyer can backward integrate

*Implication:* High supplier power → suppliers extract margin from business. Strategy: multiple sourcing, backward integration, develop substitutes.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến:** Underestimate supplier power khi business is small/new. "Chúng ta chỉ mua một lượng nhỏ nên chúng ta không phải priority với supplier." Exactly — low priority = worst terms, last allocation, first to be cut in shortage. Build relationships và volume to improve supplier position over time.

**Force 4: Threat of Substitutes**

*Cao khi:* Many alternative ways to fulfill same need, substitutes improving in price-performance, buyer propensity to switch is high

*Thấp khi:* No good substitutes, strong brand preference for category, high cost of switching to substitute

*Implication:* Substitutes cap price premium. Strategy: differentiate versus substitute, not just versus direct competitors.

> **Bài học:** Substitute analysis cần be broader than direct competitors. Taxi threat không phải chỉ từ taxi company khác — mà từ ride-sharing, public transit improvement, và remote work reducing commute need. Restaurant substitute không chỉ từ restaurant khác — mà từ meal kit delivery, convenience stores, và cooking at home. Frame the substitute threat broadly.

**Force 5: Competitive Rivalry**

*Cao khi:* Many competitors, slow growth (fight for share), undifferentiated products, high fixed costs, high exit barriers

*Thấp khi:* Few players, fast growing market, differentiated products, low exit barriers

*Implication:* High rivalry → aggressive price wars và margin compression. Strategy: differentiate to reduce direct comparison, find segments with less competition.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Porter's insight là that **industry structure**, not individual company performance, is the primary determinant of average profitability. Average airline profit margin is near zero because industry structure is terrible (all 5 forces unfavorable): many competitors, powerful buyers (frequent flyers và travel agents), powerful suppliers (Boeing/Airbus, pilots), easy substitutes (train, video call), và easy entry for regional carriers. Understanding industry structure tells you the size of the pie available to all players.
