### Warren Buffett's Moat Framework

Buffett's key question khi evaluating business: "Cái này có moat (hào phòng thủ) không?" — tức là lợi thế competitive bền vững protect business khỏi competition.

**5 loại Moat chính:**

**1. Network Effects**
Value của product/service tăng khi có thêm users.

Examples: Facebook (more users = more valuable), Visa (more merchants accept → more valuable to cardholders → more cardholders → more merchants)

> **Bài học:** Network effects create self-reinforcing loops — harder to enter và harder to leave. Strongest moat khi: it's a two-sided network (platform connecting two user groups), switching costs high, và data accumulates as network grows. But building network effects requires getting to critical mass first — classic chicken và egg problem.

**2. Switching Costs**
High cost (time, money, risk, learning) của moving to competitor.

Examples: Enterprise software (SAP, Salesforce), banking relationships, B2B contracts với deep integrations

> **Phân tích:** Switching costs có thể be financial (termination fees), procedural (retraining, migration effort), hoặc relational (lose account history, trusted relationships). Companies with high switching costs có more predictable revenue và pricing power. Design products với intentional switching costs — deep integrations, data lock-in, workflow dependencies.

**3. Cost Advantages**
Structural cost advantage không easily replicated.

Sources:
- Process advantages (proprietary manufacturing, unique operations)
- Unique access to inputs (exclusive mining rights, strategic locations)
- Scale advantages (fixed cost spread over larger volume)
- Learning curve advantages

> **Sai lầm phổ biến:** Assume scale always creates cost advantage. Scale creates cost advantage only trong businesses với significant fixed costs. In businesses với mostly variable costs, scale không provide structural advantage. Assess: what portion of your cost structure is fixed vs. variable?

**4. Intangible Assets**
Brand, patents, regulatory licenses, exclusive data.

- **Brand:** Willingness to pay premium that competitors cannot easily command (Apple, Hermès)
- **Patents:** Temporary monopoly on innovation (Pharma)
- **Licenses:** Regulatory permission that's difficult to obtain (banks, telecoms, airports)
- **Proprietary data:** Accumulated data advantage (Google's search data, Amazon's purchase data)

> **Bài học:** Brand as moat works best khi brand creates trust trong high-stakes decisions (medical devices, financial services) hoặc identity/status value (luxury). Brand trong commodity categories với low differentiation = weak moat. Build brand in contexts where it creates genuine willingness to pay.

**5. Efficient Scale**
Market niche nhỏ đủ serve only one or two players profitably — entry by third player would make everyone unprofitable.

Examples: Local utilities, small-town newspapers, niche B2B services trong specific industries

> **Phân tích sâu:** Efficient scale moat explains why many niche businesses are extremely defensible. The market is too small to attract big competitors but large enough to be profitable for the incumbent. Strategy: identify niches where efficient scale applies and entrench deeply.
