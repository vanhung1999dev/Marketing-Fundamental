### Product-Led Growth (PLG)

Modern growth model: product itself là primary vehicle cho acquisition, activation, retention, và expansion.

Examples: Slack, Dropbox, Zoom, Notion.

Mechanics:
- Free tier hoặc freemium model drives viral adoption
- Product designed to be immediately valuable — minimal onboarding friction
- Natural viral loops (invite colleagues, share files, etc.)
- Upgrade triggers built into product experience

> **Bài học:** PLG works best khi: (1) Product delivers value quickly (time-to-value short); (2) Natural sharing/collaboration built into product; (3) Free tier genuinely valuable, không crippled; (4) Upgrade path obvious và natural. PLG không mean "no sales" — rather, sales focuses trên expansion of existing users, không acquisition.

> **Phân tích sâu:** PLG dramatically changes unit economics. Traditional SaaS: CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) = significant sales & marketing expense. PLG: product does the acquiring, so paid customers have much lower effective CAC. This allows faster scaling with less capital. But requires upfront product investment to create viral mechanics.
