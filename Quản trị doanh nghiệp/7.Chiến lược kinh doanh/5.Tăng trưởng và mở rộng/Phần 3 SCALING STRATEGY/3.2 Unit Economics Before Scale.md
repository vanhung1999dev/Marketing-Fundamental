### Unit Economics Before Scale

Chỉ scale khi unit economics proven positive.

**Key unit economics:**

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):**
```
CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Spend / Number of New Customers Acquired
```

**LTV (Lifetime Value):**
```
LTV = Average Revenue Per Customer × Gross Margin × Average Customer Lifetime
```

**LTV:CAC Ratio:**
- < 1:1: Business is destroying value — each customer costs more than they bring
- 1:3 minimum: Acceptable; typically target for scaling
- 3:1+: Healthy để scale

> **Bài học:** Many startups scale với negative unit economics — "grow now, figure out economics later." This can work nếu unit economics will improve at scale (network effects, scale advantages) và capital available. Nhưng nhiều businesses scale with permanently negative unit economics → burning capital without path to profitability. Know your unit economics BEFORE scaling.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Unit economics look good at small scale nhưng deteriorate as you scale — hidden issues become apparent. CAC increases vì you exhaust easy-to-reach customers và move to harder ones. LTV decreases vì later customers are less sticky. Monitor unit economics by customer cohort và acquisition channel.
