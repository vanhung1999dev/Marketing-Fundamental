### Khi Nào Persist vs. Pivot

Một trong những hardest decisions trong business: khi gặp difficulty, đây là temporary setback cần persist through, hay là signal rằng strategy cần fundamental change (pivot)?

**Signals để Persist:**
- Results are improving, even if slowly
- Customer feedback positive — they value what you're building
- Challenges are execution-related, not fundamental fit issues
- Team believes in direction và has ideas to fix
- Market timing may just be early

**Signals để Pivot:**
- Fundamental market assumption proven wrong
- Customers don't have the problem you thought they had
- Problem exists but customers won't pay for solution
- Better path to solving customer problem discovered
- Market structure changed making current approach unviable

> **Bài học:** Eric Ries (Lean Startup): Pivot không phải failure — đó là learning. Instagram started as Burbn (a check-in app) → pivot đến photo sharing sau discovering users love the photo feature. YouTube started as dating site → pivot đến video sharing. The pivot was strategy, enabled by maintaining optionality và learning from early users.

> **Phân tích sâu:** **Pivot vs. Abandon** distinction matters. Pivot = change direction but preserve core asset (team, technology, customer relationships, capital). Abandon = shut down. Pivot should preserve what's valuable while changing what's not working. Question: "What have we built (capabilities, relationships, data) that remains valuable even if we change direction?"

> **Sai lầm phổ biến:** Pivot too early — before giving strategy enough time to show results. Distinguish: has strategy been genuinely tested (enough customers, enough time, right execution) and failed? Or has strategy not been properly tested yet? Premature pivot wastes the learning from current strategy.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Pivot too late — attachment to original vision prevents recognizing clear market signals. Sunk cost fallacy: "Chúng ta đã đầu tư quá nhiều vào hướng này để bỏ." Relevant question: "Given what we know now, is this the best use of remaining resources?" — not "how much have we already spent?"
