### Objectives and Key Results

OKR (Andy Grove at Intel, popularized by Google) là framework để cascade strategy vào measurable quarterly và annual goals.

**Structure:**
- **Objective:** Qualitative, inspiring statement of what to achieve ("Build the world's best recommendation system")
- **Key Results:** 2–5 quantitative measures proving you achieved the Objective ("Recommendation click-through rate ≥ 25%", "Recommendation-driven revenue ≥ 35% of total")

**OKR Hierarchy:**

```
Company OKRs (annual + quarterly)
    ↓ aligned với
Team OKRs (quarterly)
    ↓ aligned với
Individual OKRs (quarterly, optional)
```

> **Bài học:** OKR không phải task list — Key Results measure outcomes, không activities. "Hold 5 customer interviews" = activity (task). "NPS score ≥ 50" = outcome (Key Result). Focus on outcomes force clarity về gì actually matters.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Google's OKR rule: Set objectives ambitious enough that achieving 70% is a success. Nếu consistently achieving 100%, objectives không ambitious enough. This "stretch goal" philosophy differentiate OKR từ traditional performance targets (where 100% = expected và below = failure). Creates environment where ambitious goals are set and partial achievement is celebrated.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến:** OKRs cascade từ trên xuống hoàn toàn. Best practice: ~60% OKRs emerge từ team (bottom-up), ~40% cascaded from above. Bottom-up OKRs create ownership; cascaded OKRs ensure alignment. Pure top-down = compliance without commitment.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Too many OKRs. Thông thường: 3–5 Objectives, 2–4 Key Results mỗi Objective. Nhiều hơn = dilute focus. Rule: nếu bạn có 10+ OKRs, bạn thực sự không có priorities — bạn có wishlist.
