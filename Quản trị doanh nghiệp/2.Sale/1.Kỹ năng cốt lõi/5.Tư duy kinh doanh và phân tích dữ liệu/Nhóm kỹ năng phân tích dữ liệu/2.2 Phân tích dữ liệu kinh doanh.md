### Phân Tích Dữ Liệu Kinh Doanh

Chuyển raw data thành actionable insights thông qua đúng analytical frameworks và tools là kỹ năng có giá trị ngày càng tăng trong môi trường kinh doanh data-rich. Không cần phải là data scientist — nhưng cần đủ analytical literacy để ask right questions, interpret results, và spot when analysis is misleading.

**Core Analytical Techniques cho Sales**: (1) *Cohort Analysis* — nhóm customers theo time of acquisition và track behavior over time. Reveals: are newer customers better or worse than older cohorts? Are retention rates improving? (2) *Funnel Analysis* — track conversion rates tại mỗi stage của sales funnel. Reveals: where are leads dropping off? Which stages have highest leverage? (3) *Segmentation Analysis* — phân khúc data theo dimensions (industry, deal size, channel) để find which segments perform best; (4) *Trend Analysis* — track metrics over time để identify growing, stable, hoặc declining trends; (5) *Attribution Analysis* — credit which activities/channels contributed to wins.

**Descriptive vs Diagnostic vs Predictive Analytics**: (1) *Descriptive* (What happened?) — basic reporting: "Revenue last quarter was X, up Y%"; (2) *Diagnostic* (Why did it happen?) — "Revenue grew because enterprise segment expanded 40% while SMB contracted 15% due to..."; (3) *Predictive* (What will happen?) — "Based on pipeline coverage và historical close rates, next quarter revenue will be..."; (4) *Prescriptive* (What should we do?) — "To hit next quarter target, we need to add X deals to pipeline at stage Y by Z date." Progress từ descriptive toward prescriptive creates more actionable intelligence.

**Statistical Thinking Basics**: không cần advanced statistics, nhưng cần: (1) *Sample size awareness* — "3 customers complained về X, so we need to fix X" — is 3 out of 10 significant? Out of 1,000 less so; (2) *Correlation vs causation* — "Customers who attended webinars have higher retention" — does webinar cause retention hoặc are engaged customers more likely to attend webinars AND have higher retention?; (3) *Outlier identification* — one unusual deal distorting averages; (4) *Time lag effects* — activities today affect outcomes weeks or months later.

**Visualization Best Practices**: good data visualization makes analysis accessible và actionable. Key principles: choose right chart type for data type (trends → line charts; comparisons → bar charts; proportions → pie or treemap; relationships → scatter plots); eliminate chartjunk (unnecessary decoration); use color purposefully, not decoratively; always label axes và include context.

> **Bài học:** Build one "North Star Dashboard" với 5-7 metrics most critical to business health — review weekly. Supporting metrics go in secondary dashboards for drill-down. Focus creates signal; too many metrics create noise. "What 5 numbers, if I knew them every Monday morning, would most help me make better decisions this week?" — start there.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Research on decision quality by Paul Nutt (Ohio State) analyzed 400+ major decisions và found: decisions based on single-option evaluation (Is this a good idea?) fail at twice the rate of decisions based on comparative evaluation (Which of these options is best, and why?). Good analysis always generates multiple options for comparison — never "should we do X?" alone but "should we do X, Y, hoặc Z?"

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Presenting analysis without recommendation. "Data shows X, Y, và Z." What should we do about it? Analysis without recommendation transfers burden back to listener. Best analytical communication concludes with clear "so what" and "now what" — here's what this means, and here's what I recommend we do.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Cherry-picking data that supports predetermined conclusion. Confirmation bias in data analysis is extremely common — we look for data that confirms what we already believe và ignore disconfirming data. Good analysis actively seeks data that challenges your hypothesis. "What would have to be true for my current plan to be wrong?"

> **Cạm bẫy:** Precision theater — presenting numbers with false precision (revenue will be exactly $4,237,450 next quarter) suggesting more certainty than the analysis supports. Better to communicate confidence intervals and assumptions: "Revenue will likely be $4-4.5M based on current pipeline và historical close rates — key assumption is that Q4 enterprise deals close on schedule."
