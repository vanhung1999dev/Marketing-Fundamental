### Tư Duy Chiến Lược

Nhìn xa hơn tactical day-to-day để thấy patterns lớn hơn, kết nối dots giữa xu hướng thị trường và business position, và ra quyết định hôm nay với hiểu biết về hệ quả dài hạn là tư duy phân biệt leader khỏi executor. Strategic thinking không phải là IQ hay experience — đó là thói quen đặt câu hỏi đúng.

**Strategic Questions vs Tactical Questions**: *Tactical*: "Tuần này chúng ta nên gọi cho khách nào?" *Strategic*: "Trong 3 năm tới, segment khách hàng nào sẽ có giá trị cao nhất với chúng ta và tại sao?" Tactical thinking operates within current constraints; strategic thinking questions and changes constraints. Cả hai cần thiết — nhưng nhiều professionals spend 95% thời gian tactical và 5% strategic, khi ratio tốt hơn là closer to 70/30 cho người ở leadership positions.

**Strategic Frameworks cho Sales Leaders**: (1) *BCG Growth-Share Matrix* — classify portfolio theo market growth và relative market share: Stars (high growth, high share), Cash Cows (low growth, high share), Question Marks (high growth, low share), Dogs (low growth, low share); (2) *Ansoff Matrix* — 4 growth strategies: Market Penetration (existing products, existing markets), Product Development, Market Development, Diversification; (3) *Blue Ocean Strategy* — create uncontested market space rather than competing in existing crowded markets.

**Systems Thinking**: strategic thinkers see systems, not just events. Ví dụ: "Sales giảm" (event) → "Do customer churn tăng" (pattern) → "Do product quality issues in Q3" (structure) → "Do QA resources được redirected to new product launch" (root cause). Surface solutions fix events; strategic solutions address structures. Ask "why" 5 lần để get to systemic causes.

**Scenario Planning**: thay vì forecasting một future, plan cho multiple plausible futures: "Nếu thị trường contract 30% trong 12 tháng, strategy của chúng ta là gì?" "Nếu competitor mới với funding lớn enter market, chúng ta respond thế nào?" "Nếu technology disruption thay đổi cách customers buy trong ngành này, chúng ta position thế nào?" Thinking through scenarios in advance prepares organization to respond faster and better when they occur.

> **Bài học:** Reserve 2 giờ mỗi tuần cho "strategic thinking time" — no meetings, no email, chỉ là uninterrupted thinking về big picture questions. Điều này sounds luxurious khi daily pressures are immense; nhưng research on executive effectiveness consistently shows "thinking time" is the highest-ROI activity for leaders. Protect it aggressively.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Roger Martin trong "The Opposable Mind" argues that strategic thinkers hold two opposing ideas simultaneously without resolving the tension — và find creative resolution that integrates both. Ví dụ: "We need to maximize short-term profit AND invest in long-term customer relationships" — not choose one or balance between them, but find approaches that genuinely do both. This integrative thinking capacity is core to strategic intelligence.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Mistake activity for strategy. "Strategy" that consists of list of tactics ("we'll do more LinkedIn, host more webinars, hire 3 more SDRs") is not strategy — it's tactic list. Strategy requires: clear choices about where to play và how to win, with explicit tradeoffs about what you will NOT do.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Strategy that lives only in documents and presentations. Strategy must be translated into daily decision-making: "Does this choice align with our strategy?" If strategy is not being used to make real decisions và trade-offs, it's not real strategy — it's aspiration document.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Copying competitors' strategy. "Company X is doing Y successfully — we should do Y too." This ignores that Y might work for X given their specific position, capabilities, và customer base, while being wrong for you. Strategy must be grounded in your specific context và competitive position, not simply imported from others' success stories.
