### Phân Tích Nguyên Nhân Gốc Rễ

Đào sâu qua lớp triệu chứng và nguyên nhân bề mặt để tìm đến nguyên nhân gốc rễ thực sự của vấn đề là điều kiện để giải quyết vấn đề một lần thay vì liên tục fix symptoms. Tổ chức thiếu kỹ năng root cause analysis thường tốn enormous energy "fighting fires" mà không bao giờ eliminate nguồn gốc của những đám cháy đó.

**5 Whys Technique**: hỏi "Tại sao?" 5 lần liên tiếp cho đến khi reach root cause. Example: *Problem*: Sales pipeline thiếu. *Why?* Rep không đủ first meetings. *Why?* Low email reply rates. *Why?* Email messaging không compelling. *Why?* Messaging không specific đến pain points của ICPs. *Why?* ICP definition quá broad, rep không biết specific pain points. Root cause: ICP definition problem — not a sales activity problem. Fix: refine ICP và develop pain-point specific messaging. Without 5 Whys, response might be "increase email volume" — treats symptom, not cause.

**Fishbone (Ishikawa) Diagram**: visual tool for organizing multiple potential root causes. Main categories: People (skills, training, motivation), Process (workflow, procedures), Systems/Tools (technology, data), Environment (market, competition), Materials (product, content). Mapping all potential causes under categories before analyzing prevents tunnel vision on the first obvious cause.

**Pareto Analysis (80/20)**: find the 20% of root causes responsible for 80% of the problem. Often, multiple root causes exist — Pareto analysis helps prioritize which to address first for maximum impact. Fix the vital few, not the trivial many.

**Correlation vs Causation Rigor**: in root cause analysis, easy to stop at correlation. "Churn is highest among customers who didn't attend onboarding training" — does lack of training cause churn (causal) hoặc do customers planning to churn also skip training (reverse causation) hoặc là both driven by third variable (low engagement)? Causal rigor requires ruling out alternatives — ideally through testing.

> **Bài học:** After every significant miss (below target quarter, lost deal, customer churn) conduct structured root cause analysis meeting: (1) State the problem precisely; (2) Run 5 Whys; (3) Identify top 2-3 root causes; (4) Assign clear owner và deadline to each fix. Without this ritual, teams repeat same mistakes in different forms.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Toyota Production System's approach to problems: every defect is a gift that reveals a system weakness. "Blame the process, not the person" — most problems come from systems và processes that allow or enable errors, not from individual bad actors. This mental model shifts root cause analysis from blame-seeking to system-improvement seeking — much more productive.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Stopping at first-level cause. "We missed quota because the market was tough" is a first-level explanation that prevents learning. Market toughness is context, not root cause. What specifically happened trong that tough market? Which activities or approaches underperformed? The real root cause is usually within your control.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Doing root cause analysis blame-style — "Who messed up?" Root cause analysis is most effective when focused on systemic issues ("What in our process allowed this to happen?") rather than individual fault. Blame-focused RCA leads to people hiding information to avoid blame, preventing accurate root cause identification.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Root cause finding without implementation. Many organizations are good at identifying root causes in retrospectives but poor at systematically fixing them. Track root causes found in retrospectives alongside fix implementation status. If the same root causes appear repeatedly across incidents, the fix implementation is failing — address that.
