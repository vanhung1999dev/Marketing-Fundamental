### Tư Duy Dựa Trên Bằng Chứng

Đưa ra quyết định dựa trên evidence — data, research, tested hypotheses — thay vì solely dựa trên gut feeling, past experience, hoặc HiPPO (Highest Paid Person's Opinion) là foundation của modern business decision-making. Evidence-based thinking không loại trừ intuition; nó uses evidence để calibrate và challenge intuition.

**Evidence Hierarchy**: không phải mọi evidence đều equal. Từ most to least reliable: (1) *Systematic reviews và meta-analyses* — aggregate evidence từ nhiều studies; (2) *Randomized Controlled Trials* — direct experimental evidence; (3) *Observational studies* — correlation evidence from real data; (4) *Expert opinion* — valuable but subject to bias; (5) *Anecdotal evidence* — single cases; informative but not generalizable. Business decisions often rely on 3-5 in hierarchy; being aware of limitations is key.

**A/B Testing Mindset**: wherever possible, test before scaling. "I think email subject line A will outperform B" — test it with small sample. "I think changing pricing structure will increase conversion" — test it in one market segment first. A/B testing replaces opinion with data, especially valuable for decisions where intuition is unreliable (customer psychology, pricing sensitivity, content effectiveness).

**Hypothesis-Driven Decision Making**: before any major analysis or decision, state your hypothesis explicitly: "My hypothesis is that churn is driven primarily by poor onboarding rather than product quality." Then design analysis to test it. This prevents confirmation bias in analysis và makes learning more explicit — you either confirm hypothesis (plan accordingly) hoặc reject it (update your mental model).

**Pre-mortem Analysis**: before implementing a decision, run a pre-mortem: "Imagine it's 12 months from now và this decision failed completely. What most likely caused the failure?" This is powerful because it surfaces risks và assumptions that optimism bias suppresses in normal planning. Evidence-based thinking also means being honest about what could go wrong, not just what you hope will go right.

> **Bài học:** Start meetings about important decisions with "What do we know?" (evidence) separate from "What do we believe?" (opinion và hypothesis). Explicitly labeling the difference prevents opinion from being presented as fact và creates space for more rigorous evidence-seeking before committing. "We know X because of [data], we believe Y because of [reasoning], and we're uncertain about Z."

> **Phân tích sâu:** Daniel Kahneman's Thinking Fast and Slow identifies two decision-making systems: System 1 (fast, intuitive, pattern-based) và System 2 (slow, deliberate, analytical). System 1 is efficient for familiar decisions; System 2 is necessary for complex, novel, or high-stakes decisions. Evidence-based thinking is essentially activating System 2 deliberately for decisions that deserve it.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Treating "I've seen this before" as sufficient evidence. Past experience is valuable prior; it's not infallible guide. Markets change, customer preferences evolve, competitive dynamics shift. "This worked in 2019" is weakly predictive of "this will work in 2024." Combine experience với current data.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Waiting for perfect evidence before acting. In business, perfect evidence is rarely available và decisions often can't wait. The right standard is "sufficient evidence for this decision at this risk level" — not certainty. Risk-adjust your evidence threshold: high-stakes decisions warrant higher evidence bar than low-stakes reversible ones.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Evidence shopping — seeking out data that confirms a decision already made emotionally. This is common and insidious because it feels like being rigorous. Challenge: when you find yourself gathering evidence, ask "Am I testing my hypothesis or am I building a case for what I already want to do?" The honest answer changes the process.
