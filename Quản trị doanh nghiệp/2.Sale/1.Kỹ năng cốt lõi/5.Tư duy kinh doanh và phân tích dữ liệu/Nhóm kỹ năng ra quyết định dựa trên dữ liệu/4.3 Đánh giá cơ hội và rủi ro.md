### Đánh Giá Cơ Hội Và Rủi Ro

Đánh giá cơ hội và rủi ro một cách systematic — không phải intuitive chỉ theo gut feeling — và communicate risk-opportunity trade-offs rõ ràng là kỹ năng của người ra quyết định kinh doanh tốt. Opportunity without risk analysis is gambling; risk without opportunity analysis is paralysis.

**Opportunity Assessment Framework**: khi đánh giá business opportunity (new market, new product, new deal), assess 4 dimensions: (1) *Size* — market size, revenue potential nếu capture target market share; (2) *Fit* — alignment với current capabilities và strategy; cost và time to build what's missing; (3) *Timing* — is market ready? Are we early, right time, hoặc late? (4) *Competition* — current landscape, barriers to entry, sustainable differentiation. Score each dimension để get comparable view across multiple opportunities.

**Risk Taxonomy**: classify risks by type để ensure comprehensive coverage: (1) *Market risk* — demand không như expected, competition intensifies, pricing pressure; (2) *Execution risk* — can we deliver? Do we have capabilities, capacity, process?; (3) *Financial risk* — cash flow impact, ROI below threshold, cost overruns; (4) *Relationship risk* — impact on existing customer và partner relationships; (5) *Regulatory/Legal risk* — compliance, contract obligations; (6) *Dependency risk* — relying on specific person, partner, or technology.

**Expected Value Thinking**: risk-adjusted decision making uses Expected Value: EV = (Probability of Outcome × Value of Outcome) summed across scenarios. Ví dụ: *Scenario A* (60% probability × $5M revenue = $3M EV) vs *Scenario B* (30% probability × $15M revenue = $4.5M EV). EV approach prevents over-weighting of certain small outcomes vs uncertain large outcomes — a common cognitive bias.

**Risk Mitigation vs Risk Acceptance**: not all risks need mitigation. Classify risks by: *Probability* (high/low) × *Impact* (high/low). High-probability, high-impact risks need mitigation. Low-probability, low-impact risks can be accepted. High-probability, low-impact risks need process efficiency. Low-probability, high-impact risks need contingency plans. This classification focuses mitigation effort where it creates most value.

> **Bài học:** Before any major initiative, run "Risk Register" exercise: list all potential risks, rate each on probability (1-5) và impact (1-5), multiply for risk score, rank by score. Then assign mitigation plan for top risks. This 30-minute exercise consistently surfaces risks that intuition misses và creates shared understanding of risk landscape across stakeholders.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Daniel Kahneman's work on prospect theory shows: people are loss-averse (losses feel roughly 2x worse than equivalent gains feel good). This means businesses systematically over-weight downside risk relative to upside opportunity — leading to excessive conservatism. Awareness of loss aversion doesn't eliminate it but allows explicit override when expected value calculation supports taking asymmetric risk.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Binary risk thinking — "This is risky" or "This is safe" — without nuance about magnitude và probability. Most opportunities have manageable risk at right scale và timing. Avoid blanket risk judgments; instead quantify and compare.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Not considering cost of inaction as a risk. "Let's wait until we're more certain" has a risk: market moves, competitor gains ground, opportunity closes. Inaction is itself a choice with risk profile that should be explicitly evaluated.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Risk assessment becomes CYA (Cover Your Ass) exercise — documenting risks to protect against blame if things go wrong rather than to inform decisions. When risk assessment is weaponized for organizational politics, honest risk evaluation becomes impossible. Foster culture where surfacing risks is valued, not penalized.
