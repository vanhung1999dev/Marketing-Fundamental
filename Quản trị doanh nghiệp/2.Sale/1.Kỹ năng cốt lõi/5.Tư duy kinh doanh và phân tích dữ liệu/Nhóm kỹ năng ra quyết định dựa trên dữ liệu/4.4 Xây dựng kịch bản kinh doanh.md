### Xây Dựng Kịch Bản Kinh Doanh

Phát triển nhiều kịch bản plausible cho tương lai — thay vì chỉ một dự báo point — và prepare responses cho mỗi kịch bản là cách doanh nghiệp "pre-adapt" thay vì chỉ react khi tương lai unfolds. Scenario planning không dự báo future chính xác — nó expands thinking về what futures are possible và ensures you're not blindsided.

**Scenario Planning vs Forecasting**: *Forecasting* dự báo most likely future với precision: "Revenue Q4 sẽ là $4.2M." *Scenario planning* develops multiple plausible futures với distinct narratives: "In high-growth scenario (25% probability), revenue is $5M. In base case (50% probability), $4M. In downturn scenario (25% probability), $2.5M." Scenario planning more honest about uncertainty; forecasting more actionable for operational planning. Best practice: use both — scenarios for strategy, forecasts for operations.

**3-Scenario Standard Set**: minimum viable scenario planning uses 3 scenarios: (1) *Upside/Best case* — if key assumptions prove more favorable than expected; (2) *Base case* — most likely outcome given current information và trends; (3) *Downside/Stress case* — if key risks materialize. For each scenario: what would need to be true? What are the key indicators we'd see early? What's our response?

**Business Case Structure**: trong B2B sales context, building business case for customer decision: (1) *Problem quantification* — cost of current situation in dollars; (2) *Solution benefit quantification* — expected improvement và value; (3) *Investment required* — total cost of ownership (not just purchase price); (4) *ROI calculation* — payback period, 3-year NPV; (5) *Risk factors* — what could make expected benefits not materialize; (6) *Comparison alternatives* — vs doing nothing, vs other solutions.

**Sensitivity Analysis**: identify key assumptions in your scenario / business case và test how results change when assumptions change. "If conversion rate improves 5% instead of expected 10%, ROI drops from 3x to 2.1x." Sensitivity analysis reveals which assumptions most critically drive the outcome — these become key monitoring targets.

> **Bài học:** Với major investment proposals (hiring, technology, expansion), always present 3-scenario business case: base, upside, và downside. Include "minimum conditions for success" — what must be true for this to work at all? Presenting scenarios signals analytical rigor và helps decision-makers think probabilistically rather than reacting to single-point projection.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Royal Dutch Shell pioneered scenario planning in 1970s and is credited with being better prepared for 1973 oil crisis because they had already developed scenarios including one resembling what occurred. The value wasn't predicting the crisis but having thought through it in advance — enabling faster, more coherent response. "Scenario planning doesn't predict the future; it makes you better at responding to any future."

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Scenarios that are too similar — optimistic case is "things go slightly better than expected," pessimistic case is "things go slightly worse." True scenario planning explores genuinely different futures driven by different assumptions — not just variance around one central expectation.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Developing scenarios but not pre-deciding response triggers. "If market contracts 30%, we'll have to figure out what to do then." Better: "If our monthly new pipeline add drops below $X for 2 consecutive months — that's our signal for downturn scenario — and our pre-committed response is [specific actions]." Pre-commitment prevents panic-driven reactive decisions.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Scenario theater — elaborate scenario planning documents that impress but don't actually change how decisions are made. Scenario planning is only valuable if it changes behavior: different resource allocation, different risk mitigation actions, different monitoring priorities. If nothing changes because of the scenarios, the effort was wasted.
