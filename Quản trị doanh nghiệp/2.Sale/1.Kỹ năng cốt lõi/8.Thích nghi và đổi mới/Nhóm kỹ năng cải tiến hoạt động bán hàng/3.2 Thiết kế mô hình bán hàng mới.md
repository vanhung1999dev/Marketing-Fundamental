### Thiết Kế Mô Hình Bán Hàng Mới

Khi assessment reveals cần thay đổi fundamental — không chỉ optimize hiện tại mà redesign — thách thức là thiết kế mô hình bán hàng mới một cách thoughtful, grounded in evidence, và implemented một cách minimizes disruption. Đây là cả art và science: đủ creative để imagine something genuinely better, đủ analytical để design it rigorously, và đủ pragmatic để implement it successfully while maintaining current business.

**Sales Model Design Dimensions**: designing new sales model requires decisions across multiple dimensions simultaneously. *Go-to-market motion* — inbound vs. outbound, product-led growth vs. sales-led, field vs. inside vs. digital; *Coverage model* — SMB self-serve, mid-market inside sales, enterprise field sales, or some combination; *Specialization level* — full-cycle AEs vs. specialized SDR/AE/AM model; *Channel strategy* — direct vs. through partner/reseller channel; *Pricing and packaging* — subscription vs. consumption vs. perpetual, land-and-expand vs. full platform sale. Each decision has implications for required talent, process, technology, and economics.

**Evidence-Based Model Design**: new model should be designed from evidence, not aspirations. Evidence sources: customer research ("how do companies like our ICP actually prefer to buy? What's their buying process?"); market research (how are competitors selling? What's working in analogous markets?); financial modeling (what unit economics does each model option produce?); capability assessment (what can we actually build and staff, versus what requires capabilities we don't have?). Models designed from evidence rather than inspiration have much higher success rate.

**Phased Transition vs. Big Bang**: transitioning from current to new model has two approaches: *Phased transition* — run new model in parallel with current model (e.g., new PLG motion for SMB while maintaining enterprise sales motion), validate, then expand; safer but slower and creates operational complexity; *Big bang* — switch entirely to new model at once; faster but higher risk if new model has flaws. Most B2B companies with existing revenue opt for phased approach: protect existing revenue with current model while building and validating new model in controlled environment.

**Pilot Design for New Model**: before scaling new model, pilot với controlled scope: specific segment (one vertical or company size), specific geography, specific team (2-4 reps volunteer for pilot). Define success criteria before pilot starts (what specific results would validate the model?); collect comprehensive data during pilot (not just revenue, but activity metrics, conversion rates, rep experience); debrief rigorously after pilot (what worked? what didn't? what needs adjustment?). Well-designed pilot generates actionable learnings that reduce risk of broader rollout.

> **Bài học:** Before finalizing new model design, run "Future Back" exercise: envision what success looks like 3 years from now if new model works as hoped — describe it in concrete detail (metrics, customer types, team composition, processes). Then work backwards: "what needs to be true at 2 years for that 3-year vision to be achievable? At 1 year? In 6 months?" This backward mapping reveals critical path assumptions và dependencies that forward planning often misses, and creates clearer milestones for assessing whether new model is on track.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Harvard Business Review research on business model innovation shows: the most successful sales model transitions start by understanding "jobs to be done" — what fundamental job does the customer hire our solution to do? — và design selling model around enabling customers to achieve that job most effectively. Companies that design around product features vs. customer jobs-to-be-done consistently produce models that feel seller-centric rather than buyer-centric, generating friction in adoption.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Designing new model in executive team isolation without input from frontline reps or customers. Executives have strategic perspective but often lack operational detail about where current model actually fails và what customers actually experience. Involving frontline perspective in model design produces both better design and better adoption (reps who contributed to design feel ownership over implementing it).

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Designing perfect model on paper without considering implementation feasibility. "We'll need 20 enterprise AEs with 10 years of experience each" — but your current team has 5 reps and talent market is competitive. Model design must be grounded in what you can actually staff, lead, và execute given realistic constraints. Beautiful model that you can't implement is a business plan fantasy.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Model redesign that ignores existing customer relationship implications. Transitioning from "assigned rep" model to "pooled CS" model might improve efficiency but damages relationships with existing customers who value their dedicated contact. New model must account for impact on existing customer experience, not just new customer acquisition.
