### Đánh Giá Hiệu Quả Mô Hình Hiện Tại

Trước khi thay đổi bất cứ điều gì, cần hiểu rõ current state — không chỉ kết quả (revenue, quota attainment) mà còn underlying drivers, structural strengths, hidden assumptions, và true constraints. Nhiều tổ chức "improve" mô hình bán hàng mà thực ra chỉ đang thêm complexity vào một foundation chưa được hiểu rõ. Đánh giá hiệu quả mô hình hiện tại là foundation của mọi cải tiến có chủ đích.

**Sales Model Assessment Framework**: đánh giá toàn diện mô hình bán hàng hiện tại cần xem xét: *Strategy* — are we targeting the right customers với the right value proposition? *Process* — is our sales process efficient, does it match buyer journey, where are bottlenecks?; *People* — do we have right number of reps with right skills in right roles?; *Performance* — how do our metrics compare to industry benchmarks và our own historical performance?; *Technology* — is our tech stack enabling or hindering?; *Culture* — does team culture reinforce right behaviors? Each dimension affects others; improvement in one dimension while ignoring others produces suboptimal results.

**Benchmarking: Internal và External**: effective model assessment requires two types of benchmarking. *Internal* — how are we performing now versus 3-12 months ago? Are metrics improving, flat, or declining? Which metrics are improving while others aren't (revealing where leverage is)? *External* — how are we performing versus relevant industry benchmarks? Sources: SaaS benchmarks from OpenView, Bessemer, ChartMogul; sales benchmarks from Salesforce State of Sales, CSO Insights, RAIN Group research. Benchmarking provides context: 25% win rate might be excellent in some markets and mediocre in others.

**Unit Economics Deep Dive**: sales model efficiency is best evaluated through unit economics lens: *CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)* — what does it cost to acquire one new customer? (Total sales + marketing spend / new customers acquired); *CAC Payback Period* — how long to recover acquisition cost from customer revenue?; *CLV/CAC ratio* — is lifetime value high enough relative to acquisition cost to justify the model?; *Sales Efficiency* — how much new ARR does each dollar of sales spend generate? These metrics reveal whether current model is economically sustainable and where improvement creates most leverage.

**What to Preserve, What to Change**: good model assessment distinguishes between elements to preserve (working well, part of competitive advantage, don't fix what isn't broken) và elements to change (underperforming, creating friction, misaligned with market). Common error: "transformation" that changes everything indiscriminately, destroying things that worked while fixing things that didn't. The assessment should produce a clear map: "These are our genuine strengths to protect and amplify; these are specific constraints and inefficiencies to address."

> **Bài học:** Conduct "Sales Model Health Check" annually — not just reviewing results but assessing the model that produces results. Use structured template: grade each dimension (Strategy, Process, People, Performance, Technology, Culture) on 1-10 scale with evidence supporting each rating. Low grades trigger deep-dive analysis; high grades trigger questions about whether you're measuring correctly. Share assessment with leadership and senior reps for additional perspective — your view as manager is one of several important perspectives.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Bain & Company research on sales effectiveness: "most sales performance improvement programs focus on rep skills training, but the highest ROI interventions address structural issues: wrong ICP, misaligned incentives, territory inequities, process bottlenecks." The implication: if you've tried skills training multiple times without sustained performance improvement, the root cause may be structural — model-level issues that training cannot fix. Assessment must look below symptom level to structural level.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Assessing only what's easy to measure (revenue, quota attainment) while ignoring what's harder to measure (sales culture, ICP fit, buyer experience, team morale). The less-measurable dimensions often hold key to unlock the measured ones. Invest in qualitative assessment — customer interviews, rep surveys, deal autopsies — alongside quantitative dashboard review.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Assessment that produces insights but no decisions. "We identified 7 areas for improvement" → nothing changes. Assessment must produce prioritized action plan: top 2-3 highest-leverage interventions to pursue in next 6-12 months, with owners, timelines, và success metrics. Without clear output, assessment is academic exercise.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Assessment biased toward validating current approach rather than genuinely questioning it. When assessment is conducted by people who designed và are invested in current model, it has natural tendency to explain problems as execution issues ("reps need to be coached harder") rather than model issues ("our ICP assumption is wrong"). Periodic external perspective — outside consultant, peer company benchmark, board advisor — provides less biased view.
