### Tiếp Nhận Phản Hồi (Receiving Feedback)

Khả năng tiếp nhận phản hồi — đặc biệt là negative feedback — một cách genuinely open, without defensiveness, và using it constructively, là một trong những learning capabilities quan trọng nhất. Many salespeople are high achievers who are confident in their approach — which is strength in execution but can become weakness in learning if confidence becomes defensiveness to feedback. "I already know how to sell" is the statement that stops learning và starts decline.

**The Defensive Reaction Mechanism**: khi nhận negative feedback, brain's default response is defensive — amygdala triggers threat response ("my competence is being questioned") before prefrontal cortex can engage with the content of feedback. Physical signs: tightening, urge to explain/justify, difficulty really hearing what's being said. Recognizing this reaction in yourself is first step to overriding it. Practices: pause before responding (let defensive reaction subside); ask yourself "what can I learn from this?" before responding; thank the person before engaging with content ("thank you for sharing this").

**Separating Feedback Quality from Emotional Impact**: not all feedback is equally valid — some feedback is well-observed and actionable; some is poorly calibrated or not representative. But emotional impact of feedback (even from source you respect) can compromise your ability to distinguish high-quality from low-quality feedback in the moment. Practice: write down feedback you receive; sleep on it; then evaluate quality with fresh perspective: "What's accurate here? What's not? What can I apply? What should I discount?" Immediate emotional processing often produces either over-acceptance ("they're totally right about everything") or over-rejection ("they don't know what they're talking about").

**Seeking Feedback Proactively**: top performers don't wait for feedback to be delivered — they seek it actively. Post-call: "What did you notice that I could have done differently?" Post-deal: "From your perspective, what was our biggest weakness in this process?" From customers: "If you were to advise a competitor of ours on how to beat us, what would you tell them?" Proactive feedback seeking signals: you're serious about development; you can handle honest input; you value the other person's perspective. It also generates higher quality feedback than feedback delivered without being asked, because the asker controls the question specificity.

**Feedback from Customer as Gold Standard**: customer feedback on sales experience is rarest and most valuable — they see things from entirely different angle than internal observers. Sources: post-close survey ("what could we have done better in the sales process?"); win/loss interviews conducted by neutral party (customers more candid when not talking to their salesperson); customer advisory board feedback; NPS comment themes. Customer feedback cuts through internal blind spots and assumptions to deliver ground truth about what's working from buyer's perspective.

> **Bài học:** After every significant loss, reach out to the economic buyer (not just your main contact) with a specific request: "We genuinely want to learn how to serve companies like yours better. Would you be willing to spend 20 minutes sharing what drove your decision and what we could have done differently? No sales agenda — purely learning." Most buyers who respected the process will say yes. The insights from 5 such conversations often reveal patterns that no amount of internal analysis can surface.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Research on high-performer learning agility (Korn Ferry) shows: willingness to seek and accept feedback — specifically including feedback about weaknesses and blind spots — is one of the strongest predictors of career performance and progression. The mechanism: those who seek feedback identify và address weaknesses faster; those who avoid feedback continue on path with uncorrected errors. Over a 5-10 year career, this compounds dramatically into a large performance gap.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Soliciting feedback in ways that discourage honest response. "You don't have any feedback for me, do you?" or "I think that went really well, what did you think?" biases the response toward positive. Ask in ways that genuinely invite honest input: "What's one specific thing I could have done better?" Specificity invites specific useful feedback; open-ended praise-seeking invites validation.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Seeking feedback but not visibly applying it. If you ask for feedback but person giving it never sees you do anything differently, they learn: "He asks but doesn't really want it." Close the loop: "You gave me feedback on [X] last month. I've been trying [Y] as result. Here's what I've observed." This signals that feedback actually changed behavior, which makes the person more willing to give candid feedback in future.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Accepting all feedback uncritically. Some feedback is poorly calibrated, reflects the giver's preferences rather than objective observation, hoặc is delivered without adequate context. Receiving feedback constructively doesn't mean accepting all of it as truth. Evaluate each piece: is this consistent with other feedback I've received? Does the giver have relevant vantage point to observe this? Is there evidence that supports or contradicts this assessment? Thoughtful evaluation, not wholesale acceptance or rejection, is the goal.
