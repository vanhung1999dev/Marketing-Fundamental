### Kiểm Soát Cảm Xúc Trong Đàm Phán

Quản lý cảm xúc của bản thân và recognize cảm xúc của đối tác để đưa ra phản ứng có chiến lược thay vì phản ứng instinctive là kỹ năng nền tảng của high-stakes negotiation. "Never make a permanent decision based on a temporary emotion" — nguyên tắc này đặc biệt critical trong đàm phán nơi mỗi decision có consequences dài hạn.

**Negotiation Emotional Traps**: các cảm xúc phổ biến phá hủy negotiation outcomes: (1) *Anger* — làm đối tác defensive, tạo ra hardened positions, và damages relationship; occasionally theatrical anger có thể be tactical but genuine anger rarely helps; (2) *Anxiety* — drives premature concessions và over-talking; anxiety là dấu hiệu của weak BATNA hoặc over-preparation gap; (3) *Excitement at winning* — signals bạn got a great deal → đối tác feels they over-conceded → relationship damaged → future negotiations harder; (4) *Guilt at demanding* — many salespeople feel guilty asking for what they deserve; guilt leads to premature concessions.

**The STOP Technique**: khi sensing emotional escalation — của mình hoặc của họ: *Stop* (pause conversation, "Cho tôi một phút nghĩ về điều này"), *Think* (what's happening? what do I need?), *Observe* (what's driving their/my emotional state?), *Proceed* (với clearer head và intentional response). Physical pause — glass of water, note-taking break — creates circuit-breaker between trigger và response.

**Tactical Empathy**: khi đối tác escalates emotionally, direct engagement với logic thường backfire. Acknowledge emotional state first: "Tôi thấy điều này frustrating — tôi muốn understand concern của anh tốt hơn." Labeling emotions ("Có vẻ như anh đang concerned về...") deflates emotional charge mà không agreeing with the position. Research shows: labeling negative emotions reduces amygdala activation — literally calming down the emotional brain.

**Using Positive Emotions Strategically**: không phải mọi emotion cần suppress. Enthusiasm về possibility, genuine curiosity, và satisfaction khi finding creative solutions are positive forces. Showing genuine positive emotion at deal progress (không phải about winning, mà về solving problem together) builds collaborative atmosphere.

> **Bài học:** Develop personal "emotional decompression routine" cho trước high-stakes negotiations: light exercise, breathing exercises, hoặc simply structured quiet time to ground yourself. Research confirms physiological state affects negotiation performance. Entering with elevated cortisol (stress) impairs prefrontal cortex function — literally makes you worse at strategic thinking.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Research bởi Alison Wood Brooks tại Harvard shows controlled expression của strategic anxiety in negotiation backfires — người lo lắng make more concessions và achieve worse outcomes. However, reframing anxiety as excitement ("Tôi excited về opportunity này") achieves better outcomes. Biologically, anxiety và excitement share same arousal state — cognitive reframe changes subjective experience và performance.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Responding to tactics rather than interests — khi bị lowballed hay time-pressured, responding với anger or defensiveness to the tactic rather than calmly refocusing on interests. Tactics are designed to elicit emotional reactions; recognizing them as tactics (not reality) maintains emotional equilibrium.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Emotional suppression instead of regulation — trying to feel nothing rather than acknowledging and channeling emotions. Suppression consumes cognitive resources (it's exhausting to suppress) and often leaks through nonverbal channels anyway. Acknowledge → label → refocus is more sustainable.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Using emotional display as manipulation — theatrical anger, manufactured tears, or performed frustration. Experienced negotiators quickly identify manufactured emotions; when identified, it catastrophically damages credibility và relationship. Strategic emotional awareness is different from emotional manipulation.
