### Quản Lý Hiệu Suất Đội Ngũ

Team performance management — cả giữ team on track và address khi nó không on track — là operational core của sales leadership. Nó encompasses: clear goals và expectations, regular progress monitoring, timely intervention when performance deviates, và creating accountability system that motivates without micromanaging. The best sales teams are "high accountability, high support" — clear expectations held firmly, with genuine manager investment in helping each rep succeed.

**Establishing Clear Expectations**: performance management begins before performance period starts — establishing crystal-clear expectations. Every rep should know: their quota and how it breaks down (monthly, quarterly); what activities are expected (call volume, pipeline coverage minimum); what processes must be followed (CRM logging, deal review cadence); what behaviors are non-negotiable (customer integrity standards, collaboration expectations). Vague expectations create plausible deniability for underperformance và confusion for honest reps trying to succeed. Written expectations, confirmed with rep, remove ambiguity.

**Regular Performance Conversations**: performance management is continuous, not point-in-time. Weekly 1:1 has performance component: "How is your week tracking versus plan? Where are you getting stuck? What support do you need?" Monthly review goes deeper: "Here's your performance versus targets this month, here's what I observed in your work, here are 1-2 specific things I want you to focus on next month." Quarterly formal review: full assessment across all dimensions — results, activities, skills, behaviors — with formal documentation. Each cadence serves different purpose; all are necessary.

**Early Warning System**: don't wait until end of quarter to discover performance problems. Build early warning system: weekly activity tracking reveals if rep is behind on prospecting; mid-month pipeline review reveals whether month is at risk; 6-week-out pipeline assessment for quarter reveals Q-end risk early enough to course-correct. Early warning enables coaching intervention at a time when intervention can still affect outcome — not post-mortem conversation after quarter ends.

**Accountability Without Micromanagement**: there's critical difference between "I hold you accountable for outcomes" and "I monitor every activity and override your judgment." High accountability: clear metrics, regular honest reviews, consequences real when performance is persistently below standard. Micromanagement: checking every email, requiring approval for every call, second-guessing every rep decision. The former motivates ownership and develops judgment; the latter creates dependency and resentment. Aim for "tight on expectations, loose on how."

> **Bài học:** After each quarterly review cycle, do "Review Quality Assessment" — did each rep receive honest, specific, actionable feedback? Did each rep know their performance status accurately before formal review (no surprises)? Did reviews lead to clear action plans that were followed through? If reviews feel like compliance exercise rather than genuine development conversations, redesign the process. Best indicator of review quality: how quickly can rep articulate what they're working on based on their last review conversation.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Deloitte research on performance management shows: companies that replaced annual performance reviews with more frequent ongoing conversations saw 14% improvement in employee engagement and 12% improvement in productivity. The reason: infrequent formal reviews are too lagged to change near-term behavior, create anxiety disproportionate to their content, and feel disconnected from day-to-day work reality. Continuous performance conversations — embedded in normal work cadence — are more effective than annual review theater.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Holding performance conversations only when things go wrong. Reps learn to associate performance conversations with negative feedback; manager conversations become something to dread rather than value. Regular performance conversations that include both "here's what's working — keep doing this" and "here's what to develop" normalize the conversation and reduce its threat level.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Different standards for different reps. If some reps are held to strict pipeline hygiene standards while others are allowed to maintain messy CRM because "they're senior," the inconsistency erodes team morale và trust. Consistent standards, consistently applied — with appropriate coaching support — communicate that performance management is about enabling success, not about selectivity.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Confusing "tight performance management" với "high-pressure environment." Tight performance management means: clear expectations, regular honest feedback, genuine support to improve, consequences when performance is persistently below standard despite support. High-pressure environment means: constant threat, public shaming, fear-based motivation, no genuine support. First creates high performance culture; second creates high-turnover culture. The manager's intent and method — not the rigor itself — determines which environment is created.
