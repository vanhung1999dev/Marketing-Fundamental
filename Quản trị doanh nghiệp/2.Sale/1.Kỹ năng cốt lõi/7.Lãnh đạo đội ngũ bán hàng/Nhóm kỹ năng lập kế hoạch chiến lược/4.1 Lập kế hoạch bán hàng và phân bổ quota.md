### Lập Kế Hoạch Bán Hàng Và Phân Bổ Quota

Sales planning — setting annual goals, strategies, và resource allocation — là foundation của year's performance. Done well: team có clarity về what they're trying to accomplish, how, và why resources are deployed certain way. Done poorly: quota feels arbitrary, team doesn't understand the plan, và strategic priorities shift quarterly because no solid plan exists. The planning process itself — inclusive, analytical, reality-based — is as important as the plan output.

**Top-Down + Bottom-Up Planning**: effective sales planning combines both directions. *Top-down*: company sets overall revenue target based on investor expectations, market opportunity, và growth stage; this cascades down as guidance to sales. *Bottom-up*: each rep or territory owner builds their own assessment of what they can realistically achieve given territory potential, current pipeline, account expansion opportunities, and historical performance. Reconciling gap between top-down target và bottom-up estimate is where honest planning happens. If gap is large, it reveals either territory/headcount gaps that need addressing, or expectation calibration needed.

**Quota Allocation Principles**: how you divide team quota across individual reps determines who can win and who is structurally disadvantaged. Principles: *Equal opportunity, not equal quota* — rep in larger territory may have higher quota but proportional to opportunity; *Historical baseline* — start with historical performance plus realistic growth rate; *Ramp adjustments* — new reps shouldn't have full year quota in first year; *Coverage for risk* — total team quota typically set at 100-120% of company target to account for attrition and misses. Quota that feels achievable yet challenging is highly motivating; quota that feels impossible regardless of effort demoralizes team.

**Go-to-Market Strategy Components**: annual plan should specify: *Target segments* (which customer types, company sizes, industries are priority?); *Territories and accounts* (who owns what?); *Sales motion* (how do we sell — inbound vs. outbound, product-led vs. sales-led, full-cycle vs. specialized?); *Coverage model* (SMB/mid-market/enterprise ratios); *Headcount plan* (how many reps needed, when to hire, how fast to ramp?). Without these elements, quota numbers float without strategic grounding — there's no shared understanding of how the team will achieve its targets.

**Scenario Planning in Sales**: best plans account for uncertainty. Build 3 scenarios: *Base case* (most likely outcome); *Upside case* (conditions go better than expected: deal sizes larger, market momentum builds, new product launch accelerates); *Downside case* (things go worse: key rep leaves, competitive entrant disrupts, market softens). Pre-agreeing on trigger conditions for each scenario enables fast response when real situation unfolds, rather than reactive panic mid-year.

> **Bài học:** Include senior reps in annual planning process — both for their market intelligence (what's realistic in their territory, what customers are saying about priorities) và for buy-in. Reps who helped build the plan own it more than those who receive it from above. This doesn't mean reps set their own quota — but their input should visibly inform the final plan. "I saw your input in the territory allocation, and it shaped my thinking" is more motivating than "here's your number, good luck."

> **Phân tích sâu:** McKinsey research on sales force effectiveness shows: companies that conduct rigorous annual sales planning — explicitly allocating resources to highest-opportunity segments, adjusting coverage to match market shifts, and building realistic ramp assumptions into headcount plans — achieve 15-20% higher revenue attainment than companies with informal or budget-based planning. The discipline of explicit planning forces confrontation with resource gaps and strategic misalignments that informal planning glosses over.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Quota set as simple percentage increase over last year's number without analyzing territory potential or go-to-market changes. "Last year was $10M so this year is $12M per rep" — but if market is saturating, team is fully ramped, and no new product drives expansion, 20% growth per rep may require 40% more effort for same results. Quota should be informed by bottoms-up market analysis, not just top-down math.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Plan finalized in December, never revisited. Markets change, competitive landscape shifts, products evolve, headcount turns over — yet annual plan stays static. Build formal mid-year replan process: reassess assumptions, update territory assignments if needed, reallocate resources to where biggest opportunities have emerged. A plan that was created with January's knowledge but never updated with July's reality is a management liability.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Overly complex sales plans that nobody understands or remembers. "Here's our 40-page Go-to-Market Plan" — 37 pages will never be read past initial rollout. For strategic alignment, less is more: one-page plan summary with top 3 strategic priorities, key metrics, and critical resource decisions. Complexity signals unclear thinking; simplicity signals clarity of strategy.
