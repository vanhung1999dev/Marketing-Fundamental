### Thiết Kế Hệ Thống Lương Thưởng

Compensation design là một trong những most impactful và least-often-revisited management decisions in sales. The comp plan communicates — more clearly than any strategy document — what the company actually values and rewards. A well-designed comp plan aligns individual rep incentives với company goals, attracts và retains the right talent, và creates natural motivation without constant management intervention. A poorly designed comp plan creates perverse incentives, unfair situations, và drives away the talent you most want to keep.

**On-Target Earnings (OTE) Structure**: sales comp typically has two components: *Base salary* (paid regardless of performance — provides financial stability and baseline incentive to show up); *Variable/Commission* (paid based on performance — aligns rep incentive with company revenue outcome). The ratio (commonly 50/50 or 60/40 base-to-variable in enterprise B2B, 30/70 in transactional sales) should reflect: how much control does rep have over their own outcomes? (higher control → more variable); how long is ramp period? (longer ramp → more base during ramp); how complex is role? (more complex → more base to attract stronger talent). OTE should be competitive: benchmark annually against market.

**Commission Structure Design**: once OTE and base/variable split defined, commission structure details matter. Key decisions: *What is commissionable?* (new ARR only? Renewals? Expansions?); *At what rate?* (% of deal value, or flat amount per deal, or tiered rates?); *When is it paid?* (on signature, on payment, on invoice?); *Accelerators* — when rep exceeds quota, rate typically increases (100-110% → 1.25x rate; 110%+ → 1.5x rate). Accelerators are important: they create powerful incentive to exceed quota, not just meet it, and help retain the highest performers who generate the most revenue.

**SPIFFs and Contests**: short-term incentive programs (SPIFFs — Sales Performance Incentive Funds) for specific behaviors or products can be effective for directing attention to priority: "Close 3 deals in Product X this month → $2K bonus." Effective SPIFFs are: time-limited (urgency), achievable (realistic), for behaviors you actually want more of (not just disguised quarterly pressure), và paid quickly (reinforcement value decreases with delay). Overuse of SPIFFs can distract from core responsibilities — use sparingly and strategically.

**Compensation Transparency and Fairness**: unclear comp plans create anxiety và distrust. Reps should clearly understand: exactly how their commission is calculated; when they'll be paid; what happens to in-flight deals if they leave; how clawback provisions work (if any); and how plan may change. When reps can calculate their own commission from their own data and it matches what finance pays, trust is built. When commissions feel like black box — rep never quite sure how they were calculated — trust erodes steadily.

> **Bài học:** Mỗi năm trước khi finalize comp plan, run "Rep Impact Analysis" — model what each rep's commission would have been under new plan versus old plan given actual last year performance. Identify: who would earn more, who would earn less, and why. Address potential dissatisfiers proactively rather than discovering after rollout that top performers take compensation hits. The best time to find and fix comp plan problems is before announcement, not after.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Alexander Group research on sales compensation shows: companies whose compensation plans are strongly aligned to their sales strategy — rewarding behaviors that actually drive strategic outcomes — achieve 15% higher revenue growth than companies with misaligned comp plans. The most common misalignment: companies say "focus on enterprise accounts" but comp plan treats all deal sizes equally, so reps rationally pursue easier SMB deals that hit quota faster. What you measure and pay for is what you get.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Too many metrics in comp plan. If 7 different metrics affect variable pay, reps can't optimize across all simultaneously và none receive meaningful attention. Best plans have 2-3 core metrics that drive the vast majority of variable pay — typically new ARR (primary) plus 1-2 behavioral metrics (pipeline coverage, customer health score). Simplicity creates focus.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Changing comp plan mid-year without strong justification. Reps make financial decisions (car payments, mortgage) based on expected OTE. Unexpected mid-year changes — especially ones that reduce expected earnings — destroy trust even when company intent is sound. If mid-year change is necessary, grandfather existing pipeline under old plan and apply new plan to new business.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Using compensation as substitute for management. "If we just pay them more, they'll figure it out" — compensation is not a substitute for clear strategy, good coaching, and supportive culture. Reps in poorly managed, unclear, unsupportive environments leave regardless of compensation level — especially top performers who have options. Comp attracts; management and culture retain.
