### Quản Lý Lãnh Thổ Và Phân Công Khách Hàng

Territory management — quyết định ai sở hữu which accounts và prospects — có impact lớn lên cả rep performance và company revenue. Territory inequity không chỉ là management fairness issue — đó là business performance issue. Khi một rep có territory với 10x potential của rep bên cạnh nhưng cùng quota, kết quả trông như skill differential nhưng thực ra là territory differential. Research cho thấy 30-40% performance variance across reps explained by territory quality, not individual skill.

**Territory Definition Models**: các cách phổ biến để define territories: *Geographic* — rep owns specific region (Southeast Asia, Northern Vietnam); *Account-based* — rep owns named list of specific companies; *Industry vertical* — rep owns specific industries (Financial Services, Manufacturing); *Company size* — rep owns specific size bands (SMB, Mid-Market, Enterprise); *Product* — rep specializes in specific product line. Most B2B organizations use combination. Choosing right model depends on buyer journey (how geography-dependent is buying?), product complexity (does industry expertise matter?), và team size (can you afford specialization?).

**Territory Balancing**: mỗi năm, review territory balance as key annual planning activity. Metrics to balance: total addressable accounts by tier; estimated revenue potential (using firmographic data); historical win rate in each geography/segment; current pipeline penetration (what % of territory has been worked?); churn risk of existing accounts. Redistribute accounts or territory boundaries when imbalance is detected — even if it creates short-term disruption. Fairness in territory allocation is a trust issue with your team; being seen as objective and data-driven in allocation decisions builds credibility.

**Named Account Management**: for strategic enterprise accounts, "named account" list separates most valuable targets from general territory pool. Named accounts typically get: dedicated rep coverage (not shared or rotated), more executive selling resources, co-ordinated multi-functional pursuit (field + inside + executive), and board-level relationship investment. Named account list should be regularly reviewed: accounts that don't advance should be de-prioritized from named list; emerging high-potential accounts should be added. Named accounts consume disproportionate resources — ensure they're delivering disproportionate return.

**Account Transition Management**: when rep leaves or territory is reassigned, account transitions are high churn-risk moments. Best practice transition: outgoing rep documents all key relationships, outstanding discussions, commitments made, and deal status; incoming rep is introduced to key contacts by manager (not just handed account list); 30-day overlap period if possible where both reps work account together. Accounts where transition is handled poorly — customer suddenly gets no contact, or gets contact from someone who clearly doesn't know their history — are churn risks that careful transition prevents.

> **Bài học:** Run annual "Territory Audit" — for each rep's territory, assess: total account count and potential value, % of accounts contacted in last 90 days, active pipeline vs. potential, win rate, and average deal size compared to team average. Audit reveals: territories with untapped potential (rep needs to expand coverage), territories that are tapped out (rep needs new territory or headcount), và structural imbalances (some reps have dramatically higher potential than others with same quota). Use audit to drive territory adjustments before they become performance issues.

> **Phân tích sâu:** ZS Associates research on territory design shows: optimally balanced territories can increase sales productivity by 7-10% without any change in headcount, compensation, or strategy — purely through better alignment of rep resources to market opportunity. In a 10-rep team generating $15M, 8% productivity improvement equals $1.2M incremental revenue at zero additional cost. Territory optimization is among highest-ROI management activities available precisely because it's underinvested in.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Letting reps keep territories indefinitely based on tenure. "They've had this territory for 5 years, so it's theirs" — regardless of whether they're covering it effectively or whether territory has grown to point where it needs a team. Territories belong to the company, not to reps. Regular rebalancing, while occasionally uncomfortable, ensures optimal coverage of market opportunity.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Assigning new reps to worst territories as rite of passage. "Earn a good territory" culture puts new reps in setup-to-fail situations during vulnerable ramp period. Better: give new reps territories with enough opportunity to ramp successfully, even if not premium territories. Struggling to ramp in poor territory creates wrong lessons — rep may conclude they're not cut out for role when they're actually just in wrong territory.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Territory changes that lack clear communication of rationale. "Your territory is being split" announced without explanation feels arbitrary and punitive, especially when it reduces rep's potential earnings. Always explain reasoning for territory changes — what changed in market, how this enables better coverage, how rep's earnings potential is protected or enhanced. Transparency prevents loss of trust in management fairness.
