### Phát Triển Lộ Trình Sự Nghiệp

Lộ trình sự nghiệp rõ ràng (career pathing) là một trong những retention tools mạnh nhất và underused nhất trong sales management. Great salespeople — especially younger ones — want to know: "What can I become here? How do I progress? What does success look like in 2-3 years?" If you can't answer these questions compellingly, you'll lose best talent to companies that can. Conversely, organizations with clear career progression consistently report higher engagement và lower voluntary turnover.

**Multiple Career Paths in Sales**: nhiều sales orgs chỉ có one path: rep → senior rep → manager → director → VP. Nhưng not everyone wants to manage — some của your best performers want to stay in individual contributor roles. Design parallel tracks: *IC Track* — Rep → Senior Rep → Principal Rep → Enterprise Rep (with increasing territory size, deal complexity, và compensation); *Management Track* — Rep → Team Lead → Manager → Director → VP. Both tracks should have comparable compensation potential at senior levels, so going into management isn't the only way to earn more.

**Promotion Criteria Transparency**: vague promotion criteria ("when you're ready") creates resentment và political decision-making. Replace với explicit promotion rubrics: "To be promoted from Rep to Senior Rep: 2 consecutive quarters above 100% quota, demonstrated mastery of discovery methodology (assessed via role play), consistent pipeline generation above 3x coverage, AND tenure of minimum 18 months." When criteria are explicit, reps can self-assess progress và have meaningful conversations với managers about development gaps.

**Individual Development Plans (IDPs)**: every rep should have a written IDP — co-created by rep và manager — that outlines: current performance level, career goal (where do they want to be in 2-3 years?), skills they need to develop to get there, specific actions và timeline, manager commitments (what support will manager provide?). IDP is a living document reviewed quarterly, not a one-time HR exercise. Reps who feel their manager is invested in their development stay; reps who feel their manager only cares about this quarter's quota leave.

**Stretch Assignments as Development**: formal training is one development vector; stretch assignments — giving reps opportunity to do things beyond their current role — develop capabilities faster và more sustainably. Examples: asking senior rep to co-lead onboarding of new hire; involving high-potential rep in territory planning or strategy; assigning complex enterprise deal as learning opportunity with coaching support. These create development opportunity, signal trust và investment, and reveal management potential in reps who aspire to it.

> **Bài học:** Hold annual "Career Conversations" with each team member — separate from performance reviews — focused entirely on their goals và development: "Where do you want to be in 2-3 years? What are you most excited about in your work? What would you change? How can I help you develop?" Document discussion and follow up throughout year on commitments made. The act of having this conversation — genuinely caring about their trajectory, not just quarterly number — builds the relationship capital that retains good people when competitors call.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Gallup research on employee engagement shows: the single largest driver of voluntary turnover among high-performers is "not enough growth and development opportunities." It ranks above compensation, management quality, và work-life balance. In sales specifically, where talent is mobile và recruiters are relentless, the ability to articulate a compelling development path — and then deliver on it — is existential for retention of top quartile performers.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Promoting best rep to manager because they're best rep, without assessing management aptitude or desire. Top individual contributors and effective managers require different skill sets. A rep who excels at personal sales execution may struggle with coaching others, handling underperformance, or stepping back from deals to let reps learn. Always assess management readiness before promoting; make the management track optional not default for senior reps.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Career conversations only when someone is at risk of leaving. By that time, their decision is often already made. Regular career conversations throughout someone's tenure — when things are going well, not just in crisis — build relationship that prevents the quiet decision to start looking before it becomes a resignation conversation.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Creating career paths on paper that don't match actual promotion decisions. If official criteria say X but actual promotions go to people with Y, the stated criteria are fiction và people know it. Integrity between stated criteria và actual decisions is essential — inconsistency destroys trust faster than having no stated criteria at all.
