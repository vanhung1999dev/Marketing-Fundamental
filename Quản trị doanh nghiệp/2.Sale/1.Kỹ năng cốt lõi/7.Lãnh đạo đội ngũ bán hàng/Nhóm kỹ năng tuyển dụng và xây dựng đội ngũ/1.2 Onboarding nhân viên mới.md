### Onboarding Nhân Viên Mới

Onboarding là investment that determines how quickly new hire starts contributing and whether they stay long-term. Research shows: new hires who experience structured onboarding are 58% more likely to be with the company after 3 years, và reach full productivity 50% faster. Yet most sales onboarding is "here's your laptop, meet the team, shadow some calls, now start selling" — which is why average sales rep ramp time is 6-9 months and many reps never reach full potential because foundational gaps are never addressed.

**Ramp Plan Architecture**: effective sales onboarding có structured timeline. *Week 1*: product deep dive, company history and values, tools setup, meet key cross-functional partners. *Weeks 2-3*: ICP and customer personas, discovery methodology, value proposition mastery (rep can deliver crisp 2-minute pitch). *Weeks 4-6*: shadow top performers on live calls, practice sessions with manager feedback, first independent prospecting. *Month 2*: own full sales cycle with coaching support; begin working real opportunities with manager co-pilot. *Month 3*: full independence with weekly coaching; first quota target (ramped). Define milestones at each phase với objective assessments, not just time passage.

**Knowledge Transfer vs. Skill Building**: new hire onboarding often front-loads knowledge transfer (here's everything about the product, company, market) while underinvesting in skill building (actually applying that knowledge in simulated and real situations). Knowledge fades without practice; skills stick. Design onboarding to balance: 40% learn (content, training), 40% practice (role plays, mock calls, simulated demos), 20% apply (real customer interactions with coaching support). The practice component is what separates effective from ineffective onboarding.

**Buddy System and Social Integration**: paired với experienced peer ("buddy") who helps navigate the informal organization — who to go to for what, unwritten rules, team culture nuances — accelerates integration beyond formal training. Buddy should be top performer willing to invest time, not someone with extra capacity. Social integration also matters: new hire who feels connected to team is more likely to persist through the inevitable tough first months before full ramp.

**Milestone-Based Ramp Assessment**: instead of "90-day probation" with arbitrary endpoint, use milestone-based assessment: rep demonstrates product knowledge (assessed via quiz or demo), delivery of value proposition (assessed via scored role play), first opportunity created (objective), first qualified pipeline stage reached (objective). Milestones create clarity for both rep and manager about what "on track" means, and enable early identification of reps who will struggle before significant time is invested.

> **Bài học:** Create "Ramp Dashboard" per new hire tracking: knowledge assessments completed, skills assessed via role plays, prospecting activities (calls, emails, meetings booked), pipeline generated. Review weekly for first 90 days. Reps who are behind on multiple dimensions at 45 days have significantly lower probability of success at 6 months. Early identification enables: more intensive coaching, role adjustment, or difficult conversation before both sides have invested 9 months in wrong fit.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Salesforce State of Sales research: companies with formal onboarding programs achieve full rep productivity 34% faster than companies with informal or minimal onboarding. In a 5-rep team where each rep generates $1M ARR at full productivity, cutting ramp time by 2 months saves $833K in foregone revenue per rep × 2 reps hired annually = $1.7M annual impact. The ROI of excellent onboarding is consistently one of highest in sales talent management.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Classroom-only onboarding with no field application. New reps who spend 2 weeks in training and then hit the phone have zero practice in realistic situations. Interleave learning with doing from Week 1: after learning discovery methodology in morning, do practice discovery call with manager in afternoon. Massed practice beats spaced instruction.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** One-size-fits-all onboarding regardless of prior experience. A rep with 10 years in the industry needs different onboarding than someone making first sales role transition from customer success. Personalize: assess prior knowledge và skills first, skip what they already know, invest heavily in genuine gaps. Experienced reps who sit through basics they already know disengage; new reps who skip basics they need struggle.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Treating 90-day mark as "onboarding complete, you're on your own." For most complex B2B sales roles with 6+ month sales cycles, rep has barely started their own opportunities at Day 90. Formal onboarding ends, but coaching support, regular check-ins, and skill development continue through full Year 1. The transition from "onboarding" to "ongoing development" should be gradual, not abrupt.
