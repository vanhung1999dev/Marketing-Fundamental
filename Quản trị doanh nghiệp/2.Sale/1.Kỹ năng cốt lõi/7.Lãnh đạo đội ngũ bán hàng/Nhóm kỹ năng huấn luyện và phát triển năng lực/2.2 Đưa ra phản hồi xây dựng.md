### Đưa Ra Phản Hồi Xây Dựng

Phản hồi (feedback) là nguyên liệu thô của development — không có feedback rõ ràng, specific, và actionable, development chậm lại đáng kể. Tuy nhiên, nhiều managers ngại đưa ra negative feedback vì sợ làm tổn thương cảm xúc, tạo conflict, hoặc lose relationship. Kết quả: rep tiếp tục thực hành patterns xấu vì không biết, manager tiếp tục frustrated với results mà không nói thẳng, và relationship ở surface level. Học cách đưa feedback rõ ràng nhưng constructive là core management skill.

**SBI Model — Situation, Behavior, Impact**: một framework feedback hiệu quả: *Situation* — specific context ("In your discovery call with Acme on Tuesday..."); *Behavior* — what you observed without judgment ("I noticed you moved to demo within 10 minutes without asking about their current process or pain points..."); *Impact* — effect of that behavior ("which resulted in demo not connecting to their specific problems, and they seemed disengaged in the second half"). SBI gives rep clear, objective information versus vague ("your demos aren't connecting") or judgmental ("you don't listen enough") feedback.

**Balancing Positive and Developmental Feedback**: positive feedback (recognition of what's working) is as important as developmental feedback (what needs to change). Common mistake: managers only give feedback when something is wrong, so rep learns to associate feedback with criticism. Regularly call out specific things rep did well: "The way you handled that objection about price in the last call — naming their concern before addressing it — was very effective. Keep doing that." Positive feedback reinforces good behaviors AND builds relationship safety that makes developmental feedback easier to receive.

**Timeliness of Feedback**: feedback is most effective when delivered close to the behavior in question. Post-call debrief immediately after call is more effective than waiting until weekly 1:1. Memory fades, specific details blur, and the opportunity to practice the corrected approach is delayed. Build culture of "quick feedback": 5-minute debrief right after call, meeting, or significant customer interaction — "One thing that worked, one thing to try differently next time." Fast, lightweight feedback loops build skills faster than monthly formal review.

**Receiving Feedback — Manager's Own Practice**: managers who model receptiveness to feedback create teams where feedback flows in all directions — rep to rep, rep to manager, across functions. When you receive feedback: listen fully without defending; ask clarifying questions ("Can you give me an example?"); thank the person explicitly ("I appreciate you telling me this directly"); follow up with visible change. When managers visibly change behavior in response to feedback, it signals: "It's safe to give feedback here. Feedback is taken seriously."

> **Bài học:** After giving developmental feedback, close with: "What will you try differently? How will you know it's working?" This transforms feedback from one-way communication into two-way commitment. Rep's answer reveals whether they understood and internalized the feedback, and the commitment to specific action creates accountability to actually change — not just nod and continue as before.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Kim Scott's research for "Radical Candor" framework identifies: the most effective feedback sits at intersection of "Care Personally" (genuinely invested in rep's success) và "Challenge Directly" (willing to say hard things clearly). Too much care without challenge = "Ruinous Empathy" (nice but unhelpful, avoids difficult truths); too much challenge without care = "Obnoxious Aggression" (direct but damages relationship). The combination of genuine care AND clear directness enables trust-based feedback culture.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** "Feedback sandwich" — positive, negative, positive — that buries developmental message. Many managers learned this technique as a way to soften criticism, but experienced reps learn to ignore the "bread" and wait for the negative content. Worse, it can obscure the importance of the developmental message. Better: deliver developmental feedback directly and clearly, with care and context, not buried between unrelated positives.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Saving all feedback for formal performance review. Annual or quarterly review feedback comes too late to be actionable on the specific behaviors that generated it — those situations have long passed. Continuous feedback (weekly, immediate) is dramatically more effective for behavior change than periodic formal review. Formal reviews should summarize patterns already discussed throughout the period, not reveal surprises.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Giving feedback only on outcomes, not behaviors. "Your Q3 was disappointing" tells rep that results weren't good (which they know). "Across our deal reviews this quarter, I've noticed a pattern: you're moving to proposal before you've identified the economic buyer. In 4 of your 7 lost deals, economic buyer wasn't engaged. Let's change that." The second version gives rep specific, actionable behavioral insight to address.
