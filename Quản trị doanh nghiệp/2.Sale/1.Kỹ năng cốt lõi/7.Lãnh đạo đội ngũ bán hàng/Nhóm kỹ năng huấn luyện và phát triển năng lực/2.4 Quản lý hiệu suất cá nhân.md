### Quản Lý Hiệu Suất Cá Nhân

Quản lý hiệu suất cá nhân (individual performance management) là việc ensure mỗi rep trong team performing at their potential — celebrating và amplifying success, developing those not yet at potential, và having difficult conversations with those who persistently underperform. This is emotionally và tactically one của the hardest parts of sales management: caring about people as individuals while holding them accountable for business results. Getting both right is what separates great managers from good ones.

**Performance Tiers and Differentiated Management**: reps on a team fall into different performance tiers requiring different management approaches. *Top performers* (consistently above quota): protect their time từ administrative burden, invest in stretch development, recognize publicly, create retention plan; *Solid performers* (consistently meeting quota): invest in development that can push them to top tier; *Developing performers* (growing toward quota): structured coaching with clear milestones và timeline; *Underperformers* (consistently missing quota): structured PIP (Performance Improvement Plan) với clear expectations, support, và consequences. Treating all four tiers identically is unfair và ineffective.

**Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)**: when rep consistently underperforms despite coaching and support, formal PIP is appropriate. Effective PIP: *Specific* — exactly which metrics are below standard (not "your attitude is negative" but "your pipeline coverage is 1.5x vs. 3x target for 3 consecutive months"); *Measurable* — clear standard that must be reached ("pipeline coverage above 3x by end of 45 days"); *Supported* — what manager will provide to help (weekly coaching, co-pilot on discovery calls, territory review); *Time-bound* — specific end date for assessment; *Consequence clear* — explicit statement that continued underperformance leads to role change or separation. PIP is both support mechanism and documentation; it serves rep if they succeed; it serves organization if separation becomes necessary.

**Addressing Underperformance Early**: the most common performance management mistake is waiting too long — hoping underperformance resolves itself, avoiding difficult conversation, telling yourself "they're turning around." Research on involuntary turnover shows: most managers wait 6-9 months after recognizing a performance problem before addressing it directly. Early conversations are more effective (more time for rep to improve), more humane (giving person chance to address before situation becomes critical), and better for team (underperformance is visible to peers and demoralizing if unaddressed).

**Recognizing and Retaining High Performers**: performance management isn't only about addressing underperformance — it's equally about actively retaining and motivating top performers who are often least managed (because they "don't need it"). Retention of top performers requires: regular recognition of specific accomplishments; increasing challenge and responsibility; competitive compensation with upside; transparent conversation about their career trajectory; genuine relationship — not just "what's my number this quarter." Top performer voluntary turnover is often avoidable; it typically signals a management, compensation, or growth opportunity gap.

> **Bài học:** Conduct "Stay Interviews" with top performers annually — a conversation structured not around performance but around retention: "What's making you stay? What would make you leave? What could we do better? What opportunities would you be excited about?" Unlike exit interviews (too late), stay interviews surface retention risks while you can still act on them. Most managers assume their good people are happy; many aren't.

> **Phân tích sâu:** CEB (Gartner) research on manager effectiveness in performance management shows: managers who differentiate their time investment by performance tier — spending disproportionately more time developing solid performers toward top tier rather than spending most time on bottom performers — achieve better overall team results. The intuition to invest heavily in struggling reps makes emotional sense but often produces lower ROI than investing in reps who are close to breakthrough.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Inconsistent performance standards — holding some reps accountable while making exceptions for others. When team sees that some reps face consequences for missing quota while others (perhaps longer tenured or more politically connected) consistently miss without consequence, trust in management fairness collapses. Consistent standards, consistently applied, are foundation of performance culture.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Conflating performance problem with motivation problem. "They just don't want it enough" is often lazy diagnosis. Before concluding motivation, investigate: do they have right skills? Right territory? Right product-market fit? Right tools? Clear expectations? Addressing structural barriers that are making it hard to succeed often unlocks performance that motivation coaching alone cannot.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Managing to averages while ignoring distribution. "Team average is 87% attainment — we're doing OK." But if that average is 2 reps at 150% và 5 reps below 70%, the distribution reveals a different story: performance concentrated in few people with most of team underperforming. Average can mask bimodal distribution that signals serious structural or management issue.
