### Coaching Hiệu Quả

Coaching — giúp rep develop their own capability rather than telling them what to do — là highest-leverage activity một sales manager thực hiện. The difference between coaching và managing: managing gets results from today's deal; coaching builds capability that improves all future deals. A manager who spends 80% of time firefighting và directing gets short-term results; a manager who invests in coaching builds compounding capability that delivers better results quarter after quarter. The ROI of great coaching is not visible này week but is dramatic over 12-18 months.

**The GROW Coaching Model**: một framework widely used trong sales coaching: *Goal* — "What specifically do you want to achieve in this conversation / for this deal?" (establishes focus); *Reality* — "What's actually happening right now? What have you tried?" (assessment of current state without judgment); *Options* — "What are all possible approaches? What else could you try?" (generates alternatives — manager resists temptation to just give answer); *Will* — "What specifically will you do? By when? How will I know?" (commitment to action). GROW keeps manager in coaching mode (asking questions) versus consulting mode (giving answers).

**Coaching in Deal Reviews**: best coaching happens in context of real work — specific deals, specific calls, specific customer situations. In deal review: "Walk me through where this deal is and what your strategy is." Manager listens, then asks: "What's the risk in that approach? What would happen if economic buyer says X? Who else might influence this decision?" These questions help rep think more clearly without manager solving the problem for them. Over time, rep starts asking these questions of themselves — internalization of good thinking patterns.

**Call Coaching Methodology**: listening to recorded calls (with rep's knowledge and consent) provides ground truth về actual execution quality. Framework: rep listens to their own call first and self-assesses ("What did I do well? What would I do differently?"); then manager và rep discuss, starting with rep's self-assessment. This builds rep's ability to self-diagnose rather than depending on manager diagnosis. Focus coaching on 1-2 specific skills per session — depth over breadth builds more durable capability than surface feedback on 10 things at once.

**Frequency and Consistency of Coaching**: coaching works only when consistent. Research shows weekly 1:1 coaching produces better outcomes than bi-weekly or monthly sessions. In weekly 1:1 (30-45 min): pipeline review (tactical: what needs to happen this week?), deal strategy (coaching: how are you thinking about this deal?), skill development (1 specific area to work on this week), and rep check-in (how are you feeling? What do you need from me?). Consistency matters more than perfection of any single session.

> **Bài học:** Track "coaching topics" across your team — keep a simple log of what skills you're developing with each rep. Over 90 days, pattern emerges: "I've been coaching 4 of 7 reps on discovery questioning — is this a training need rather than individual coaching need? Should I run team training session instead?" Coaching log also shows whether you're spending proportionate time — are you over-investing in struggling reps while neglecting top performers who have different but equally valuable development needs?

> **Phân tích sâu:** Sales Management Association research shows: reps who receive effective weekly coaching achieve quota attainment 28% higher than reps who receive monthly or less frequent coaching. More specifically — coaching focused on specific skill development (discovery, objection handling, negotiation) versus generic motivation or pipeline status checking produced 3x better performance outcomes. Quality and specificity of coaching, not just frequency, is what drives development.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Coaching that's really consulting. "Here's what you should say in this situation" gives rep answer for one deal; coaching that develops their ability to think through situations gives them capability for all future deals. Resist urge to solve problems for reps — guide them to find solutions themselves. It's slower in the moment, faster over time.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Coaching only on current deal performance without forward-looking skill development. "Your Q3 close rate was 25%, what happened?" is useful retrospective analysis; "What skill would most improve your Q4 performance? How are we going to develop it?" is development coaching. Both are needed; the second type is underutilized.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Coaching only struggling reps while neglecting average và high performers. Stars often receive the least coaching attention ("they're fine, they don't need me") — but they have highest growth potential AND are most likely to leave if they feel invisible. Excellent performers who receive no development investment start looking for environments where they'll be challenged và stretched.
