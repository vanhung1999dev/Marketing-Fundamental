### Quản Lý Cơ Hội Bán Hàng (Opportunity Management)

Opportunity management là nghệ thuật và khoa học của việc advance individual deals from qualified to closed — prioritizing the right opportunities, developing the right strategy for each, và executing consistently to maximize win rate. Nếu prospecting là "đưa đủ deals vào pipeline," opportunity management là "close tỷ lệ cao nhất có thể từ pipeline đó." Cả hai đều critical; biết phân biệt khi nào mình đang struggling với problem nào là step đầu tiên để fix.

**Opportunity Strategy Development**: mỗi significant opportunity cần một strategy — không phải improvise tại chỗ. Core elements của opportunity strategy: *Champion identification* — ai trong buying org đang advocate for your solution?; *Economic buyer access* — have you reached the person who controls budget?; *Decision criteria* — what criteria will they use to choose?; *Decision process* — who needs to approve, in what sequence?; *Timeline drivers* — what's driving their need to decide by a certain date?; *Competitive positioning* — who else are they evaluating?. Without answers to these, you're navigating blind.

**Multi-Stakeholder Management trong Complex Deals**: B2B deals thường có 5-10 stakeholders với different roles và priorities. Mapping these stakeholders: *Economic Buyer* (controls budget, ultimate authority); *Technical Evaluators* (assess product fit); *User Buyers* (who will use it day-to-day); *Champions* (advocate for your solution internally); *Blockers/Detractors* (skeptics hoặc those who favor competitor or status quo). Strategy for each type is different — economic buyer needs ROI story; technical evaluators need proof of capability; champions need tools to sell internally on your behalf.

**Deal Reviews và Coaching**: regular deal reviews — where manager và rep together review specific opportunities — are the highest-ROI coaching activity in sales management. Effective deal review asks: "What's the current situation?" → "What's the strategy?" → "What are the risks?" → "What's the next action?" Not a status update — a strategic conversation. Manager role is not to direct every move but to ask questions that surfaces blind spots và help rep think more clearly.

**Pipeline Prioritization**: not all deals deserve equal time. Prioritize based on: fit quality (how well does our solution match their needs?), strategic value (anchor account, reference potential, logo?), probability-adjusted value (deal size × win probability), và time to close. A small high-probability deal this quarter may deserve more attention than a large low-probability deal với uncertain timeline. Make prioritization decisions explicit, không default to working what's newest or most urgent.

> **Bài học:** For every deal over a certain size threshold, write a one-page "Deal Brief": situation summary, key stakeholders và their positions, our strategy, competitive situation, key risks, và specific next actions với owners. Writing it forces clarity; sharing it enables manager coaching; updating it weekly tracks strategy evolution. Deals with written strategies close at higher rates than deals managed entirely in salesperson's head.

> **Phân tích sâu:** RAIN Group Win/Loss analysis shows: salespeople who win complex deals are 20% more likely to have conducted thorough stakeholder analysis và have an explicitly named champion who advocates internally. The champion's value isn't just information — it's internal access and influence that seller cannot achieve from outside. "No champion" is one of the strongest predictors of deal loss in complex B2B sales.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Selling to comfort zone contacts rather than building access to decision-makers. Reps often have great relationships with users or technical evaluators but never reach economic buyer. Result: champion can't get internal approval và deal stalls. If you've been in a deal 3+ months và haven't met the economic buyer, you likely have a champion problem.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Failing to re-qualify at each stage. A deal that was well-qualified 6 months ago may have changed: budget reallocated, champion left company, project priorities shifted. Deals need to be re-qualified whenever significant time has passed hoặc significant change in buying org has occurred.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Emotional attachment to deals that should be disqualified. After investing 3 months in a deal, losing it is painful — so reps sometimes hold on to zombie deals rather than face reality. "Happy ears" — hearing what you want to hear từ prospect signals rather than what's actually being said — is a common failure mode. Build peer deal review into process so others can provide reality checks on deals where rep may be too close.
