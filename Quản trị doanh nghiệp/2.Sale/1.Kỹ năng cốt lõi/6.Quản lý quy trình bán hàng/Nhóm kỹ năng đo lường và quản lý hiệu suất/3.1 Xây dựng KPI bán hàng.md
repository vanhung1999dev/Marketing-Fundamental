### Xây Dựng KPI Bán Hàng

KPI (Key Performance Indicators) bán hàng là hệ thống đo lường giúp tổ chức understand performance của sales team — không chỉ kết quả cuối (revenue) mà còn toàn bộ activity và leading indicators dẫn đến kết quả đó. KPI được thiết kế tốt trả lời câu hỏi: "Nếu team làm đúng những việc này với chất lượng này, doanh thu mong đợi sẽ là bao nhiêu?" Thiếu hệ thống KPI rõ ràng, managers chỉ react khi kết quả tệ thay vì intervene khi leading indicators xấu.

**KPI Hierarchy — Activity, Pipeline, Outcome**: sales KPIs hoạt động theo 3 tầng. *Activity KPIs* (leading indicators nhất): calls/emails/meetings per week per rep, demos scheduled, outreach volume. *Pipeline KPIs* (lagging sau activity, leading trước revenue): pipeline generated, pipeline coverage ratio, stage conversion rates, average deal size, average sales cycle length. *Outcome KPIs* (lagging indicators): revenue attainment, quota attainment %, new ARR, win rate, customer count. Managing only outcome KPIs là "steering by looking in the rear-view mirror" — bạn chỉ biết sai khi quá muộn để fix.

**The Funnel Metrics**: mỗi transition trong sales funnel nên có conversion rate tracked: Leads → Qualified → Demo/Meeting → Proposal → Negotiation → Close. Nếu bạn biết conversion rate tại mỗi bước, bạn có thể work backwards từ revenue goal: "Cần 50 closed deals → cần 100 at proposal stage (50% close rate) → cần 200 at demo stage (50% proposal rate) → cần 400 qualified leads (50% demo conversion) → cần 2000 total leads (20% qualification rate)." This "Funnel Math" converts revenue goals into activity goals.

**Designing Meaningful KPIs**: KPIs phải satisfy vài criteria: *Measurable* — có thể đo được với data available; *Actionable* — rep có thể directly influence; *Relevant* — correlated với outcomes that matter; *Time-bound* — evaluated over specific period. Avoid vanity metrics: "number of LinkedIn connections" không actionable; "number of qualified first meetings" là. Also apply Goodhart's Law: when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure. If reps know they're measured on calls/day, they'll make short meaningless calls to hit number. Measure calls + connection rates + meetings from calls to prevent gaming.

**Individual vs. Team KPIs**: team-level KPIs (total team pipeline, team win rate) tell you how the team is doing; individual KPIs tell you which reps need what kind of help. Rep with high activity but low pipeline → qualification problem. Rep with high pipeline but low close rate → late-stage execution problem. Rep with good win rate but insufficient pipeline → prospecting/territory problem. Different KPI failure modes require different coaching responses.

> **Bài học:** Hạn chế số lượng KPIs theo dõi: 5-7 là maximum có thể theo dõi meaningfully. Nhiều hơn thì tạo "measurement paralysis" — mọi người spend time tracking thay vì selling. Choose the fewest KPIs that give fullest picture of health. Typically: (1) pipeline coverage, (2) win rate, (3) average deal size, (4) sales cycle length, (5) new pipeline generated per period. These 5 tell you almost everything.

> **Phân tích sâu:** HubSpot research on sales performance tracking reveals: teams that track leading indicators (activity và pipeline metrics) alongside lagging indicators (revenue) achieve quota 19% more often than teams that track only revenue outcomes. The reason: leading indicators provide enough advance warning to course-correct — if pipeline is thin in March, you know April revenue will suffer and can act now; if you wait for April results to see this, you've lost 4 weeks.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Setting KPI targets without baseline data. "Each rep should generate 10 qualified meetings per week" — is that realistic? Without historical data on what top performers actually generate, targets may be aspirational fiction that demoralizes rather than motivates. Set initial targets based on what your best reps actually do, then incrementally raise the bar.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Changing KPI definitions mid-period. If "qualified meeting" definition changes halfway through quarter, you can't compare performance over time. Establish clear definitions before period starts; if definitions must change, create clean break points and document the change explicitly.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Using KPIs as punishment tool rather than coaching guide. "Your pipeline coverage is only 2x" should trigger "Let's look at what's blocking pipeline generation and how I can help" — not just "your number is bad." When reps fear KPI reporting, they game the data or hide bad news. When they see KPIs as useful diagnostic information, they're more candid and proactive.
