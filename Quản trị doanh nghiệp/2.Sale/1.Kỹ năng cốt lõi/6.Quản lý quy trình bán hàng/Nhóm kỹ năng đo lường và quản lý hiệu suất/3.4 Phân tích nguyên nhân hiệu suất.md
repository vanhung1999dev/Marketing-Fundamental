### Phân Tích Nguyên Nhân Hiệu Suất

Khi một rep miss quota, hoặc một team underperforms, hoặc win rate drops — đó là lagging indicator của problems that occurred upstream weeks hoặc months ago. Performance root cause analysis — đào sâu vào why performance deviated from expected — là kỹ năng quản lý then chốt, phân biệt managers phản ứng với kết quả so với managers phòng ngừa vấn đề. "Work harder" là không đủ; "Work on these specific things differently" là actionable.

**Root Cause Diagnostic Framework**: khi performance drops, trước khi conclude và prescribe, diagnose systematically: *Activity level* — is rep doing enough? (calls, meetings, outreach volume); *Activity quality* — when they do activities, are they done well? (qualification rate, demo-to-proposal conversion); *Pipeline health* — is there enough opportunity in pipeline? correct stages? fresh enough?; *Skill gaps* — discovery, objection handling, negotiation, closing?; *Knowledge gaps* — product knowledge, competitive knowledge, industry knowledge?; *Motivation/mindset* — is rep engaged, motivated, and believes success is achievable?; *External factors* — territory problem, product issue, pricing uncompetitive? Each diagnosis points to different intervention.

**Win/Loss Analysis**: most valuable source of performance insight is systematic Win/Loss analysis — structured review of why deals close and why they don't. Win analysis reveals: what capabilities are valued most, which messages resonate, which customer profiles we win consistently. Loss analysis reveals: where we're losing to competitors, what objections we fail to overcome, what product gaps cost us deals. Best practice: after every significant deal outcome, conduct brief structured debrief with rep using consistent questions.

**Cohort Performance Analysis**: compare performance of rep cohorts hired at different times — particularly, how long does it take reps to ramp? Where do most reps get stuck in their first year? If you're onboarding 5 reps per year and most of them underperform in months 6-12, the problem is onboarding and early enablement, not rep quality. Cohort analysis surfaces systemic problems in talent pipeline that individual rep performance reviews miss.

**Manager Attribution in Performance**: when multiple reps in a manager's team underperform, the common variable is the manager — not the reps. Rep-level performance problems are often systemic problems in management, coaching quality, territory assignment, or compensation design. Hold yourself to same diagnostic rigor when evaluating manager performance as when evaluating rep performance: is it skill (manager doesn't know how to coach)? Knowledge (doesn't understand product or market)? Time (too many reps)? Motivation? External?

> **Bài học:** Sau mỗi tháng, run "What Went Wrong và What Went Right" analysis — not just review the numbers but identify 3 specific things that drove positive performance và 3 specific things that drove negative performance. Document và share broadly. This creates organizational learning loop where insights from both wins và losses are captured và available for future decision-making, rather than living only in individual manager's memory.

> **Phân tích sâu:** Research on sales manager effectiveness by Sales Management Association shows: reps who receive regular, skill-focused coaching from their manager are 19% more likely to attain quota than reps who receive infrequent coaching. More specifically — coaching on specific identified skill gaps (e.g., discovery questioning technique) produces better results than generic motivational coaching ("work harder, stay positive"). Diagnosis-specific coaching is the bridge between root cause analysis và performance improvement.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #1:** Jumping to conclusions before diagnosing. "Rep isn't closing" → immediately move to close training. But if root cause is poor qualification (letting wrong deals into pipeline), close training solves wrong problem. Diagnose first; prescribe second. The right prescription depends entirely on correct diagnosis.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến #2:** Treating single data points as patterns. One bad month doesn't mean systemic problem; one great month doesn't mean you've "fixed" a chronic issue. Distinguish between statistical noise và meaningful signal by looking at multiple periods, multiple metrics, và controlling for external factors (market conditions, territory changes, headcount changes).

> **Cạm bẫy:** Attribution to "motivation" when motivation isn't actually the problem. "They just don't want it enough" is often the lazy diagnosis when real issue is skill gap, knowledge gap, territory inequity, or compensation misalignment. Lack of motivation is both overdiagnosed (as excuse for failing to identify real root cause) và underdiagnosed (when real performance issue is disengagement that needs addressing). Probe carefully before concluding motivation is root cause.
