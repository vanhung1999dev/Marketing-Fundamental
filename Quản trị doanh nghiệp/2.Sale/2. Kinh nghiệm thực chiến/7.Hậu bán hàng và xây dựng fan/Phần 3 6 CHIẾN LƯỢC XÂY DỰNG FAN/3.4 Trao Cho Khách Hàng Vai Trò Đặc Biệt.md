### Trao Cho Khách Hàng Vai Trò Đặc Biệt

Khi bạn trao cho khách hàng role trong success của bạn — advisory board member, case study subject, webinar speaker, beta tester — bạn đang doing something powerful: making them part of your story. People who contribute to something feel ownership; people who feel ownership become advocates.

**Types of special roles to offer:**

*Advisory board:* "Anh/chị có muốn join một advisory group nhỏ của chúng tôi không? Chúng tôi muốn input từ customers như anh/chị để guide product direction. 4 meetings/year, 90 phút mỗi lần." → High honor, high involvement, highest loyalty.

*Case study co-author:* "Chúng tôi muốn document kết quả mà anh/chị đã đạt được — có thể là case study cho cả hai bên. Sẽ useful cho anh/chị trong presentations và helps us show others what's possible." → Shared visibility benefit.

*Webinar speaker:* "Anh/chị có muốn share experience của mình tại webinar của chúng tôi không? Audience là [relevant professionals]." → Positions them as expert, builds their professional brand.

*Beta tester for new features:* "Chúng tôi đang develop [feature]. Anh/chị là một trong những customers đầu tiên we think would benefit — muốn early access và provide feedback?" → Makes them feel valued và special.

*Reference customer:* "Anh/chị có willing để thỉnh thoảng speak with prospects in your industry? We'd only refer similar companies, và we always ask permission first." → Trust-based honor.

**Why this works psychologically:** Commitment and consistency principle (Cialdini): once people commit to a role (advisor, speaker, advocate) they behave consistently with that role. A customer who agrees to be a reference customer doesn't churn — it would be inconsistent với their self-image as advocate.

> **Bài học:** Identify 3 customers this month who could benefit from a special role. Make the offer personal và specific: why them, what role, what they'll gain. Even one "yes" to advisory board or case study creates powerful advocate who will stay and refer.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến:** Offering roles without genuine value for the customer. "Be our reference" without any benefit for them = extractive. "Join our advisory board where your input actually shapes our product" = genuine partnership. Make sure the role delivers real value to them.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Creating too many "special" roles, diluting the specialness. Advisory board should be genuinely exclusive (5-10 people). Case studies should feel like collaboration, not just extraction of testimonials. Scarcity of meaningful roles makes them more valuable.
