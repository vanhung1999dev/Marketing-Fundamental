### Lắng Nghe Và Hành Động Theo Phản Hồi

Collecting feedback is table stakes. Acting on it visibly — và telling customers you acted on their input — is where most companies fail. "We listened and this is what we did about it" is one of the most powerful loyalty-building statements you can make. It transforms feedback from one-way data collection into genuine dialogue.

**Feedback collection mechanisms:**

*Active (you ask):*
- Quarterly check-in call with structured questions
- Post-onboarding survey (30 days in)
- Annual relationship review
- After every significant service interaction: "How did that go for you?"

*Passive (they volunteer):*
- Support tickets pattern analysis
- Community discussion themes
- Social media mentions
- Unprompted emails và conversations

**5-step Feedback Action Loop:**

*1. Collect:* Systematic approach, không ad-hoc.

*2. Synthesize:* What themes emerge? What's being said most frequently?

*3. Decide:* What can/will you act on? Be honest — not every piece of feedback can be acted on.

*4. Act:* Change something based on feedback.

*5. Communicate back:* "Nhiều customers, bao gồm anh/chị, đã feedback về [X]. Chúng tôi đã [specific action taken]." This step is the most important and most skipped.

**When you can't act on feedback:**
Be transparent: "Chúng tôi hear concern về [X]. Hiện tại chúng tôi không có resource để address trong Q3, nhưng đây là context và đây là alternatives trong meantime..."

Honesty about limitations builds trust; silence or false promises destroy it.

> **Bài học:** After collecting feedback, always close the loop with customers who provided it — even if answer is "we heard you but here's why we can't change this now." Closing the loop costs minutes but creates significant loyalty. Customers who feel heard và responded to are dramatically more retained than those who feel ignored.

> **Sai lầm phổ biến:** NPS score collection without action or communication. "We got 4/10 rating → filed in database → never mentioned to customer." Customer knows nothing changed → next survey, same rating → you have data but no improvement and no relationship benefit.

> **Cạm bẫy:** Over-collecting feedback và underacting. Survey fatigue is real — if you ask for feedback frequently but customers see nothing change, they stop responding. Act on fewer pieces of feedback but act visibly và communicate loudly when you do.
