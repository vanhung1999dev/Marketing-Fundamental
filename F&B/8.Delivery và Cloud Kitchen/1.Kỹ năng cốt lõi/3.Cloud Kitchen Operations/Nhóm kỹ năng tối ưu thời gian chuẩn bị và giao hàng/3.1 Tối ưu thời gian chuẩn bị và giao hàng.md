### Tối Ưu Thời Gian Chuẩn Bị Và Giao Hàng

Trong delivery, thời gian là currency và competitive advantage. Mỗi phút thêm vào ETA làm giảm conversion rate (khách chọn faster alternative), tăng chance khách cancel sau khi order, và ảnh hưởng platform algorithm ranking.

Nhưng rushed prep là another danger: errors increase, quality suffers, packaging là điểm dễ mistake nhất. Mục tiêu không phải "nhanh nhất có thể" mà là "consistently fast within quality standards."

---

## 3 Time Metrics Cần Track

**1. Preparation time (bạn control hoàn toàn):**
Từ khi accept order trên platform → order sealed và ready tại pickup area.

This is your primary controllable metric.

**2. Shipper wait time (partially controllable):**
Từ khi shipper arrive → order in their hands.

Ideal: shipper arrives, order is ready. Any wait time beyond 2-3 phút is operational failure.

**3. Total delivery time (platform shows to customer):**
Platform-calculated from order placed to estimated delivery. Includes your prep time + shipper travel time.

Your prep time directly affects this number. Platform algorithm rewards consistent short prep time.

## Target Prep Times Theo Loại Hình

| Loại F&B | Target Prep Time | Threshold Gây Review Tệ |
|---|---|---|
| Đồ uống đơn giản (cà phê, trà) | 5-8 phút | > 12 phút |
| Fast food / burger | 8-12 phút | > 18 phút |
| Cơm / bún / phở cơ bản | 10-15 phút | > 20 phút |
| Multi-item combo orders | 15-20 phút | > 25 phút |
| Large group orders (5+ items) | 20-25 phút | > 35 phút |

**Tại sao thresholds matter:**
Customer mental model: "Ordered at 12:00, expected at 12:30, received at 12:40" = bad experience. "Ordered at 12:00, expected at 12:45, received at 12:35" = good experience. Same food. Same actual time. Different expectation management.

## Techniques Giảm Prep Time

### Batch Preparation — Balance Freshness Và Speed

**Principle:** Pre-prepare frequently ordered components, not entire orders.

**What to batch prep:**
- Rice: always have cooked rice ready, refresh every 30 minutes (quality maintained, instant-ready)
- Broths và soups: always simmering (add fresh noodles and protein à la minute)
- Marinated proteins: done at start of service, cook to order
- Pre-portioned toppings: divided into single-serve containers at start of service
- Sauces và condiments: pre-measured, ready to include

**What NOT to batch:**
- Final assembly (appearance degrades)
- Items that don't hold quality (fresh salad tossed)
- Protein cooked to completion (holds poorly)

**Refresh cycle:**
Batch items refreshed every 30-45 phút, not left indefinitely. Quality maintenance and food safety both served.

### Mise En Place Cho Delivery

Every item for every popular dish staged at station at start of service. Nothing hunted for during service.

**Station readiness check (15 minutes before service opens):**
- [ ] Top 5 menu items' ingredients at station
- [ ] Pre-portioned toppings ready
- [ ] Packaging materials full
- [ ] Sauce containers pre-labeled
- [ ] Order management screen up and ready

### Parallel Processing

Multiple orders processed simultaneously, not sequentially.

**Anti-pattern:** Order 1 complete → start Order 2. (Sequential)

**Good pattern:** Order 1 protein on heat → while cooking, start Order 2 prep → Order 1 done → plate and pack → continue Order 2. (Parallel)

**Station assignment:**
- Hot station: cooking
- Cold station: cold prep, assembly
- Packaging station: receives from both, packs

Dedicated packaging person during peak = fastest throughput without errors.

### Order Routing Efficiency

Orders routed to correct station immediately upon acceptance — no delay in routing.

**KDS (Kitchen Display System) benefit:**
Orders appear at each station automatically upon acceptance. No printing, no verbal relay, no transcription. Station cook sees their tasks immediately.

**Without KDS:** Paper ticket printed, someone carries to station, information sometimes verbal + written errors. Add 1-3 minutes per order in coordination overhead.

## ETA Management — Promise Realistically

Setting ETA is arguably the most important skill in delivery time management.

**Critical insight:**
Platform ETA = your prep time + estimated transit time. You control your prep time setting.

**Calculate your real prep time:**

Measure 30-50 orders. Record actual prep time for each. Calculate:
- Average: X minutes
- 90th percentile: Y minutes (90% of orders complete by this time)

Use 90th percentile as your default prep time setting, NOT average.

**Why 90th percentile:**
If average is 12 minutes but 90th percentile is 18 minutes, setting ETA based on 12-minute average means 10% of orders will be late. With 100 orders/day, that's 10 late orders per day = 10 potential negative reviews. Setting based on 18-minute 90th percentile means only ~10% late — half the negative review risk.

**Peak vs. off-peak ETA:**
Set longer ETA during peak hours (Friday dinner, weekend lunch). Same ETA for all times = wrong.

Configure platform prep time: 15 phút off-peak, 22 phút peak. This adjustment alone significantly improves on-time rate.

## Shipper Coordination

**Shipper should not wait:**
Ideal scenario: order ready 2-3 minutes before shipper arrival. When shipper arrives = immediate handoff.

**If shipper is consistently waiting > 5 minutes:**
Packaging bottleneck. Too slow sealing and labeling. Or order accepted before kitchen was ready to execute.

**Pickup area readiness:**
- Insulated bags available at pickup area (shipper bags it themselves or staff helps)
- Order clearly labeled with shipper name/order number
- No confusion about which order is which
- Shipper doesn't need to enter kitchen

**Don't rush packaging:**
Rushing to reduce shipper wait = more packaging errors = negative reviews. Better to have shipper wait 2 extra minutes than send wrong order.

## Xương Máu Từ Chiến Trường

> **Bài học #1 — Set Prep Time "Nếu Mọi Thứ Thuận Lợi" — 30% Orders Late**
>
> Một cloud kitchen set prep time 12 phút cho tất cả orders ("chúng mình làm được" trong test conditions). Actual kitchen at peak: average 16 phút, nhiều orders 20+ phút.
>
> On-time rate: 65%. Constant late delivery reviews. Platform warning about on-time rate.
>
> Investigation: the 12-minute target was achievable in training/low volume conditions. At real peak (multiple concurrent orders, full packaging, shipper coordination): consistently 16-20 minutes.
>
> Fix: re-measured 50 orders at actual peak conditions. 90th percentile: 19 minutes. Set prep time to 20 minutes for peak hours.
>
> On-time rate improved from 65% to 91% within 2 weeks. Same kitchen, same speed. Just honest ETA.
>
> **Phân tích sâu:** Prep time setting should reflect real peak conditions, not training conditions or "best case." Most merchants measure prep time when testing, when relaxed, or when volume is low — then apply that number to peak service. This structurally causes late delivery. Measure prep time specifically during busy periods. Use the 90th percentile from those peak measurements. Update seasonally (summer may have higher volume than what was measured in spring).

> **Bài học #2 — No Batch Prep For Top Items: Kitchen Bị Overwhelmed Từ Minute 1 của Service**
>
> A pho cloud kitchen prepped everything à la minute. Each pho bowl: boil water, prepare broth (5 minutes minimum), cook protein (3 minutes), assemble. Total per bowl: 10-12 minutes.
>
> With orders coming in fast at lunch peak, single cook trying to start each bowl from scratch: 4 orders received in 5 minutes = 40-48 minutes of sequential work. Bowl 4 wouldn't be done for 40+ minutes after order received.
>
> Implement batch broth: large pot of broth always simmering. Each order: ladle broth (30 seconds), add protein (2-3 minutes cooking), assemble (1 minute). Total per bowl: 4-5 minutes.
>
> Result: 4 orders in 5 minutes = all delivered in 15-20 minutes (parallel processing). Triple throughput from same 1 cook.
>
> **Phân tích sâu:** Batch prep isn't about compromising freshness — it's about identifying which elements don't suffer from batch preparation (broth quality doesn't degrade from 30-minute simmering; it improves) vs. which elements must be à la minute (fresh herbs, noodle texture). Strategic batch prep means pre-building the components that hold quality while reserving à la minute effort for the components that need it. Result: throughput increases dramatically with no quality sacrifice.

> **Bài học #3 — Shipper Wait 10 Phút Average: Packaging Bottleneck Discovered**
>
> Customer reviews occasionally mentioned "delivery took forever" despite platform showing reasonable ETA. Investigation: shipper wait time logs (from platform data) showed average shipper wait at pickup: 9-12 minutes.
>
> Shippers waiting 10 minutes means 10 minutes added to every delivery time — after platform ETA already counted. Customer receiving order significantly later than expected ETA.
>
> Root cause: single person doing both final cooking AND packaging simultaneously during peak. Person couldn't be in two places at once.
>
> Fix: during peak hours (11am-2pm, 6pm-9pm), dedicated packaging person separate from cooking. Packager focuses only on packing and labeling; cooks focus only on cooking; handoff between them.
>
> Shipper wait time reduced from 9-12 minutes to 1-2 minutes. Total delivery time improved by 7-10 minutes average. Reviews about delivery time dropped significantly.
>
> **Phân tích sâu:** Packaging is often the hidden bottleneck in delivery operations. When cooks are also packaging, the context-switching reduces both cooking quality and packaging speed. The fix — dedicated packaging person during peak — costs one additional person's wages for peak hours (perhaps 3-4 hours/day). Compared to the review damage and customer retention loss from consistently late deliveries, this is clearly ROI-positive. In delivery, packaging speed determines shipper wait time, which directly determines customer experience.

---

*Tóm lại từ người trong nghề: Time optimization trong delivery là systems work, không phải asking people to move faster. Measure actual prep time in realistic conditions, not ideal conditions. Set ETA at 90th percentile, not average. Implement batch prep for high-volume components. Assign dedicated packaging person during peak. These changes compound: better ETA accuracy + faster execution + no shipper waiting = better on-time rate = better platform ranking = more orders = more revenue from same kitchen.*
