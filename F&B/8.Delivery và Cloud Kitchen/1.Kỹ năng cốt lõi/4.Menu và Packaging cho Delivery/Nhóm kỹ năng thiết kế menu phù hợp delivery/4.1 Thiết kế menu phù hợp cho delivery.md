### Thiết Kế Menu Phù Hợp Cho Delivery

Menu cho delivery không phải là copy của menu dine-in. Delivery có constraints khác hoàn toàn: food phải survive 15-35 phút vận chuyển, presentation không thể kiểm soát sau khi seal bag, không có server để explain món hoặc fix issue real-time. Menu tốt cho delivery được thiết kế từ đầu với những constraints này.

Sai lầm phổ biến nhất: upload toàn bộ dine-in menu lên delivery platform mà không test, không evaluate delivery performance của từng món. Kết quả: signature dish được order nhiều, arrive poorly, nhận bad reviews specific cho món đó, và kéo rating down cho entire quán.

---

## Delivery Menu Engineering — Khác Với Dine-In Menu Engineering

**Dine-in menu engineering criteria:**
- Popularity (order frequency)
- Margin
- Preparation complexity

**Delivery menu engineering criteria (thêm):**
- Holding time performance (quality after 20-30 phút không chế biến tiếp)
- Packaging ease và safety
- Review history (platform shows item-level reviews nếu có)
- Modification risk (item có nhiều customization = higher error rate = more bad reviews)

## Delivery-Friendly Tiêu Chí Cho Từng Món

### Nên Có Trong Delivery Menu

**Braised và slow-cooked:**
Kho, hầm, ninh — saucy dishes giữ nhiệt tốt, texture improve khi resting, không bị khô trong 30 phút.

**Noodle soup (packed riêng):**
Phở, bún, hủ tiếu — nếu broth packed riêng từ noodles và toppings. Khách assemble khi nhận. Quality excellent vì không có steam buildup issue.

**Rice-based dishes:**
Cơm gà, cơm tấm, cơm chiên — stable, holds well, satisfying AOV. Sauces/gravies help maintain moisture.

**Beverages:**
Nếu packaging đúng (dome lid, thermal cup). High margin, fast to make, consistent quality.

**Baked và wrapped sandwiches:**
Bánh mì — holds well if properly wrapped. Avoid pre-cutting (moisture penetrates cut surface faster).

### Cần Xem Xét Kỹ / Special Treatment

**Deep-fried items:**
Quality degrades significantly after 15-20 phút. Solutions: (1) double-fry technique (partially fry, customer finishes at home — include instruction card), (2) vent-hole packaging to minimize steam buildup, (3) limit delivery radius for fried items, (4) accept that fried items are higher review-risk.

**Fresh salads:**
Wilt rapidly if dressed. Solution: always separate dressing, design salad with hardier greens (not just lettuce), consider chopped format (more surface area = worse wilting) vs. kept whole.

**Delicate plating:**
Multi-component aesthetic dishes lose presentation in transit. Option: design delivery version with simpler, stable presentation và note in description "delivery presentation may differ."

**Ice cream và cold desserts:**
Radius-limited (< 3km or < 20 phút travel max). Higher risk of negative experience.

**Multi-component dishes requiring assembly:**
Some items need to be assembled at table. If sending pre-assembled: quality drops. If sending components separate: customer may not know how to assemble → confusion → complaint.

## Menu Size — Ít Hơn Là Tốt Hơn

**Lý do thu nhỏ delivery menu:**

Delivery menu lý tưởng = 60-70% items so với dine-in menu.

- Fewer items = more focused prep = better quality consistency
- Fewer items = easier inventory management (ít item risk bị 86ed mid-service)
- Fewer items = less complexity for kitchen during peak
- Platform algorithm: highly-ordered items rank higher. 30 items mỗi item ordered ít vs. 15 items mỗi item ordered nhiều → algorithm prefers latter

**Identify items để remove từ delivery menu:**

1. Items mà quality consistently drops after 20-30 phút rest
2. Items với item-specific bad reviews (platform data)
3. Items rất complex để pack mà error-prone
4. Low-margin, low-popularity items (dead weight)
5. Items heavily dependent on fresh-from-kitchen timing

## Delivery-Specific Menu Additions

Items không nhất thiết tốt trong dine-in nhưng excellent cho delivery:

### Family / Group Bundles

Single order, higher AOV, shared meals. Delivery appeals to families ordering together → bundle makes ordering easier and AOV increases.

Example: "Cơm Gia Đình 4 người — 2 cơm gà, 1 cơm chiên bò, 1 canh chua + 4 nước uống = 280K" (vs. ordering individually = 320K)

### Add-on và Upgrade Anchors

Prominently placed add-ons in menu structure increase basket size:
- "Thêm Trứng +15K"
- "Thêm Cơm +10K"
- "Upgrade lên size L +20K"

Well-placed add-ons can increase AOV 15-25% without changing core menu.

### Lunch Set / Meal Deal

Combo pricing for time-compressed buyers. "Cơm + Canh + Đồ uống = 89K" (value perception vs. ordering separate).

### Happy Hour Items

Slow hours (2pm-5pm) specific offerings: simplified menu, possible slight discount, designed for low-volume periods to fill kitchen idle time.

## Naming Và Description Cho Platform

Platform customers are browsing on mobile, making quick decisions. Item naming and description must work for mobile-speed decision-making.

### Item Naming — Platform SEO + Appetite Appeal

**Format:** [Ingredient/Cooking Method Descriptor] + [Base Item] + [Key Differentiator]

| Tên Dine-In | Tên Delivery-Optimized |
|---|---|
| "Cơm Đặc Biệt" | "Cơm Sườn Nướng Mật Ong — Bì Chả" |
| "Gà Chiên" | "Gà Rán Giòn Sốt Tương Ớt Hàn" |
| "Bún Đặc Biệt" | "Bún Bò Huế Giò Heo — Ăn Kèm Rau Tươi" |
| "Combo A" | "Combo Cơm Gà Rán + Canh + Tráng Miệng" |

**Why this matters:**
Platform search matches customer query with item name. If customer searches "gà rán giòn" and your item is named "Gà Chiên Đặc Biệt" — you may miss the match. Keywords in item names = free discovery.

### Description — Inform, Không Phải Literature

2-3 sentences maximum. Customers are scanning, not reading.

**Good format:**
"Sườn nướng mật ong với hành phi, kèm bì và chả, cơm tấm đúng kiểu Sài Gòn. Không chứa gluten. Nước mắm chua ngọt tự làm."

**Include:**
- Key ingredients
- Cooking method nếu relevant (nướng, chiên, hầm)
- Dietary flags (vegetarian, không gluten, halal nếu applicable)
- Size note nếu relevant ("Portion đủ cho 1 người ăn no")

**Don't include:**
- Brand storytelling (belongs on restaurant profile, not item description)
- Excessive adjectives ("siêu ngon," "thần thánh")
- Long history of the dish

## Menu Testing Protocol Before Launch

Before adding any item to delivery menu, it must pass:

**Step 1 — Preparation:** Cook item as per recipe

**Step 2 — Packaging:** Pack in intended delivery packaging

**Step 3 — Time test:** Leave undisturbed 25 minutes (simulate delivery time)

**Step 4 — Evaluate four criteria:**
- Temperature: acceptable warmth/coldness?
- Texture: còn acceptable texture (not soggy, not dried out)?
- Presentation: when opened, looks appetizing?
- Taste: flavor holds?

**Fail any criterion → not launch ready.** Either modify packaging, modify recipe for delivery, or remove from delivery menu.

## Xương Máu Từ Chiến Trường

> **Bài học #1 — Full Dine-In Menu Trên Delivery: Signature Dish Kills Rating**
>
> Một restaurant nổi tiếng với cá kho tộ nguyên con — signature dish, always gets praise in dine-in. Launch delivery, include full menu including cá kho tộ.
>
> Problem: nguyên con cá kho tộ in delivery packaging = impossible to present well. Fish breaks apart during transit. Customer receives broken fish in sauce, không trông like the beautiful photo.
>
> Reviews for this specific item: consistently 2-3 stars. "Khác hình nhiều quá." "Cá bị vỡ hết." Enough negative reviews pull down overall rating.
>
> Fix: two options. Option 1: remove cá kho tộ from delivery menu, redirect to dine-in only (works if substantial dine-in volume). Option 2: redesign delivery version — cá kho tộ cắt khúc, smaller portion, different packaging (container that holds fish pieces), different photo showing the delivery version.
>
> **Phân tích sâu:** Every item on delivery menu should earn its place through delivery performance testing, not by default inclusion. A signature dish that performs brilliantly in-restaurant can be a liability on delivery if it doesn't travel well. The fix isn't always to remove it — sometimes it's to redesign a "delivery version" with different presentation or portion. But the key is: test delivery performance before launch. Never assume dine-in success predicts delivery success.

> **Bài học #2 — 30-Item Menu vs. 12-Item Menu: Cùng Bếp, Khác Kết Quả**
>
> A cloud kitchen launched with 30 items — "more choice = more customers." Reality: kitchen couldn't maintain quality consistency across 30 items during peak. Different items ready at different quality levels. Error rate high (more items = more complexity).
>
> After 3 months, platform analytics showed: top 12 items accounted for 85% of revenue. Bottom 18 items: rarely ordered, but added inventory complexity and prep diversity.
>
> Decision: cut to 12 core items. Results over following 3 months: kitchen more focused, quality consistency improved, prep time decreased, error rate fell from 5% to 1.8%, rating improved from 4.1 to 4.5.
>
> Revenue: slightly lower initially (fewer options), but conversion rate higher (easier choice = more orders) and repeat rate higher (consistent good experience).
>
> **Phân tích sâu:** Menu size for delivery is constrained by operational capacity, not demand. A 30-item menu sounds like it gives customers more choice — but if the kitchen can't execute 30 items consistently at peak, it actually gives customers 30 items of inconsistent quality. The better equation: fewer items, all executed to high standard, each with good delivery performance history. Focus creates quality. Quality creates ratings. Ratings create revenue.

> **Bài học #3 — Không Test Holding Time: "Bánh Mì Bò Sốt Tiêu" Soggy Sau 20 Phút**
>
> Một cloud kitchen added a Vietnamese-French fusion bánh mì as delivery menu item without testing holding time. Sauce was poured directly onto bánh mì in store (like dine-in).
>
> Customer at 20+ minutes delivery: soggy bread, sauce soaked through. Multiple reviews: "bánh bị ướt hết," "vỏ bánh không còn giòn." Item-specific rating: 2.8 stars.
>
> Root cause analysis: sauce + bread contact for 20+ minutes = total sogginess. Simple prevention: sauce in separate small container, customer pours when ready to eat.
>
> Fix implemented: sauce always separate, include instruction note ("Đổ sốt vào bánh trước khi ăn để bánh giòn hơn"). Item-specific rating recovered to 4.4.
>
> The holding time test (Step 3 above) would have caught this BEFORE launch. Cost of catching it post-launch: weeks of bad reviews and rating damage.
>
> **Phân tích sâu:** Holding time testing is non-negotiable before adding any item to delivery menu. The 25-minute test mimics the minimum realistic delivery window. It catches every structurally problematic food/packaging combination before a customer does. The fix is almost always either a packaging adjustment (separate the sauce) or a recipe adjustment (different bread that holds better). Both are trivial changes that prevent weeks of negative reviews.

---

*Tóm lại từ người trong nghề: Delivery menu là curated collection của items mà perform well under delivery conditions — không phải copy of dine-in menu. Every item earns its place by passing delivery performance testing. Menu size should match kitchen capacity, not customer desire for choice. Item names work as platform SEO keywords. Descriptions inform quickly. And the whole system is regularly audited: remove underperformers, add items that test well, keep the menu live and accurate. Menu design is ongoing product management, not a one-time setup task.*
