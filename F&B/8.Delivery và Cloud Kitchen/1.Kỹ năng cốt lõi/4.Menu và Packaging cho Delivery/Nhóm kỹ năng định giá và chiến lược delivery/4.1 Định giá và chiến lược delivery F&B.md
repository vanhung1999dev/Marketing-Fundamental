### Định Giá Và Chiến Lược Delivery F&B

Delivery pricing là bài toán phức tạp hơn dine-in vì nhiều lớp cost thêm vào mỗi order — platform commission (20-30%), packaging cost tăng so với dine-in, và competitive landscape khác biệt (khách compare price trực tiếp nhiều quán trong 1 màn hình). Nhiều người chủ quán set giá delivery giống dine-in mà không tính các cost này, và bán từng order mà không nhận ra đang lỗ.

Pricing sai theo 2 hướng: quá thấp (margin âm, không sustainable) hoặc quá cao (visible trên app so với competitor, conversion thấp). Mục tiêu là find the number that covers all real costs với healthy margin, trong range competitive nhưng justified by quality và rating.

---

## True Cost Của Mỗi Delivery Order

Đây là công thức mà nhiều người bỏ qua:

| Cost Component | % của Order Value (Estimate) |
|---|---|
| Food cost (ingredients) | 28-35% |
| Platform commission | 20-30% |
| Packaging | 3-8% |
| Labor (pro-rated per order) | 10-15% |
| Overhead (electricity, gas, water, rent per order) | 5-8% |
| **Total cost per order** | **66-96%** |
| **Gross margin per order** | **4-34%** |

**Tại sao range rộng như vậy:**
Với platform commission 25% và food cost 30% = 55% của order value chưa tính packaging và labor. Margin có thể rất mỏng — hoặc âm — nếu giá không reflect reality.

**Ví dụ tính cụ thể:**

Món phở gà delivery giá 75K:
- Food cost: 22K (29%)
- Platform commission 25%: 18.75K
- Packaging: 4K (5%)
- Labor pro-rate: 8K (11%)
- Overhead: 4K (5%)
- **Total cost: 56.75K (76%)**
- **Margin: 18.25K (24%)**

Is 24% adequate? Depends on volume and fixed cost structure. But the key point: you must know this number for every item before setting prices.

---

## Delivery Pricing Formula

```
Minimum viable delivery price = (Food cost + Packaging cost) / (1 - Commission rate - Target margin)
```

**Ví dụ:**
- Food cost: 22K
- Packaging: 4K
- Platform commission: 25%
- Target gross margin (after commission): 30%

Minimum price = (22K + 4K) / (1 - 0.25 - 0.30) = 26K / 0.45 = **57.8K minimum**

Any price below 57.8K = margin below target. Any price above = better margin. But price too high vs. competitors = low conversion.

## 3 Pricing Strategy Options

### Option 1 — Delivery Price Premium (+20-30% so với dine-in)

Delivery menu prices explicitly higher than dine-in.

**Pro:**
- Dine-in không bị inflated
- Delivery margin maintained
- Honest về pricing difference

**Con:**
- Khách compare dine-in vs. delivery (if they know dine-in price) → feel penalized for delivery
- Risk: customer discovers markup, feels exploited

**Khi nào phù hợp:**
Strong dine-in brand. Delivery is supplementary channel. Customers accept delivery premium because they value convenience.

### Option 2 — Unified Pricing (Same giá everywhere)

Same menu price trên delivery và dine-in.

**Pro:**
- No confusion, no comparison friction
- Consistent brand perception

**Con:**
- Delivery margin thấp hoặc âm (commission eating into dine-in price)
- Requires dine-in volume to subsidize delivery margin

**Khi nào phù hợp:**
High-volume dine-in operation. Delivery is convenience play, not primary revenue driver. Margin acceptable at blended level.

### Option 3 — Delivery-Exclusive Items

Items trên delivery không có trên dine-in menu.

**Pro:**
- Avoids direct price comparison
- Allows pricing based on delivery economics, không bị anchored to dine-in price
- Can build delivery-specific brand identity

**Con:**
- Menu management complexity increases
- Separate inventory planning
- Staff training for two different menu sets

**Khi nào phù hợp:**
Cloud kitchen hoặc delivery-first operation. Want to build delivery brand independent of physical restaurant brand.

## Competitive Pricing Research

Trước khi finalize delivery prices:

**Step 1:** Open GrabFood/ShopeeFood như một khách thật

**Step 2:** Search cùng category trong cùng area (radius 2-3km từ quán)

**Step 3:** Note top 5 competitors' pricing cho same/similar items

**Step 4:** Determine positioning:

| Positioning | Approach | Khi nào phù hợp |
|---|---|---|
| Price leader | 10-15% below market average | Volume play, low-cost structure |
| Market rate | Within 10% of average | Competing on quality/rating, not price |
| Premium | 15-25% above market | Differentiated product, strong rating |

**Không cần là cheapest:**
Rating và speed (ETA) often more important than price for many customer segments. A 4.8-star quán at 15% premium may convert better than a 3.9-star quán at market price.

## Promotion Strategy

### Tại Sao Promotions Matter For Platform Algorithm

Platforms algorithmically boost merchants running promotions in search and discovery feeds. Running a 10K discount on a 100K item = 10% revenue sacrifice but potentially 20-40% more impressions on platform.

**ROI calculation:**
```
Promotion ROI = (Additional orders from promotion × Net profit per order) - Cost of discounts
```

If promotion generates 50 additional orders at 20K net profit each (after discount), and costs 30K discount × 50 orders = 1.5 triệu → Additional profit 1 triệu → ROI positive.

### Types Of Promotions

**First-order discount:**
Convert new customers who would browse but not commit. Platform often subsidizes these (merchant pays partial, platform pays partial).

Effect: best for new customer acquisition. One-time cost, but if customer has good experience → repeat orders without discount needed.

**Free delivery (hoặc platform-subsidized delivery):**
Many customers filter "free delivery" in search. If distance to most customers is short enough, absorbing delivery fee can significantly increase conversion.

**Bundle deal (combo):**
"Cơm + Canh + Drink = X" at discount vs. individual items. Customer perceives value. Merchant gets higher AOV even at discount.

**Happy hour:**
Lower prices during off-peak (2-5pm). Goal: fill kitchen capacity during slow period. Even at lower margin = better than zero orders + fixed cost running.

**Platform campaigns (GrabFood Super Sale, Shopee Double Day):**
Participate when ROI is positive. These bring platform-generated traffic that you wouldn't get otherwise. Evaluate: last campaign participation — revenue vs. margin. If net positive: participate.

## Commission Negotiation

Standard commission: 20-30% depending on platform.

**With high volume and strong performance metrics:**
- Platform partner manager may offer negotiated rate
- Typically after 6+ months of consistent high volume
- Typical negotiable range: 1-5 percentage points reduction
- At 100 orders/day average: 1% commission reduction = 1K/order × 100 orders × 30 days = **3 triệu/month savings**

**How to initiate:**
Contact platform partner manager (not customer service). Bring data: monthly order volume, rating, completion rate, on-time rate. Use these as leverage.

**Alternative:** Promote self-owned ordering channel (Zalo, call-in, website) alongside platform. Mention this to platform during negotiation — platform would rather keep you at lower commission than lose you.

## Xương Máu Từ Chiến Trường

> **Bài học #1 — Delivery Cùng Giá Dine-In: Bán 200 Đơn/Tháng Mà Lỗ**
>
> Một quán phở (dine-in focus) launch delivery để tăng revenue. Giá delivery same as dine-in (70K/tô). Logic: "Giá giống nhau, tiện cho khách."
>
> Sau 3 tháng: 200 orders/tháng delivery. Chủ vui vì "thêm doanh thu."
>
> Kế toán run the numbers tháng 4: food cost per delivery tô = 21K. Platform commission 25% = 17.5K. Packaging 3K. Overhead 5K. Total = 46.5K. Revenue 70K. Margin per order: 23.5K = 33.6%.
>
> "That's fine," said owner. Not fine: labor cost not included. Adding labor pro-rate 8K/order = total cost 54.5K. Margin = 15.5K = 22%. Still okay?
>
> Then adding: promo spend (10K/order average for first-order discounts running constantly) = effective revenue 60K. Total cost 54.5K. Margin 5.5K = 9%.
>
> At 200 orders/month: 1.1 triệu total contribution margin from delivery — barely covering the time spent managing the channel.
>
> **Phân tích sâu:** Delivery profitability requires explicit unit economics modeling — not intuition about price. Many restaurant owners don't include all cost layers (commission, packaging, labor, promo spend) in their mental model. The result is that delivery "revenue" looks good while actual margin is thin or negative. Run the full model for your top 5 delivery items. If margin < 15% after all costs, pricing needs adjustment or item needs to be removed from delivery menu.

> **Bài học #2 — Happy Hour Promotion: 40 Orders 2-5pm Từ Zero**
>
> A cloud kitchen was idle from 2-5pm daily. Fixed costs (rent, utilities, standing labor) running regardless.
>
> Implement: 2-5pm Happy Hour promotion — 15% off entire menu. Marketed through platform (set as time-limited promotion in merchant dashboard).
>
> Month 1: 40 orders/month during 2-5pm window. Margin per order lower (discount), but marginal contribution positive (ingredients + packaging costs covered, labor already paid anyway, overhead already paid).
>
> Month 3: 90 orders/month during happy hour. Some happy hour customers returning in non-happy-hour periods (acquired customer at discount, retained at full price).
>
> Net impact: 40-90 additional orders/month, mostly marginal cost (kitchen running anyway). Platform algorithm also improved (more orders = higher ranking in discovery).
>
> **Phân tích sâu:** Happy hour promotions solve the idle capacity problem that many F&B operations ignore. Fixed costs are sunk during operating hours regardless of order volume. Any order that covers variable cost (ingredients + packaging) contributes positively during otherwise idle periods. The mistake is applying full-price margin expectations to promotions that serve a different purpose: filling capacity that would otherwise be empty. Evaluate happy hour promotions not against full-price margin, but against marginal cost of additional orders.

> **Bài học #3 — Commission Negotiation: 3% Giảm = 18 Triệu/Năm**
>
> After 8 months of consistent delivery performance (4.7 rating, 2,500 orders/month average, 92% on-time rate, 97% completion rate), owner contacted platform partner manager.
>
> Data presented: monthly order volume, performance metrics, mention that competitor platform had approached with 22% commission offer.
>
> Negotiation result: commission reduced from 28% to 25% — 3 percentage point reduction.
>
> Impact at 2,500 orders/month × average order value 85K: 3% × 85K × 2,500 orders = 6.375 triệu/month savings = **76.5 triệu/year**.
>
> Time invested in negotiation: 2 meetings with partner manager. ROI: significant.
>
> **Phân tích sâu:** Commission negotiation is one of the highest-leverage financial levers available to delivery-heavy operations — and most merchants never attempt it. The platform partner manager has discretion to negotiate within a range. The key inputs: volume (more orders = more leverage), performance (better metrics = platform wants to keep you), and alternatives (having or mentioning a competitor offer). The 8-month track record of strong performance was the foundation. The negotiation itself was the mechanism. Most merchants accept the standard rate because they don't know negotiation is possible or don't feel they have leverage. With volume and good metrics, you always have leverage.

---

*Tóm lại từ người trong nghề: Delivery pricing is financial engineering, không phải gut feeling. Model every item's true cost (food + packaging + commission + labor + overhead). Price based on margin requirements, not competitor copying. Test promotions with ROI measurement. Negotiate commission after you've built volume and performance track record. And review pricing quarterly — food costs change, platform commission structures change, competitive landscape shifts. Static pricing in a dynamic market is margin erosion by default.*
